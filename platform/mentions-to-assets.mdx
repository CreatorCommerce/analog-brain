Mentions are the simplest public signal that an IRL moment happened.

Read this after [Proof And Spend](/platform/proof-and-spend). Proof And Spend defines the broader trust layer. This page goes deeper on Host and Venue mentions.

For events, they matter more than generic social proof. A mention is someone raising their hand and saying: I was here, this was real, and this place or Host mattered enough to tag.

Analog's job is to stop those moments from disappearing into the feed. Authorized mentions should become durable assets: proof, benchmarks, rate signals, Sponsor reporting, and future funding leverage.

## Product Thesis

IRL has two primary mention beneficiaries:

| Beneficiary | What the mention proves |
|---|---|
| Host | The Host can gather people, create energy, and make the Sponsor presence visible without breaking trust. |
| Venue | The space has cultural pull, foot traffic, and social resonance that can make a campaign more measurable. |

Most sponsorships only chase Host proof. Analog should also capture Venue proof when the Venue is part of the value chain.

This matters most for space-intensive campaigns: dinners, salons, fitness studios, recovery spaces, retail activations, galleries, coworking rooms, bars, restaurants, hotels, and public-space programs.

## Venue Reward Flow

When a Sponsor saves a campaign that depends on a Venue, Analog should prompt a mention-coverage upsell:

```text
Increase attribution coverage by adding a Venue reward.
```

The intended reward is a flat campaign-funded amount, such as a planning default of $200, released only when the Venue authorizes social listening or connects eligible social accounts for the campaign window.

| Step | Product action | Value created |
|---|---|---|
| Sponsor designs campaign | Campaign identifies whether Venue proof matters. | Sponsor sees attribution coverage as a budget choice. |
| Sponsor saves campaign | Analog prompts an optional Venue reward. | Sponsor can increase proof coverage before launch. |
| Host coordinates Venue | Host tells the Venue a Sponsor reward is available for authorization. | Host gives the Venue a positive reason to participate. |
| Venue authorizes listening | Venue connects socials or approves campaign-window mention capture. | Venue monetizes mentions that usually create no value. |
| Campaign runs | Analog records Host mentions, Venue mentions, attendance, spend, and proof. | Sponsor sees a fuller picture of IRL performance. |
| Sponsor reviews proof | Analog compares ratios and benchmarks. | Better underwriting, renewal, and rate setting. |

The reward is not payment for fake content or required posting. It is compensation for permissioned proof coverage.

## Actor Value

| Actor | Why this is rational |
|---|---|
| Sponsor | More social listening coverage, better attribution, stronger benchmarks, and less off-platform risk. |
| Host | A useful benefit to offer the Venue, less awkward proof coordination, and more complete credit for the room they created. |
| Venue | Extra money for authorizing signals around mentions the Venue already receives. |
| Analog | Better IRL culture data, cleaner rate intelligence, stronger renewal proof, and more reason for campaigns to stay on-platform. |

The clean Host line:

```text
The tool funding this event can also pay you a flat Venue reward if you authorize social listening for the campaign window.
```

## Metrics To Capture

| Metric | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Attendance | Baseline for how many people likely experienced the activation. |
| Host mentions | Public proof that the Host moved the room. |
| Venue mentions | Public proof that the space amplified the room. |
| Sponsor mentions | Public proof that the Sponsor presence traveled. |
| Host mentions per attendee | How much social energy the Host creates relative to turnout. |
| Venue mentions per attendee | How much social energy the space creates relative to turnout. |
| Venue mentions per Host mention | Whether the space or the Host drove the visible signal. |
| Sponsor mentions per Host or Venue mention | Whether Sponsor presence was naturally included or ignored. |
| Historical Venue percentile | Whether this event was unusually strong for that Venue. |
| Historical Host percentile | Whether this event was unusually strong for that Host. |

These ratios are private market intelligence unless the Host, Venue, Sponsor, and Analog have agreed on what can be shared.

## Graph The Value Chain

Analog should graph mention ratios over time, not only store them.

| Graph | What it shows |
|---|---|
| Host mentions vs attendance | Whether the Host creates more visible signal as turnout grows. |
| Venue mentions vs attendance | Whether the space itself increases the event's social surface area. |
| Venue mentions vs Host mentions | Whether the campaign's visible value came from the organizer, the room, or the combination. |
| Sponsor mentions vs total event mentions | Whether Sponsor presence traveled naturally or stayed outside the community signal. |
| Host percentile over time | Whether the Host is getting more fundable through better proof, better rooms, or better fit. |
| Venue percentile over time | Whether a Venue is becoming a reliable campaign asset for a category or market. |

This graph is private IRL value-chain data. It should help Analog explain the rhythm and reason behind success: why a room worked, who deserved credit, what rate was fair, and which Sponsor should fund the next activation.

## Benchmarks

The useful Sponsor sentence is not:

```text
We got some posts.
```

It is:

```text
This event scored in the top 5% of social performance for this Venue and in the top 10% for this Host's Analog history.
```

That is the move from recap to underwriting.

Benchmarks should help answer:

| Question | Benchmark use |
|---|---|
| Was the room alive? | Compare mentions per attendee against similar events. |
| Did the Venue amplify the moment? | Compare Venue mentions against the Venue's normal event pattern. |
| Did the Host deserve more credit? | Compare Host mentions and attendance against prior Host activations. |
| Did the Sponsor travel naturally? | Compare Sponsor mentions against Host and Venue mentions. |
| Should rates change? | Use proof history, supply rarity, and repeatability to recommend better rates. |
| Should the Sponsor renew? | Use ratios, spend stewardship, sentiment, and outcome signals together. |

## Product Surfaces

| Surface | Rule |
|---|---|
| Sponsor campaign save flow | Prompt for Venue reward when the campaign has a Venue, space, or location-dependent activation. |
| Campaign budget | Keep Host allocation, Venue reward, services/tools, media, and Analog revenue separated. |
| Host event setup | Ask whether the Venue should be invited into mention coverage. |
| Venue authorization link | Future surface for Venue permission, social connection, payout details, and campaign-window scope. |
| Proof And Spend | Store Host mentions, Venue mentions, ratios, and benchmarks as proof lanes. |
| Sponsor Data | Show aggregate ratios, confidence, percentile language, and renewal recommendation. |

Current MVP surfaces can approximate the flow with campaign settings, collab notes, Host event location, files/docs, campaign content, story mentions, tasks, and Sponsor data summaries.

Do not describe automated Venue payouts, tax handling, or production-grade social authorization as current behavior unless those rails exist.

## Guardrails

| Risk | Rule |
|---|---|
| Consent | Capture only authorized accounts, public signals, or scoped imports with permission. |
| Fake proof | Reward authorization, not posting, tagging, or positive sentiment. |
| Privacy | Do not sell raw attendee identity or private social data as market intelligence. |
| Host trust | The Host should not pressure a Venue into unwanted monitoring. |
| Sponsor clarity | The Venue reward is controlled activation budget, not Analog revenue. |
| Legal/payment | Payout, tax, social platform, and account-connection flows need partner and counsel review before production. |

The product posture is simple: every authorized mention should either help the Host, help the Venue, help the Sponsor decide the next dollar, or stay out of the system.
