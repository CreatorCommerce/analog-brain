Proof and spend are the trust layer. They let Sponsors increase allocations without treating every activation as a vague recap.

Read this after [Marketplace Logic](/platform/marketplace). The prior page explains how campaigns route to receptive Hosts, communities, Venues, event types, and visibility modes. This page explains why spend and proof make the work renewable.

The question is not only "what happened?" It is:

```text
Should this Sponsor deploy more capital through this Host, city, category, or playbook?
```

In active `cc-analog`, proof starts with public directory and Host-readiness signals: listing content, reviews, bookmarks, volunteer interest, organization-owned listing events, media, founding grant answers, and search/category fit. Spend cards, Stripe Issuing, Sponsor data, campaign UGC, story mentions, and transaction records are preserved full-platform logic until those archived surfaces are restored.

Cash funding raises the standard for proof. If Analog funds coffee, libations, space, supplies, print, operator time, public-good access, or grassroots field work, proof should show both spend stewardship and whether the Host's community goal became easier to accomplish.

High-ticket, public/nonprofit, and political action funding raise the standard again. Cards govern distributed Host and vendor spend; larger programs also need contracts, invoices, vendor/payee records, tax-document status, milestones, risk reviews, eligibility, restricted-use checks, public-safe summaries, compliance-safe field rollups, and AI-assisted tranche recommendations.

Social mentions should not depreciate into feed noise. For IRL, authorized Host and Venue mentions are proof assets: they show who moved the room, which space amplified the moment, and whether the Sponsor presence traveled naturally. Use [Turn Mentions Into Assets](/platform/mentions-to-assets) for the product rule.

Two-way reviews should sit on top of AI review and social listening. They capture what the partners lived: responsiveness, respect, clarity, spend stewardship, proof quality, admin burden, and whether the relationship should repeat. Their trust job is to keep good actors on Analog, constrain or remove bad actors, and create social reinforcement through proof. Use [Two-Way Reviews](/platform/two-way-reviews) for the review flow and guardrails.

## Spend Infrastructure Focus

Spend infrastructure is the MVP's fintech enablement wedge. The point is to make budget deployment controllable enough that Analog can learn which conventions earn trust.

| Convention to learn | Product signal |
|---|---|
| Budget shape | campaign budget, collab allocation, event set, Host allocation, dinner balance, fund, program lane |
| Spend permission | amount, category, merchant, expiration, event context, proof expectation |
| Proof standard | transactions, receipts, content, files, attendance, tasks, shipments, Sponsor data |
| Funding history signal | prior checks written, prior checks received, amount ranges where permitted, funding reliability, proof completion, renewal or increase history |
| Review rule | Sponsor review, Host review, 90-day checkpoint, closeout review, visibility setting |
| Reallocation rule | proof packet, Sponsor summary, unused funds, anomalies, unlock decision |
| Vendor and tax record rule | vendor/payee record, W-9 status where needed, payment context, duplicate detection, 1099 support flag |
| Public trust rule | eligibility notes, receipts, anomalies, public summaries, access or participation report |
| Political action rule | entity authorization, approved uses, receipts, field proof, privacy boundaries, compliance notes, national/state/district/county/city/ward/event rollups |
| Investment safety rule | investment memo, allocation model, contracts, invoices, risk register, proof gates, tranche recommendation |

Every controlled dollar should teach which Hosts are dependable, which Sponsor asks pull supply, which proof is persuasive, and which product conventions should become defaults.

## Vendor Records And 1099 Support

IRL spend creates many small records: Hosts, Venues, photographers, printers, artists, caterers, local operators, market vendors, contractors, and service providers. If those records stay scattered, Sponsors inherit the worst part of community-led IRL: back-office cleanup.

Analog should absorb that burden as needed. The product direction is to keep reusable vendor/payee records and support 1099 workflows through secure, approved rails when payment context requires it.

| Product job | What Analog should simplify | Guardrail |
|---|---|---|
| Payee record | Name, entity, role, campaign context, Host/Venue relationship, files, payment rail, and proof links. | Keep sensitive tax details in secure workflows, not general docs or open notes. |
| Tax-document status | Whether a W-9 or other tax document is needed, requested, received, expired, or blocked. | Do not collect more tax data than the payment workflow requires. |
| 1099 support | Track reportable-payment context and support exports, partner filing, or filing workflows where required. | Do not promise automated filing or tax advice until production partners and counsel-reviewed controls exist. |
| Duplicate detection | Catch the same Venue, contractor, Host, or vendor appearing under multiple names. | Preserve source and campaign context so dedupe does not erase useful local history. |
| Sponsor reporting | Show that Analog is handling vendor records, not handing the Sponsor a year-end mess. | Keep Sponsor views operational: status, exceptions, readiness, and next action, not raw sensitive tax data. |

This is part of spend stewardship. A Sponsor should feel that running a campaign through Analog reduces vendor administration, tax-record chasing, and year-end reporting friction in addition to improving proof.

## Future Host Treasury Rail

One-off spend cards are right for event-scoped budgets, micro grants, and early trust building. Deeper financial partnerships need a different rail.

For trusted Hosts, Analog should eventually support an Analog checking-style Host treasury account through compliant banking partners. Instead of issuing only temporary cards, a Sponsor could fund a larger approved balance into the Host's central Analog operating account.

```text
Sponsor allocation
-> funding agreement and proof standard
-> Host treasury balance
-> Host pays vendors, venues, staff, artists, supplies, rent, and recurring costs
-> Analog monitors spend patterns, receipts, budget pacing, and proof
-> Sponsor receives recipient-authorized summary grades and renewal recommendations
```

This is not an MVP promise and should not be described as current banking. It is the long-term financial infrastructure path for community and IRL businesses.

| Layer | What changes |
|---|---|
| Host autonomy | The Host gets a real operating balance for recurring work instead of a card for one event. |
| Sponsor control | The Sponsor moves from transaction-by-transaction control to rules, authorized summary grades, anomaly review, proof gates, and renewal logic. |
| Analog oversight | Analog reviews fraud/waste/abuse signals, budget pacing, restricted-use risk, missing receipts, vendor concentration, proof completeness, and mission drift. |
| Privacy boundary | Sponsors receive abstracted reporting by default, not a raw private bank feed, unless contract terms and permissions justify deeper review. |
| Product depth | Hosts need banking-quality tools: balance, envelopes, sub-ledgers by fund, cards, ACH/bill pay, invoices, vendor records, receipt capture, proof prompts, and renewal history. |

Use this rail when the Host is a recurring operator, arts organization, venue, nonprofit partner, city lead, community business, or strategic connector that deserves a deeper balance and better financial tools.

Do not use it for untrusted Hosts, one-off activations, weak proof programs, or Sponsors that require direct control of every transaction. Human approval, KYC/KYB, tax, contract, privacy, and banking-partner requirements are part of the product, not afterthoughts.

## Derived Financial Grade Rule

A grade derived from bank or treasury activity is sensitive derived financial data if it is tied to a recipient. It should be treated that way even when Analog does not share raw transactions, balances, vendors, or account details with the Sponsor.

The cleaner operating posture:

| Requirement | Rule |
|---|---|
| Recipient authorization | The recipient explicitly authorizes Analog or the fintech partner to analyze account activity. |
| Sponsor sharing | The recipient explicitly authorizes sharing a summary grade or status with the Sponsor. |
| Purpose disclosure | The Sponsor use is disclosed, such as: "used to determine whether additional grant funds are released." |
| Objective methodology | The grade methodology is documented, objective, and not misleading. |
| Explainability and dispute | The recipient can see the grade or at least the key reasons, and can dispute errors. |
| Safer labels | Avoid vague labels like "irresponsible." Prefer "within expected grant-use range," "review recommended," or "outside program norms." |
| Contract limits | The Sponsor may use the grade only for the disclosed program purpose and may not treat it as general financial reputation data. |

Eligibility decisioning raises the sensitivity. "Unlock more funds" means the grade can become a gatekeeping factor.

| Scenario | Risk note |
|---|---|
| Business entity recipient | Lower risk than consumer use, but still sensitive and contract-bound. |
| Individual or consumer recipient | Higher risk; consumer reporting, privacy, fair-lending, or similar rules may become relevant. |
| Government Sponsor | RFPA, privacy, procurement, grant, public-record, or program-specific rules may matter. |
| Repayable, advanced, or credit-like funds | Credit, fair-lending, FCRA, ECOA, or similar concerns can come back in. |
| Routine third-party scoring of financial behavior | Counsel should check whether the product is drifting toward consumer reporting agency territory. |

Frame this as potentially acceptable only when it is consented, purpose-limited, non-credit, grant-specific, accurate, explainable, and contractually controlled, with counsel review for sensitive scenarios. Treat the grade as sensitive derived financial data, not as harmless because it lacks raw account details.

## Dogfood Spend Rule

Analog should use the same spend and proof standards on itself that it asks Sponsors and Hosts to trust.

For round-one market formation, AnalogNYC spend and any active AnalogSF spend should be categorized through Analog. Internal spend should teach which budget categories, merchant categories, receipts, proof requirements, and anomaly checks are clear enough to sell to customers.

| Internal KPI | Spend/proof requirement |
|---|---|
| Social growth | Attach spend to the owned IRL, media, or field moment that produced audience growth. |
| Category growth | Connect spend to the category, Host, ritual, or city supply it strengthened. |
| Community perspective | Capture Host notes, field notes, sentiment, referrals, and trust risks. |
| Partnership review | Capture Sponsor and Host review notes after closeout or every 90 days for active collabs. |
| Direct response | Tie invitations to RSVPs, claims, replies, check-ins, attendance, or applications. |
| Spend transparency | Categorize city spend by campaign, event set, Host event, merchant, use case, and proof expectation. |

If Analog cannot explain its own city spend, it should not ask Sponsors to believe the platform will explain theirs.

## Proof Lanes

| Lane | What it proves |
|---|---|
| Spend transactions | Money was used through approved workflows. |
| Funding history | Sponsors have written checks before; Hosts have received and stewarded checks before. |
| Contracts and invoices | Large or non-card spend has scope, milestone, approval, and deliverable evidence. |
| Attendance or RSVP | People showed up or claimed interest. |
| Host mentions | The Host created public signal around the activation. |
| Venue mentions | The space created or captured public signal around the activation. |
| Sponsor-visible UGC | The activation produced media proof where appropriate. |
| Mention ratios | Host mentions, Venue mentions, Sponsor mentions, and attendance can be compared for underwriting. |
| Benefit claim | Sponsor created useful attendee value through a claim, discount, sample, access benefit, or QR flow. |
| Files and docs | The operator captured context, brief, proof, or research. |
| Tasks and shipments | Required logistics, product, print, or asset flows were completed. |
| Audience graph | Hosted pages, imports, check-ins, or claims support privacy-safe audience learning. |
| Two-way reviews | Sponsors and Hosts recorded whether the partnership was responsive, respectful, clear, clean, and worth repeating. |
| Public-space activation readiness | The Host prepared the permit path, event risk tier, insurance or COI needs, site plan, weather plan, vendor/revenue notes, and official status before funding or renewal. |
| Public-space social impact | Participants reported belonging, connection, trust, agency, respect, meaningfulness, or cross-group contact where safe to measure. |
| Play and sport access | The program made play, youth sport, school and community sport, rec leagues, or active public-space participation easier to access, with safeguards and public-safe reporting. |

Do not show comments as Sponsor UGC, raw private attendee data, or analytics-heavy Host surfaces that expose too much context.

Funding history should create comfort without exposing sensitive data. A Sponsor can see that a Host has received USD-range or count-based funding before and completed proof. A Host can see that a Sponsor has funded prior collabs and behaved reliably. Exact amounts, source documents, private contracts, and raw financial data should stay permissioned.

Authorized Venue mention capture is especially useful when the space is part of the value chain: dinners, retail activations, fitness studios, recovery spaces, galleries, hotels, coworking rooms, restaurants, bars, public-space programs, and venue-anchored series.

## Two-Way Review Lane

Two-way reviews answer questions that receipts and social listening cannot.

The default trigger:

```text
90-day checkpoint for active long-running collabs
or closeout when funds are spent, the campaign ends, or the collab closes
```

| Reviewer | What to ask | What it informs |
|---|---|---|
| Sponsor reviewing Host | Was the Host responsive, easy to work with, clean with funds, useful with proof, additive to the room, and worth funding again? | Future Sponsor approval, allocation size, renewal memo, risk note. |
| Host reviewing Sponsor | Was the Sponsor clear, respectful, responsive, reliable with budget/assets, low-friction, and worth working with again? | Sponsor quality, Host trust, matching guardrails, admin-burden product work. |
| Analog/operator review | Did the proof, spend, files, field notes, and review text point to a repeatable pattern? | Product conventions, no-go rules, support needs, marketplace learning. |

Reviews should become structured signals, not raw gossip. Store dimension ratings, written context, recommendation, visibility, and dispute status. Summaries can feed future matching, but private comments should stay permissioned.

The best review output is practical:

```text
Strong renewal candidate.
Clean spend, fast communication, complete proof packet.
Sponsor asked for more asset lead time next round.
```

Do not turn reviews into a public star-score board. Use them to explain future approvals, protect Hosts from bad-fit Sponsors, and make the next allocation smarter.

The lane should produce action, not only sentiment:

| Review pattern | Proof/spend response |
|---|---|
| Strong proof plus strong reviews | Increase confidence, recommend renewal, raise allocation, and make the actor easier to match. |
| Strong proof but poor partner review | Investigate whether the result was extractive, high-burden, or not worth repeating. |
| Weak proof plus poor reviews | Pause renewal, require remediation, tighten spend/proof controls, or route away from the actor. |
| Disputed or sensitive review | Keep matching constrained until a human resolves the review, evidence, and visibility state. |

## Mention Asset Ratios

Use mention ratios to move beyond recap screenshots.

| Ratio | What it answers |
|---|---|
| Host mentions / attendance | Did the Host create visible energy relative to turnout? |
| Venue mentions / attendance | Did the space amplify the moment relative to turnout? |
| Venue mentions / Host mentions | Was social signal driven more by the space, the Host, or both? |
| Sponsor mentions / Host or Venue mentions | Did Sponsor presence travel naturally or stay invisible? |
| Event percentile for Host | Was this activation unusually strong for the Host's own history? |
| Event percentile for Venue | Was this activation unusually strong for the Venue's normal pattern? |

The strongest proof sentence is benchmarked:

```text
This event scored in the top 5% of social performance for this Venue
and in the top 10% for this Host's Analog history.
```

Keep raw account access, private signals, and attendee identity behind permissioned workflows. Sponsors should get useful ratios, confidence, and renewal recommendations by default, not unnecessary raw data.

Graph these ratios over time when possible. The goal is to understand the IRL value chain: which Host, Venue, market, or Sponsor fit created the visible signal, and which part deserves the next dollar.

## Audience Capture

Audience proof should help Sponsors decide what to fund next without turning communities into raw ad inventory.

| Capture path | Use it for | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Hosted page | RSVP, ticketing, check-in, benefit claim, consent, event-story context, and future recap proof. | Prefer when Analog controls or co-hosts the invite. Treat the page as a community brand asset for fundable moments, not just a generic invite. |
| Attendance import | Luma, Eventbrite, Partiful, LineLeap, CSV, member list, venue guest list. | Import only with scoped permission and consent source. |
| QR or claim flow | Discounts, samples, class credits, venue benefits, retail or hospitality offers. | Capture claim and redemption state, not unnecessary PII. |
| Authorized Venue social connection | Venue mention coverage, Venue benchmark, space-intensive activation proof. | Reward authorization only; do not buy fake posting or positive sentiment. |
| Sponsor outcome match | Shopify, CRM, POS, booking, loyalty, lead, app install, reservation, or CSV/webhook. | Match privately and report tiers, counts, or confidence before row-level detail. |

## Underwriting Lanes

| Lane | What it answers |
|---|---|
| Spend stewardship | Was capital used responsibly across budgets, receipts, vendors, approved uses, unused funds, and anomalies? |
| Host goal progress | Did funded cash remove the blocker the Host named? |
| Investment safety | Is the sponsorship still safe to fund based on allocation, contracts, invoices, proof gates, brand safety, public risk, and tranche performance? |
| Public/nonprofit grant safety | Did the program preserve eligibility, restricted uses, public trust, privacy, mission fit, fraud/waste/abuse controls, and defensible reporting? |
| Public-space activation safety | Did the activation preserve permit readiness, insurance/COI status, site-plan clarity, weather contingency, vending/revenue boundaries, accessibility, and public-safe impact proof? |
| Youth and community sport safety | Did the program preserve school and nonprofit eligibility, youth safeguards, partner records, approved uses, safe participation, and access proof? |
| Political action safety | Did the program preserve campaign/advocacy controls, approved uses, privacy boundaries, Sponsor authorization, local trust, and compliant field proof? |
| Social/category growth | Did content, repeat participation, category mentions, habit formation, or market activation improve? |
| Mention coverage | Did authorized Host and Venue mentions make the event more attributable, benchmarkable, and renewable? |
| Operator upside | Is the Host dependable, happy with the partnership, and earning enough for the work to continue? |
| Community sentiment | Was the Sponsor presence additive, brand-safe, and welcomed? |
| Partnership quality | Did the Sponsor and Host review each other as clear, responsive, respectful, and worth repeating? |

## Unlock Logic

A Host should earn a bigger or next allocation when:

- market-growth proof is stronger than expected
- spend stewardship is clean
- the community welcomed the Sponsor presence
- the Host earned enough operator upside to keep caring
- two-way reviews show the partnership was healthy enough to repeat
- review history shows the Host is a good actor who should stay on-platform
- the Sponsor can explain the value internally
- the activation can repeat with less custom work

Direct attribution can help, but it is not the only reason to unlock more budget. A credible underwriting signal can be enough when spend, sentiment, category growth, and repeatability are strong.

Analog should pause, constrain, or stop future allocation when repeated proof and review patterns show misuse of funds, disrespect for community norms, retaliation, missing proof, unresolved disputes, or Sponsor behavior that makes good Hosts less likely to stay on-platform.

## Category Benchmark Rule

Marketplace proof should compare an activation to the Sponsor's category, not to a generic event average.

| Sponsor category | Benchmark should emphasize |
|---|---|
| eComm/CPG | product trial, claims, category mentions, shopper or attendee sentiment, repeat participation, sales-adjacent signal where available |
| SaaS/Tech | attendee fit, account or partner relevance, follow-up meetings, adoption moments, strategic room quality |
| Civic/Public-Non-profit | access, eligible participation, local legitimacy, public-safe reporting, spend stewardship, mission fit |
| Political Action | compliant spend, geography, volunteer/action proof where permitted, privacy-safe field learning |
| Local retail/hospitality | Venue mentions, neighborhood signal, merchant fit, local sentiment, repeat local engagement |

The renewal question should be:

```text
For an organization like this Sponsor,
did this Host, Venue, community, or event type outperform enough
to deserve the next dollar?
```

## Public And Nonprofit Proof Rule

For public, foundation, NGO, or nonprofit funds, proof should answer:

1. Did money reach eligible downstream Hosts or partners under clear rules?
2. Did spend match approved uses, receipts, timing, and program scope?
3. Did the activation create privacy-safe evidence of access, participation, local legitimacy, recurrence, mission fit, or participant-reported social impact?
4. Can the funder explain the result without exposing sensitive data or overclaiming outcomes?

Keep public-safe summaries separate from internal risk notes. Public reports should tell the truth clearly; internal notes can carry anomalies, conflicts, exceptions, missing documentation, or sensitive community context.

Use raw PII only inside scoped matching workflows where legally and commercially allowed.

For public-space, park, library, corridor, wellness access, arts, youth, sports, cleanup, stewardship, and social connection programs, use [Public Space Social Impact Measurement](/references/public-space-social-impact-measurement) to decide whether a one-time survey, pre/post design, or public-space entry/exit survey belongs in the proof packet.

For public-space activations with permitting, insurance, weather, site-plan, vending, or revenue complexity, use [Public Space Activation Infrastructure](/references/public-space-activation-infrastructure) before funding or renewal. The proof packet should show not only that the event happened, but that the Host understood the official path, named the support gaps, and kept public-risk boundaries visible.

The safe public readout should be specific:

```text
spend was clean
participation happened
respondents reported belonging, connection, trust, or meaningful contact
known groups or response gaps are named
the next allocation has a clear improvement hypothesis
```

Do not turn a small participant survey into a claim that the program solved loneliness, improved public health, reduced crime, or changed civic behavior unless that claim is backed by an appropriate evaluation design.

## Political Action Proof Rule

For campaigns, PACs, ballot initiatives, advocacy organizations, party committees, or donor coalitions, proof should answer:

1. Did money reach authorized local Hosts, organizers, vendors, or event contexts under clear rules?
2. Did spend match approved uses, receipts, timing, geography, and campaign/advocacy scope?
3. Did the activation create permissioned evidence of attendance, volunteer energy, issue action, field learning, or local sentiment where legally allowed?
4. Can the Sponsor decide the next state, district, county, city, ward, Host, or event allocation without exposing sensitive voter, donor, volunteer, attendee, or political-affiliation data?

Keep political proof purpose-limited. Do not sell raw political-affiliation data, volunteer lists, voter files, donor data, or attendee identity as general market intelligence. Counsel-reviewed controls should govern production programs.
