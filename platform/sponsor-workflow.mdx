Use this workflow when the Sponsor already has a goal, budget, or campaign idea.

The MVP workflow should answer one commercial question: can Analog earn the right to deploy this Sponsor's community activation budget again? Every campaign should test a budget convention, a supply-creation path, and a proof standard.

## 1. Create The Campaign

In the Sponsor dashboard, create a campaign with:

- campaign name
- sponsor perspective: eComm Brand, Tech Company, or Public/Non-profit Fund
- objective or sponsor thesis
- budget
- optional end date
- mission
- market persona
- main value-add
- special requests
- host visibility and eligibility rules
- mailing-address requirement when product, supplies, or printed materials may ship

Write the objective as a testable market-growth question. Example: "Can LA recovery creators bring new runners into a brand-funded Saturday morning ritual?"

Write the budget as a learning contract:

| Budget field | What to decide |
|---|---|
| Amount | How much capital the Sponsor is comfortable putting into controlled field spend. |
| Allocation rule | Whether budget is reserved for one Host, one event set, a dinner balance, a city/category fund, or a broader program. |
| Spend guardrails | Approved categories, timing, proof, assets, and operational boundaries. |
| Renewal rule | What evidence would justify another allocation, larger balance, or broader program. |

When the Sponsor is funding a program rather than a one-off activation, express it as a market:

| Market setup | What to define |
|---|---|
| Demand signal | The fund/program mission, audience, geography, and reason the Sponsor is spending now. |
| Supply invitation | Who can apply or be prospected: hosts, spaces, restaurants, connectors, clubs, communities, or local operators. |
| Selection criteria | What makes a Host eligible, sponsor-safe, and likely to produce useful proof. |
| Spend convention | Whether the budget is a Host allocation, dinner balance, event-set lane, access fund, city fund, or recurring program. |
| Proof and unlock | What evidence earns renewal, a bigger budget, or more supply creation. |

Frame the proof by sponsor perspective:

| Sponsor perspective | Campaign proof should emphasize |
|---|---|
| eComm Brand | Product trial, content, category mentions, customer overlap, repeat participation, and sales-adjacent signals. |
| Tech Company | Strategic accounts, qualified attendees, community credibility, adoption moments, partner/customer conversations, and pipeline-adjacent signals. |
| Public/Non-profit Fund | Access, participation, public benefit, community formation, local outcomes, equity, and civic proof. |

Also capture the Sponsor's non-negotiables:

- brand safety
- approved Host and category boundaries
- spend control and receipt requirements
- sentiment or social proof expectations
- category-growth evidence
- operational support needed from Analog

## 2. Create Event Sets

Use event sets to split the campaign into prospectable lanes.

Event sets are marketplace enablement objects. They turn a Sponsor's broad intent into a supply lane the product can observe.

Each event set should have:

- name
- market persona
- special requests
- files or assets Hosts can use
- optional prospecting plan

Examples:

- Austin neighborhood tastings
- LA recovery run clubs
- NYC founder breakfasts
- Bowling venue access nights

## 3. Publish Or Update The Application Portal

Use the application portal when the Sponsor wants inbound Host pitches.

Collect:

- community or host identity
- event or activation idea
- requested amount
- expected attendees
- relevant socials
- proof surfaces
- sponsor fit

## 4. Share, Prospect, Or Invite Hosts

Choose the path:

- sponsor-shared campaign or fund page when the goal is to let the market route qualified supply toward the opportunity
- direct invite for known hosts
- application portal for standardized inbound
- event set prospecting for off-platform supply
- city lead when supply is relationship-driven
- catalog search when the sponsor needs more options

When the goal is supply creation, make the campaign shareable before treating sourcing as a list-building exercise. The shared ask should carry who qualifies, what budget range or balance exists, what spend is allowed, what proof is required, and where the host should apply or request an invite.

Sponsors can share through their own channels. Analog can share through city leads, `The Connector`, partner networks, social, and owned IRL rooms where community builders can hear the ask directly and refer the right operators.

Use [Build Event Prospecting List](/how-to/build-event-prospecting-list) for external sourcing.

Managed outreach, agency-style GTM, city leads, media, and partner lists are acceptable in the MVP when they create supply for a clear brand fund or program. Keep the output product-readable: applications, host profiles, pitch docs, event sets, proof plans, or explicit no-go learning.

Agency services should be used to create the market and expose the repeatable convention. If a service step repeats across two or more campaigns, route it into product, a template, a contractor playbook, or a priced managed-support package.

## 5. Review Applications

Evaluate applications with the same questions used in [Testing Grid](/market/testing-grid):

- Does this host have trust?
- Is sponsorship native?
- Can spend be controlled?
- Can proof be captured?
- Could success unlock more budget?

## 6. Accept The Host

Accepting an application creates or links a host and creates a collab. The collab grants access to the sponsor budget.

## 7. Monitor The Activation

Use campaign detail and sponsor-host detail screens to review:

- sponsored hosts
- host-created events
- spend activity
- content or proof
- story and post mention signals when available
- asset requests and shipments
- shared files and docs
- host tasks and special requests

## 8. Decide The Unlock

After the activation, decide:

- renew
- increase allocation
- repeat with changes
- move budget to another host
- create more host supply through city leads or RFEs

Use the underwriting lanes in [Proof And Spend](/platform/proof-and-spend): spend stewardship, social growth, operator upside, category growth, brand growth, and community sentiment.

If the sponsor would deploy more budget with the same rules, the convention is getting stronger. If the sponsor needs custom service work to believe the result, decide whether to productize the repeated step, price it as managed support, or narrow the use case.
