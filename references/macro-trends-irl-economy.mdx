This page is the evidence shelf behind Analog's why-now thesis. It does not try to prove that events are interesting. It explains why a sponsor-funded, governed, measurable IRL layer can become strategically important as digital channels get crowded, AI makes content cheaper, people seek real connection, and sponsors look for trusted ways to create market affection.

<Info>
Last reviewed: May 28, 2026. Treat this as directional market context, not a financial forecast.
</Info>

Read this as the first Market Education page. It explains why the market is opening. Then read [Tech Company Landscape](/references/tech-company-landscape), [eComm Brand Landscape](/references/ecomm-brand-landscape), and [Public And Nonprofit Fund Landscape](/references/public-nonprofit-fund-landscape) to see why existing tools do not own the funding layer, followed by [Social Proof: AI To IRL](/strategy/social-proof-ai-to-irl) for founder-supplied live signals.

```text
Digital attention is crowded
-> AI makes digital abundance cheaper
-> people still need trust, belonging, and useful rooms
-> sponsors need channels that create real affinity
-> events work, but are operationally heavy
-> Analog underwrites which IRL deserves the next sponsor dollar
```

## Evidence Map

| Trend | Signal | Analog implication |
|---|---|---|
| Digital channels are huge and crowded | IAB/PwC reported that U.S. internet advertising reached nearly $300B in 2025, the highest level in the report's history. | Sponsors already spend heavily in digital environments where attention is expensive, copied, and algorithmic. Analog should position IRL as a proofable market-growth channel, not a vibes-only brand spend. |
| Marketing budgets are constrained while AI takes budget share | Gartner's 2026 CMO Spend Survey put average marketing budgets at 7.8% of company revenue, with 56% of CMOs saying they lacked enough budget to execute strategy, while AI initiatives take 15.3% of marketing budgets and only 30% report mature AI readiness. | Analog has to reduce operational waste. The pitch is not "run more events"; it is "fund better Hosts, govern spend, capture proof, and know what deserves renewal." |
| Events generate business value but need sharper execution | Cvent's 2026 Event-Led Growth report says 83% of marketers report steady revenue from events and 72% say deals close faster when prospects attend, while average events per year dropped from 29 to 16 as teams focused on smarter strategies. | This supports a quality-over-volume thesis. Analog should help Sponsors fund fewer, better, trusted rooms and know which should repeat. |
| Live experiences still pull mass demand | Live Nation reported Q1 2026 sponsorship AOI up 21%, tied to brand demand around its fan base, while 2026 concert ticket sales through April were up 11% to more than 107M. | Large-scale live demand gives permission for the category. Analog's job is to build the sponsor layer for distributed, smaller, community-level IRL that Live Nation-scale infrastructure will never touch. |
| Creator spend is mainstream, but creator selection and measurement are hard | IAB projected U.S. creator ad spend at $37B in 2025, up 26% year over year, while naming creator selection, measurement, and standards as major challenges. | Dot connectors and community builders can become the IRL version of trusted creator supply. Analog must make them legible, fundable, measurable, and safer for brands to work with. |
| Social connection is now a public issue | The U.S. Surgeon General frames social connection as important to individual, community, and societal health. Harvard's 2024 Making Caring Common report found 21% of U.S. adults feel lonely, and 75% wanted more fun community events and connection-focused public spaces. | Public, nonprofit, and government funders should see Analog as infrastructure for funding local connection, not just as a sponsorship tool. Proof should include participation, access, repeatability, and community trust. |
| Social and dating platforms are moving toward IRL | Hinge launched a $1M One More Hour fund for in-person Gen Z connection. Bumble IRL created city-based in-person events with partners across concerts, fitness, food, volunteering, and more. | Digital-native social companies are admitting that real-world context matters. Analog should help sponsors and platforms turn offline connection into a governed, repeatable market motion. |
| AI makes trusted rooms more valuable | Pew found in February 2025 that 52% of U.S. workers felt worried about future workplace AI use, while only 36% felt hopeful. | As digital work and content become more automated, trusted rooms become adoption surfaces: dinners, salons, run clubs, founder circles, customer councils, and community rituals where people decide what to believe and who to work with. |

## Sponsor Translation

| Sponsor perspective | Best interpretation of the macro shift |
|---|---|
| eComm Brands | Digital performance remains important, but physical-product brands need memorable, repeatable community moments that create sustained memories and impression, social status, product trial, social proof, and category affection. |
| SaaS/Tech Companies | AI, SaaS saturation, and enterprise fatigue make trusted local rooms more valuable for adoption, ecosystem building, partner learning, and account access. Investment remains concentrated in hubs today. |
| Civic/Public-Non-profit Funds | Loneliness, local disconnection, civic trust, and social infrastructure create a fundable public-good lane for real-world programming. |
| Political Action Sponsors | Campaigns and advocacy organizations still depend on real-world field trust, local leaders, and grassroots participation; Analog should treat this as a high-control lane, not generic event marketing. |
| Partner Platforms | Ticketing, creator, venue, newsletter, and community platforms can use Analog to attach governed sponsor capital and proof to the supply they already aggregate. |

## Strategy Implications

| If this is true | Analog should |
|---|---|
| Events work, but execution is too heavy | Productize host funding, spend governance, proof packets, and renewal logic. |
| Digital abundance keeps rising | Make IRL proof more credible than impressions alone. |
| Creator spend has become mainstream | Treat connectors and community builders as the next trusted supply class, with better permission, proof, and funding rules than generic influencer spend. |
| Public connection is a civic problem | Build a clear public/nonprofit/government lane around access, participation, trust, and local outcomes. |
| Political action depends on local trust | Keep political and advocacy programs separate from public/nonprofit work, with compliance-safe spend, proof, privacy, and field-allocation controls. |
| Sponsors are budget constrained | Prioritize market-entry paths where one operating model can repeat across hosts, cities, and sponsor categories. |
| The strongest rooms are local | Use city leads to find hosts, record field context, package opportunities, and route proof back into the underwriting loop. |
