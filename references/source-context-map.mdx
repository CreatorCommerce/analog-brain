## Founder Agentic Sponsor UX Context Captured

The founder clarified the desired Sponsor and Host product feel:

- Sponsor-side should feel agentic even when the Sponsor is clicking through UI instead of chatting.
- The product should understand why the Sponsor is in the session and help finish that job quickly.
- UI should start simple, then render the next useful field, setting, recommendation, workflow, or proof view.
- Advanced features can stay hidden until context makes them relevant.
- If the Sponsor keeps drilling deeper, the product should keep routing to more data, settings, files/docs, recommendations, or human approval instead of hitting a dead end.
- Host-side should stay simple and snappy, only revealing deeper controls when they help with funding, execution, proof, or visibility.

## Founder Two-Way Review Context Captured

The founder added a product feature direction for review-driven trust:

- Two-way reviews should sit on top of AI reviews and social listening.
- Sponsors should rate Hosts in a thoughtful, step-by-step review experience after meaningful engagement checkpoints.
- The practical standard should be every 90 days for active long-running collabs and closeout when funds are spent, a campaign ends, or a collab closes.
- Sponsor review questions should include responsiveness, ease of work, whether funds were used appropriately and as intended, proof quality, and whether the Sponsor would work with the Host again.
- Host-side review should also exist so Analog can protect good Hosts from unclear, high-burden, or bad-fit Sponsors.
- Review data should become future approval context for Sponsors looking at a Host, but it should use summaries, visibility controls, and guardrails rather than raw public star scores.

## Analog Seed Deck Context Captured

The seed deck sharpens the operating story:

- Analog is the operating layer for powering sponsor participation in the IRL economy.
- The macro shift is digital fatigue, loneliness, and a move from buying things to buying experiences.
- IRL communities and local events are becoming a more important trust surface, but the economics are fragile.
- The missing layer is partnership formation, governed budget allocation, and attributable earned media that is structured, measurable, and renewable.
- Sponsors need brand safety, spend controls and receipts, sentiment and social proof, category-growth evidence, and operational support.
- Hosts carry a hidden balance sheet: space, setup, supplies, catering, vendors, speakers, insurance, promotion, content, and unpaid coordination time.
- The host promise is to move from unpaid or scattered community work to funded, scoped, repeatable work: apply once, avoid fronting money when possible, build a track record, work only with brand-fit sponsors, and earn operator upside.
- The product motion is `Discover -> Collaborate -> Measure`: match trusted communities, execute governed spend, then renew based on spend, sentiment, category lift, and outcomes.
- AI can assist sponsorship proof and community-growth analysis across spend stewardship, social growth, operator upside, category growth, brand growth, and community sentiment.
- The seed deck emphasized running as the strongest first vertical because it has dense supply, clear brand demand, recurring weekly rituals, obvious spend categories, and a category every wellness, apparel, and beverage sponsor is courting. Later founder direction supersedes this as the company-wide focus: running is a proof case, while cash-based Host enablement is the broader strategic layer.
- The broader market map includes 12 community families, 90+ club types, and an estimated 3M+ global clubs, scored by how cleanly Analog's proof loop applies.
- The business model can include Sponsor plans, usage credits, campaign fees, sourcing services, managed activation support, and platform fees on funded partnerships. Later payments, card, interchange, and treasury/float revenue can be part of the model once compliant rails exist; they are not the near-term product promise.
- The strategic race is to make a new behavior feel trusted and normal: funding individuals and small businesses to steward a sponsor's IRL mission with clear scope, proof, and spend controls.

## Founder Cash Funding Playbook Context Captured

The founder clarified that Analog should not overfit to run clubs or product gifting:

- Run clubs and movement communities are useful proof cases, not the whole vertical strategy.
- Analog should rally around cash-based funding as the vehicle that helps Hosts accomplish their own goals.
- Product gifting and spray-and-pray influencer seeding are a different market, optimized for unboxing odds and direct-response logic. Analog should not chase that as the center.
- Micro Grants can fund small but meaningful costs such as coffee, libations, supplies, rentals, affordable space, print, and accessibility.
- Macro Grants can fund the best organizers at roughly USD 1k to USD 5k/month for recurring and deeper activations, strategic rooms, operator margin, and high-leverage community access.
- Some operators may become so aligned and effective that they look like a form of employment or retainer.
- Services and tools matter: insurance flows, print-on-demand, space orchestration later, documents/contracts, files, vendors, local designers, and proof kits.
- Print and publication are a near first race-to-market objective. AnalogNYC, AnalogSF, and later city brands should publish profitable eComm- and retail-driven city media with upcoming events, community founder profiles, scene data, fund opportunities, and subscription FOMO.
- Municipal, NGO, and public-good money is strategic if Analog can reduce fraud, waste, abuse, reporting friction, and slow reimbursement while getting money into honest builders' pockets.
- The Lululemon/Goodstack grant flow is a training example for corporate funds: public fund story, registration form, eligibility and verification, invitation to apply, application review, award, reporting, and renewal story.
- Nike's Community Impact Fund adds a support model where a global brand mission becomes local grantmaking through employee proximity, nonprofit and school eligibility, trained teammate review, regional grant partners, annual application windows, and play and sport access.
- Analog should learn the corporate grant and support mental model, then add the IRL layer: Event set fit, Host trust, local review context, approved spend, services/tools, safeguards, proof packets, and next-dollar recommendations.

## Founder Public Space Social Impact Measurement Context Captured

The founder asked to better store and understand Trust for Public Land's Community Compass concepts for measuring the social impact of public spaces:

- Use the Community Compass as a reference pattern for public-space, park, library, corridor, cleanup, stewardship, wellness access, youth, arts, sports, civic, and nonprofit downstream funding programs.
- Measure more than attendance: new connections, cross-difference interactions, belonging, social connection, openness, norms of respect, agency, engagement intent, trust, desire for cross-group interaction, meaningfulness, and open-ended reflection.
- Use measurement as both design and proof. Start from the program's theory of change or logic model, collect feedback in real time, analyze who benefits or is left out, and route learning into the next allocation.
- Survey design should match the moment: one-time post-event surveys, public-space visit surveys, multi-session pre/post surveys, or entry/exit surveys where practical.
- Demographic data can reveal equity gaps, but it needs a safety, appropriateness, and comfort test, optional/skippable questions, aggregate reporting, and no small-cell disclosure.
- Public-space social impact proof should become part of Analog's public/nonprofit proof lane through files/docs, hosted pages, QR assets, proof packets, Sponsor data summaries, public-safe reports, and future survey templates or response imports.
- Keep claims cautious. A lightweight survey can support public-safe learning and renewal decisions; it should not claim long-term health, safety, economic, or civic transformation without a stronger evaluation design.

## Founder Untaped Public Space Activation Context Captured

The founder asked to digest Untaped's NYC public-space recommendations and consider how Analog can help:

- Untaped shows a public realm ecosystem with strong community creativity but structural barriers: fragmented permitting rules, outdated digital tools, disproportionate insurance costs, siloed agency processes, and overstretched public-space teams.
- The research involved 55+ stakeholders across all five boroughs and frames public-space programming as a driver of social cohesion, local economic growth, public health and safety, and access to arts and culture.
- The "what we learned" pattern matters for Analog: fragmentation, administrative burden, hidden costs, mobile-unfriendly tools, weather vulnerability, community groups needing structural support, costly impact measurement, and vending/concession/revenue complexity.
- The eight recommendations are: centralize public programming leadership, create a tiered event qualification rubric, establish a public realm evaluation platform, improve permitting UX/UI, build a one-stop events permitting shop, launch a public-space event stager, create a Seasonal Activation Partner Permit, and establish a NYC Public Space Stewardship Fund.
- Analog should not claim to be the City or official permitting authority. The product role is readiness, support, funding, and proof: reusable Host records, public-space activation readiness files, event risk tier inputs, permit-path notes, insurance/COI support, site-plan support, weather plans, vending/revenue flags, stewardship fund operations, and public-safe reports.
- The near-term Analog layer can run through existing objects: universal application, Event set, files/docs, tasks, application portal, Event Prospecting Catalog, Asset Need Intelligence, spend card, vendor/payee records, proof packet, and regional grant shelf.
- The future product layer may include a permit navigator, event risk tier engine, space stager, permit status integrations, stewardship fund dashboard, and public-space impact dashboard only when source-of-truth, partner, agency, legal, and privacy boundaries are clear.

## Founder Sponsor Perspective Context Captured

The founder clarified that Analog should separate four commercial lanes while owning regional supply first:

- eComm Brands sell physical things to many people.
- SaaS/Tech Companies sell digital things to fewer strategic users, enterprises, accounts, or ecosystems.
- Civic/Public-Non-profit Funds underwrite public-good experiences and outcomes.
