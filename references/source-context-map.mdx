## Analog Seed Deck Context Captured

The seed deck sharpens the operating story:

- Analog is the operating layer for powering sponsor participation in the IRL economy.
- The macro shift is digital fatigue, loneliness, and a move from buying things to buying experiences.
- IRL communities and local events are becoming a more important trust surface, but the economics are fragile.
- The missing layer is partnership formation, programmable budget allocation, and attributable earned media that is safe, measurable, and renewable.
- Sponsors need brand safety, spend controls and receipts, sentiment and social proof, category-growth evidence, and operational support.
- Hosts carry a hidden balance sheet: space, setup, supplies, catering, vendors, speakers, insurance, promotion, content, and unpaid coordination time.
- The host promise is to move from side hustle to funded vocation: apply once, avoid fronting money, build a track record, work only with brand-fit sponsors, and earn operator upside.
- The product motion is `Discover -> Collaborate -> Measure`: match trusted communities, execute programmable spend, then renew based on spend, sentiment, category lift, and outcomes.
- AI is used to underwrite sponsorship proof and community growth across spend stewardship, social growth, operator upside, category growth, brand growth, and community sentiment.
- The strongest first vertical is running because it has dense supply, clear brand demand, recurring weekly rituals, obvious spend categories, and a category every wellness, apparel, and beverage sponsor is courting.
- The broader market map includes 12 community families, 90+ club types, and an estimated 3M+ global clubs, scored by how cleanly Analog's proof loop applies.
- The business model can include campaign fees, sourcing services, payout administration, percent of ad spend on marketed partnerships, and future interchange or treasury revenue on funds.
- The strategic race is to make a new behavior feel safe and normal: funding individuals and small businesses to steward a sponsor's IRL mission.

## User Media Strategy Context Captured

The user added a binding strategy for media and owned IRL:

- Analog should use media and its own IRL to build the next great gathering economy.
- Social should document communities and amazing events happening everywhere.
- `The Connector` should exist as a Substack newsletter.
- `The Connector` should also become a quarterly print volume with regional editions, such as `The Connector Vol. 2: The Northeastern Edition`.
- YouTube should feature documentary-style videos about city leads and the scenes they are building.
- City leads remain the boots-on-the-ground center: circulating through communities, meeting people, recording interviews, gathering video/audio, and hosting their own community-leader meetups.
- A Head of Content should plan the editorial calendar, channel strategy, city priorities, sponsor perspective, and production system.
- A contracted or assembled media team should handle writing, design, editing, video, copy, and publishing from content submitted through Slack.
- The loop is city lead field capture -> content team production -> distribution -> more people in the tent -> owned Analog IRL -> more field capture and host/sponsor supply.

## User Sponsor Perspective Context Captured

The user clarified that Analog should build for three sponsor perspectives:

- eComm Brands sell physical things to many people.
- Tech Companies sell digital things to fewer strategic users, enterprises, accounts, or ecosystems.
- Public/Non-profit Funds sell altruistic experiences and outcomes to the public.

These three perspectives supersede broader sponsor-category sprawl. Other sponsor examples should route through one of the three unless the user later adds a fourth perspective.

## User Connector Rockstar Context Captured

The user asked how Analog can turn dot connectors and community builders into the next rockstars.

Treat this as a binding strategy direction:

- Dot connectors and community builders should become visible talent inside the gathering economy.
- Analog should make their hidden trust labor legible, fundable, bookable, and culturally respected.
- Media, `The Connector`, YouTube, print, owned IRL, city lead programs, connector profiles, proof ledgers, and sponsor-safe packaging should all reinforce this motion.
- Guard against flattening community builders into generic influencers or exposing fragile communities without permission.

## User Prestige Media And Funding Context Captured

The user asked how Analog can bring Vogue/GQ-style energy to community builders, event organizers, and dot connectors on the Analog platform, with media prestige and ongoing funds they can tap.

Treat this as a binding strategy direction:

- Analog should bring high-status editorial treatment to community builders: profiles, photography, video, print, covers, lists, interviews, and city reports.
- The platform should convert prestige into fund access, not just attention.
- Builders should feel like there is an ongoing universe of sponsor, public, category, city, and editorial fund opportunities to unlock.
- The funding experience must remain governed: approved funds, spend scopes, proof requirements, community trust constraints, and unlock logic.
- Do not frame this as unrestricted money or an ungoverned wallet until compliant payout, tax, and money movement rails exist.

## Documentation Style Context Captured

This repo follows the `cc-dev-docs` discipline:

- every live page has `title`, `description`, and `pageType`
- canonical facts live in reference pages
- strategy pages explain doctrine
- how-to pages explain operator execution
- glossary changes follow terminology changes
- release notes are added for new use cases, workflows, or product rules
- custom CSS uses the same Poppins/Lora direction and compact Mintlify styling posture

## Conflict Rule

When sources conflict, update the canonical page first and then update summaries, how-to pages, navigation, glossary, and release notes as needed.

If the user later supplies a use case, correction, or preference, it supersedes conflicting material here and in the upstream summaries.
