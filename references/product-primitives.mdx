## MVP Boundary

Analog is currently an MVP for Sponsors and Hosts to fund, operate, and prove community-led IRL activations through structured campaigns, host events, application portals, files/docs, managed outreach, hosted event pages, and temporary operational spend cards.

The wedge is governed IRL spend: Sponsor budget becomes campaign structure, Host-side execution, shared spend, proof, reporting, sentiment, and renewal logic. The current product is still an MVP and is not yet running production-grade auth, billing, payout, or payment infrastructure.

The platform focus is fintech enablement plus marketplace enablement. Analog is not trying to become a bank in the MVP. It is shoring up the spend infrastructure, campaign conventions, Host approval flow, proof loop, and supply-creation motion needed to earn the right to deploy community activation investments on behalf of brands and businesses.

The strategic funding model is cash-based Host enablement. Product, samples, and merch can support a collab, but the core product should learn how cash, controlled spend, services, tools, proof, and renewal help Hosts accomplish their actual community goals.

## IRL Culture Graph Primitive

Analog's product memory should be more than event records. The larger primitive is an IRL culture graph: a privacy-safe, provenance-aware map of events, recurring rituals, cultural operators, audience hypotheses, sponsor fit, spend behavior, proof quality, and renewal logic.

The strategic analogy is simple:

| Data category | What it makes actionable |
|---|---|
| B2B account data | Companies, contacts, roles, and prospecting paths. |
| Digital creator data | Creator reach, digital audiences, content performance, and social distribution. |
| Analog IRL culture data | Which real-world communities gather, who can move them, what sponsors they fit, and which funded moments create audience or category potential. |

In the MVP, this graph is assembled from product objects rather than a standalone data product: campaigns, event sets, applications, host profiles, hosted pages, spend cards, transactions, files/docs, proof packets, city lead notes, media capture, and renewal decisions.

## Brand Fund Market Loop

The MVP should create small, observable markets around brand funds and programs.

```text
Brand fund -> supply response -> governed spend -> proof -> selection rule
```

| Step | Product primitive | What Analog learns |
|---|---|---|
| Brand fund declares demand <Tooltip headline="Brand fund" tip="A sponsor-backed budget lane with a mission, eligibility, geography, spend rules, proof standard, and renewal condition."><span className="analog-help">?</span></Tooltip> | Campaign, event set, application portal, outreach, files/assets | Which sponsor asks are clear enough for hosts to understand and act on. |
| Supply responds <Tooltip headline="Supply response" tip="Hosts, spaces, connectors, and operators apply, are invited, or are created through GTM and city work against a clear fund/program."><span className="analog-help">?</span></Tooltip> | Campaign applications, host opportunities, prospecting, pitch docs, city lead packets | Which host profiles, pitch formats, incentives, and channels create quality options. |
| Spend is governed | Collab, allocation, spend card, allowed categories, expiration, transactions | Which spend controls are strict enough for sponsors and usable enough for hosts. |
| Proof is captured | Sponsor data, campaign content, event analytics, files, tasks, shipments | Which evidence sponsors actually use to renew, expand, or reallocate. |
| Selection rule improves <Tooltip headline="Selection rule" tip="The practical logic for deciding who receives the next dollar, who needs more support, and which motions should stop."><span className="analog-help">?</span></Tooltip> | Unlock decision, renewal packet, no-go note, product blocker, playbook | Which conventions should become defaults in the platform. |

This is what "free market" should mean in the MVP: not an ungoverned open marketplace, but a constrained learning market where sponsors declare demand, supply has room to emerge or compete, and the product watches what deserves the next allocation.

## Cash Funding Ladder Primitive

Strategy pages may refer to a funding ladder. In the MVP, these are campaign/event-set conventions, not separate financial products.

| Funding ladder | Current product approximation | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Micro Grant | Small collab allocation, spend card, event-scoped budget, or campaign application request | Use for $100 to $500/month style support: coffee, libations, supplies, rentals, print, accessibility, or light venue help. |
| Starter Series | Event set, collab allocation, multiple Host events, required todos, proof packet | Use when a Host can repeat monthly or across a small series. |
| Macro Grant | Larger recurring campaign allocation with clearer proof and renewal conditions | Use for the best Hosts, strategic rooms, operationally excellent organizers, or deeper recurring activations. |
| Operator Retainer | Managed support, city lead work, agency engagement, or contracted operator outside the core MVP object model | Keep separate from unrestricted Host wallet language; route product learning back into campaigns, event sets, catalog, and proof. |
| Public Fund | Campaign or event set with public-good eligibility, controlled spend, reporting, and review requirements | Use transparent admin/support framing and avoid promising public-sector outcomes the product cannot measure. |

Until compliant payout and tax rails exist, cash funding means controlled allocations, grants, event-scoped budgets, spend cards, approved program budgets, or managed support. It does not mean withdrawable balances.

## Operating Layer Shape

The product direction is an operating layer, not a directory, generic fintech app, or one-off agency service.

| Layer | Product job |
|---|---|
| Discover | Proven hosts, clubs, gatherings, venues, and operators are vetted, ranked, matched, and routed into campaigns or event sets. |
| Collaborate | Sponsor budget becomes governed spend, controls, guardrails, tasks, assets, docs, and execution support. |
| Measure | Receipts, content, attendance, sentiment, category lift, and outcomes become proof for renewal or unlock decisions. |
| Learn | Repeated budget, supply, proof, and services patterns become marketplace and fintech conventions that the platform can standardize. |

The current MVP implements pieces of this loop. Strategy pages should describe the full loop, while how-to and reference pages should stay honest about current product boundaries.

## Future Media And Funding Direction

Strategy pages may refer to builder profiles, connector profiles, editorial features, fund pages, funding tabs, proof ledgers, editorial fund drops, and high-ticket sponsorship underwriting as future product directions.

Do not describe these as current MVP objects unless they map back to existing surfaces:

| Future direction | Current MVP approximation |
|---|---|
| Builder or connector profile | Host story, pitch page, files, docs, and catalog context |
| Editorial feature | Workspace file, Analog Doc, campaign content, or external media artifact |
| Fund page | Campaign, event set, application portal, RFE, RFC, or grant-like intake |
| Funding tab | Host sponsor budgets, collabs, spend cards, and available campaign allocations |
| Proof ledger | Sponsor data snapshot, campaign event analytics, content proof, spend transactions, and files/docs |
| Editorial fund drop | Campaign or event set launched alongside media, owned IRL, or `The Connector` |
| Sponsorship investment memo | Deep research doc, Sponsor brief, Analog Doc, proof packet, catalog context, and assisted outcome summary |
| Allocation model | Campaign budget, event sets, collab allocations, planned invoices/contracts, and approval notes |
| Risk register | Files/docs, tasks, proof QA notes, public reporting constraints, insurance docs, and anomaly notes |
| Tranche gate | Unlock decision, renewal packet, proof standard, budget state, and Sponsor summary |

Until production money movement exists, fund access means governed sponsor allocations, grants, event-scoped budgets, spend cards, or approved program budgets. It does not mean unrestricted wallet balances.

Brand funds and programs are the cleanest market-making primitive: they give sponsors a reason to declare demand, hosts a reason to apply or create supply, and Analog a controlled environment to observe pricing, proof, spend behavior, and renewal.

High-ticket sponsorships should not be described as card-only flows. For large programs, Analog's product direction is sponsorship governance: cards for distributed spend, plus files/docs, invoices, contracts, deliverables, risk reviews, proof gates, and AI-assisted renewal or tranche recommendations.

## Account And Org Posture

The strategy target is one organization model with roles and permissions. The current MVP still exposes `Sponsor` and `Host` as product actors, but both can carry `organizationId` and `organizationRoles`.

| Object | Current meaning | Practical rule |
|---|---|---|
| Organization role | Host, sponsor, or both on one org | Treat dual-role orgs as the direction of travel; do not create strategy that assumes rigid account types forever. |
| Sponsor | Product actor that funds campaigns; in the market this means any capital deployer that wants structure, oversight, and proof | Owns campaign budget, application pages, event sets, outreach, data, files, proof, and host allocations. |
| Host | Product actor that creates events and operates against sponsor budgets | Owns host story, events, tasks, files, sponsor budgets, social connections, and pitch surfaces. |
| Brand kit or design kit | Reusable identity assets | Stores logo/mark, colors, accent media, and other media for sponsor pages, host pages, and event surfaces. |
| Host story | Sponsor-facing host narrative | Stores short/long description, organization name, and website for pitch and trust surfaces. |

## Funding And Campaign Objects

| Object | Meaning | Key rules |
|---|---|---|
| Campaign | Sponsor-owned funding and strategy container | Stores mission, market persona, main value-add, budget, funding state, required todos, host visibility, gift/address requirement, auto-renew preference, and outreach settings. |
| Event set | Campaign subgroup for a market/persona or activation lane | Stores market persona, special requests, assets, file attachments, status, host count, tracked event count, and prospecting settings. |
| Campaign outreach | Campaign-level managed email/direct mail settings | Current MVP exposes managed outreach first and a future self-serve view; outreach requires campaign market persona. |
| Event set prospecting | External prospecting run tied to one event set | Uses standard or heavy plan, dedicated inboxes, warm-up period, daily send limits, application-link CTA, and a three-month auto-disable concept. |
| Application portal | Sponsor-owned pitch intake page | Can be sponsor-wide or campaign-scoped, themed, translated, customized with questions/CSS, and tied to GA4 measurement. |
| Campaign application | Host sponsorship pitch | Structured pitch fields plus custom answers and attachments; accepting can create/link a host and create a collab. |
| Collab | Sponsor-host relationship inside a campaign | Grants allocation, allowed categories, status, optional event set, and the host's access to sponsor budget. |
| Campaign event | Sponsor/campaign-side tracking event | Tied to a collab and optionally an event set; used for sponsor-side event analytics. |

## Host Execution Objects

| Object | Meaning | Key rules |
|---|---|---|
| Host event | Host-created event or activation | Can exist without sponsor backing, and can attach sponsor support. The creation wizard currently selects at most one sponsor budget, while the data model has sponsor summary arrays for future multi-sponsor shape. |
| Hosted event page | Public `/e/:eventId` page configuration | Stores draft/published state, theme, background, RSVP fields, ticket types, custom CSS, and custom HTML. |

| Shipment log | Sponsor-side shipment proof | Tracks product samples, event supplies, printed promo materials, merch, signage, order/tracking references, and linked asset requests. |

| Object | Meaning | Key rules |
|---|---|---|

## Product-Facing Terms

Use:

- Sponsor, not only brand
- Host, not only creator
- Collab for sponsor-host relationship
- Host event for host-created event
- Campaign event for collab-attached tracking event
- Event set for a campaign's market/persona or activation lane
- Workspace file or Analog Doc for files and docs inside the product
- Spend card for temporary operational card

## Current Product Boundaries

The MVP intentionally does not include:

- payouts or creator withdrawals
- reimbursements
- unrestricted wallet balances
- Stripe Connect payouts
- 1099 workflows
- production ticketing or payment collection
- native mobile apps
- wallet passes
- fully automated attribution
- fully automated legal contracting, insurance, or payout gates
- fully automated high-ticket sponsorship underwriting
- native invoice, purchase order, escrow, or procurement rails

Money remains platform-controlled. Hosts receive event-scoped operational spend access, not withdrawable balances.

## UGC And Social Proof

Sponsor-visible proof can include:

- image or video post mentions
- story mentions with usable media or source URLs
- uploaded or seeded proof items

Do not show comments as sponsor UGC. Do not put sponsor analytics-heavy UGC grids on host screens.

## Implementation Reality

Current product facts to preserve in docs:

- Web app: Vite React routes under `apps/web/src/routes`.
- API: Fastify routes under `apps/api/src/routes`.
- Store: JSON-backed model in `packages/database/src/jsonStore.ts`.
- Services and handlers enforce most product rules.
- Money is integer minor units.
