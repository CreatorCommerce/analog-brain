## MVP Boundary

Analog is currently an MVP for Sponsors and Hosts to fund, operate, and prove community-led IRL activations through structured campaigns, host events, application portals, files/docs, managed outreach, hosted event pages, and temporary operational spend cards.

The wedge is governed IRL spend: Sponsor budget becomes campaign structure, Host-side execution, shared spend, proof, reporting, sentiment, and renewal logic. The current product is still an MVP and is not yet running production-grade auth, billing, payout, or payment infrastructure.

The platform focus is fintech enablement plus marketplace enablement. Analog is not trying to become a bank in the MVP. It is shoring up the spend infrastructure, campaign conventions, Host approval flow, proof loop, and supply-creation motion needed to earn the right to deploy community activation investments on behalf of brands and businesses.

The strategic funding model is cash-based Host enablement. Product, samples, and merch can support a collab, but the core product should learn how cash, controlled spend, services, tools, proof, and renewal help Hosts accomplish their actual community goals.

The strategic proof model is mention-aware. Public Host and Venue mentions should become proof assets when they are authorized and campaign-relevant. Analog should help Sponsors understand which rooms, spaces, Hosts, and categories created visible IRL signal instead of letting mentions disappear into recap screenshots.

The hosted event page posture is not "win because we have event pages." Basic event pages are becoming commodity front doors. Analog should use hosted pages as community brand assets: high-context surfaces that make the gathering feel worth attending, express the Host and Venue, explain the Sponsor's additive role, capture permissioned RSVPs or claims, and preserve proof for renewal.

The aspirational event-page standard is closer to a small Coachella or Outside Lands-style web experience for a trusted community than a carbon-copy calendar card. The current MVP has the primitives for this through hosted event pages, themes, backgrounds, RSVP fields, ticket types, custom CSS, custom HTML, QR assets, and proof capture. Future ad and retargeting constructs should build from that permissioned event asset, not from raw attendee data or generic invite traffic.

Public and nonprofit programs are a high-stakes version of the same model: mission capital stays accountable upstream while governed budgets, services, and proof move downstream to Hosts, community groups, small businesses, spaces, and local operators.

Political action programs are another high-stakes version: campaign or advocacy capital stays accountable upstream while governed budgets, approved uses, privacy boundaries, and compliance-safe proof move downstream to trusted local Hosts. Treat this as a strategic use case layered onto the same MVP objects, not as a claim that Analog currently provides production campaign-finance infrastructure.

## IRL Culture Graph Primitive

Analog's product memory should be more than event records. The larger primitive is an IRL culture graph: a privacy-safe, provenance-aware map of events, recurring rituals, cultural operators, venues, audience hypotheses, sponsor fit, spend behavior, authorized mentions, proof quality, and renewal logic.

The strategic analogy is simple:

| Data category | What it makes actionable |
|---|---|
| B2B account data | Companies, contacts, roles, and prospecting paths. |
| Digital creator data | Creator reach, digital audiences, content performance, and social distribution. |
| Analog IRL culture data | Which real-world communities gather, who can move them, what sponsors they fit, and which funded moments create audience or category potential. |

In the MVP, this graph is assembled from product objects rather than a standalone data product: campaigns, event sets, applications, host profiles, hosted pages, spend cards, transactions, files/docs, proof packets, city lead notes, media capture, and renewal decisions.

## Marketplace Matching Primitive

The marketplace primitive is not a public directory. It is the AI-assisted, human-reviewed logic that pairs Sponsor dollars with receptive Hosts, communities, Venues, event types, and proof standards.

Use [Marketplace Logic](/platform/marketplace) for the canonical Platform page.

```text
Sponsor category + goal + budget
-> campaign visibility mode
-> receptive Host/community/Venue/event-type match
-> governed spend
-> benchmarked proof
-> next-dollar decision
```

| Primitive | Current approximation | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Campaign visibility mode | Campaign copy, application portal setting, outreach plan, event set prospecting, media launch | Every campaign should know whether it is private link-only, broadly marketed, or ICP-targeted. |
| Social receptiveness | Host notes, prior proof, content/mention history, Venue context, category fit, community exclusions, application answers | Match on whether the Sponsor is likely to be welcomed, not only whether the audience exists. |
| Category benchmark | Sponsor perspective, event type, proof packet, spend and mention history, assisted summaries | Compare success to organizations of the same category before recommending renewal. |
| Match reason | Deep research doc, Sponsor brief, event set note, catalog record, assisted summary | Explain why this Host, Venue, community, market, or event type fits the Sponsor's goal. |

This is future product direction until explicit visibility modes, receptiveness labels, automated benchmark libraries, and recommendation reason codes exist in production. Current MVP work can approximate the loop with campaigns, event sets, application portals, prospecting, catalog records, files/docs, proof packets, and Sponsor data summaries.

## Cash Funding Ladder Primitive

Strategy pages may refer to a funding ladder. In the MVP, these are campaign/event-set conventions, not separate financial products.

| Funding ladder | Current product approximation | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Micro Grant | Small collab allocation, spend card, event-scoped budget, or campaign application request | Use for USD 100 to USD 500/month style support: coffee, libations, supplies, rentals, print, accessibility, or light venue help. |
| Starter Series | Event set, collab allocation, multiple Host events, required todos, proof packet | Use when a Host can repeat monthly or across a small series. |
| Macro Grant | Larger recurring campaign allocation with clearer proof and renewal conditions | Use for the best Hosts, strategic rooms, operationally excellent organizers, or deeper recurring activations. |
| Operator Retainer | Managed support, city lead work, agency engagement, or contracted operator outside the core MVP object model | Keep separate from unrestricted Host wallet language; route product learning back into campaigns, event sets, catalog, and proof. |
| Public Fund | Campaign or event set with public-good eligibility, controlled spend, reporting, and review requirements | Use transparent admin/support framing and avoid promising public-sector outcomes the product cannot measure. See [Public And Nonprofit Funding Infrastructure](/strategy/public-nonprofit-funding-infrastructure). |
| Community Collective Fund | Sponsor-owned campaign backed by many local contributors, co-Sponsors, pledges, invoices, in-kind support, or future payment-link contributions | Use for neighborhood or scene funds such as Greenpoint Collective. Track contributor source, restrictions, allowed uses, proof, and renewal rules. Do not imply production pooled-wallet or donation infrastructure before it exists. |

Until compliant payout and tax rails exist, cash funding means controlled allocations, grants, event-scoped budgets, spend cards, approved program budgets, or managed support. It does not mean withdrawable balances.

## Operating Layer Shape

The product direction is an operating layer, not a directory, generic fintech app, or one-off agency service.

| Layer | Product job |
|---|---|
| Discover | Proven hosts, clubs, gatherings, venues, and operators are vetted, ranked, matched, and routed into campaigns or event sets. |
| Collaborate | Sponsor budget becomes governed spend, controls, guardrails, tasks, assets, docs, and execution support. |
| Measure | Receipts, content, attendance, sentiment, category lift, and outcomes become proof for renewal or unlock decisions. |
| Learn | Repeated budget, supply, proof, and services patterns become marketplace and fintech conventions that the platform can standardize. |

The current MVP implements pieces of this loop. Strategy pages should describe the full loop, while how-to and reference pages should stay honest about current product boundaries.

Until production money movement exists, fund access means governed sponsor allocations, grants, event-scoped budgets, spend cards, or approved program budgets. It does not mean unrestricted wallet balances.

Future embedded Host treasury should be treated as a new rail, not a renamed spend card. The direction is an Analog checking-style operating account for trusted Hosts and community businesses through compliant banking partners. A Sponsor could transfer a larger funded balance into that Host treasury, while Analog continues to provide rules, receipt capture, anomaly review, recipient-authorized summary grades, and renewal recommendations.

The strategic distinction:

| Rail | Best for | Sponsor visibility |
|---|---|---|
| Spend card | event-scoped budget, micro grant, local vendor purchase, early trust test | transaction, receipt, category, event proof |
| Contracts/invoices | large scope, venue, production, media, rights, legal, public program | scope, milestone, invoice, deliverable, approval |
| Host treasury | trusted recurring Host, arts org, venue, nonprofit partner, city operator, community business | recipient-authorized summary grade, anomalies, proof completeness, budget pacing, restricted-use risk, renewal recommendation |

Host treasury is not current MVP behavior. Do not imply Analog currently provides bank accounts, stored balances, insured deposits, payouts, ACH, bill pay, or tax tooling unless those capabilities exist through approved partners and production rails.

Vendor/payee records and 1099 support are part of the future operating layer because Sponsors should not have to rebuild tax and vendor files for every Host, Venue, photographer, contractor, operator, or service provider. The product direction is reusable records, secure tax-document status, payment context, duplicate detection, and filing/export support through approved partners where required.

Do not imply the MVP automatically files, furnishes, withholds, or advises on tax forms. Current docs can describe vendor notes, files/docs, tasks, proof, and product direction. Production 1099 support needs secure tax-document handling, payment-rail awareness, audit logs, permissions, counsel review, and approved tax/payment partners.

A grade derived from bank or treasury activity is sensitive derived financial data if tied to a recipient. Future product specs must require explicit recipient authorization to analyze account activity, explicit authorization to share a summary grade with the Sponsor, disclosed Sponsor use, objective methodology, recipient visibility into grade reasons, dispute rights, safer labels, and contract limits. Use [Proof And Spend](/platform/proof-and-spend) for the detailed rule.

Brand funds and programs are the cleanest market-making primitive: they give sponsors a reason to declare demand, hosts a reason to apply or create supply, and Analog a controlled environment to observe pricing, proof, spend behavior, and renewal.

Selective brand funds are the pre-marketplace version of this primitive. Analog should design a few Sponsor-funded public funds around specific markets before the open marketplace is liquid. The Sponsor supplies activation capital. Analog supplies fund-market fit, local distribution, application flow, regional dashboard placement, spend governance, and proof.

High-ticket sponsorships should not be described as card-only flows. For large programs, Analog's product direction is sponsorship governance: cards for distributed spend, plus files/docs, invoices, contracts, deliverables, risk reviews, proof gates, and AI-assisted renewal or tranche recommendations.

Public and nonprofit programs should be described as governed downstream funding, not unrestricted grant wallets. The current MVP can approximate them with campaigns, event sets, application portals, collabs, spend cards, files/docs, proof, and Sponsor data summaries; future product work should add eligibility, conflict checks, restricted uses, public-safe reporting, grant-report drafts, and funder-facing allocation logic.

## Account And Org Posture

The strategy target is one organization model with roles and permissions. The current MVP still exposes `Sponsor` and `Host` as product actors, but both can carry `organizationId` and `organizationRoles`.

| Object | Current meaning | Practical rule |
|---|---|---|
| Organization role | Host, sponsor, or both on one org | Treat dual-role orgs as the direction of travel; do not create strategy that assumes rigid account types forever. |
| Sponsor | Product actor that funds campaigns; in the market this means any capital deployer that wants structure, oversight, and proof | Owns campaign budget, application pages, event sets, outreach, data, files, proof, and host allocations. |
| Host | Product actor that creates events and operates against sponsor budgets | Owns host story, events, tasks, files, sponsor budgets, social connections, and pitch surfaces. |
| Brand kit or design kit | Reusable identity assets | Stores logo/mark, colors, accent media, and other media for sponsor pages, host pages, and event surfaces. |
| Host story | Sponsor-facing host narrative | Stores short/long description, organization name, and website for pitch and trust surfaces. |

## Current Product Boundaries

The MVP intentionally does not include:

- payouts or creator withdrawals
- reimbursements
- unrestricted wallet balances
- Host treasury or Analog checking accounts
- ACH, bill pay, or banking-quality cash management
- Stripe Connect payouts
- production automated 1099 filing, furnishing, withholding, or tax-advice workflows
- automated vendor/payee tax classification
- production ticketing or payment collection
- native mobile apps
- wallet passes
- fully automated attribution
- production Venue reward payout rails
- fully automated Venue social authorization
- automated mention benchmark scoring
- fully automated legal contracting, insurance, or payout gates
- fully automated high-ticket sponsorship underwriting
- native invoice, purchase order, escrow, or procurement rails

Money remains platform-controlled. Hosts receive event-scoped operational spend access, not withdrawable balances.

## UGC And Social Proof

Sponsor-visible proof can include:

- image or video post mentions
- story mentions with usable media or source URLs
- uploaded or seeded proof items
- authorized Host, Venue, or Sponsor mention assets

Do not show comments as sponsor UGC. Do not put sponsor analytics-heavy UGC grids on host screens. Do not treat Venue mention capture as current automated payout behavior unless payment, tax, authorization, and platform-connection rails exist.
