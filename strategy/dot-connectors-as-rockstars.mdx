Dot connectors and community builders should become the visible talent class of the gathering economy.

The goal is not to turn them into generic influencers. The goal is to make their invisible trust labor visible, fundable, bookable, and respected.

## Core Thesis

The next great gathering economy needs people who know who should meet, which spaces matter, which hosts are trusted, which sponsors belong, and what would make a community moment feel alive instead of manufactured.

Those people are often underpaid, unnamed, or treated as behind-the-scenes operators. Analog can change that by giving them:

- public visibility
- proof of impact
- sponsor-safe packaging
- economic upside
- media distribution
- owned IRL stages
- repeatable product tools
- a prestige media and funding platform that turns attention into governed opportunities

## Rockstar Definition

In Analog language, a rockstar connector is:

```text
trusted taste + local relationships + repeated convening ability + sponsor judgment + proof of unlocked IRL
```

Rockstar status should mean the connector is known for creating real participation, not just attention.

## The Rockstar Ladder

| Stage | What changes | Analog support |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Seen | The connector is no longer invisible | Profile them in `The Connector`, social, print, or YouTube. |
| 2. Legible | Their network, taste, and city context become understandable | Build a connector profile, catalog links, proof ledger, and sponsor-safe summary. |
| 3. Trusted | Hosts and sponsors understand why this person has judgment | Capture interviews, references, prior gatherings, and community sentiment. |
| 4. Bookable | Sponsors and hosts can ask them to help assemble a gathering or scene | Package Activation Opportunities, Requests for Sponsorship, city briefs, and owned IRL formats. |
| 5. Paid | The connector earns for sourcing, hosting, programming, recruiting, or producing | Use operator margin, city lead fees, event budgets, grants, sponsor allocations, or rev share where appropriate. |
| 6. Celebrated | Their work becomes culturally admired | Put them on stage, in print, in documentaries, in city lists, and at Analog-owned gatherings. |
| 7. Compounding | Their reputation brings more hosts, sponsors, and dot connectors into the tent | Turn the person into a city lead, recurring columnist, documentary subject, or regional program owner. |

## Productize The Person Without Flattening Them

The product should help a connector become more powerful without making them less trusted.

Create or support:

| Surface | Job |
|---|---|
| Connector profile | Shows who they are, what scenes they know, what they can unlock, and what proof exists. |
| Proof ledger | Tracks gatherings influenced, hosts discovered, sponsor matches, funds unlocked, media shipped, and repeat participation. |
| City map | Shows the communities, venues, operators, and sponsor perspectives they understand. |
| Activation Opportunity | Lets a connector package "I can make this happen" before a sponsor campaign exists. |
| Request for Sponsorship | Lets a connector bring a host or scene to sponsor budget. |
| Media packet | Gives the content team interview notes, footage, field stories, and sponsor perspective. |
| Owned IRL format | Gives the connector a repeatable gathering they can host under the Analog umbrella. |
| Funding tab | Shows governed funds, briefs, and allocations the connector can apply to, unlock, or help route. |

See [Prestige Media And Funding Platform](/strategy/prestige-media-funding-platform) for the fuller platform direction.

## Media Makes The Rockstar

Analog's media engine should turn connector work into culture.

Formats:

- `The Connector` profile: "Who this person brings together and why it matters."
- City field note: "What they are seeing before the market sees it."
- YouTube mini-doc: "A day with the city lead."
- Social clips: short interviews, introductions, event recaps, and scene explainers.
- Print volume feature: regional connector lists, maps, and essays.
- Live recording: connector interviews at Analog-owned gatherings.

The editorial posture should be generous and specific. Do not just say someone is connected. Show the gatherings they made possible, the communities they protect, and the judgment sponsors should trust.

The energy should be premium and editorial: cover-worthy photography, sharp profiles, city lists, documentary video, and print artifacts that make community builders feel culturally important.

## Owned IRL Gives Them A Stage

Analog should create recurring stages where connectors can be seen doing the work.

Examples:

- community-builder happy hours
- city lead salons
- connector dinners
- host and sponsor listening rooms
- neighborhood walks
- live `The Connector` interviews
- regional print launch gatherings
- "who should know who" nights

These gatherings should dogfood Analog: clear event setup, spend discipline where relevant, field capture, sponsor perspective, recap, and follow-up.

## Sponsor Perspective

Each sponsor perspective should understand why connectors matter differently.

| Sponsor perspective | Why connectors matter |
|---|---|
| eComm Brands | They know where product trial, taste, content, and recurring rituals can happen without feeling forced. |
| Tech Companies | They know which builders, founders, creators, operators, and strategic accounts should be in the room. |
| Public/Non-profit Funds | They know who has local legitimacy and which gatherings can create real access, trust, and public outcomes. |

The connector is not just a lead source. The connector is an underwriting signal.

## Compensation Paths

Pay should be tied to real work and real trust, not extractive bounty chasing.

Possible paths:

- city lead retainer
- event production fee
- sourcing fee for qualified host supply
- operator margin on funded programs
- editorial or creator fee for field capture
- grant administration fee
- sponsor-funded programming budget
- rev share on repeatable partnership packages
- governed fund access through city, category, sponsor, public, or editorial fund drops

When in doubt, pay for the work of gathering people, protecting trust, and making the opportunity legible.

## Recognition System

Analog can make connectors famous through repeated proof and public artifacts:

- city connector lists
- regional `The Connector` print features
- annual gathering economy awards
- documentary series
- featured connector profiles
- verified connector pages
- hosted stages at owned Analog events
- sponsor briefings led by connectors

Recognition should never expose fragile communities or private relationships without permission.

## Guardrails

- Do not make connectors choose attention over trust.
- Do not turn private community knowledge into public inventory without consent.
- Do not reward spammy introductions.
- Do not rank people only by media reach.
- Do not let sponsor pressure make a connector burn local credibility.
- Do not treat community builders as unpaid ambassadors.

The standard is simple: visibility should increase the connector's power, income, and trust at the same time.

## What To Measure

Track:

- hosts discovered
- communities made legible
- sponsor fits identified
- funds unlocked
- gatherings hosted or improved
- repeat participation created
- interviews and field stories captured
- media shipped
- owned IRL attendance and follow-up
- community sentiment
- sponsor renewal or next allocation

The best signal is compounding demand: more hosts want to work with the connector, more sponsors trust their judgment, and more community builders want to join the network.
