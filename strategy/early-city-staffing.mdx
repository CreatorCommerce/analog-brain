In the earliest days, Analog should not hire four full local teams.

The right shape is a strong central operating spine plus one high-taste city lead in each priority market: NYC, LA, Miami, and SF. Each city lead then assembles a flexible pod of connectors, producers, media stringers, and operators as the city proves demand.

## Core Rule

Staff the judgment first. Staff the operations second. Staff the full city pod only after the city proves it can produce supply, media, sponsor demand, owned IRL, and repeat funding.

```text
Central team -> city leads -> flexible city pods -> hardened city teams
```

## Central Spine

The first central team should make each city lead more powerful without pulling them out of the field.

| Role | Owns | Why it matters early |
|---|---|---|
| Head of Cities / Field GM | City strategy, lead recruiting, weekly operating rhythm, city scorecards, and escalation | Keeps NYC, LA, Miami, and SF comparable instead of four scattered experiments. |
| Head of Content | Editorial calendar, `The Connector`, social, YouTube, print, quality bar, and media team coordination | Turns field work into status, inbound demand, and sponsor belief. |
| Sponsor Partnerships Lead | eComm, Tech, and Public/Non-profit sponsor pipeline, briefs, fund design, and sponsor POV | Makes sure city supply maps back to real budget. |
| Producer / Field Ops Lead | Owned IRL templates, vendor bench, spend rules, event runbooks, proof capture, and city lead support | Keeps gatherings excellent without turning the company into bespoke agency chaos. |
| Product Ops / Market Intelligence Lead | Catalog hygiene, proof ledger, asset-need notes, city dashboards, and learning loops | Converts field context into product objects and strategy. |

At the very beginning, founders can cover some of these seats. The first dedicated hire should be whichever function is most bottlenecked: usually Head of Cities or Head of Content.

## City Lead Profile

Each city lead should be a local trust node, not a generic event scout.

Look for:

- respected taste in the local scene
- relationships across community builders, spaces, creators, and operators
- ability to host and convene
- comfort capturing interviews, field notes, and media
- sponsor judgment across the three perspectives
- enough operational discipline to use budgets and proof rules
- instinct for who should become a connector rockstar

Avoid leads who only know nightlife, only know brand activations, or only know influencer marketing. The job is to build the gathering economy, not fill a party.

## First Four Cities

| City | Why it matters | Lead archetype | First wedges |
|---|---|---|---|
| NYC | Highest density of media, fashion, finance, founders, runners, dinners, arts, and sponsor decision-makers | High-taste connector with media, founder, fashion, wellness, and neighborhood range | Running/walking, founder breakfasts, dinner clubs, arts/culture salons, community-builder happy hours |
| LA | Creator, wellness, entertainment, production, fitness, outdoor, and brand culture density | Creator/community operator who understands production and wellness without making everything feel like an activation | Creator-led IRL, wellness/recovery, running, film/photo walks, founder/operator dinners |
| Miami | Hospitality, luxury, wellness, nightlife, Latin America bridge, real estate, crypto/finance, and civic/public-private energy | Hospitality and cultural connector with sponsor taste and strong local trust | Wellness and run clubs, founder/investor salons, art/design gatherings, hospitality-led owned IRL |
| SF | Tech, AI, founder, developer, climate, civic, and builder-community density | Builder-community connector who can convene technical, founder, and public-interest scenes | AI/builder dinners, founder breakfasts, run/walk clubs, climate/civic meetups, hackathons |

## City Pod Shape

Start each city with one accountable city lead and a flexible bench.

| Pod role | Start as | Responsibilities |
|---|---|---|
| City lead | Retainer plus upside | Owns city map, relationships, field capture, owned IRL, sponsor perspective, and weekly city scorecard. |
| Connector bench | Paid per project or small retainer | Opens scenes, introduces hosts, scouts spaces, identifies builders, and helps package opportunities. |
| Local producer | Contract | Runs owned IRL, vendor coordination, checklists, venue logistics, and event-day operations. |
| Field media stringer | Contract | Captures photo, video, audio, interviews, and BTS for the central content team. |
| Sponsor scout | Central plus local support | Finds local sponsor leads and routes them to the Sponsor Partnerships Lead. |

Do not make every pod role full-time. The first goal is to learn which city deserves more fixed capacity.

## Ninety-Day Launch Plan

| Time | Goal | Output per city |
|---|---|---|
| Days 1-15 | Recruit and calibrate city leads | Lead scorecard, city thesis, initial scene map, top 25 connectors/builders/spaces |
| Days 16-30 | Start field capture and owned IRL | 10 interviews or field notes, 1 owned Analog gathering, 3 sponsor-perspective briefs |
| Days 31-60 | Package opportunities | 10 catalog records, 3 connector profiles, 3 Activation Opportunities, 1 media feature, 1 fund hypothesis |
| Days 61-90 | Test repeatability | 1 sponsor-funded or fund-like pilot, 1 owned IRL repeat, proof report, city scorecard, staffing recommendation |

## City Scorecard

Score each city weekly.

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| Supply density | Are there enough real communities, builders, connectors, spaces, and recurring rituals? |
| Lead quality | Is the city lead opening real doors and making good taste calls? |
| Media yield | Are we getting stories, interviews, visuals, and editorial concepts worth publishing? |
| Sponsor fit | Which of the three sponsor perspectives has believable budget for this city? |
| Owned IRL quality | Can Analog host something people are proud to attend and share? |
| Product conversion | Are field notes becoming catalog records, profiles, proof, fund ideas, or sponsor briefs? |
| Funding unlock | Did the city reveal fundable opportunities that could repeat? |

## When To Add Headcount

Add fixed local headcount only when a city shows:

- repeatable owned IRL attendance
- sponsor or fund demand
- strong media yield
- several trusted hosts and connector profiles
- operational load that the central team cannot absorb
- evidence that more staffing will create more fundable supply, not just more activity

The first local add after city lead is usually a producer if events are operationally heavy, or a field media/stringer if the city is story-rich but under-captured.

## Compensation Philosophy

Pay city leads and connectors enough that the role feels real, but keep upside tied to proof.

Possible components:

- monthly retainer
- paid field capture assignments
- owned IRL hosting fee
- production fee
- sponsor/fund opportunity bonus when quality thresholds are met
- operator margin on funded programs
- editorial fee for published work

Avoid pure commission. It encourages spammy intros and bad sponsor fit.

## Operating Cadence

Run one weekly city review:

```text
City:
Lead:
Top scenes:
Best new builders/connectors:
Best media captured:
Best sponsor perspective:
Owned IRL plan:
Fundable opportunities:
Blockers:
Next 7 days:
```

The Head of Cities owns the meeting. Head of Content, Sponsor Partnerships, Field Ops, and Product Ops should join or review the output.

## Kill Or Double-Down Rules

Double down when:

- the city lead is exceptional
- media yield is obvious
- owned IRL is getting real attendance and follow-up
- sponsors understand the scenes
- fundable opportunities are repeating

Pause or redesign when:

- the city lead is only producing lists, not trust
- events require too much manual production
- media feels generic
- sponsor fit is forced
- communities seem fragile or resistant
- the city is busy but not creating product learning

The early goal is not to prove every city works. The goal is to find which city lead plus city pod can become a repeatable market-formation engine.
