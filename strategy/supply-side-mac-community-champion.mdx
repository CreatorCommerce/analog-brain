This page defines the contract between MAC and CAC.

Read this after [Market Formation](/strategy/market-formation). Market formation explains how Analog enters a market. [Why Analog Wins](/strategy/why-analog-wins) explains the broader goal: Analog should become the only sane place for Hosts and Sponsors to run community sponsorships.

This page is narrower. It answers one operating question:

```text
Before unit-level attribution is credible,
how should Analog model the cost of acquiring a market?
```

The answer is MAC: market acquisition cost.

```text
MAC = temporary spend and support used to win a market's supply graph

CAC = later-stage spend used to acquire one community once its attributable value is knowable
```

MAC is not a softer name for CAC. MAC buys access, density, proof, and legibility in a finite market before Analog can price every individual community with confidence.

CAC eventually matters. But in the first six months, forcing every supply-side dollar into unit CAC creates false precision. Analog can sell access to a market before it can perfectly attribute the value of each Host inside that market.

## Read This In Three Ideas

| Idea | Plain meaning | Operator implication |
|---|---|---|
| MAC comes before CAC | Early Analog is not buying one Host at a time. It is unlocking a finite city, circuit, or category graph. | Budget by market stage: NYC run clubs, SF SaaS dinners, Milwaukee community layer, or another bounded graph. |
| MAC must have a replacement path | Company-funded MAC is what Analog pays. Sponsor, public, partner, venue, media, network, and in-kind support are replacement value. | The best MAC gets replaced by Sponsor funds, public grants, media revenue, venue rewards, and referrals. |
| CAC begins when attribution is real | A market is ready for community-level CAC when Analog can estimate what one community produces and what it is worth to acquire more like it. | Graduate from market-access models to unit models only when proof, outcomes, and repeat value are credible. |

## The Contract

| Contract point | What Analog means | What Analog should not do |
|---|---|---|
| Model MAC first | For the first six months, judge supply spend by the cost to unlock a market, circuit, city, or category. | Pretend the business already knows the payback of acquiring one average community. |
| Keep MAC bounded | MAC applies to a named market graph with a thesis, target nodes, proof goals, and Sponsor/public-fund paths. | Call random perks, events, or awareness spend MAC because they feel good. |
| Separate company spend from activation capital | Company-funded MAC is Analog's operating spend. Sponsor, public, partner, venue, in-kind, and media dollars are outside support routed through Analog. | Hide pass-through activation dollars inside company burn or treat them as Analog revenue. |
| Measure replacement | Good MAC makes future supply acquisition cheaper by replacing company dollars with outside money, referrals, proof, and market artifacts. | Keep subsidizing the same motion after the market should be compounding. |
| Graduate to CAC later | CAC becomes useful when Analog can price the attributable value of one community, Host segment, or acquisition channel. | Force CAC too early and make bad decisions from fake precision. |

The near-term question is not:

```text
What is the CAC of one community?
```

The better question is:

```text
What does it cost to unlock a market,
and what can Sponsors pay for access once it is unlocked?
```

Analog can sell market access before it can perfectly monetize user-level attribution.

| Market-access question | Why it is sellable now |
|---|---|
| What is access to the Milwaukee community layer worth? | A Sponsor can value regional access before every community has perfect attribution. |
| What is access to the New York run club circuit worth? | The circuit is finite, dense, and culturally legible. |
| What is access to the San Francisco SaaS dinner and meetup circuit worth? | SaaS buyers already understand the value of qualified rooms and account-adjacent access. |

Over time, MAC should evolve into CAC. The platform should eventually show a Sponsor that comparable communities produced average attendance, social engagement, booked demos, search lift, shopper traffic, purchase intent, or other outcome signals.

Until that attribution is credible, do not pretend the model is more precise than it is. Model market density, supply creation, activation cost, proof quality, Sponsor willingness to pay, and repeat access.

## How To Read The Numbers

Treat the numbers below as planning assumptions, not a finance plan or price card.

```text
company MAC per qualified node =
  company-funded MAC / qualified supply nodes

replacement ratio =
  external support routed through Analog / company-funded MAC
```

A qualified supply node is a Host, Venue, connector, operator, or recurring ritual with enough profile, proof, permission, Sponsor fit, or local trust to become useful supply.

External support means Sponsor activation budget, public grants, foundation money, partner-funded perks, venue rewards, in-kind services, or media dollars that move through the Analog system. It is not Analog revenue unless explicitly priced as a fee.

| Signal | How to read it |
|---|---|
| Company MAC per qualified node is high in the first 30 to 50 nodes | Acceptable if the work creates proof, referrals, and a better map. The first cohort is research and credibility. |
| Company MAC per qualified node stays above USD 500 after the first 100 nodes | Warning sign unless a Sponsor or public-fund path is clearly replacing the spend. |
| Replacement ratio moves above 1.0x | Outside money is carrying more activation value than Analog's company-funded MAC. This is the first scaling signal. |
| Replacement ratio moves above 2.0x | Strong signal. Analog is using market thesis, product flow, and distribution to move more capital than it spends. |
| Supply grows but proof does not improve | Bad MAC. Analog is creating activity, not acquiring a market. |
| Sponsors ask for the market before Analog finishes explaining it | Great MAC. The market has become legible enough to sell. |

```text
Can Analog spend one company dollar

```

## MAC vs CAC Model

Use this table when a forecast, budget, or investor memo tries to force early supply spend into unit CAC too soon.

| Question | MAC, first six months | CAC, later |
|---|---|---|
| Core question | What does it cost to unlock a market, circuit, city, or category? | What does it cost to acquire one community with known attributable value? |
| Unit | Market graph: Hosts, connectors, venues, rituals, proof, artifacts, and Sponsor demand in one place. | Individual Host, community, account, or repeatable acquisition segment. |
| Spend included | City lead time, research, pitch tools, QR assets, photo/video, print, owned IRL, micro grants, in-kind grants, grant discovery, Host packaging. | Paid acquisition, referral, sales development, partner channel, self-serve conversion, lifecycle activation. |
| Revenue it supports | Market unlock packages, Sponsor funds, city/category retainers, fund administration, public/nonprofit programs, media packages. | Community-level margin, outcome-based pricing, repeat Sponsor value, payback period, channel efficiency. |
| Data Analog can use now | Host density, application quality, proof completeness, Sponsor meetings, committed funds, referrals, QR response, print/newsletter response. | Community-level attribution, audience overlap, booked demos, regional sales/search lift, repeat activation value, churn/payback. |
| Good signal | Per-Host manual support falls as supply density rises and Sponsor/public funds replace company spend. | One acquisition channel produces communities with predictable value and payback. |
| Bad signal | Spend creates events, content, or applications without market density, proof, or Sponsor willingness to pay. | CAC is modeled before Analog can value the community it acquired. |
| Stop condition | The market does not compound: no referral loop, no proof loop, no Sponsor urgency, no fundable supply. | The channel cannot acquire profitable communities after attribution is credible. |

MAC is not a softer name for CAC. MAC is the cost of making a market accessible, visible, and fundable.

</Info>

| 100 to 1,000 nodes | 25k to 60k per market cycle | grant pool, raffle grants, public-grant support, office hours, templates, newsletter drops | 50k to 200k | one-off service labor, reimbursement chaos, per-Host education | many Hosts can apply, prove, and repeat without bespoke help. |

The curve should bend down in company effort per useful Host. If it does not, Analog is funding activity rather than acquiring the market.

## Cost Replacement Model

The best MAC is designed to disappear from Analog's balance sheet.

| Early company cost | Replace it with | Product proof required | Good sign |
|---|---|---|---|
| Photo/video for first champions | Sponsor-funded proof packages, in-kind grants, or media packages | approved assets, Host consent, event recap, Sponsor-safe usage rights | Sponsors pay for proof because it improves renewal quality. |
| Company-funded micro grants | selective brand funds, public grants, foundation support, or partner perks | eligibility, allowed uses, spend rules, receipts, outcomes | Hosts apply because real money is visible, not because Analog begged them to join. |
| Manual Host packaging | pitch/proof tool, Host event history, AI-assisted Sponsor fit, templates | complete Host profile, prior events, audience notes, proof history | Hosts can become Sponsor-ready without a custom operator thread. |
| Manual supply sourcing | Event Prospecting Catalog, referrals, regional dashboard flags, demand beacons | source, fit, category, market, proof, owner, next action | the market routes more qualified supply into Analog than cold outbound finds. |
| Analog-funded print or media | local Sponsor placements, media revenue, fund-drop sponsorship, QR-driven demand | distribution, scans, applications, Sponsor leads, editorial boundaries | the artifact funds itself or clearly lowers supply acquisition. |
| Campaign education for Sponsors | benchmarks, comparable event data, market unlock memos, renewal packets | attendance, social proof, mention coverage, spend integrity, sentiment | Sponsors understand the next dollar faster after each activation. |
| Venue coordination | campaign-funded Venue rewards and authorized Venue mention capture | Venue authorization, campaign window, social account scope, payout controls | Sponsors pay for broader attribution coverage because it improves the investment record. |

## Grant Desk As MAC Replacement

Grant discovery is one of the cleanest ways to reduce company-funded MAC because it gives Hosts a free-money reason to enter Analog while moving outside capital into the market.

| MAC cost Analog might carry | Grant-desk replacement | What Analog keeps |
|---|---|---|
| Company micro grants for first events | CitizensNYC-style microgrants, arts regrants, BID funds, foundation awards, Sponsor-backed public funds. | Host record, eligibility data, application packet, proof standard. |
| Photo/video or print support | In-kind grants, public art/culture support, Sponsor proof packages, media partnerships. | Asset request, usage rights, recap proof, market media. |
| Venue or storefront activation support | Vacant storefront programs, corridor grants, small-business grants, public/private downtown activation funds. | Venue record, authorization, spend proof, social/attendance benchmarks. |
| Manual Host pitch work | Auto-generated grant packet and Sponsor pitch packet from the same Analog record. | Reusable Host profile, event history, rates, proof, fundability score. |
| City lead research | Grant opportunity catalog plus local public/nonprofit funding map. | Market intelligence, agency targets, public-fund calendar. |

The goal is not to make Analog a grant consultant. The goal is to make the Host's Analog record valuable enough that grant applications, Sponsor pitches, and campaign eligibility all improve at once.

Use this ratio when reviewing the grant desk:

```text
grant-desk MAC leverage =
  outside capital applied for or won through Analog-supported packets
  / company-funded grant-desk cost
```

For early strategy, "applied for" matters because it shows addressable outside capital and Host demand. "Won" matters more as soon as the desk has enough cycles to judge quality.

## Free Money Pitch Tool

The free pitch tool should not only help a Host make a prettier deck. It should help the Host find money.

The promise:

```text
Load your community history once.
Analog helps you find public grants, prepare your pitch, apply where you authorize it,
and graduate toward brand or Sponsor money on-platform.
```

Being on Analog should create a reusable funding file: event history, audience notes, proof, photos, venue context, spend needs, public-good fit, and Sponsor fit. That file can support government grants, foundation grants, public/private funds, the Host's own outbound pitch, and future Sponsor campaigns.

| Layer | Free or low-friction Host value | Product action | Why it captures supply |
|---|---|---|---|
| Event history | Host gets credit for what they already built. | Turn past events, photos, attendance, Sponsors, and proof into a pitch/proof page. | Hosts join because Analog makes their hidden work legible. |
| Grant discovery | Host sees public, nonprofit, foundation, or local grant opportunities they may qualify for. | Research grant sources by city, category, audience, and allowable use. | "Free money" becomes a real supply magnet, not just a slogan. |
| Auto-apply support | Host can authorize Analog to prepare or support applications using their Analog record. | Use OpenClaw-style grant tactics: find opportunities, match eligibility, assemble files, draft answers, track deadlines, and submit or co-submit only with permission. | Hosts get a reason to keep their profile current. |
| Sponsor pitch packet | Host can use the same proof to pitch brands directly. | Generate clear asks, rates, proof, audience fit, and use-of-funds language. | Even off-platform pitching strengthens the Host's Analog record. |
| On-platform Sponsor funds | Host becomes eligible for brand funds, raffles, and public/private programs. | Route qualified Hosts into campaigns, Event sets, collabs, spend cards, proof, and renewal. | Free money becomes the path to repeat Sponsor money. |

Do not overpromise grant awards. The tactical strategy is to make Analog the place where Hosts discover funding, become application-ready, and build the proof that makes the next dollar easier to win.

## Predictable Overhead Rule

Every support item should become a repeatable unit.

| Support unit | Bound it by |
|---|---|
| Photo/video | one market, one Host cohort, one event, one asset package |
| Print support | templates, vendor menu, QR standards, quantity bands |
| Grant pool | eligibility, allowed uses, proof, renewal rules |
| Government grant help | templates, office hours, partner referrals, no unsupported legal claims |
| Social/newsletter inclusion | editorial criteria, market calendar, consent, proof follow-up |
| Insurance/vendor discounts | partner terms, request flow, proof of use |

The goal is high affection with predictable overhead. If a support item cannot be repeated or priced, it should stay an experiment.

Artifacts make a market feel real.

| Market type | Artifact | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| Top market | print magazine | Communities feel they have to be included; Sponsors see a real market. |
| Mid-tier market | seasonal festival posters, city guides, and fund posters | The market gets a physical object without full magazine overhead. |

## Brand Urgency

MAC should create Sponsor urgency, not only Host affection.

The Sponsor pitch:

```text
We are activating this market now.
The supply graph is forming.
The right communities are opting in.
We are opening 20 customer funds with committed budget.
Enter before the moment gets crowded.
```

Use research to find concentrations: where communities are clustered, which formats are spreading, which brands fit the moment, which Hosts can create viral loops, and when brand entry will feel additive instead of late.

Key customer funds should resonate with the market. A run recovery fund, superconnector dinner fund, chess club fund, arts access fund, or founder room fund should feel like it belongs there.

## Selective Brand Funds Before Marketplace

Before the full brand marketplace is liquid, Analog should manufacture a small number of public, market-fit funds with enterprise Sponsors.

The rule:

```text
Sponsor supplies the activation capital.
Analog supplies the market thesis, distribution, application flow, proof loop, and operating energy.
```

This is not Analog putting its own money up as MAC. It is Analog using selective marketing energy and local distribution to turn Sponsor capital into a supply-capture engine.

Analog already has a key contractor or hire profile in place for this motion: an ex-Crocs partnerships leader suited to closing Crocs-like enterprise Sponsors and bringing in companies such as Midol, Baden, and other major brands. Use that muscle selectively. The goal is not to build a broad sales team before the marketplace works. The goal is to close the few funds that can create the most supply gravity in the right markets.

| Selective brand fund rule | Standard |
|---|---|
| Market fit | The fund should feel native to the city or circuit. A Crocs Run Club Recovery Fund can make sense in New York run/wellness. It should not be copied into every market by default. |
| Sponsor narrative | The Sponsor should be able to explain why it is funding this community moment without sounding forced. |
| Supply density | There must be enough Hosts, venues, operators, and rituals for the fund to create visible response. |
| Public CTA | The hook should be simple enough to publish: apply, win, get support, or bring your community into the fund. |
| Host value | The fund should give Hosts useful cash or in-kind support: recovery benefits, supplies, photo/video, venue support, print, insurance, or operator help. |
| Proof path | Analog must be able to capture attendance, spend, authorized mentions, content, sentiment, and renewal evidence. |
| Regional placement | The fund should appear in the right local Host dashboard, media drop, newsletter, print artifact, or QR flow. |

Examples:

| Fund | Best-fit market or circuit | Why it can work | Do not do this |
|---|---|---|---|
| Crocs Run Club Recovery Fund | NYC run, walk, wellness, recovery, and movement communities. | The Sponsor can fund a real post-run need while Analog captures the credible movement circuit. | Treat it as a generic national run-club sponsorship before New York proof exists. |
| Midol Wellness Fund | NYC women's wellness, movement, education, and care-adjacent communities where the fit is welcomed. | The fund can support useful gatherings while giving the Sponsor a respectful local role. | Force the Sponsor into communities where the topic feels extractive or awkward. |
| Baden Rec League Fund | Rec sports, game nights, club sports, school-adjacent community play, and local tournament circuits. | Equipment, space, tournament support, and proof are easy to understand. | Launch without a real local play circuit and trusted organizers. |
| Tapcart Superconnector Fund | SF founder/operator dinners, eCommerce rooms, partner salons, and high-trust connector networks. | SaaS Sponsors understand qualified rooms and relationship density. | Sell it as broad awareness instead of trusted access. |
| Anthropic or Shopify Builder Room Fund | SF AI, developer, founder, and ecosystem rooms. | The market has dense technical communities and high-value partner context. | Let the Sponsor overwhelm the room or make the gathering feel like a demo. |

The fund thesis should be written before outreach:

```text
This Sponsor should fund this market because this local circuit is forming now,
the Host-side supply exists or can be created,
the Sponsor has a credible reason to help,
and Analog can prove what happened.
```

Once a selective brand fund is live, it should route through the same MAC system: regional dashboard placement, application portal, event sets, Host packaging, QR asset packs, media, proof, renewal, and the next fund thesis.

## Regional Host Dashboard

MAC should show up in the Host product experience.

A New York Host should not feel like they are using a generic national app. They should feel they are inside the local Analog layer. The dashboard can carry a market marker such as `AnalogNYC` and show the local opportunities that matter now.

| Regional dashboard module | What it does |
|---|---|
| Local identity | Shows the Host that Analog understands their market: `AnalogNYC`, `AnalogSF`, or the active city layer. |
| Market-fit funds | Surfaces selective brand funds, public grants, raffles, and category funds relevant to that market. |
| Perk requests | Lets Hosts request photo/video, print, insurance, QR asset packs, venue support, grant help, or other MAC perks deployed in that city. |
| Media inclusion | Routes Hosts toward newsletters, social drops, print features, city guides, and event roundups. |
| Market flags | Lets Analog tag which city, circuit, and Sponsor-fit lane the Host belongs to. |
| Proof reminders | Shows what evidence will help the Host earn Analog credit and future funding. |

The regional dashboard is both Host experience and operating infrastructure. It gives the Host a reason to stay close to Analog, and it gives Analog a control surface for deploying MAC without making every perk a custom thread.

## Supply-Side MAC Rules

- Spend on market escape velocity, not random perks.
- Model MAC before CAC until attribution can price individual communities credibly.
- Give free tools to everyone, but reserve high-touch support for priority markets and high-signal Hosts.
- Use selective brand funds before the full marketplace only when Sponsor capital, market fit, supply density, public CTA, and proof are clear.
- Do not use company money when Sponsor capital can fund the public hook. Analog should spend marketing energy, product flow, and local operating attention before its own activation dollars.
- Use regional Host dashboards to deploy market-specific funds, perks, media requests, and proof reminders.
- Use in-kind grants when they create proof, content, access, or lower Host friction better than cash.
- Make every artifact route into product memory: QR, application, profile, claim, proof, Sponsor lead, or catalog record.
- Pre-sell Sponsor funds against market timing, not abstract brand interest.
- Keep Host upside visible. Do not let discounts or credits reduce what Hosts earn.
- Stop MAC when it creates activity without supply density, proof, or Sponsor demand.
