Analog should not only fund IRL. Analog should make the next great gathering economy visible.

The strategy is to run a city-by-city media and owned-IRL engine that documents real communities, brings more people into the tent, gives sponsors a sharper view of local demand, and keeps city leads in the field.

## Core Doctrine

Media is not a side channel. It is market formation.

The motion:

```text
City lead meets the scene -> captures story -> content team produces -> media distributes -> more hosts and sponsors enter -> city lead runs owned IRL -> more stories and supply surface
```

This should feel editorially real before it feels like a sponsor campaign. The purpose is to document amazing communities and events already happening everywhere, then use that attention to build more sponsor-ready supply.

The editorial bar should be closer to a high-status cultural publication than a brand blog. Analog should bring premium profile, photography, video, list, cover, and print energy to community builders, then route that prestige into governed fund access on the platform.

## Channels

| Channel | Job |
|---|---|
| Social media | Show communities, rituals, city scenes, host stories, event recaps, field clips, and sponsor-relevant moments in public. |
| `The Connector` on Substack | Publish the recurring newsletter that maps the gathering economy: profiles, city notes, event roundups, host interviews, sponsor perspectives, and market theses. |
| `The Connector` print volume | Publish a quarterly print edition with regional editions, such as `The Connector Vol. 2: The Northeastern Edition`. Treat it as a collectible field journal, directory, and proof artifact. |
| YouTube | Produce documentary-style videos about city leads, local scenes, host models, and the communities Analog is helping make legible. |
| Owned IRL | Run Analog-hosted happy hours, salons, dinners, walks, founder breakfasts, and community-leader meetups that dogfood the product. |
| Analog platform | Host profiles, connector profiles, proof ledgers, fund pages, Activation Opportunities, and spend/proof workflows. |

## Operating Roles

| Role | Owns |
|---|---|
| City leads | Boots-on-the-ground discovery, relationship building, interviews, event footage, local meetups, host scouting, and sponsor context. |
| Head of Content | Editorial calendar, channel strategy, city priorities, quality bar, sponsor perspective, print schedule, and team coordination. |
| Analog team | Strategic direction, sponsor angles, product questions, city/category priorities, proof needs, and which stories should become product work. |
| Contracted media team | Writing, copyediting, design, video editing, clipping, thumbnailing, layout, publishing support, and packaging for each channel. |

The media team can be assembled and contracted. The important rule is that Analog and the city leads provide the raw field intelligence, while the content team turns it into polished writing, video, social, design, and print.

## City Lead Field Loop

A city lead should constantly circulate through the local gathering economy.

Their recurring loop:

1. Attend community events, meet organizers, and learn the local scene.
2. Record short interviews, audio notes, video clips, event footage, and photos when permission is clear.
3. Capture the sponsor perspective: which categories fit, what funding could unlock, what proof would matter, and what brand presence would be tasteful.
4. Submit raw content and field notes into the shared content intake, usually Slack.
5. Convert strong discoveries into catalog records, host profiles, asset-need notes, RFEs, RFCs, or sponsor opportunities.
6. Run owned Analog gatherings for community leaders, sponsors, hosts, and dot connectors.
7. Feed those owned gatherings back into media and product learning.

## Make Connectors The Talent

The media engine should make dot connectors and community builders recognizable.

Do this by repeatedly showing:

- who they bring together
- what scenes they understand
- what gatherings they helped unlock
- which sponsors they can guide with taste
- what public or community outcomes they make possible
- why other hosts trust them

## Production Workflow

| Step | Owner | Output |
|---|---|---|

## Owned IRL

Analog should run its own city-level gatherings because the company is trying to build the gathering economy, not merely observe it.

Owned IRL can include:

- happy hours for community leaders
- city lead office hours
- host dinners
- sponsor-listening salons
- walks, runs, recovery mornings, or neighborhood meetups
- live recordings or interview nights for `The Connector`
- launch gatherings for regional print editions

These events should dogfood Analog's own motion: event setup, sponsor perspective, spend controls where relevant, field capture, proof, recap, and follow-up.

## Sponsor Perspective

The media engine should be sponsor-aware without becoming generic sponsored content.

For every city or scene, capture the three sponsor perspectives:

- whether the scene is best for eComm Brands, Tech Companies, Public/Non-profit Funds, or more than one perspective
- what funding would improve without making the experience cringe
- what proof a sponsor would believe
- what brand presence the community would welcome
- what local operators or hosts deserve more support
- what category growth signal is emerging

This lets Analog sell and underwrite with more taste. Sponsors should see not only inventory, but cultural context.

## City-By-City Operating Model

Run this as a regional playbook:

1. Choose a city or region with real community density and a strong city lead.
2. Pick 2 to 4 scene priorities, such as running, dinner clubs, founder breakfasts, cleanup clubs, or creative communities.
3. Define which of the three sponsor perspectives each scene serves and what proof each would need.
4. Build the content calendar across social, Substack, YouTube, print, and owned IRL.
5. Use city lead field capture to keep the calendar grounded.
6. Publish and distribute consistently.
7. Route inbound organizers, sponsors, and dot connectors back into Analog product objects.
8. Package the strongest quarterly learnings into a regional print volume.
9. Launch fund drops or sponsor/public funding opportunities around the strongest editorial themes.

## Non-Negotiables

- City leads are the center of the motion. Media should increase their local surface area, not pull them away from the field.
- Content must lead with real communities and excellent events.
- Sponsor perspective should be dialed, but community trust wins over advertiser pressure.
- The media team produces and packages; city leads source, interview, host, and interpret.
- Every strong story should create a product follow-up: catalog record, host profile, sponsor lead, owned event, or asset-need insight.
