Analog should make the gathering economy visible, then turn that visibility into trusted supply, sponsor belief, governed funds, and repeatable product workflows.

This is the page for stakeholders who ask why Analog cares about media. The answer is practical: media is how the company discovers supply, builds affection, gives connectors status, shows sponsors the market, and routes attention back into governed funding.

<Info>
The job is not content for its own sake. Every story, gathering, profile, or fund drop should create a product follow-up: catalog record, host profile, connector profile, sponsor lead, Activation Opportunity, proof asset, fund, or asset-need note.
</Info>

## Core Loop

```text
AnalogNYC funds owned IRL -> city lead runs supply -> field context becomes media -> more hosts and sponsors enter -> profiles and funds unlock -> proof creates the next story
```

The media should feel culturally serious before it feels like a campaign. The platform should turn attention into governed funding, not unrestricted money.

## Analog City Brands

AnalogNYC, AnalogSF, AnalogLA, and later city brands are the owned media and supply surfaces for Analog's dogfood motion.

For the first six months, focus solely on AnalogNYC. The city brand should make local IRL feel legible, desirable, and fundable while using the platform to run campaigns, event sets, spend, proof, and follow-up.

| City brand job | What it creates |
|---|---|
| Social growth for Analog | Owned audience, recurring content, inbound Hosts, sponsor awareness, and cultural pull. |
| Category growth for IRL | More participation, stronger recurring rituals, better Hosts, and more fundable supply. |
| Community perspective | Qualitative proof that Analog is additive, tasteful, and welcome in the scene. |
| Direct response | Invite RSVPs, claims, applications, replies, and attendance from real people. |
| Spend transparency | Categorized city spend, receipts, proof packets, and a clearer view of what funding created. |

The city brand is not a vanity account. It is the visible front of a local operating system.

## Campaign Sharing Job

Media and owned IRL should help sponsor-backed campaigns travel through the right trust networks.

| Channel | Campaign-sharing role |
|---|---|
| Social | Show that a fund or program exists, who it is for, what it enables, and how qualified hosts can respond. |
| `The Connector` | Explain the market need behind a campaign and profile the kinds of builders it should attract. |
| YouTube and documentary clips | Make city leads, hosts, and fund opportunities feel real without reducing communities to inventory. |
| Owned IRL | Bring community leaders into the same room, explain money on the table, collect referrals, and route interest into applications. |
| Platform | Convert attention into campaign applications, host profiles, proof assets, collabs, and unlock decisions. |

The strongest media motion is not "look at this cool event." It is "this is a real market with trusted people, real sponsor demand, governed funding, and a path for the right operators to earn the next allocation."

## Roles

| Role | Owns |
|---|---|
| City leads | Field discovery, relationships, interviews, local meetups, host scouting, raw media, and sponsor context. |
| Head of Content | Editorial calendar, channel strategy, city priorities, quality bar, print schedule, and media team coordination. |

City leads source, interview, host, and interpret. The media team produces. The Analog team decides what becomes strategy, product, or GTM.

## Field Loop

| Step | Output |
|---|---|
| Attend and listen | Field notes on communities, rituals, spaces, builders, and trust constraints. |
| Capture | Short interviews, audio, video, photos, event footage, and sponsor perspective. |
| Submit | Slack intake with city, people captured, permissions, media links, story angle, sponsor fit, and product follow-up. |
| Produce | Social clips, essays, newsletters, video edits, graphics, print spreads, or recaps. |
| Route | Catalog update, host outreach, connector profile, sponsor pitch, owned event plan, fund idea, or asset-need note. |

Keep intake lightweight. If city leads stop submitting because the form is too heavy, the system is broken.

## Connector Talent

Dot connectors and community builders should become visible talent inside the gathering economy. The goal is not to turn them into generic influencers. The goal is to make their trust labor visible, fundable, bookable, and respected.

| Stage | Meaning | Analog support |
|---|---|---|
| Seen | The connector is no longer invisible | Profile, clip, interview, field note, or print mention |
| Legible | Their network, taste, and city context become understandable | Connector profile, catalog links, proof ledger, sponsor-safe summary |
| Bookable | Sponsors and hosts can ask them to assemble a moment | Activation Opportunity, RFE/RFC response, city brief, owned IRL format |
| Paid | They earn for real work | City lead retainer, production fee, editorial fee, operator margin, grant/admin fee, or rev share |
| Celebrated | Their work becomes culturally admired | Connector lists, documentaries, live stages, print features, awards, and owned gatherings |

The standard is simple: visibility should increase the connector's power, income, and trust at the same time.

## Funding Loop

Prestige should convert into governed funding access.

| Step | Platform motion |
|---|---|
| Discover | Create or update catalog record, host profile, connector profile, or asset-need note. |
| Elevate | Attach `The Connector`, social, print, or YouTube coverage to the profile and proof ledger. |
| Match | Recommend relevant sponsor briefs, funds, RFEs, RFCs, or campaigns. |
| Fund | Allocate approved scope, budget, spend rules, proof requirements, and spend access. |
| Prove | Capture spend, sentiment, content, attendance, claims, or outcomes. |
| Unlock | Recommend larger funds, repeat partnerships, owned IRL stages, or more editorial coverage. |

Use fund types such as city funds, category funds, public outcome funds, sponsor brief funds, strategic ecosystem funds, and editorial fund drops. Until compliant payout and tax rails exist, "funds to tap" means governed allocations, grants, event-scoped budgets, spend cards, or approved program budgets.

## Editorial Formats

Use repeatable formats that create status and product data.

| Format | Job |
|---|---|
| `City 25` | People making a city gather. |
| `The New Hosts` | Emerging organizers and community builders. |
| `Scene Report` | What is happening before sponsors understand it. |
| `How They Gather` | Photo essay on rituals and formats. |
| `The Funded Class` | Builders who turned trust into recurring funded work. |
| `Field Notes` | City lead notes from the ground. |
| `The Table` | Dinner or salon series with interviews and sponsor context. |

## Sponsor Lens

The media engine should be sponsor-aware without becoming generic sponsored content.

| Sponsor perspective | What the story should clarify |
|---|---|
| eComm Brands | Where product trial, taste, content, and recurring rituals can happen naturally. |
| Tech Companies | Which builders, founders, creators, operators, or strategic accounts should be in the room. |
| Public/Non-profit Funds | Which gatherings create access, participation, local legitimacy, civic value, or public outcomes. |

Sponsors should see edited context, not random inventory: why the person or scene matters, what funding would make possible, what proof exists, and what trust constraint matters.

## Owned IRL

Analog should run its own gatherings because the company is trying to build the gathering economy, not merely observe it.

Owned IRL should test the product and the lifestyle brand:

- event setup
- sponsor perspective
- spend controls where relevant
- field capture
- proof
- recap
- follow-up
- invite response
- city spend categorization
- whether the community wants Analog in orbit without needing a sponsor logo

Owned IRL can be community-builder happy hours, host dinners, sponsor-listening salons, walks, runs, live recordings, or regional print launches.

Use [Dogfooding Analog](/strategy/dogfooding-analog) to decide how owned IRL should route through AnalogNYC before expansion to other city brands.

## Guardrails

- Do not make community builders perform like influencers.
- Do not publish fragile communities without permission.
- Do not expose private networks as inventory.
- Do not make funds feel like unrestricted cash.
- Do not let sponsor pressure burn local credibility.
- Label sponsor-funded content clearly when needed.
- Keep community trust above media reach.

## Success Signals

Track:

- hosts and connectors discovered
- catalog records created
- connector profiles created
- interviews and field stories captured
- media shipped
- owned IRL attendance and follow-up
- invite RSVPs, claims, applications, and other direct response
- categorized spend for owned city activity
- sponsor fits identified
- funds unlocked
- proof assets attached
- sponsor renewal or next allocation

The best signal is compounding demand: more hosts want to be seen by Analog, more sponsors trust Analog's taste, and more connectors want to join the network.
