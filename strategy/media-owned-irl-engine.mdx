Analog should make the gathering economy visible, then turn that visibility into trusted supply, Sponsor belief, governed funds, and repeatable product workflows.

Read this after [Burn Bets And Operating Models](/strategy/burn-bets-operating-models). Burn explains what Analog can afford to test. This page explains how media and owned IRL make those tests visible.

Media is practical infrastructure. It helps Analog discover supply, build trust, give connectors status, show Sponsors the market, and route attention back into governed funding.

<Info>
Every story, gathering, profile, issue, or fund drop should create a product follow-up: catalog record, Host profile, connector profile, Sponsor lead, Activation Opportunity, proof asset, fund, or asset-need note.
</Info>

## Core Loop

```text
AnalogNYC and AnalogSF field work
-> seasonal print, newsletters, and owned IRL create access
-> distribution drives Hosts, connectors, Sponsors, and subscribers
-> funds and briefs move money into the right gatherings
-> proof creates the next issue, fund, renewal, or no-go
```

The media should feel culturally serious before it feels like a campaign. The platform should turn attention into governed funding, not unrestricted money.

## City Media System

| Surface | Job |
|---|---|
| Social | Show real communities, rituals, Hosts, spaces, recaps, and field clips. |
| `The Connector` | Publish profiles, city notes, event roundups, Sponsor perspectives, and market theses. |
| Analog city print | Build a profitable city object with upcoming events, Host profiles, scene data, fund drops, retail/eComm placements, and QR flows. |
| Seasonal posters and guides | Give mid-tier markets a physical artifact without full magazine overhead. |
| YouTube | Tell documentary-style city lead, builder, and scene stories. |
| Owned IRL | Host salons, dinners, walks, live recordings, founder breakfasts, and community-leader meetups. |
| Platform | Convert attention into Host profiles, connector profiles, proof ledgers, fund pages, spend cards, and unlocks. |

For round one, focus on AnalogNYC and AnalogSF. NYC remains the first operating proof; SF becomes the transfer market when local access and Sponsor demand justify it. Each city brand should make local IRL legible, desirable, and fundable while using the platform to run campaigns, event sets, spend, proof, and follow-up.

## Analog Print Mag Strategy

Print should not be treated as merch. It should be a profitable consumer and retail media product that owns city supply.

For NYC and SF especially, print can cut through because it is a real object in the places community already moves: boutiques, cafes, corner stores, gyms, coworking spaces, hotels, venues, and events. Seasonal mini-zines such as "AnalogNYC: Find Your Summer Community" should be paired with newsletters so the same field work can travel in smaller markets without requiring full print production.

The magazine creates the core physical circular motion:

```text
interview or feature
-> physical object people keep, share, and display
-> retail/subscription/event distribution
-> QR response into events, funds, claims, and applications
-> governed spend and proof
-> next issue, next Sponsor, next Host allocation
```

| Print job | What it does |
|---|---|
| Access wedge | An interview, profile, field note, event-calendar mention, or next-issue feature is a warmer ask than a sponsorship pitch. |
| Status object | Makes community builders, Hosts, and dot connectors feel culturally important. |
| Consumer surface | Subscriptions, coffee shops, gyms, coworking spaces, hotels, boutiques, events, and retail create distribution. |
| Supply engine | Routes featured builders into catalog records, fund pages, applications, QR claims, and Sponsor leads. |
| Sponsor-safe surface | Gives Sponsors tasteful placement when the fit supports the editorial product and is clearly labeled. |
| Proof artifact | Gives funders a physical record of what money enabled and which scenes are growing. |

Each issue should have four rails:

| Rail | Required output |
|---|---|
| Editorial | profiles, interviews, scene reports, event calendars, and fund stories worth keeping |
| Distribution | subscriber, retail, venue, event, and partner drop points with owners and quantities |
| Funding | QR flows into event pages, fund applications, Sponsor briefs, claims, and Host profiles |
| Product memory | catalog records, connector profiles, proof assets, Sponsor leads, and renewal notes |

The quality bar should feel premium and collectible, closer to an Apartamento-caliber independent design magazine than a disposable events flyer. The operating model still needs scale: repeatable interview kits, photo direction, local designer benches, print templates, vendor routing, issue calendars, QR standards, and a production system that can travel city by city.

Pair city print with Play Fair and owned Analog gatherings. Together, they should build a brand around the entrepreneurship and support of IRL, community building, and old-school marketing: rooms, print, relationships, local trust, and real-world distribution.

For markets that cannot yet support a print magazine, use the artifact ladder: seasonal festival posters and city guides for mid-tier markets; high-frequency social and newsletter drops for emerging markets; QR asset packs for any loaded event.

## Connector Talent

Dot connectors and community builders should become visible talent inside the gathering economy. The goal is not to turn them into generic influencers. The goal is to make their trust labor visible, fundable, bookable, and respected.

| Stage | Analog support |
|---|---|
| Seen | profile, clip, interview, field note, print mention |
| Legible | connector profile, catalog links, proof ledger, Sponsor-safe summary |
| Bookable | Activation Opportunity, RFE/RFC response, city brief, owned IRL format |
| Paid | retainer, production fee, editorial fee, operator margin, grant/admin fee, rev share |
| Celebrated | connector lists, documentaries, live stages, print features, awards, owned gatherings |

Visibility should increase the connector's power, income, and trust at the same time.

## Field And Funding Loop

| Step | Output |
|---|---|
| Attend and listen | field notes on communities, rituals, spaces, builders, and trust constraints |
| Capture | interviews, audio, video, photos, event footage, Sponsor perspective |
| Produce | social clips, essays, newsletters, video edits, graphics, print spreads, recaps |
| Route | catalog update, Host outreach, connector profile, Sponsor pitch, owned event plan, fund idea, asset-need note |
| Fund | approved scope, budget, spend rules, proof requirements, and spend access |
| Prove and unlock | spend, sentiment, attendance, claims, proof packet, larger fund, repeat partnership, more coverage |

Use fund types such as city funds, category funds, selective brand funds, public outcome funds, Sponsor brief funds, strategic ecosystem funds, and editorial fund drops. Until compliant payout and tax rails exist, "funds to tap" means governed allocations, grants, event-scoped budgets, spend cards, or approved program budgets.

Selective brand funds should be published through the city media system and the regional Host dashboard. A Crocs-style recovery fund in New York, a Baden-style rec league fund, or a Tapcart-style superconnector fund should have a clear local thesis, a public CTA, an application path, and proof standards before it is promoted.

## Guardrails And Signals

- Do not make community builders perform like influencers.
- Do not publish fragile communities without permission.
- Do not expose private networks as inventory.
- Do not make funds feel like unrestricted cash.
- Label sponsor-funded content clearly when needed.
- Keep community trust above media reach.

Track Hosts/connectors discovered, catalog records created, interviews captured, media shipped, owned IRL response, applications, QR claims, Sponsor fits, funds unlocked, proof attached, and repeat allocation.

The best signal is compounding demand: more Hosts want to be seen by Analog, more Sponsors trust Analog's taste, and more connectors want to join the network.
