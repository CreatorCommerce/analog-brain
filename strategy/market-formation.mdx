Analog should run four motions at the same time.

The seed deck's market-formation thesis is that IRL community was not manufactured by capital. It arrived through demand. Analog expands the number of IRL communities that can exist by making funding, proof, and renewal work.

For the next 12 months, these motions should concentrate around causeways where sponsor budget, city supply, media, owned IRL, and product proof reinforce each other. The first six months should focus on AnalogNYC as the dogfood city brand. Use [12-Month Market Focus](/strategy/twelve-month-market-focus) and [Dogfooding Analog](/strategy/dogfooding-analog) to decide which market-formation lanes deserve repeated investment.

Use [Cash Funding Playbooks](/strategy/cash-funding-playbooks) as the strategic center for this page. Market formation should be built around cash-based Host enablement, not product-only gifting or a single vertical.

## Motion 0: Owned City Dogfood

Analog funds Analog through Analog.

AnalogNYC should operate as the first owned city brand: a product lab, lifestyle brand surface, media engine, supply-side business, and spend/proof test in one motion.

This motion proves:

- city leads can run the supply side of a local Analog business
- owned IRL can create direct response, such as invite RSVPs and claims
- Analog can categorize its own NYC spend in a useful way
- social growth, category growth, community perspective, and spend transparency can live in the same proof system
- communities want Analog in orbit before a sponsor marketplace is fully live

The product path is:

```text
Analog city budget -> campaign -> event set -> Host event -> spend card -> proof -> media -> agency or sponsor demand -> next city allocation
```

## Motion 1: Selective Sponsor-Funded Pilots

These are controlled tests with serious sponsors.

They prove:

- sponsor demand
- spend governance
- host execution quality
- proof quality
- renewal logic
- which use cases deserve more product build

The product path is:

```text
Sponsor budget -> campaign -> event set or creator/community cohort -> brief -> host event or location activation -> spend watch -> proof -> unlock recommendation
```

## Motion 2: Market Formation

This motion creates sponsor-ready supply before the marketplace is liquid.

It can use:

- sponsor-shared campaigns, funds, and programs
- micro grants and macro grants
- Requests for Events
- Requests for Communities
- hosted sponsor forms
- Play Fair
- city leads
- owned Analog activations
- partner APIs
- host-side Requests for Sponsorship
- Event Prospecting Catalog records
- Asset Need Intelligence notes

Market formation proves:

- who exists
- who can execute
- which cities have missing spaces, hosts, insurance, vendors, or operators
- what sponsors want but cannot source yet
- what new supply should be created
- which shared campaign messages, budget shapes, and proof standards pull the strongest supply response

## Cash Funding Ladder

Market formation needs a funding ladder.

| Funding type | Use when | What it teaches |
|---|---|---|
| Micro Grant | A small amount of cash can fund coffee, libations, supplies, rentals, printed materials, accessibility, or light venue support. | Whether removing tiny costs increases cadence, quality, attendance, or Host goodwill. |
| Starter Series | A Host can repeat monthly or across a small sequence with basic support. | Whether the format becomes a reliable playbook rather than a one-off. |
| Macro Grant | The organizer is unusually strong, strategic, or operationally reliable. | Whether deeper funding creates category growth, strategic access, public value, or a durable operator relationship. |
| Operator Retainer | The Host is effectively a city, category, or ecosystem builder. | Whether they can become a recurring supply engine for Analog and its Sponsors. |

The point is to fund the Host's actual goal. Coffee, space, supplies, design, insurance, printing, or a speaker fee may do more to create community value than product placement ever could.

## Motion 3: Media And Owned IRL

This motion makes the gathering economy visible while feeding the product and sales motions.

It can use:

- social media about communities and events happening everywhere
- `The Connector` Substack newsletter
- quarterly regional print editions of `The Connector`
- YouTube documentary videos about city leads and local scenes
- city lead interviews, field notes, audio, video, and event footage
- owned Analog happy hours, salons, dinners, walks, and community-leader meetups
- a Head of Content and contracted media team for writing, design, editing, and distribution

Media and owned IRL prove:

- which communities deserve attention
- which cities have momentum
- which hosts and dot connectors should enter the catalog
- which of the three sponsor perspectives naturally fit a scene
- which stories create inbound host, sponsor, and attendee interest
- which owned gatherings Analog should repeat or productize
- which sponsor-shared campaigns should be launched, promoted, or refined in-market

## Agency Engagement Bridge

Before the marketplace is materially live, Analog can run agency engagements with brands that want access to the human network and prospecting practices Analog is building.

Agency engagements should still feed product objects: sponsor perspective, brief, event set, supply search, Host package, collab, governed spend, proof packet, and renewal logic. They are valuable when they produce sponsor demand, city supply, and product learning. They are dangerous when they become disconnected bespoke services.

## Funding Curve Rule

Funding is not just a reward after a community succeeds. Funding can create the next stage of supply by reducing burnout, reactivating dormant clubs, raising event quality, and helping first-timers become regulars.

Use this rule when evaluating a market:

| Market state | Analog response |
|---|---|
| Existing trusted community, weak economics | Add sponsor funding, spend controls, proof, and operator upside. |
| Sponsor demand, no obvious host | Use city leads, grants, RFEs, RFCs, or owned activations to create supply. |
| Community demand, poor sponsor fit | Protect the community and search for a better sponsor perspective or sponsor fit. |
| Strong behavior, no trust layer | Make the new funding behavior feel safe and normal through approval, transparency, and proof. |
| Public money, weak distribution | Build eligibility, controlled spend, local proof, and reporting so honest builders can receive funds faster. |

## How They Compound

```text
Sponsor-shared campaign -> visible money on the table -> supply response -> owned IRL and media amplify the ask -> governed spend -> proof -> sharper next campaign
```

## Product Rule

The four motions should converge into the same product objects. A grant applicant should be able to become catalog supply. A city lead discovery should be able to become a host record. A host-side sponsorship package should be able to become a sponsor campaign. A completed pilot should become a reusable playbook. A strong interview, social clip, newsletter profile, or owned Analog meetup should become a catalog record, host profile, sponsor lead, asset-need note, or event-set prospect.

## Producer Bench Rule

Early pilots and owned activations need real operators. The producer bench should not become an open-ended services trap; its job is to:

- run early activations well enough to earn trust
- reveal where product objects are missing
- convert manual runbooks into repeatable docs, tasks, files, proofs, and templates
- protect community taste while satisfying sponsor requirements
- teach Analog which city/category motions are actually repeatable
