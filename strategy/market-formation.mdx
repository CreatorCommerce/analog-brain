Analog should enter the market by running four motions together:

Read this after [12-Month Market Focus](/strategy/twelve-month-market-focus). That page says where Analog should focus first. This page explains how those markets get formed.

1. Owned city dogfood.
2. Selective Sponsor-funded pilots.
3. Supply creation before the marketplace is liquid.
4. Media, print, connectors, and owned IRL.

The thesis is simple: IRL community was not manufactured by capital, but capital can help more trusted communities exist when funding, governance, proof, and renewal are handled well.

The sharper market-formation rule is supply-first: own the regional supply layer before overfitting to any upstream Sponsor category. SaaS/tech, eComm, civic/nonprofit, and political action keep opening doors into the same communities and IRL leaders. Analog should win the city supply map, then sell governed access, proof, and cultural judgment to the organizations that need that map.

CAC should look like overhead-bet market formation, not unit-driven referral spend. Analog should use small funds, free tools, editorial access, and owned IRL to find powder-keg supply: the Hosts, gatherers, dot connectors, spaces, and scenes where one good unlock creates more demand behind it.

Use [12-Month Market Focus](/strategy/twelve-month-market-focus) for the first-year priority, [Cash Funding Playbooks](/strategy/cash-funding-playbooks) for the grant ladder, [Public And Nonprofit Funding Infrastructure](/strategy/public-nonprofit-funding-infrastructure) for grant discovery and public-fund controls, and [Media, Connectors, And Funding Engine](/strategy/media-owned-irl-engine) for the media/print loop.

## The Motions

| Motion | What it does | Product output |
|---|---|---|
| Owned city dogfood | AnalogNYC uses Analog to run owned IRL, categorize spend, capture proof, publish media, and learn the city playbook. | city brand, campaign, event set, Host event, spend card, proof packet, catalog update |
| Selective Sponsor pilots | Controlled tests with serious Sponsors prove budget, Host fit, spend governance, proof, and renewal. | Sponsor brief, event set, collab, Host event, spend watch, proof, unlock recommendation |
| Selective brand funds | Analog designs a small number of market-fit public funds with enterprise Sponsors before the full marketplace exists. | fund thesis, campaign, event set, application portal, regional dashboard placement, proof packet |
| Market formation | Analog creates sponsor-ready supply before the marketplace is liquid. | fund, RFE/RFC, application, catalog record, asset-need note, Host profile |
| Media and owned IRL | Stories, print, Connector profiles, Play Fair, and Analog gatherings make the market visible and trusted. | media asset, inbound Host, Sponsor lead, connector profile, fund drop |
| Agency bridge | Brands pay for Analog's human network and prospecting practices before software liquidity exists. | brief, supply search, Host package, proof packet, renewal logic |
| City department buildout | Campaign demand funds a deeper regional layer when the market needs more local infrastructure. | city lead, advisory board, grant pool, city P&L, local media, operator bench, regional intelligence |

Agency work is useful when it produces sponsor demand, city supply, and product learning. It is dangerous when it becomes disconnected bespoke services.

## Supply Capture Stack

For summer through fall, dominate three supply-capture motions before chasing more surface area:

| Motion | Operating rule | What to avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Pitch tools | Give Hosts and connectors a free, useful way to package verifiable event history, social proof, reach, Sponsor fit, photos, and asks. | A generic profile builder with no Sponsor-ready output. |
| Grants and raffles | Turn each customer campaign into a visible fund, raffle, or application flow that recruits more qualified supply. | Free-money hooks that hide eligibility, spend rules, or proof requirements. |
| Selective brand funds | Use market-fit enterprise capital to launch public funds that should work in a specific circuit, such as New York run recovery or SF superconnector dinners. | Copying a fund into markets where the Sponsor fit, supply density, or Host value is weak. |
| Government grant discovery | Find public, nonprofit, foundation, BID, university, hospital, and public/private funds Hosts may qualify for, then prepare application materials with Host authorization. | Custom grant consulting that does not create reusable Host records, grant maps, proof, or Sponsor-ready supply. |
| Seasonal print and newsletters | Use city zines and newsletters as reasons to interview leaders, list events, create status, and route readers into funds. | Content that does not create catalog records, applications, QR response, or Sponsor leads. |

## Generated Supply Loop

Analog should not treat supply as a fixed list. The platform can access existing supply, activate latent supply, generate missing supply, and expand proven supply.

The loop:

```text
Sponsor, public, or community money becomes visible
-> existing and latent Hosts raise their hands
-> Analog funds or supports the best options
-> new proof makes the market more legible
-> existing supply moves closer to Analog to access future money
```

This is how generated supply pulls existing supply inward. If Analog funds a new run series, dinner circuit, chess night, farmers market activation, founder salon, venue program, or community collective through visible rules and proof, the established operators around that market see where funding is being deployed. The strongest existing Hosts then have a reason to claim their record, upload proof, join the regional dashboard, respond to funds, and keep their Analog profile current.

| Supply state | What Analog does | Product memory created | Pull effect on existing supply |
|---|---|---|---|
| Existing supply | Find trusted Hosts, venues, rituals, and operators already gathering people. | Catalog record, Host profile, proof history, Sponsor fit, funding ask. | Existing Hosts join because Analog can bring money, proof, media, and better Sponsors. |
| Latent supply | Find people with trust, audience, or venue access who are not yet running a repeatable gathering. | Application, city lead note, asset-need note, starter event, service/tool request. | Nearby existing Hosts see new funded peers and come closer to defend status, access, and future opportunity. |
| Missing supply | A Sponsor, public fund, or community need exists, but the right Host, space, operator, vendor, insurance, or proof path is missing. | RFE/RFC, Event set, operator brief, Asset Need Intelligence, grant or bounty, no-go rule. | Existing supply is invited into a specific paid gap instead of being asked to join a generic marketplace. |
| Expanded supply | A proven Host can add a second event, new chapter, partner venue, seasonal series, or cross-community collab. | Collab, Host event, recurring proof, rate learning, new Venue or connector node. | Peer Hosts see that Analog rewards growth, not just one-off sponsorship. |
| Institutional supply | Venues, BIDs, nonprofits, schools, coworking spaces, and local businesses can host or underwrite more recurring IRL. | Sponsor account, public/nonprofit fund lane, Venue record, vendor/payee record, proof standard. | Existing community operators move closer because institutions route budgets through Analog. |

## Generated Supply Motions

Use generated supply when the market has demand or energy but not enough fundable options.

| Motion | Use when | What Analog creates | How it pulls existing supply closer |
|---|---|---|---|
| Money-on-the-table fund | Sponsor or public money can name a clear audience, city, format, or outcome. | Brand fund, public fund, raffle, application portal, regional dashboard placement. | Existing Hosts apply because the money is real and the rules are visible. |
| RFE or RFC | A Sponsor wants events or communities that are not obvious yet. | Request for Events or Request for Communities with a clear budget and proof standard. | Existing Hosts respond to a concrete paid ask instead of cold outreach. |
| Starter grant | A trusted operator needs a small push to make a ritual repeat. | Micro Grant, starter series, spend card, QR assets, proof checklist. | Established Hosts see Analog can fund the first version of a real community business. |
| Operator commission | The idea, audience, or venue exists, but no one owns execution. | Operator retainer, city lead brief, producer bench task, managed activation support. | Existing operators come closer because Analog can route paid operating work. |
| Venue activation | A space has community potential but weak programming or proof. | Venue profile, Venue reward, event series, social listening authorization, local fund. | Existing Hosts and Venues want to be in the venue graph where Sponsor budgets land. |
| Media and print artifact | The scene exists but lacks shared visibility or status. | Print inclusion, city guide, newsletter drop, profile, QR path, fund drop. | Existing supply claims its place before the artifact becomes the market record. |
| Public grant desk | Free public or nonprofit money can unlock Hosts that Sponsors do not yet see. | Universal application, guided grant flow, grant packet, eligibility map, report draft. | Existing Hosts keep Analog records current because the next grant or Sponsor fund can reuse them. |
| Community collective fund | Local businesses or funders want more IRL nearby without waiting for a national brand. | Sponsor-owned collective campaign, contributor records, applications, proof packets. | Existing supply moves toward the neighborhood funding pool because it becomes the local capital center. |

Generated supply must stay honest. Analog should not manufacture fake community, inflate weak operators, or force Sponsor demand into unreceptive spaces. The job is to remove real blockers around money, proof, space, services, and coordination so trusted people can gather more often.

## Grant Desk As Market Formation

The grant desk is not a side quest. It is a supply-capture motion that gives Hosts a reason to load their event history before the Sponsor marketplace is fully liquid.

```text
free-money search
-> Host uploads proof
-> Analog packages eligibility and application materials
-> Host becomes more fundable
-> public or Sponsor money replaces company-funded MAC
-> market supply becomes visible and governable
```

| Market-formation job | What the grant desk does | What should compound |
|---|---|---|
| Pull supply forward | "Load your history and Analog will help find money" is a sharper Host promise than "make a profile." | Host records, applications, referrals, and city supply density. |
| Lower company-funded MAC | Public grants, foundation money, BID funds, and Sponsor-backed funds can pay for activation that Analog would otherwise subsidize. | External support per company dollar spent. |
| Make Hosts sponsor-ready | The same proof file used for public grants becomes the pitch file for Crocs, Tapcart, Anthropic, Shopify, or a local funder. | Better Host packages and cleaner Sponsor diligence. |
| Reveal public-buyer pain | Repeated applications show where agencies struggle with intake, eligibility, documents, reporting, and fraud/waste/abuse controls. | A workflow pitch to agencies, nonprofits, and public/private coalitions. |
| Teach allowable-use reality | Grants expose which costs are fundable: space, supplies, access, arts, public realm, wellness, youth, small business, climate, and reporting. | Better fund design and safer spend rules. |

The grant desk should stay productized. Every application must create a reusable grant opportunity, Host readiness note, files/docs packet, proof standard, or public-fund no-go rule. If the work does not improve the market map, it is consulting.

## Moat Defense

Analog's defensibility cannot be "we know events" or "we have nice invites." The moat has to compound in assets that are hard to copy quickly.

| Moat asset | How it compounds | How it fails |
|---|---|---|
| Trusted Host and connector graph | City leads, referrals, interviews, owned IRL, proof history, and print features reveal who can be trusted with capital. | The graph becomes a stale directory with no spend, proof, or relationship depth. |
| Funding and proof memory | Each campaign teaches budget shape, approved uses, spend behavior, proof quality, sentiment, and renewal logic. | Analog runs one-off activations without improving underwriting. |
| Print and media distribution | Analog Print Mag, `The Connector`, social, and Play Fair make hidden supply visible and desirable before competitors know where to look. | Media becomes generic awareness instead of supply access, QR response, fund applications, and Sponsor leads. |
| Sponsor renewal logic | Sponsors learn that Analog can turn vague IRL ambition into packages, spend controls, proof, and next-dollar recommendations. | Sponsors treat Analog as a production agency and bypass the product after the first event. |
| Public/nonprofit trust controls | Eligibility, allowable-use rules, receipts, anomaly review, and public-safe reporting make downstream community funding safer. | Public money work becomes slow custom grant consulting without reusable controls. |
| Central processing stack | Field notes become catalog records, Host profiles, Sponsor packages, proof packets, product requirements, and no-go rules. | City leads become bottlenecks and local knowledge never becomes company memory. |

False moats to avoid: invite design, raw attendee data, vibes, generic event inventory, one-off production talent, and product gifting volume.

## Local-First Market Model

Analog should scale city by city to deeply understand regional gathering dynamics through the platform and key local leaders. Do not pursue a generic open-ended marketplace from the outset. Each market has different communities, venues, cultural rhythms, distribution channels, local enterprises, and Sponsor opportunities.

The operating model should look more like early regional marketplace operations than a pure software launch, with AI making the team leaner:

| Layer | Standard |
|---|---|
| Market selection | Pick 2 to 3 core markets where founder roots, Sponsor demand, or supply density make faster proof possible. Round one is AnalogNYC and AnalogSF. |
| City lead | Appoint one accountable local lead, FTE or contractor, who can reach trusted operators and interpret local norms. |
| Local advisory board | Build a small region-representative group that helps Analog avoid blind spots and identify trusted supply. |
| Community grant pool | Give the city lead USD 10k to USD 50k to use strategically across micro grants, fund drops, and proof cases. |
| Selective brand-fund closer | Use the existing ex-Crocs-style enterprise partnerships muscle to close a few market-fit funds before a full Sponsor marketplace exists. |
| Market-specific media | Publish print zines, event guides, profiles, social channels, or city reports designed for the market rather than generic company content. |
| Regional Host dashboard | Let Hosts see the city layer they belong to, local funds, perk requests, media opportunities, and proof prompts. |
| City KPIs | Measure supply, spend, proof, media, Sponsor demand, Host trust, and city economics by region, never only in aggregate. |

Analog city layers such as AnalogNYC and AnalogSF should become operating departments inside the company. They are lean under the hood, but loud in-market because the core platform turns field work into campaigns, event sets, collabs, spend records, proof, profiles, media, and Sponsor summaries.

When a strategic campaign needs deeper local infrastructure, campaign dollars can justify spinning up or expanding a city department. A hypothetical USD 10M Mars campaign could fund Analog Denver to build supply, understand the market, and activate communities around a tentpole initiative. A company like Anthropic or OpenAI could use Analog to support IRL communities in far markets with rails, trust, attribution models, and cultural judgment that reduce the risk of entering sensitive spaces badly.

## Cash Funding Ladder

Market formation should be built around cash-based Host enablement, not product-only gifting.

| Funding type | Use when | What it teaches |
|---|---|---|
| Micro Grant | A small amount of cash funds coffee, libations, supplies, rentals, print, accessibility, or light venue support. | Whether tiny costs unlock stronger rituals. |
| Starter Series | A Host can repeat monthly or across a small sequence. | Whether the format becomes a playbook. |
| Macro Grant | The organizer is unusually strong, strategic, or operationally reliable. | Whether deeper funding creates category, account, civic, or local scene value. |
| Operator Retainer | The Host is effectively a city, category, or ecosystem builder. | Whether they become recurring supply infrastructure. |

The point is to fund the Host's actual goal. Coffee, space, supplies, design, insurance, printing, or a speaker fee may do more to create community value than product placement.

## Supply Decision Rule

Do not start every market problem with "find more events." Pick the supply motion that fits the evidence.

| Motion | Use when | Output | Pull effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Access existing supply | Trusted Hosts, venues, and rituals already exist. | Catalog records, Host profiles, sponsor-ready packages, proof history. | The best existing operators move closer when Analog can fund, prove, and renew them. |
| Activate latent supply | Trusted people, spaces, or audiences exist, but the gathering is dormant or irregular. | Starter grants, office hours, QR assets, pitch pages, service/tool support. | Existing supply sees new competitors or collaborators gaining funding and status through Analog. |
| Generate missing supply | Sponsor or public demand exists, but the Host-side option is missing, dormant, or underfunded. | Micro grants, macro grants, RFEs, RFCs, owned IRL labs, spend cards, operator commissions. | Analog becomes the place operators check for paid gaps in the market. |
| Publish supply into view | The market has energy, but Sponsors and product cannot see it clearly. | Print, social, `The Connector`, Play Fair, inbound Hosts, city lead follow-up. | Existing supply claims records before the public market map hardens without them. |
| Prospect off-platform | The right supply probably exists but is not in Analog yet. | Event set prospecting, applications, enriched catalog records. | Off-platform supply enters Analog because a specific funded opportunity is attached. |
| Expand proven supply | One Host, Venue, or operator has proof that can support more activity. | Recurring series, new Venue node, cross-community collab, operator retainer, macro grant. | Strong existing Hosts stay close because Analog is where expansion capital is allocated. |

Repeated blockers should become catalog fields and city lead tasks, not separate strategy threads.

## Blocked Market Signals

When a pilot stalls, log the missing asset: Host, space, insurance, operator, vendor, services/tools, proof, Sponsor fit, public trust controls, or capital. Repeated gaps tell Analog what to build, partner for, fund, or stop pursuing.

## Funding Curve Rule

Funding is not only a reward after a community succeeds. Funding can create the next stage of supply by reducing burnout, reactivating dormant clubs, raising event quality, and helping first-timers become regulars.

| Market state | Analog response |
|---|---|
| Existing trusted community, weak economics | Add Sponsor funding, spend controls, proof, and operator upside. |
| Sponsor demand, no obvious Host | Use city leads, grants, RFEs, RFCs, media, or owned activations to create supply. |
| Community demand, poor Sponsor fit | Protect the community and search for a better Sponsor perspective. |
| Strong behavior, no trust layer | Use approval, transparency, and proof to make the funding behavior safe and normal. |
| Public/nonprofit money, weak distribution | Build eligibility, controlled spend, local proof, and reporting for downstream builders. |

## Product Rule

Every motion should converge into product memory:

```text
Sponsor-shared campaign
-> visible money on the table
-> supply response
-> regional dashboard and media route the ask locally
-> media and owned IRL amplify the ask
-> governed spend
-> proof
-> sharper next campaign
```

A grant applicant can become catalog supply. A city lead discovery can become a Host record. A host-side sponsorship package can become a Sponsor campaign. A completed pilot can become a reusable playbook. A strong interview or print feature can become a Host profile, Sponsor lead, asset-need note, or event-set prospect.

## Producer Bench Rule

Early pilots need real operators, but the producer bench should not become an open-ended services trap.

Use producers to run early activations well, reveal missing product objects, turn manual runbooks into templates, protect community taste, satisfy Sponsor requirements, and identify which city/category motions actually repeat.
