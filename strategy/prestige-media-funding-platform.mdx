Analog should make community builders, event organizers, and dot connectors feel like the cultural figures they already are.

The reference energy is Vogue and GQ: taste, photography, profiles, lists, covers, scene reports, authority, style, and status. Analog should not copy those brands. It should bring that level of editorial seriousness to the people building the gathering economy.

Then the platform should turn that attention into governed funding access.

## Core Thesis

Prestige creates belief. Belief attracts sponsors. Sponsors create funds. Funds create more gatherings. More gatherings create more proof, media, and status.

```text
Editorial prestige -> talent profile -> sponsor belief -> fund access -> activation -> proof -> more prestige
```

The platform should make builders feel like they have endless opportunity to tap, while keeping every dollar structured through approved funds, briefs, spend rules, and proof.

## What Vogue/GQ Energy Means

It means:

- beautiful editorial profiles of community builders, organizers, city leads, and dot connectors
- documentary photography and video, not generic brand recap content
- regional lists of the people shaping the gathering economy
- sharp writing about scenes, rituals, taste, cities, and cultural momentum
- print artifacts people want to keep
- covers, features, interviews, and awards that create status
- sponsor-safe context that explains why a person or scene matters
- platform profiles that convert prestige into opportunities

It does not mean:

- making community builders perform like influencers
- pay-to-play editorial coverage
- turning fragile communities into sponsor inventory
- over-polishing away local truth
- exposing private relationships without permission

## The Platform Loop

| Step | Media motion | Platform motion |
|---|---|---|
| Discover | City lead finds a builder, organizer, connector, scene, or event | Create or update catalog record, host profile, connector profile, or asset-need note |
| Elevate | `The Connector`, social, print, or YouTube publishes the story | Attach media to the profile and proof ledger |
| Match | Sponsors understand which perspective fits: eComm, Tech, or Public/Non-profit | Recommend relevant funds, briefs, RFEs, RFCs, or sponsor campaigns |
| Fund | A sponsor or public fund backs the builder's next gathering | Allocate approved funds, spend card, scope, rules, and proof requirements |
| Prove | The gathering happens and generates spend, sentiment, content, attendance, or outcomes | Update proof, sponsor report, profile, and unlock score |
| Compound | The builder becomes more trusted and more famous | Unlock bigger funds, more editorial coverage, owned IRL stages, and repeat partnerships |

## On-Platform Surfaces

Analog should eventually support a media-plus-funding experience with these surfaces:

| Surface | Job |
|---|---|
| Builder profile | Public or sponsor-safe page for a community builder, organizer, city lead, or dot connector. |
| Editorial feature | The prestige artifact: profile, interview, cover, list, print spread, video, or city report. |
| Proof ledger | Shows gatherings, communities helped, funds deployed, proof collected, media shipped, and sponsor outcomes. |
| Funding tab | Shows funds the builder can apply to, unlock, or be matched with. |
| Fund page | Sponsor, public, or category fund with mission, budget, geography, eligible hosts, proof needs, and guardrails. |
| Activation Opportunity | Connector-originated proposal for an event, series, city moment, or community need. |
| Owned IRL stage | Analog-hosted gathering where builders, sponsors, and communities meet and generate field context. |

## Funding Architecture

The platform should feel abundant, not chaotic.

Use fund types like:

| Fund type | Example | Best sponsor perspective |
|---|---|---|
| City fund | `NYC Community Builder Fund` | All three perspectives |
| Category fund | `Running Culture Fund` or `Founder Breakfast Fund` | eComm Brands or Tech Companies |
| Public outcome fund | `Neighborhood Cleanup Fund` or `Youth Third Places Fund` | Public/Non-profit Funds |
| Sponsor brief fund | `On Running Community Recovery Fund` | eComm Brands |
| Strategic ecosystem fund | `AI Builders Dinner Fund` | Tech Companies |
| Editorial fund drop | A fund launched alongside a Connector issue or regional print edition | Any perspective |

The feeling can be "there is always another fund to unlock." The mechanism should stay governed:

- eligible builder or host type
- geography
- sponsor perspective
- approved spend categories
- budget range
- proof requirement
- community trust constraints
- renewal or unlock condition

## Builder Funding Journey

A community builder should experience Analog like:

1. Get discovered by a city lead or apply/claim a profile.
2. Build a profile that shows taste, relationships, scene knowledge, proof, and prior gatherings.
3. Get featured through `The Connector`, social, YouTube, print, or owned IRL.
4. See matched funds and briefs they can tap.
5. Submit an Activation Opportunity or Request for Sponsorship.
6. Receive approved funds with clear rules, spend access, and scope.
7. Run the gathering.
8. Submit or connect proof.
9. Unlock more funds, better sponsors, more status, and larger opportunities.

This turns prestige into a practical career path.

## Editorial Formats

Use repeatable formats that create status:

- `The New Hosts`: profiles of emerging community builders
- `City 25`: the 25 people making a city gather
- `The Connector Cover`: one builder or connector as the face of a regional issue
- `Scene Report`: what is happening in a city before sponsors understand it
- `How They Gather`: photo essays on rituals and formats
- `The Funded Class`: builders who turned trust into funded vocation
- `The Table`: dinner or salon series with interviews and sponsor perspectives
- `Field Notes`: city lead notes from the ground
- `The List`: sponsor-safe guide to operators, hosts, spaces, and connectors

## Sponsor Experience

Sponsors should not browse random people. They should see edited context.

For each builder or scene, show:

- why this person matters
- what communities they can unlock
- which sponsor perspective fits
- what funding would make possible
- what proof exists
- what risk or trust constraint matters
- what the next right activation could be

Sponsors are buying trust, judgment, access, and outcomes. Editorial makes the judgment legible. Product makes the funding governable.

## Design Standard

The platform should feel like a high-status cultural publication attached to a serious funding system.

Design principles:

- editorial first, dashboards second
- real photography and video over generic tiles
- profiles that feel collectible
- strong typography and city/scene identity
- fund pages that feel clear and premium, not bureaucratic
- proof that supports status instead of flattening it into metrics only
- sponsor controls that stay powerful but quiet

## Guardrails

- Label sponsor-funded content clearly when needed.
- Keep editorial judgment independent from fund allocation pressure.
- Do not expose private networks as inventory.
- Do not imply funds are unrestricted cash.
- Do not create open-ended wallet balances until compliance, payouts, and tax handling exist.
- Keep money event-scoped or fund-scoped unless the product intentionally adds a compliant payout rail.
- Make community trust the status signal, not follower count alone.

## Product Boundary

Current Analog can support pieces of this through campaigns, event sets, applications, host profiles, hosted event pages, files/docs, spend cards, content proof, and data summaries.

The prestige media platform and fund marketplace are future-facing product directions. Until the platform has production funding, payout, and compliance rails, "funds to tap" should mean governed sponsor allocations, grants, event-scoped budgets, spend cards, or approved program budgets, not unrestricted withdrawable money.
