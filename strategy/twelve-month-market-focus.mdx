Analog's market is too big to attack horizontally. The next 12 months should find the causeways where community energy, sponsor budget, city supply, media attention, and product proof already reinforce each other.

<Info>
The 12-month objective is to build the first deep city dogfood loop in NYC, spend six months solely on AnalogNYC, then bring in AnalogSF after NYC proves repeatable supply, spend transparency, proof, media affection, and agency demand.
</Info>

Use [Lean GTM Operating Model](/strategy/lean-gtm-operating-model) for operating constraints and [Sponsor Personas And Pipeline](/strategy/sponsor-personas) to keep sponsor targeting from becoming a logo list.

Use [Cash Funding Playbooks](/strategy/cash-funding-playbooks) as the correction to any strategy that over-focuses on run clubs or product gifting. The first race is cash-based Host enablement plus media, services, and proof that can travel across many community families.

## Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Stream | A pre-existing flow of people, money, attention, ritual, trust, or unmet demand. Analog should swim with streams instead of inventing demand from zero. |
| Causeway | A repeatable bridge Analog can operate across a stream: a sponsor fund, city lead motion, Connector format, owned IRL series, event-set prospecting lane, or fund drop. |
| City brand | An owned local Analog surface such as AnalogNYC, AnalogSF, or AnalogLA that runs supply, media, owned IRL, spend, proof, and community memory through the product. |
| Affection moat | The trust, status, gratitude, and cultural pull that makes hosts, connectors, and sponsors want to be part of Analog before the product is fully inevitable. |
| Dogfood loop | Analog funding its own city brand through Analog, running supply through city leads, capturing customer-grade KPIs, categorizing spend, publishing media, and improving the platform. |

## The 12-Month Answer

Chase places where five currents overlap:

- sponsor budget already has a reason to move
- communities or builders are already gathering
- a city lead can reach the real operators
- the story is interesting enough for media
- the workflow can become product objects instead of bespoke services

The first 12 months should answer:

1. Which sponsor motions repeat?
2. Which supply paths compound fastest?
3. Which moats and affection loops make Analog harder to copy?

## City Sequence

Depth beats premature city spread.

| Phase | City focus | What must be true before moving on |
|---|---|---|
| Months 0-6 | AnalogNYC only | NYC has recurring owned IRL, city lead supply rhythm, categorized spend, direct RSVP response, social growth, category growth, community perspective, and agency engagement learning. |
| Months 7-12 | Add AnalogSF | SF starts only after NYC has a transferable playbook, not just a collection of impressive one-offs. |
| Later | AnalogLA, AnalogMiami, and others | Add when the city brand model can travel while preserving community trust and product discipline. |

Use [Dogfooding Analog](/strategy/dogfooding-analog) as the operating doctrine for this sequence.

## Right Sponsor Motion

Prefer reusable briefs, category funds, city funds, and fund drops over one-off custom activations.

| Sponsor perspective | Right motion | Strong early signal |
|---|---|---|
| eComm Brands | Fund product-adjacent rituals, sampling, benefits, recovery, pet-parent rituals, creator-led IRL, and category growth. | The brand already believes communities influence buying habits, content, trial, or loyalty. |
| Tech Companies | Fund strategic access to builders, founders, operators, developers, creators, customers, partners, or accounts. | A high-trust room is more valuable than broad impressions, and the sponsor has a clear adoption or relationship goal. |
| Public/Non-profit Funds | Fund access, civic participation, wellness, arts, entrepreneurship, public space, youth, climate, local commerce, or other outcomes. | The outcome needs local legitimacy, and Analog can prove participation, community formation, or public value. |

The question is not only "who pays?" It is "which budget can become a repeatable fund, brief, or underwriting loop?"

Avoid building the early thesis around product-only gifting. Product can be an ingredient, but the stronger Analog wedge is cash that lets Hosts do the job they already know needs doing.

## Right Rapid Supply Motion

Rapid supply should come from three paths at once:

| Path | Use when | Product output |
|---|---|---|
| Access existing supply | Trusted hosts, communities, venues, and recurring rituals already exist. | Catalog records, host profiles, event-set prospects, sponsor-ready packages, and proof history. |
| Create supply with funding | Sponsor or public demand exists, but the host-side option is missing, dormant, underfunded, or unassembled. | Grants, RFEs, RFCs, Activation Opportunities, owned IRL labs, spend cards, and asset-need notes. |
| Publish supply into existence | The market has energy, but sponsors and product cannot see it clearly yet. | Social, `The Connector`, YouTube, print, connector profiles, inbound hosts, and city lead follow-up. |

City leads should keep asking:

- What already exists that can become sponsor-ready?
- What would exist if funding removed the blocker?
- What becomes legible only if Analog documents and celebrates it?
- Which connector or builder can unlock multiple options, not just one event?
- Which opportunity can become a repeatable fund, series, or playbook?

## Right Moats And Affection

The first moat is not pure software. The first moat is trust that becomes software.

| Moat | How Analog builds it |
|---|---|
| City trust graph | City leads build real relationships across hosts, connectors, spaces, vendors, sponsors, and scenes. |
| Event Prospecting Catalog | Field discovery becomes structured, provenance-aware supply intelligence. |
| Connector status | Dot connectors and community builders become visible, paid, sponsor-safe talent. |
| Owned media | `The Connector`, social, YouTube, print, and field notes make Analog the publication of the gathering economy. |
| Owned IRL | Analog hosts the rooms, salons, walks, dinners, and happy hours that prove the motion from inside. |
| Funding and proof loop | Funds, spend cards, proof ledgers, and unlocks make sponsor participation safer and renewable. |
| Services/tooling layer | Insurance, documents, print, vendors, local designers, and proof kits reduce activation friction and make cash useful faster. |
| Sponsor perspective taste | Analog learns how eComm, Tech, and Public/Non-profit sponsors should show up without flattening community trust. |

Affection matters because early marketplaces are fragile. Hosts should feel seen, funded, and more powerful. Sponsors should feel Analog gives them taste, governance, and proof they cannot get from a dashboard alone.

## Dogfood The Product

Analog should use its own product to build Analog.

```text
Analog funds AnalogNYC -> city lead runs supply -> campaign or event set -> host event -> spend/proof -> Connector coverage -> inbound supply -> catalog/profile updates -> agency engagement or next fund
```

Every owned gathering should test setup, spend rules, RSVP/claim/attendance capture, proof, content intake, catalog updates, follow-up, and NYC spend categorization.

Owned IRL is the product lab, lifestyle brand engine, and supply-side operating system.

## Stream Test

Use this test before committing a city, category, or use case to the 12-month agenda.

| Question | Strong signal | Weak signal |
|---|---|---|
| Is there a community stream? | People already gather, repeat, invite, and care. | Analog has to manufacture all attendance and desire. |
| Is there a sponsor stream? | One of the three sponsor perspectives already has budget logic. | The sponsor fit requires too much explanation or taste compromise. |
| Is there a city lead stream? | A city lead can reach trusted operators quickly. | Discovery depends only on cold research and public lists. |
| Is there a media stream? | The people, rituals, and city story are worth publishing. | The story only exists as a recap for the sponsor. |
| Is there a product stream? | The motion maps to campaigns, event sets, host events, spend, proof, profiles, catalog records, or funds. | The work stays trapped in custom operations. |
| Is there a funding stream? | Money would reduce burnout, raise quality, improve access, or create repeat supply. | Money mostly buys a one-time stunt. |
| Is there a repeat stream? | The motion can become a series, fund, city playbook, or category loop. | Success cannot be reused without rebuilding from scratch. |

## Twelve-Month Cadence

| Stage | Goal | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Months 0-3 | Build AnalogNYC as the first city brand and dogfood loop. | NYC city lead rhythm, first owned IRL calendar, spend categories, social/content cadence, invite response baseline, first catalog/profiles, and initial agency brief hypotheses. |
| Months 4-6 | Prove NYC depth before expanding. | Governed NYC activations, categorized spend, proof reports, field media, connector profiles, agency engagement learning, and city scorecard. |
| Months 7-9 | Bring in AnalogSF if NYC proof is strong enough. | SF city thesis, city lead onboarding, first SF owned IRL, and a transfer test against the NYC playbook. |
| Months 10-12 | Decide what becomes the city brand operating model. | Renewed sponsor or agency budgets, standardized playbooks, product roadmap priorities, SF expansion or NYC deepening decision, and proof of affection. |

## Graduation Metrics

A causeway is working when it can answer yes to most of these:

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| Sponsor repeatability | Can the same brief, fund, or sponsor perspective run again without starting over? |
| Supply velocity | Can a city lead produce qualified host-side options quickly and ethically? |
| Proof quality | Does the sponsor believe the evidence enough to renew, unlock, or refer? |
| Media yield | Does the story attract hosts, connectors, sponsors, or attendees into the tent? |
| Affection | Do builders feel seen, funded, and more powerful because Analog exists? |
| Product learning | Did the motion improve a durable product object or workflow? |
| Dogfood | Did Analog use its own product, categorize its own spend, and learn from the friction directly? |

## Kill Or Double-Down Rule

Double down when a causeway creates sponsor demand, trusted supply, product proof, media, and affection in the same loop.

Pause or redesign when:

- the community has to be invented from nothing
- sponsor fit feels forced or extractive
- city leads cannot reach trusted operators
- the motion depends on too much bespoke production
- proof is too weak for renewal
- media feels generic
- hosts or connectors do not feel more powerful

The best outcome is not maximum surface area. It is finding the streams where Analog can move with the market and turn real-world trust into a product and brand people want to join.
