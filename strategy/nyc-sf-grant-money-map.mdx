As of June 3, 2026, the opportunity is clear: NYC and SF both have fragmented public, nonprofit, and public/private funding surfaces that can help Hosts, small businesses, artists, venues, cultural groups, and local operators run better community work.

Read this after [Public And Nonprofit Funding Infrastructure](/strategy/public-nonprofit-funding-infrastructure). The prior page explains the doctrine. This page applies it to NYC and SF.

Analog should treat this as supply capture and government conversion.

```text
Host completes one Analog application
-> Analog finds relevant free money
-> Host one-clicks apply intent from the geo-dashboard
-> Analog asks grant-specific follow-up questions in a guided flow
-> Analog agents and operators package the application
-> Host wins or learns
-> Analog proves application quality, spend stewardship, and outcomes
-> governments adopt Analog to reduce workflow pain, fraud/waste/abuse risk, and attribution gaps
```

This page is not a legal, procurement, or grants-administration opinion. Always re-check the live grant page before advising a Host.

## Grant Desk Operating Model

Analog should run the NYC/SF grant desk like a productized market-acquisition system, not like bespoke grant writing.

| Layer | What Analog does | Required output |
|---|---|---|
| Source map | Monitor city agencies, arts councils, BIDs, foundations, small-business portals, nonprofit newsletters, and public/private funds. | grant opportunity catalog with amount, deadline, geography, eligible entities, allowed uses, docs, and source URL. |
| Host intake | Use one Analog application to capture event history, dates, venue, photos, attendance, community need, budget needs, entity status, fiscal sponsor, and proof. | Host grant readiness file that can be reused across public grants, private grants, and Sponsor funds. |
| Eligibility match | Compare Host facts against geography, legal status, category, budget size, prior programming, deadlines, and restricted uses. | eligibility notes and no-go reasons. |
| Packet creation | Draft narratives, budgets, work samples, impact language, proof summaries, and reporting plans. | Analog Doc, files/docs packet, application checklist. |
| Authorized submission | Submit, co-submit, or prepare for Host submission only with permission. | submission log, date, owner, status, next step. |
| Post-award proof | If funded, track allowed uses, receipts, vendors, attendance, photos, QR response, Host notes, and report deadlines. | proof packet and report draft. |
| Agency learning | Aggregate where applicants struggle and where Analog reduces confusion. | government workflow pitch and fraud/waste/abuse control memo. |

The desk should be lightweight at first. The first win is not perfect automation. The first win is proving that Hosts will maintain better Analog records when those records help them find outside money.

## One Application To Rule Them All

The Host-facing free product should make the grant desk feel simple:

```text
Fill out one Analog application.
See the grants and funds that fit your geography.
Click apply.
Answer the extra questions only that grant needs.
Analog prepares the packet in the background.
Approve before anything is submitted.
```

The same application should route to public grants, private foundation grants, BID or neighborhood funds, community collective funds, Sponsor-backed public funds, selective brand funds, and the Host's own pitch packet.

The right analogy is a guided tax-filing product. Analog should not ask a Host to become a grant administrator. It should show a plain-language checklist, ask only for the missing grant-specific deltas, generate a budget or narrative draft where possible, and make the official submission path visible before final approval.

| Product layer | Host experience | Analog work in the background | Boundary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Universal Analog application | Host answers once and keeps the record current. | Normalize organization, community, geography, event history, budget, proof, entity status, fiscal sponsor, insurance, and files. | Do not ask the Host to rewrite the same story for every fund. |
| Geo-dashboard grant shelf | Host sees available grants and funds for `AnalogNYC`, `AnalogSF`, or another active market. | Match opportunities by location, Host type, category, amount, allowed use, deadline, and missing fields. | Show fit and gaps; do not imply guaranteed eligibility. |
| One-click apply intent | Host clicks to apply or request help for a specific opportunity. | Create application intent, missing-field prompts, owner, deadline, and status. | One-click apply is authorization to prepare or route, not an award promise. |
| Guided grant application UX | Host answers a short follow-up flow only when the chosen grant needs more than the universal application already contains. | Collect required deltas, attach files, map answers to the grant form, and preserve reusable answers for future matches. | Keep official requirements visible; do not bury eligibility risk behind a friendly interface. |
| Background Agent MCP preparation | Host does not start from a blank page. | Draft answers, build budgets, assemble files, reuse proof, track source links, and prepare submission materials. | Human/Host review is required before submission. Sensitive programs may need specialist review. |
| Application status log | Host can see where each opportunity stands. | Track draft, needs Host info, ready for approval, submitted, follow-up, rejected, awarded, report due. | Keep the status honest and source-backed. |

This is the MAC wedge. A Host who joins Analog to find free money becomes easier to fund with public grants, private grants, Sponsor funds, and future brand campaigns because the same record keeps getting stronger.

## MAC Effect

Grant discovery should be evaluated like market acquisition, not philanthropy.

| Metric | Why it matters | Early target |
|---|---|---|
| Qualified Hosts matched | Shows whether the market has fundable supply. | 50 to 200 Hosts across NYC/SF in the first serious desk cycle. |
| Eligible applications prepared | Shows whether Analog is improving application readiness, not only collecting profiles. | 20 to 75 prepared packets. |
| Outside capital applied for | Shows the size of fundable demand Analog can route toward. | Track by program, market, Host type, and allowed use. |
| Outside capital won | Shows whether Analog improves real funding outcomes. | Small early base is fine; quality matters more than volume. |
| Company hours per packet | Keeps the desk from becoming custom consulting. | Should fall each cycle as templates and product fields improve. |
| Qualified supply created | Counts Hosts, Venues, connectors, and operators that become Sponsor-ready because of the process. | Every application should improve at least one catalog or Host record. |
| Agency conversion signals | Shows whether governments or intermediaries may become workflow customers. | Repeated missing-doc, eligibility, reporting, or fraud/waste/abuse pain from the same funder category. |

The simplest MAC question:

```text
Did grant-desk work create more fundable supply
than the same company spend would have created through cold sourcing?
```

## Government Workflow Pitch

Application volume becomes useful when Analog can show that it reduces public-sector pain without taking over public decisions.

| Government pain | Analog evidence to collect | Buyer story |
|---|---|---|
| Incomplete or ineligible applications | missing-doc rates, eligibility no-go notes, applicant confusion themes. | Analog prechecks applicants and reduces low-quality review load. |
| Small applicants need too much support | office-hour questions, repeated packet gaps, support time per Host. | Analog gives small Hosts a reusable readiness file. |
| Reporting is weak after awards | receipt gaps, photo gaps, proof delays, unclear allowed uses. | Analog gives funders cleaner post-award proof and report drafts. |
| Fraud/waste/abuse risk is hard to see | duplicate vendors, weak receipts, odd timing, restricted-use risk, conflict signals. | Analog offers exception queues and audit trails without replacing agency judgment. |
| Outcomes are hard to explain | attendance, public access, local commerce, public-space use, cultural participation, sentiment, repeat activity. | Analog turns awards into public-safe attribution by district, corridor, category, and fund. |

The government pitch should stay narrow:

```text
Keep grant authority with the funder.
Let Analog improve applicant readiness, downstream spend proof,
reporting quality, and market-level attribution.
```

## Operating Rules

- Do not promise awards.
- Do not submit without Host authorization.
- Do not replace government eligibility, review, scoring, legal, or award decisions.
- Do not create fake precision around outcomes.
- Do not call private foundation money government money.
- Do not let grant help become custom consulting without reusable Host files, grant maps, proof rails, and agency-learning notes.
