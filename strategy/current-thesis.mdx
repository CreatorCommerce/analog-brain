Analog is the financial infrastructure for the community-led IRL economy: the funding layer Sponsors use to turn real-world participation into category growth, shared incentives, and brand value.

As AI makes digital creation cheaper and more abundant, trusted real-world presence becomes more valuable. Sponsors need to deploy community activation investments anywhere, anytime without rebuilding bespoke event services. Hosts need funding to create better gatherings, lower-friction access, stronger local rituals, and a path to earn again. Analog wins if the exchange becomes safe, measurable, and repeatable through product.

Keep three lines in view:

```text
Invest in the positive upside to AI: more IRL.
Communities make markets. Brands monetize those markets.
IRL is vibrant. But the economics are fragile.
```

Analog dogfoods this by using Analog to build Analog. City brands such as AnalogNYC, AnalogSF, and AnalogLA should be funded, operated, measured, and learned from inside the product.

## What The Next 12 Months Must Prove

Do not chase every IRL use case. Spend six months on AnalogNYC, prove the dogfood loop, then bring in SF only if the NYC playbook travels.

The constraint: fewer than 9 full-time people, 3 product/build, 4 GTM, 1 CS, and city work through leads, contractors, and repeatable playbooks.

## What The MVP Is Testing

The MVP should prove seven things:

1. Sponsors will fund governed IRL when budget, Host fit, spend, and proof are clear.
2. Hosts will use sponsor budget when it is simple, event-scoped, and not reimbursement chaos.
3. Spend cards and receipts make small activations easier to trust and audit.
4. Proof can drive renewal decisions before perfect attribution exists.
5. City leads can create sponsor-ready supply faster than generic prospecting.
6. Hosted pages, claims, imports, and social proof can feed privacy-safe audience learning.
7. AnalogNYC can judge itself with customer-grade KPIs: social growth, category growth, community perspective, direct response, and categorized spend.
8. Community proof can ladder into high-ticket sponsorship underwriting, where Analog governs risk, allocation, proof gates, contracts, invoices, sentiment, and renewal logic even when not every dollar moves through a spend card.
9. Public and nonprofit Sponsors can fund downstream communities faster while preserving eligibility, spend stewardship, fraud/waste/abuse controls, privacy-safe participation proof, and defensible reporting.

## What Analog Is Not

Analog is not an event directory, invite tool, ticketing platform, creator monetization tool, social network, or reimbursement app.

It is the trusted operating layer around:

- sponsor budget
- host qualification
- campaign structure
- brief and approval logic
- spend governance
- proof
- renewal and unlock logic

## Operating Rule

Market growth comes first. Direct attribution is valuable, but the stronger early signal is whether the activation grows category participation, content, recurring rituals, sponsor-ready supply, and host reliability.

## First Funding Logic

Start where community already creates demand and cash removes a real blocker. Running and walking communities are useful because they combine recurring rituals, obvious uses of funds, dense supply, Sponsor demand, and familiar social proof. They are not the whole strategy.

The broader first logic is cash-based Host enablement:

```text
Micro grant or macro grant
-> Host accomplishes a real community goal
-> proof shows spend, participation, sentiment, and recurrence
-> best Hosts unlock more budget, services, tools, media, or public funding
```

Analog should learn playbooks for coffee and libations, supplies, rentals, affordable space, strategic rooms, print, insurance, local design, public access, and operator retainers. Product gifting can support these motions, but it should not define the market.
