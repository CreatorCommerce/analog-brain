Use `Sponsor` for the product actor. In the market, a Sponsor is anyone deploying capital into a community with structure, oversight, and proof behind it.

Read this after [Cash Funding Playbooks](/strategy/cash-funding-playbooks). The prior page explains what funding does. This page explains which buyers should fund it and how to turn names into briefs.

Use [MAC Versus CAC Contract](/strategy/supply-side-mac-community-champion) for the supply-acquisition model. This page starts after that decision: once Analog knows a market or circuit is worth acquiring, which Sponsors should fund it, and what first brief should they run?

Analog gives Sponsors a way to deploy community activation investments anywhere, anytime while keeping the exchange safe, measurable, and repeatable. The best Sponsors are not buying exposure; they are earning a trusted role in the categories and local scenes they care about.

The strongest Sponsor motions are cash-based. Product gifting can support a campaign, but Analog should prioritize Sponsors willing to fund the Host's real blocker: hospitality, supplies, rentals, space, local design, insurance, print, vendor support, operator time, or proof capture.

## Four Commercial Lanes

Use the lanes to separate buyer language, proof expectations, and risk controls. Do not let them fragment the company. The strategic asset is trusted regional supply.

| Sponsor lane | What they sell/fund | What Analog must prove |
|---|---|---|
| eComm Brands | Physical things to many people. | IRL participation, product trial, content, community sentiment, category growth, sales-adjacent proof. |
| SaaS/Tech Companies | Digital things to fewer, higher-value users, accounts, or ecosystems. | Trusted access to founders, operators, developers, customers, partners, or strategic accounts. |
| Civic/Public/Non-profit Funds | Public-good experiences and outcomes. | Access, participation, civic value, wellness, climate, arts, youth, entrepreneurship, public reporting. |
| Political Action Sponsors | Campaign, advocacy, ballot, donor, or field-action outcomes. | Grassroots participation, national/state/district/county/city/ward/event spend stewardship, compliant action proof, local learning, field allocation decisions. |

Everything else should route through these lanes. A hospitality group usually behaves like an eComm or local-experience Sponsor. A foundation, family office, civic institution, or individual giver usually behaves like a Civic/Public/Non-profit Fund unless electioneering, advocacy, or campaign-field work makes Political Action the cleaner lane. A partner platform may route Sponsor demand, but it is not an additional Sponsor lane unless it deploys capital itself.

Community collective funds are also not a fifth lane. A neighborhood leader can create a Sponsor account and pool capital from local businesses, funders, landlords, BIDs, or aligned operators. The collective behaves like a local market-access or civic/public-good Sponsor depending on its goal. Use [Community Collective Funds](/use-cases/community-collective-funds) when the fund is backed by many smaller contributors rather than one large corporate Sponsor.

## Supply-First Rule

Every Sponsor call should ask: which city supply does this teach us to own?

SaaS/tech, eComm, civic/nonprofit, and political action often point to the same local leaders from different angles. The same trusted Host might create a founder dinner for a SaaS company, a wellness ritual for an eComm brand, a social-connection program for a nonprofit, or a compliant grassroots gathering for a campaign. Analog should build the city graph and regional intelligence layer first, then wrap each lane with the right proof, pricing, and risk language.

Strategic customers should be chosen for their ability to dogfood the loop and pull more supply into the network. Crocs, Midol, Anthropic, Shopify, and similar Sponsors are useful when their campaigns become visible funds, raffles, strategic rooms, or city media programs. SaaS demand is especially strong when the brief resembles Cafe Cursor: a trusted founder/operator room with access that generic demand generation cannot buy.

Public grant discovery also improves Sponsor supply. A Host who has event history, budget language, proof, and eligibility notes is easier for a brand, SaaS company, nonprofit, or public/private Sponsor to evaluate. The grant desk should therefore create better Sponsor candidates, not only public-money applicants.

## Selective Brand Fund Targets

Before Analog has a fully liquid brand marketplace, target a small number of Sponsors that can underwrite public, market-fit funds.

This is a selective partnerships motion, not a volume sales motion. Analog can use the current contractor or hire profile with ex-Crocs partnerships leadership experience to close a few enterprise Sponsors that are likely to work: Crocs, Midol, Baden, Lululemon-style wellbeing Sponsors, Nike-style play and sport Sponsors, Anthropic, Shopify, Tapcart-style SaaS Sponsors, and similar companies with credible category reasons to fund communities.

The offer is not "buy ads around events." The offer is:

```text
Fund a market-fit community program through Analog.
Analog will package the market, recruit the right Hosts, govern spend,
capture proof, and show you who deserves the next dollar.
```

| Sponsor target question | Standard |
|---|---|
| Is there fund-market fit? | The fund should feel native to a city, circuit, or category. |
| Can the fund be public? | The CTA should be simple enough to publish through media, partners, and regional Host dashboards. |
| Does it create supply? | Hosts, venues, connectors, or operators should raise their hands because the fund solves a real need. |
| Can Analog govern it? | Eligibility, allowed spend, Host approval, Venue rewards if useful, proof, and renewal rules must be clear. |
| Does it teach the marketplace? | The fund should improve pricing, proof, supply discovery, rate intelligence, and Sponsor renewal logic. |

Example fund theses:

| Sponsor | Fund thesis | Strong first market |
|---|---|---|
| Crocs | Fund recovery and comfort for run, walk, wellness, and movement communities. | NYC run and wellness circuits. |
| Lululemon-style wellbeing Sponsor | Fund community-led movement and wellbeing programs through a registration-first grant, then route eligible Hosts into specific IRL activations, access programs, and proof standards. | NYC, SF, or a priority city with dense movement, wellness, and public-health-adjacent supply. |
| Nike-style play and sport Sponsor | Fund youth play, school and community sport access, rec leagues, community coaches, equipment, safe play spaces, and nonprofit partners through employee and community insight and grant-administration discipline. | Employee-dense priority cities with youth sport, parks, schools, nonprofits, rec leagues, and trusted local operators. |
| Midol | Fund respectful wellness, education, and care-adjacent gatherings where the Sponsor role is additive. | NYC wellness and movement communities. |
| Baden | Fund rec leagues, game nights, tournaments, and community play where equipment or space is a real blocker. | Cities or campuses with dense casual sport and game circuits. |
| Tapcart-style SaaS Sponsor | Fund superconnector dinners for eCommerce operators, agencies, founders, and partners. | SF or NYC operator rooms, depending on supply. |
| Anthropic or Shopify | Fund builder, founder, developer, or ecosystem rooms with high-trust Hosts. | SF first when the local room is strong. |

## NYC Pre-Seed Sponsor Cohort

For the immediate pre-seed motion, use [NYC Pre-Seed Sponsor Cohort](/strategy/nyc-preseed-sponsor-cohort) as the operating page.

This is not normal Sponsor sales and not self-serve marketplace pricing. The first ask is an LOI for intended NYC deployment budget plus a refundable USD 500 deposit.

| Cohort filter | Standard |
|---|---|
| Market | NYC only. |
| Target count | Focus on five serious Sponsors for Q3, while accepting more if they are aligned and can commit real budget. |
| Sponsor mix | Healthy balance of brands and SaaS/tech. |
| Budget seriousness | USD 50k intended NYC partnerships budget is enough; USD 100k+ is best. |
| Commercial posture | Analog takes no platform fee, time rake, or percentage take for the initial cohort unless terms change explicitly. |
| Access posture | Sponsors can browse public-safe directory signals, but high-context matchmaking starts after LOI/deposit commitment. |
| Strategic goal | Prove how to price, package, and deploy regional community supply in NYC before expanding the model. |

The pitch is:

```text
You are not paying Analog for software today.
You are committing serious NYC partnership budget so Analog can help deploy it
into the communities where your category can become culturally present.
```

Do not curate community lists for uncommitted Sponsors. The directory can create confidence, but Analog should protect the social capital it is earning with communities.

## Universal Sponsor Jobs

| Job | What the Sponsor needs |
|---|---|
| Brand safety | approved opportunities, Hosts, categories, and no surprises |
| Spend controls | trackable budgets, spend cards, receipts, restrictions, anomaly review, unused-fund visibility |
| Sentiment and proof | evidence the community welcomed the funding and the recap traveled naturally |
| Mention coverage | authorized Host and Venue mentions that make IRL performance benchmarkable |
| Category growth | repeat participation, new entrants, category mentions, claims, signups, habit formation |
| Operational ease | deployment that is easy to monitor and report without becoming a new job |

## Perspective Plays

| Perspective | Best fit when | Good Analog plays | Renewal proof |
|---|---|---|---|
| eComm Brands | Product benefits from trial, ritual, demos, content, customer density, or community outside paid social, especially when copycat culture, AI ad parity, influencer costs, or creator ops overload weaken the digital edge. | creator-led classes, run/walk clubs, Pilates, recovery mornings, farmers markets, dinners, workshops, pet-parent rituals, venue activations | attendance, trial, claims, content, sentiment, repeat participation, sales-adjacent signal |
| SaaS/Tech Companies | A trusted room is more valuable than broad impressions, and SaaS economics can justify repeat dinner/coffee CAC when the audience is qualified. | founder breakfasts, connector dinners, developer meetups, customer councils, partner salons, operator rooms, ecosystem grants, coffee office hours | qualified attendance, account fit, relationship density, content assets, follow-up meetings, adoption moments, partner/customer learning |
| Civic/Public/Non-profit Funds | Goal is access, civic participation, wellness, local commerce, youth, climate, arts, public health, or community formation. | micro grants, macro grants, starter kits, city lead programs, public-space activations, outcome-based local series | participation, access, local legitimacy, spend stewardship, public reporting |
| Political Action Sponsors | Field money needs to reach trusted local operators with compliance-safe controls. | grassroots house events, district/county/city/ward organizing nights, campus or issue gatherings, supporter trainings, volunteer hospitality, ballot-action community rooms | compliant spend, attendance or action proof where permitted, volunteer energy, local sentiment, national/state/district/county/city/ward allocation learning |

Government agencies, nonprofits, NGOs, and foundations belong in the Civic/Public/Non-profit Fund lane when they need trusted downstream communities to accomplish mission work. Use [Public And Nonprofit Funding Infrastructure](/strategy/public-nonprofit-funding-infrastructure) for the full model.

Campaigns, PACs, ballot initiatives, party committees, advocacy organizations, and donor coalitions belong in the Political Action lane when the work involves electoral, advocacy, field, or persuasion-sensitive activity. Use [Campaign Grassroots Activation Funds](/use-cases/campaign-grassroots-activation-funds) for the use case and keep counsel-reviewed controls in the loop.

CreatorCommerce-sourced brands are not a separate Sponsor perspective. Treat CC as a demand bridge for creator/affiliate-heavy eComm Brands or partner programs that already believe in creator trust and need an IRL test. The first Analog move should be a small creator/community activation, customer night, VIP dinner, or local market-entry pilot with clear spend and proof.

## Pipeline Rule

A Sponsor name is useful only when it becomes a testable hypothesis.

```text
[Entity] is a [Sponsor perspective] because [market trigger].
The first brief is [activation hypothesis].
The supply ask is [Host-side need].
The proof that matters is [renewal evidence].
The pricing hypothesis is [pricing model].
The next action is [specific pipeline move].
```

Do not optimize for the largest logo list. Optimize for Sponsors that teach whether a motion repeats.

## Pipeline Stages

| Stage | Exit criteria |
|---|---|
| Raw entity | Sponsor perspective and trigger are clear. |
| Persona-qualified | First brief hypothesis is written. |
| Brief-ready | Goal, audience, city, budget logic, and proof need are known. |
| Supply-matched | Credible Host, connector, space, community, or event set options exist. |
| Pilot-ready | Campaign, event set, collab, Host event, spend, and proof plan are set. |
| Proof-ready | Proof packet can support renewal, unlock, or no-go. |
| Repeatable | Motion can run again with less custom work. |

## Partner Platforms

Partner platforms are not a fifth Sponsor lane. They can route Sponsor-funded demand into qualified supply or deploy one of the four lanes through their own surface.

Best fit: they own clubs, communities, venues, ticketing, membership, creator, talent, operator workflows, or brand demand. Their job is to keep their own customer surface while Analog powers sponsorship, brief, proof, and funding workflow.

CreatorCommerce, community platforms, ticketing tools, talent networks, venue groups, and operator networks should be evaluated by the same rule: do they bring qualified Sponsor demand, trusted Host supply, or a workflow where Analog can govern budget and proof without owning the whole surface?
