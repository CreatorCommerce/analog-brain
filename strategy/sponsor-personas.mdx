Use `Sponsor` for the product actor. In the market, a sponsor is anyone deploying capital into a community with structure, oversight, and proof behind it.

Analog gives Sponsors a way to deploy community activation investments anywhere, anytime while keeping the exchange safe, measurable, and repeatable. The best Sponsors are not just buying exposure; they are earning a trusted role in the categories and local scenes they care about.

Build and qualify sponsor pipeline through three perspectives:

| Sponsor perspective | What they sell | What they need Analog to prove |
|---|---|---|
| eComm Brands | Physical things to many people | IRL participation, product trial, content, community sentiment, category growth, and sales-adjacent proof. |
| Tech Companies | Digital things to fewer, higher-value users or strategic accounts | Credible access to dense professional, creator, builder, executive, or operator communities that can shape adoption and enterprise relationships. |
| Public/Non-profit Funds | Public-good experiences and outcomes | Access, participation, community formation, civic value, wellness, climate, arts, youth, entrepreneurship, or other measurable public outcomes. |

Everything else should route through these three perspectives. A hospitality group usually behaves like an eComm or local-experience Sponsor. A foundation, family office, civic institution, or individual giver usually behaves like a Public/Non-profit Fund. A partner platform may route sponsor demand, but it is not a fourth sponsor perspective unless it deploys capital itself.

For early customers, this page should answer two questions:

1. What kind of Sponsor am I?
2. What proof would make this worth renewing?

For commercial packaging, use [Pricing Models And Experiments](/strategy/pricing-models). It explains which sponsor-paid model to test without blurring activation budgets, Analog revenue, host upside, support, fulfillment, or content rights.

## Universal Sponsor Jobs

Across categories, sponsors hire Analog for five jobs:

| Job | What the sponsor needs |
|---|---|
| Brand safety | Approved opportunities, approved hosts, approved categories, and no surprises in who they partner with. |
| Spend controls and receipts | Trackable line items, spend cards, vendor restrictions, receipts, anomaly detection, and unused-fund visibility. |
| Sentiment and social proof | Evidence that the community welcomed it and the recap circulated. |
| Category growth evidence | Repeat participation, new entrants, category mentions, downstream signups, and habit formation. |
| Operational support | Deployment that is easy to monitor and report from without becoming a new job for the sponsor. |

## Perspective Plays

| Sponsor perspective | Best fit when | Good Analog plays | Renewal proof |
|---|---|---|---|
| eComm Brands | The product benefits from trial, ritual, demos, content, community, or customer density outside crowded paid social. | Creator-led classes, run clubs, Pilates classes, recovery mornings, dinners, workshops, pop-ups, product sampling, local affinity dinners, dog treats and toys funds, retail or venue activations. | Attendance, product trial, claims, content, sentiment, repeat participation, category mentions, sales-adjacent signal. |
| Tech Companies | A trusted room is more valuable than broad impressions. | Founder breakfasts, connector-led dinners, developer meetups, customer councils, partner salons, operator rooms, category-building events. | Qualified attendance, account fit, follow-up meetings, adoption moments, partner/customer learning, ecosystem credibility. |
| Public/Non-profit Funds | The goal is access, civic participation, wellness, local commerce, youth, climate, arts, entrepreneurship, public health, public space, or community formation. | Microgrants, free or discounted spots, community starter kits, city lead programs, venue access funds, outcome-based local series. | Participation, access created, local legitimacy, outcome evidence, spend stewardship, public reporting. |

Government agencies belong in the Public/Non-profit Fund lane when they deploy capital to create access, participation, local vitality, public health, civic value, or other measurable community outcomes. Treat procurement, eligibility, reporting, and political sensitivity as design constraints.

eComm Brands that came through CreatorCommerce or a creator/affiliate partner should be evaluated with [CreatorCommerce Demand Bridge](/strategy/creatorcommerce-demand-bridge). Their first Analog motion is usually creator-led IRL, creator attendance, VIP/customer events, or local market-entry activations.

## Pipeline Rule

A sponsor name is useful only when it becomes a testable hypothesis.

```text
[Entity] is a [sponsor perspective] because [market trigger].
The first brief is [activation hypothesis].
The supply ask is [host-side need].
The proof that matters is [renewal evidence].
The pricing hypothesis is [pricing model].
The next action is [specific pipeline move].
```

Do not optimize for the largest logo list. Optimize for Sponsors that teach whether a motion repeats.

## Pipeline Object

Each pipeline record should carry these fields:

| Field | What it means |
|---|---|
| Entity | The company, fund, foundation, platform, or partner. Add names only when supplied by the founder or confirmed source context. |
| Sponsor perspective | eComm Brand, Tech Company, Public/Non-profit Fund, or Partner Platform routing one of those perspectives. |
| Market trigger | CAC fatigue, strategic access, public outcome, launch market, creator/community program, local legitimacy, or category growth. |
| First brief | The smallest sponsor ask that can become a funded pilot or fund-like test. |
| Supply ask | The Host, connector, space, Event set, community, city lead, or partner surface needed to make the brief real. |
| Proof need | Attendance, claims, content, sentiment, account fit, participation, outcomes, spend stewardship, or renewal logic. |
| Pricing hypothesis | Campaign fee, fund admin, sourcing, prospecting, managed support, content rights, or repeat retainer. |
| Next action | Discovery, brief draft, supply search, Host package, pilot launch, proof packet, renewal ask, or no-go. |

## Pipeline Stages

| Stage | Exit criteria |
|---|---|
| Raw entity | Sponsor perspective and trigger are clear. |
| Persona-qualified | First brief hypothesis is written. |
| Brief-ready | Goal, audience, city, budget logic, and proof need are known. |
| Supply-matched | Credible Host, connector, space, community, or Event set options exist. |
| Pilot-ready | Campaign, Event set, collab, Host event, spend, and proof plan are set. |
| Proof-ready | Proof packet can support renewal, unlock, or no-go. |
| Repeatable | The motion can run again with less custom work. |

## Partner Platforms

Partner platforms are not a fourth sponsor perspective. They can route sponsor-funded demand into qualified supply or deploy one of the three sponsor perspectives through their own surface.

Best fit when they own:

- clubs, communities, venues, ticketing, membership, creator, talent, or operator workflows
- brands or sponsors that want to fund those communities
- enough trust to keep their own product surface and customer relationship

Primary job:

**Embed Analog's sponsorship, brief, proof, and funding workflow into an existing partner workflow.**

Good Analog plays:

- partner API sponsorship discovery
- hosted or embedded application forms
- partner-owned sponsorship surface
- sponsor-safe proof and renewal reporting
- rev share on funded opportunities

## Common Sponsor Questions

| Question | Analog answer |
|---|---|
| Where should we spend? | Use host readiness, city supply, sponsor perspective, category fit, and proof history. |
| Will the money be used responsibly? | Use event-scoped spend cards, budget limits, transactions, receipts, and spend integrity review. |
| What did we get? | Report proof, market growth, attendance or claim data, content, optional attribution, and renewal logic. |
| Who should receive the next allocation? | Score hosts on market-growth proof, spend stewardship, repeatability, and sponsor trust. |
| Do we need to own the whole UI? | No. Partner APIs can let community, creator, venue, or ticketing platforms keep their own product surface while Analog powers sponsorship intelligence and proof workflows. |
