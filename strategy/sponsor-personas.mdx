Use `Sponsor` for the product actor. In the market, a Sponsor is anyone deploying capital into a community with structure, oversight, and proof behind it.

Analog gives Sponsors a way to deploy community activation investments anywhere, anytime while keeping the exchange safe, measurable, and repeatable. The best Sponsors are not buying exposure; they are earning a trusted role in the categories and local scenes they care about.

The strongest Sponsor motions are cash-based. Product gifting can support a campaign, but Analog should prioritize Sponsors willing to fund the Host's real blocker: hospitality, supplies, rentals, space, local design, insurance, print, vendor support, operator time, or proof capture.

## Three Sponsor Perspectives

| Sponsor perspective | What they sell/fund | What Analog must prove |
|---|---|---|
| eComm Brands | Physical things to many people. | IRL participation, product trial, content, community sentiment, category growth, sales-adjacent proof. |
| Tech Companies | Digital things to fewer, higher-value users, accounts, or ecosystems. | Trusted access to founders, operators, developers, customers, partners, or strategic accounts. |
| Public/Non-profit Funds | Public-good experiences and outcomes. | Access, participation, civic value, wellness, climate, arts, youth, entrepreneurship, public reporting. |

Everything else should route through these three perspectives. A hospitality group usually behaves like an eComm or local-experience Sponsor. A foundation, family office, civic institution, or individual giver usually behaves like a Public/Non-profit Fund. A partner platform may route Sponsor demand, but it is not a fourth Sponsor perspective unless it deploys capital itself.

## Universal Sponsor Jobs

| Job | What the Sponsor needs |
|---|---|
| Brand safety | approved opportunities, Hosts, categories, and no surprises |
| Spend controls | trackable budgets, spend cards, receipts, restrictions, anomaly review, unused-fund visibility |
| Sentiment and proof | evidence the community welcomed the funding and the recap traveled naturally |
| Category growth | repeat participation, new entrants, category mentions, claims, signups, habit formation |
| Operational ease | deployment that is easy to monitor and report without becoming a new job |

## Perspective Plays

| Perspective | Best fit when | Good Analog plays | Renewal proof |
|---|---|---|---|
| eComm Brands | Product benefits from trial, ritual, demos, content, customer density, or community outside paid social. | creator-led classes, run/walk clubs, Pilates, recovery mornings, dinners, workshops, pet-parent rituals, venue activations | attendance, trial, claims, content, sentiment, repeat participation, sales-adjacent signal |
| Tech Companies | A trusted room is more valuable than broad impressions. | founder breakfasts, connector dinners, developer meetups, customer councils, partner salons, operator rooms, ecosystem grants | qualified attendance, account fit, follow-up meetings, adoption moments, partner/customer learning |
| Public/Non-profit Funds | Goal is access, civic participation, wellness, local commerce, youth, climate, arts, public health, or community formation. | micro grants, macro grants, starter kits, city lead programs, public-space activations, outcome-based local series | participation, access, local legitimacy, spend stewardship, public reporting |

Government agencies, nonprofits, NGOs, and foundations belong in the Public/Non-profit Fund lane when they need trusted downstream communities to accomplish mission work. Use [Public And Nonprofit Funding Infrastructure](/strategy/public-nonprofit-funding-infrastructure) for the full model.

CreatorCommerce-sourced brands are not a separate Sponsor perspective. Treat CC as a demand bridge for creator/affiliate-heavy eComm Brands or partner programs that already believe in creator trust and need an IRL test. The first Analog move should be a small creator/community activation, customer night, VIP dinner, or local market-entry pilot with clear spend and proof.

## Pipeline Rule

A Sponsor name is useful only when it becomes a testable hypothesis.

```text
[Entity] is a [Sponsor perspective] because [market trigger].
The first brief is [activation hypothesis].
The supply ask is [Host-side need].
The proof that matters is [renewal evidence].
The pricing hypothesis is [pricing model].
The next action is [specific pipeline move].
```

Do not optimize for the largest logo list. Optimize for Sponsors that teach whether a motion repeats.

## Pipeline Record

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Entity | Company, fund, foundation, platform, or partner. |
| Sponsor perspective | eComm Brand, Tech Company, Public/Non-profit Fund, or Partner Platform routing one of those. |
| Market trigger | CAC fatigue, strategic access, public outcome, launch market, creator/community program, local legitimacy, category growth. |
| First brief | Smallest Sponsor ask that can become a funded pilot or fund-like test. |
| Supply ask | Host, connector, space, event set, community, city lead, or partner surface needed. |
| Proof need | attendance, claims, content, sentiment, account fit, participation, outcomes, spend stewardship, renewal logic |
| Pricing hypothesis | campaign fee, fund admin, sourcing, prospecting, managed support, content rights, repeat retainer |
| Next action | discovery, brief draft, supply search, Host package, pilot launch, proof packet, renewal ask, no-go |

## Use-Case Routing

| Use case | Best perspective | What it teaches |
|---|---|---|
| Cash micro grants | all three | Whether small recurring budgets create stronger rituals and supply trust. |
| Macro grant cohort | Tech, Public/Non-profit, and strong-fit eComm | Whether the best organizers deserve larger recurring budgets. |
| Arts and culture funds | Public/Non-profit first; local retail, hospitality, creative tools, and Tech when tasteful | Whether artists, venues, collectives, and cultural Hosts can be funded with governance and proof without turning art into ad inventory. |
| Movement/wellness funds | eComm first; Public/Non-profit when access or health matters | Whether product-adjacent rituals create repeatable proof loops. |
| Pet-parent ritual fund | eComm | Whether trusted pet-parent Hosts create safe trial, owner permission, content, sentiment, and proof inside [Authentic Brand Community Funds](/use-cases/authentic-brand-community-funds). |
| SaaS/founder dinners | Tech | Whether trusted rooms beat generic demand generation for adoption and relationship value. |
| Dinner funds | physical brands, Tech, Public/Non-profit | Whether governed hospitality creates repeatable local market access. |
| Public/municipal community fund | Public/Non-profit | Whether grant-like capital can create durable Host supply and defensible controls. |
| Nonprofit downstream funding | Public/Non-profit | Whether mission organizations can fund trusted local Hosts while preserving accountability. |

Use [MVP Use Cases](/strategy/mvp-use-cases) for the full use-case router.

## Pipeline Stages

| Stage | Exit criteria |
|---|---|
| Raw entity | Sponsor perspective and trigger are clear. |
| Persona-qualified | First brief hypothesis is written. |
| Brief-ready | Goal, audience, city, budget logic, and proof need are known. |
| Supply-matched | Credible Host, connector, space, community, or event set options exist. |
| Pilot-ready | Campaign, event set, collab, Host event, spend, and proof plan are set. |
| Proof-ready | Proof packet can support renewal, unlock, or no-go. |
| Repeatable | Motion can run again with less custom work. |

## Partner Platforms

Partner platforms are not a fourth Sponsor perspective. They can route Sponsor-funded demand into qualified supply or deploy one of the three perspectives through their own surface.

Best fit: they own clubs, communities, venues, ticketing, membership, creator, talent, operator workflows, or brand demand. Their job is to keep their own customer surface while Analog powers sponsorship, brief, proof, and funding workflow.

CreatorCommerce, community platforms, ticketing tools, talent networks, venue groups, and operator networks should be evaluated by the same rule: do they bring qualified Sponsor demand, trusted Host supply, or a workflow where Analog can govern budget and proof without owning the whole surface?
