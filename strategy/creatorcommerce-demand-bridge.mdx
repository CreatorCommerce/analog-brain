CreatorCommerce is a demand-side bridge into Analog. It should not reposition Analog as creator-commerce-for-events, but it can qualify brands that already care about creators, affiliates, ambassadors, social commerce, offers, and attribution.

## One-Sentence Strategy

Use CreatorCommerce's brand credibility and partner distribution to identify creator/affiliate programs with IRL potential, then convert the best accounts into Analog creator-led or community-led activation pilots.

## Why This Matters

Partner-deployed CreatorCommerce can reduce cold brand acquisition for Analog because the brand has already shown:

- creator, affiliate, ambassador, or influencer investment
- performance pressure
- offer mechanics
- creator/affiliate roster data
- customer or geographic density signals
- willingness to use external operating help
- interest in attribution and reporting

Analog should turn that into a practical next step:

```text
CreatorCommerce performance plan -> IRL-ready creator/community signal -> Analog activation brief -> funded event or location pilot -> proof -> renewal
```

## Analog Handoff Fields

When a CreatorCommerce-sourced brand becomes an Analog candidate, capture:

| Field | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Brand category and priority products | Decides which IRL formats are native. |
| Creator/affiliate roster | Identifies known trusted supply before cold marketplace search. |
| Relationship tier | Separates real product love from weak paid media relationships. |
| Sales or persuasion evidence | Helps underwrite creator-led IRL without guessing. |
| City or audience density | Points toward local activation feasibility. |
| Offer mechanics | Turns event attendance into a useful sponsor benefit or post-event action. |
| Conversion data readiness | Determines whether optional low-touch attribution is realistic. |
| IRL willingness | Filters creators who want to host, co-host, attend, or introduce communities. |

## First Analog Motions

| Motion | Use when | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Creator-led IRL | Brand already trusts creators with audience and product love | Creator/community activation brief, hosted page, budget, proof, compensation state |
| Creator attendance layer | Brand sponsors an existing event and wants creators/affiliates/VIPs in the room | Ticket allotment, invite brief, content lane, check-in/proof |
| Creator x community collaboration | Creator has reach but needs a real host, venue, or local operator | Multi-party activation package |
| VIP/customer night | Brand has customer density or ambassador lists | Hosted event page, claim/RSVP capture, post-event match plan |
| Local market-entry activation | Brand wants city or category presence | Event set, city lead list, host options, proof plan |

## Guardrails

- Free or partner-subsidized CreatorCommerce must remain structured and qualified, not unlimited consulting.
- Data sharing into Analog must be permissioned and explicit.
- Do not pitch IRL unless the brand has a credible outcome and event fit.
- Do not force every activation to be creator-hosted; use the creator graph as a safe first trust surface, then expand to communities, venues, and operators.
- Convert heavy custom work into managed service, paid pilot, or decline it.

## Metrics

Track:

- partner-sourced CreatorCommerce deployments
- qualified brands activated
- creator/affiliate rosters imported or reviewed
- Analog-readiness score
- Analog pilots sourced from CreatorCommerce
- sponsorship capital unlocked
- repeat spend after CreatorCommerce-sourced pilots

The clean sub-metric is:

```text
CC-sourced Analog pipeline
```
