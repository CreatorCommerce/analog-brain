## Core Use Cases

| Use case | What it tests | Best sponsor categories | Best host side |
|---|---|---|---|
| Running or movement community fund | Whether a dense, recurring vertical can become a repeatable proof loop before Analog generalizes across categories | Wellness, beverage, apparel, footwear, recovery, beauty, fintech, insurance | Run clubs, walk clubs, pickleball groups, recovery communities, local operators |
| Creator-led IRL activation | Whether known creators, affiliates, ambassadors, or superfans can become a safe bridge from digital trust to funded IRL | Ecommerce brands, apps, wellness, hospitality, media, tech companies, CC-sourced brands | Creators, talent managers, creator-led communities |
| Community or club sponsorship | Whether recurring communities can use sponsor budget responsibly and generate market-growth proof | Ecommerce brands, wellness, fintech, beverage, civic sponsors | Run clubs, rec leagues, dinners, workshops, founder groups |
| Venue or location activation | Whether sponsorship can lower consumer cost or create useful benefits inside physical places | Hospitality, travel, fintech, local services, retail, public funds | Space owners, venues, cafes, gyms, studios, entertainment locations |
| Public or non-profit funded access | Whether grant-like or civic money can create durable host supply and social value | Public funds, non-profit funds, foundations, civic sponsors, health initiatives | Community organizers, local operators, city leads |
| Request for Events | Whether sponsors can declare demand and pull matching host supply into the product | Any sponsor with audience, category, and geography goals | Existing events, recurring rituals, claimed catalog records |
| Request for Communities | Whether sponsors can fund communities to originate the right activation when no obvious event exists yet | Tech companies, civic sponsors, wellness, B2B, creator ecosystems | Dot connectors, community leads, creator groups, city leads |
| Host-side sponsor prospecting | Whether hosts can package existing assets into sponsor-ready asks before sponsors are active in the marketplace | All sponsor categories | Hosts with audience, venue, content, or local operator assets |
| Event prospecting catalog | Whether off-platform IRL supply can become sponsor-searchable, claimable, and enrichable | Sponsors with a city/category target | Events, communities, venues, spaces, operators |
| Audience graph proof | Whether hosted pages, imports, check-ins, and claims improve sponsor confidence | Sponsors with CRM, commerce, POS, booking, or app outcome data | Hosts willing to capture or import attendance data |
| CreatorCommerce demand bridge | Whether partner-deployed CC can qualify brands for Analog pilots | Creator/affiliate-heavy brands and partners | Existing creator rosters and affiliate programs |

## First Practical Bets

The cleanest product wedge from the seed deck is running and adjacent movement communities. They have dense supply, recurring participation, obvious spend categories, clear brand demand, and a category every wellness, apparel, and beverage sponsor is already courting.

The safest trust wedge is creator-led or community-led IRL with a sponsor the creator or community already understands. It gives the sponsor a familiar trust surface and gives the host a clear budget, event, proof, and unlock path.

For demand generation, the highest-leverage first wedge is often a CreatorCommerce-sourced brand with a creator or affiliate roster. Use [CreatorCommerce Demand Bridge](/strategy/creatorcommerce-demand-bridge) to decide whether the brand is ready for Analog.

## What Counts As A Good Test

A good MVP use case has:

- a real sponsor budget or strong budget proxy
- a host that can execute without heavy hand-holding
- a clear attendee or community benefit
- a proof surface the sponsor will believe
- a repeat path if the test works
- a product object the team can improve afterward
- a learning artifact for the catalog, audience graph, or asset-need map

Use [Testing Grid](/market/testing-grid) before greenlighting a new use case.
