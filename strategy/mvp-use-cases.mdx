## First Practical Bets

Start with cash-funded playbooks, then use verticals as proof cases:

- **NYC pre-seed Sponsor cohort**: close LOIs for intended NYC deployment budget, use a refundable USD 500 deposit as the commitment filter, and reserve high-context matchmaking for Sponsors that commit. Treat the budget intent as proof of demand, not Analog revenue.
- **Micro grants**: USD 100 to USD 500/month for coffee, libations, supplies, rentals, print, accessibility, or small space support.
- **Macro grants**: USD 1k to USD 5k/month for the best Hosts, strategic rooms, recurring activations, and operator margin.
- **Sponsor-backed grants and raffles**: money-on-the-table hooks that pull qualified supply into applications, collabs, spend cards, and proof.
- **Selective brand funds**: Sponsor-funded public funds designed for a specific market, such as a Crocs recovery fund in New York, a Lululemon-style community wellbeing grant, a Nike-style play and sport access fund, or a Tapcart superconnector fund in SF.
- **Corporate grant-style and community-impact flows**: eligibility gate first, local review context second, full application third, funded IRL activation fourth, with verification, safeguards, grant-administration context, and proof built into the product.
- **Pitch tools**: free or low-friction packaging for Hosts and connectors with event history, social proof, community reach, and funding asks.
- **Services and tools**: insurance, documents, contracts, print-on-demand, local designers, vendors, rentals, and proof kits.
- **Print and publication**: AnalogNYC first, then AnalogSF when transfer-market work is justified, plus seasonal mini-zines and newsletters that own supply, sell subscriptions, and route readers into events, funds, and applications.
- **Public and municipal money**: transparent, governed, reportable funding for community gatherings, access, public space, local commerce, and civic outcomes, with [public-space social impact measurement](/references/public-space-social-impact-measurement) when belonging, connection, trust, agency, respect, or cross-group contact is part of the thesis.
- **Public-space activation support**: permit readiness, event risk tiering, insurance/COI help, site-plan support, weather contingency, vending/revenue guidance, and public-safe proof for parks, plazas, sidewalks, streets, corridors, and Open Streets.
- **Community collective funds**: local pooled capital from businesses, funders, and neighborhood leaders that creates a governed market fund before a large corporate Sponsor enters, such as 20 USD 500 checks funding a USD 10k neighborhood campaign with an Analog application page.
- **Political and advocacy field funds**: compliance-safe grassroots activation budgets that test national/state/district/county/city/ward/event allocation logic without exposing sensitive data.
- **Vertical proof cases**: arts/culture, movement/wellness, farmers markets, circular fashion, wine/book clubs, pet-parent rituals, creative walks, dinners, creator-led IRL, founder rooms, games, civic service, food, and hospitality.

## Test Scorecard

A good MVP use case has:

| Criterion | What to check |
|---|---|
| Sponsor budget | Real buyer, real budget or strong budget proxy, clear approval path. |
| Host trust | The Host can execute without heavy hand-holding and has community permission. |
| Attendee value | Sponsor money makes the gathering cheaper, better, easier, or more useful. |
| Spend control | Budget, categories, receipts, and anomalies can be governed. |
| Compliance boundary | Legal, privacy, election, public-sector, safety, and community-trust constraints are clear enough to operate. |
| Proof readiness | Attendance, claims, content, sentiment, spend, or outcome data can be captured credibly. |
| Repeatability | Success can become a series, fund, template, city playbook, or Sponsor renewal. |
| Product learning | The test improves a durable object: campaign, event set, Host event, catalog record, proof packet, or spend rule. |

Greenlight when the test has a believable Sponsor, trusted Host, clear consumer/community value, proof the Sponsor will understand, and a repeat path if it works.
