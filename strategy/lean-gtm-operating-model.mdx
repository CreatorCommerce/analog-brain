Analog should run the first national GTM motion as one central operating system across four launch markets, not as four local companies.

The constraint is part of the strategy:

- fewer than 9 full-time people
- product built in-house by a three-person build team: Chai, the founder/operator, and one additional engineer
- one CS owner
- the rest of the full-time team focused on GTM
- NYC, LA, Miami, and SF covered by fractional city leads and project-based local help until a market proves repeatability

<Info>
The default operating shape is 8 full-time people: 3 product/build, 4 GTM, and 1 CS. City leads, local producers, media editors, designers, and field stringers should start as contractors or retainers.
</Info>

## Team Shape

| Seat | Count | Owns | Does not own |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Product/build team | 3 | Core platform, admin tools, workflow automation, analytics, spend/proof rails, product fixes, instrumentation | Custom event production, manual sponsor reporting, city scouting |
| GTM lead | 1 | GTM strategy, sponsor narrative, target account list, pricing/pilot economics, sales quality, close discipline | Managing every local relationship directly |
| Sponsor partnerships | 1 | Sponsor outbound, discovery, briefs, follow-up, renewals, partner/channel motion | Running host supply or event logistics |
| City/supply lead | 1 | City lead management, supply asks, catalog quality, host packaging, connector bench, city scorecards | Owning every sponsor conversation |
| Content/media lead | 1 | `The Connector`, social, field content intake, media contractors, owned IRL editorial calendar | Acting as a full local producer in every city |
| CS/implementation | 1 | Sponsor and host onboarding, launch checklists, support, proof QA, renewal packet readiness, feedback loop | Custom agency-style project management |

This keeps the company under 9 people while still covering product, sponsor demand, supply, media, and customer success.

## Coverage Model

Treat the country as a portfolio of four city labs.

| Market | Primary job | Default causeways |
|---|---|---|
| NYC | Sponsor density, media density, founder/fashion/finance/community overlap | Running/walking, founder breakfasts, dinner clubs, arts/culture salons, community-builder happy hours |
| LA | Creator, wellness, content, entertainment, and production density | Creator-led IRL, wellness/recovery, running, photo/film walks, founder/operator dinners |
| Miami | Hospitality, wellness, luxury, real estate, finance, Latin America bridge, and public-private energy | Wellness/run clubs, founder/investor salons, art/design gatherings, hospitality-led owned IRL |
| SF | Tech, AI, developer, founder, climate, civic, and builder-community density | AI/builder dinners, founder breakfasts, run/walk clubs, climate/civic meetups, hackathons |

Do not push all four markets equally every week. Each quarter, every active city should have:

- one primary causeway
- one primary sponsor perspective
- one owned IRL ritual or gathering format
- one content lane
- a top 25 list of hosts, connectors, spaces, and operators
- 3 to 5 packaged opportunities that can become pilots, fund drops, or sponsor briefs

## GTM Funnel

Run GTM as a short loop from sponsor signal to proof, not as open-ended ecosystem building.

| Step | Owner | Output |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Find sponsor signal | GTM lead and sponsor partnerships | Target account, sponsor perspective, budget proxy, city/category hypothesis |
| 2. Write the brief | Sponsor partnerships | One-page sponsor brief with objective, market, proof, budget range, guardrails, and target supply |
| 3. Create supply ask | City/supply lead | City lead prompt with the exact host, connector, space, or community profile needed |
| 4. Source options | City leads and city/supply lead | 10 to 15 candidates per active city, tagged by trust, sponsor surface, proof path, media angle, and blocker |
| 5. Package top options | City/supply lead and sponsor partnerships | 3 to 5 sponsor-ready packages or Activation Opportunities |
| 6. Launch pilot | CS, sponsor partnerships, product/build as needed | Campaign, event set, host event, spend rules, proof checklist, and support path |
| 7. Prove and renew | CS and sponsor partnerships | Proof packet, renewal ask, unlock recommendation, product blocker list |
| 8. Publish the learning | Content/media lead | Recap, Connector item, social clip, field note, case story, or city insight |

The loop should normally fit inside 2 to 4 weeks for a small pilot. If it cannot, the motion is probably too custom for the current team.

## Weekly Operating Cadence

| Day | Meeting or workflow | Owner | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monday | GTM priority review | GTM lead | Which 1 to 2 sponsor briefs matter this week? |
| Tuesday | Sponsor brief and outbound block | Sponsor partnerships | Which sponsors get a concrete pilot or fund angle? |
| Wednesday | City supply review | City/supply lead | Which candidates become packages, catalog records, or no-go? |
| Thursday | Pilot and CS review | CS | What is launching, stuck, proving, or renewing? |
| Friday | Product/media retro | Product/build and content/media lead | Which blockers become product work, content, or playbook updates? |

Keep meetings short. The artifacts matter more than the meeting:

- sponsor brief
- city supply packet
- host or connector package
- pilot launch checklist
- proof packet
- GTM product blocker list
- media intake
- city scorecard

## Product Build Rules

The product/build team should stay close to GTM without becoming an internal agency.

Build when one of these is true:

- the blocker appears in 2 or more pilots, cities, or sponsor conversations
- the blocker prevents a committed pilot from launching
- the blocker creates CS load that will repeat
- the workflow maps to a durable object: campaign, event set, application, host event, spend card, proof, catalog record, profile, file, task, shipment, or report
- the change improves spend integrity, proof quality, sponsor confidence, host onboarding, or city lead throughput

Do not build for:

- one sponsor's custom report format
- one city's one-off event logistics
- speculative features without a sponsor, host, or CS blocker
- product polish that does not improve launch, proof, renewal, or supply velocity

The weekly GTM product blocker list should have three columns:

```text
Blocker:
Affected sponsor/city/pilot:
Manual workaround:
Repeat likelihood:
Product object:
Decision: build / workaround / ignore
```

## GTM Rules

GTM should stay demand-led.

- Every sponsor conversation must end with a sponsor perspective, budget proxy, proof need, city/category hypothesis, and next step.
- Do not ask city leads for broad research. Give them sponsor-backed or causeway-backed supply asks.
- Do not sell a pilot unless supply can be found or created within 10 business days.
- Keep early pilots small: one sponsor, one or two markets, 3 to 5 host-side options, clear proof, clean renewal ask.
- Every pilot must produce one of: renewal, unlock, product blocker, catalog learning, content asset, or no-go reason.
- Do not let "national" mean scattered. National GTM means one repeatable system applied across four city labs.

## CS Rules

One CS person can support the motion only if CS is kept tight.

CS owns:

- sponsor onboarding checklist
- host onboarding checklist
- launch readiness
- support triage
- proof QA
- proof packet readiness
- renewal handoff
- recurring docs or FAQ updates

CS does not own:

- sponsor sales
- city scouting
- custom event production
- bespoke content production
- manual reporting that should become product, template, or no-go learning

If CS is overloaded, the answer is usually to narrow pilots, improve templates, reduce custom work, or build a repeated workflow. It is not to quietly turn CS into an agency project manager.

## Contractor Bench

Use variable capacity before fixed headcount.

| Contractor type | Use for | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| City lead | Relationship discovery, field capture, host/connector mapping, owned IRL hosting | A city has a clear causeway and sponsor perspective |
| Local producer | Venue, vendor, run-of-show, event-day details | A pilot or owned IRL moment has real logistics |
| Field media stringer | Photo, video, audio, interviews, event capture | A city has story yield worth publishing |
| Editor/designer | Clips, recaps, graphics, newsletter, print layout | The content/media lead has approved material |
| Specialist operator | Insurance, permits, finance, payments, compliance, public-sector navigation | A repeated blocker appears |

Contractors should always feed product objects, not just output. A city lead discovery becomes a catalog record. A media stringer capture becomes a profile or proof asset. A producer runbook becomes a reusable checklist.

## What Success Looks Like

The model is working if, with fewer than 9 full-time people, Analog can show:

- repeat sponsor briefs across at least 2 sponsor perspectives
- qualified supply in NYC, LA, Miami, and SF without full local teams
- at least 2 causeways moving from pilot to repeatable playbook
- city leads producing catalog records, host packages, content, and fundable opportunities
- CS support load staying bounded
- product blockers becoming reusable workflow improvements
- proof packets leading to renewals, unlocks, or clear no-go calls
- media and owned IRL creating inbound hosts, connectors, and sponsor interest

If the team needs heavy custom work to make every activation happen, the GTM strategy is too broad. Tighten to fewer sponsor briefs, fewer cities, fewer causeways, and more repeatable product objects.
