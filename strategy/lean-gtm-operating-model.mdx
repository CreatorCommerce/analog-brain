Analog should run GTM as a city-lead operating system.

Read this after the strategy and funding pages. Those pages explain what Analog should prove. This page explains how a city team produces the raw material.

The city lead is the gateway into local trust. Their job is to create access: interviews, IRL content, leads, intros, field notes, Host context, venue knowledge, connector maps, and local proof. The central team behind them should process that raw material into product objects, media, Sponsor packages, fund drops, proof packets, and renewal decisions.

<Info>
Every city lead action should become media, product memory, Sponsor supply, funded work, proof, or a sharper no-go.
</Info>

The operating analogy is early regional marketplace ops, made leaner with AI and product memory: win supply city by city, judge performance by local KPIs/OKRs, and use the central stack to keep each market from becoming bespoke.

```text
city lead in market
-> interviews, intros, IRL content, leads, and field notes
-> central processing stack
-> media, catalog records, Sponsor packages, fund drops, proof
-> operating budget and renewal decisions run through Analog
```

AnalogNYC and AnalogSF should be the first city lead systems to prove. NYC remains the first operating proof; SF is the paired transfer market when access and demand justify it. Expansion only makes sense when the city lead plus central stack can create qualified supply, media gravity, clean spend, proof, and repeat Sponsor demand without turning into bespoke services.

## GTM Wedge

Do not launch as a horizontal event marketplace. The first GTM wedge is AnalogNYC plus Analog Print Mag:

```text
editorial access
-> premium print issue and owned IRL
-> Host, connector, and Sponsor applications
-> governed pilots and fund drops
-> proof packet
-> renewal, next issue, or no-go
```

Sales starts with four commercial lanes: eComm, SaaS/tech, civic/nonprofit, and political action. Supply starts with interviews, profiles, connectors, fund applications, and field work. Product proves that money can move into trusted IRL without losing spend control, Host trust, compliance boundaries, or renewal clarity.

## City Lead Job

The city lead should become a super connector for community, culture, spaces, sponsors, and operators. They are not a generic event scout.

| City lead input | What Analog should do with it |
|---|---|
| Interviews | turn into Connector profiles, Analog Print Mag features, Host stories, and Sponsor-safe context |
| IRL content | turn into social clips, proof assets, field notes, issue spreads, and owned IRL recaps |
| Leads and intros | turn into Sponsor targets, Host prospects, connector profiles, venue options, and catalog records |
| Community asks | turn into micro grant, macro grant, service/tool, insurance, print, space, or vendor needs |
| Local judgment | turn into trust notes, no-go calls, Sponsor-fit warnings, and event-set strategy |
| Operating spend | run through Analog so interviews, coffees, scouting, content capture, and owned IRL become categorized proof |

## City Sequence

| Phase | City brand | Job |
|---|---|---|
| Round-one operating proof | AnalogNYC | Build the first owned city supply business through Analog. |
| Round-one transfer market | AnalogSF | Transfer and pressure-test the playbook when founder roots, Sponsor demand, or supply access make it efficient. |
| 8-16 month expansion candidates | Analog LA, Analog Toronto, Analog Chicago, Analog Miami, Analog DC, Analog Austin | Add only when a Sponsor, public/private program, founder-rooted operator, or proven city playbook justifies the work. |
| Campaign-funded city buildout | Future markets such as Analog Denver | Spin up or expand when a serious campaign requires deeper local infrastructure. |

Until the round-one loop is working, do not add more cities for coverage. Each active city needs one primary market-entry path, one Sponsor lane or dogfood objective, one owned IRL ritual, one content lane, and 3 to 5 packaged opportunities.

## Central Processing Stack

The city lead should not be left alone with a pile of notes. Analog needs a consistent backing stack that turns local effort into company memory.

| Backing layer | Takes from city lead | Produces |
|---|---|---|
| Editorial/media | interviews, voice notes, photos, video, scene reads | Connector posts, social, issue features, sponsor-safe field narratives |
| Supply operations | leads, intros, venues, community maps | catalog records, Host profiles, connector profiles, 3 to 5 packaged opportunities |
| Sponsor packaging | local context, category fit, proof angle | Sponsor briefs, Activation Opportunities, pitch docs, renewal arguments |
| Funding operations | community asks, budget needs, blockers | micro grants, macro grants, service/tool requests, fund drops, public/nonprofit lanes |
| Proof and finance | city lead spend, Host spend, receipts, field proof | categorized spend, proof packets, anomaly notes, next-dollar recommendations |
| Product operations | repeated blockers and manual work | product requirements, templates, workflow changes, no-go rules |

The central stack can include internal operators, contractors, tools, and partners. The important point is consistency: every city lead should know where raw material goes, who processes it, and which product object it becomes.

## Local Bench And Operating Budget

City leads can use a local bench when it creates more trusted supply or better proof.

| Role | Use for | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Connector bench | scene access, intros, Host scouting, opportunity packaging | referrals, trust notes, activation options |
| Local producer | venue, vendor, run-of-show, event-day details | launch-ready pilot, vendor notes, cost reality |
| Field media stringer | photo, video, audio, interviews, event capture | raw media, proof assets, issue material |
| Editor/designer | clips, recaps, graphics, newsletter, print layout | publishable media and print assets |
| Specialist operator | insurance, permits, payments, compliance, public-sector navigation | blocker removal and reusable workflow |

The city lead operating budget should run through Analog. Coffees, scouting, small owned gatherings, local travel, creator fees, photo/video, venue deposits, and print/distribution tests should be categorized by campaign, event set, Host, content lane, and proof expectation.

Each priority market should also have a small community grant pool when the city lead is mature enough to use it. The working range is USD 10k to USD 50k per market for micro grants, missing-supply creation, fund drops, service/tool support, and proof cases. The grant pool is not charity budget; it is supply acquisition, market learning, and proof generation.

## City Lead Grant Desk

Grant discovery should sit between the city lead and central processing stack.

| Owner | Job | Output |
|---|---|---|
| City lead | Find which Hosts, venues, BIDs, nonprofits, artists, small businesses, and public-space operators are real. | field notes, referrals, Host intake, local trust reads. |
| Central funding ops | Match those actors to grants, Sponsor funds, and public/private programs. | eligibility notes, opportunity list, packet checklist. |
| Proof/product ops | Turn applications into reusable records. | Host readiness file, files/docs, proof standard, catalog update. |
| Media/editorial | Use grant and fund opportunities as reasons to interview and elevate builders. | grant desk column, fund drop, Host profile, QR/application flow. |
| Partnerships | Convert repeated Host demand into Sponsor-backed funds or public/nonprofit workflow pitches. | fund thesis, agency memo, Sponsor brief. |

The city lead should not become a solo grant writer. Their leverage is access and local judgment. The central stack should carry research, packet templates, proof standards, and government conversion notes.

## City Operating Department

A mature city layer becomes an operating department inside Analog, not just a contractor list.

| Department layer | Job |
|---|---|
| City lead | Owns weekly field work, supply, local relationships, and city scorecard. |
| Local advisory board | Gives regional judgment, referrals, trust constraints, and Sponsor-fit warnings. Use [Advisory Group Operating Model](/strategy/advisory-groups) as the first cross-market structure before city-specific boards split out. |
| Operator bench | Producers, connectors, media stringers, compliance specialists, and service providers used as needed. |
| Market-specific media | Print zines, guides, social, event calendars, profiles, and local distribution. |
| Grant pool | Lets the city lead create supply and proof before larger Sponsor budgets arrive. |
| City P&L learning | Tracks sourcing cost, grant yield, managed support, media revenue, Sponsor revenue, and expansion readiness. |

Campaign demand can fund deeper infrastructure. A large Sponsor program in a new region should be allowed to pay for the city apparatus required to execute well, as long as the field work still flows into product memory.

City leads are judged by qualified supply created, trusted referrals, packaged opportunities, field notes, media yield, blocker clarity, community sentiment, and whether Sponsors or agency engagements get closer to repeatable pilots.

## GTM Funnel

| Step | Owner | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Find Sponsor signal | GTM + partnerships | target account, Sponsor perspective, budget proxy, city/category hypothesis |
| Write brief | partnerships | objective, market, proof, budget range, guardrails, target supply |
| Create supply ask | central stack + city lead | exact Host, connector, space, or community profile needed |
| Source options | city lead + connector bench | candidates tagged by trust, Sponsor surface, proof path, media angle, blocker |
| Process options | central stack | catalog records, Host profiles, connector profiles, no-go notes |
| Package top options | sponsor packaging + city lead | 3 to 5 Sponsor-ready packages or Activation Opportunities |
| Launch pilot | implementation + city lead | campaign, event set, Host event, spend rules, proof checklist |
| Prove and renew | proof/finance + partnerships | proof packet, renewal ask, unlock recommendation, product blocker list |
| Publish learning | editorial/media | recap, Connector item, social clip, field note, issue insight |

Small pilots should fit inside 2 to 4 weeks. If not, the motion is probably too custom.

## Operating Cadence

Meetings should stay light. The artifacts matter more than internal ceremony.

| Question | Artifact |
|---|---|
| What did the city lead see this week? | field notes, interviews, intros, photos/video, spend notes |
| What needs processing? | media intake, catalog queue, Sponsor lead queue, fund idea queue |
| Which opportunities are real? | 3 to 5 packaged opportunities, no-go notes, missing assets |
| What is launching or renewing? | pilot checklist, spend rules, proof packet, renewal ask |
| What should become product or a playbook? | blocker list, template update, workflow change, partner need |

## Product And Support Rules

Build when a blocker appears in 2 or more pilots, cities, or Sponsor conversations; prevents a committed pilot from launching; creates repeat support load; maps to a durable object; or improves spend integrity, proof quality, Sponsor confidence, Host onboarding, or city lead throughput.

Do not build for one Sponsor's custom report, one city's one-off logistics, speculative polish, or anything that does not improve launch, proof, renewal, city lead leverage, or supply velocity.

Support stays bounded when the city lead and central stack use templates: intake forms, interview prompts, catalog fields, launch checklists, proof standards, media upload rules, and renewal packet formats. Repeated manual work should become product, a contractor playbook, or a no-go rule.

## Success Signals

The model works when one strong city lead, backed by the central processing stack, can create a deep city brand, ship a credible media-led supply loop, repeat Sponsor or agency briefs across multiple commercial lanes, create qualified supply before adding another city, turn field work into product objects, keep city spend categorized, and produce proof packets that lead to renewals, unlocks, or clear no-go calls.

If every activation needs heavy custom work, narrow the briefs, cities, market-entry paths, and product objects.
