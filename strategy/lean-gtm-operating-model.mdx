Analog should run the first national GTM motion as one central operating system across four launch markets, not as four local companies.

<Info>
Default shape: 8 full-time people. 3 product/build, 4 GTM, 1 CS. City leads, producers, media stringers, editors, designers, and specialists start as contractors or retainers.
</Info>

## Core Rule

Staff judgment before fixed local teams.

```text
Central team -> fractional city leads -> flexible city pods -> fixed city teams only after repeatability
```

The product is built in-house by Chai, the founder/operator, and one additional engineer. The rest of full-time headcount should stay close to GTM, proof, supply, and customer learning.

## Team Shape

| Seat | Count | Owns | Does not own |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Product/build | 3 | Platform, admin tools, automation, analytics, spend/proof rails, instrumentation | Custom event production or manual sponsor reporting |
| GTM lead | 1 | GTM strategy, sponsor narrative, target accounts, pilot economics, close discipline | Managing every local relationship |
| Sponsor partnerships | 1 | Sponsor outbound, discovery, briefs, follow-up, renewals, partner/channel motion | Host supply or event logistics |
| City/supply lead | 1 | City lead management, supply asks, catalog quality, host packaging, connector bench, city scorecards | Owning every sponsor conversation |
| Content/media lead | 1 | `The Connector`, social, field intake, media contractors, owned IRL editorial calendar | Producing every city event |
| CS/implementation | 1 | Sponsor and host onboarding, launch checklists, support, proof QA, renewal packet readiness | Sales, city scouting, or agency-style project management |

## Launch Markets

| City | Primary job | First wedges |
|---|---|---|
| NYC | Sponsor density, media density, founder/fashion/finance/community overlap | Running/walking, founder breakfasts, dinner clubs, arts/culture salons, community-builder happy hours |
| LA | Creator, wellness, entertainment, production, fitness, outdoor, and brand culture density | Creator-led IRL, wellness/recovery, running, photo/film walks, founder/operator dinners |
| Miami | Hospitality, wellness, luxury, real estate, finance, Latin America bridge, and public-private energy | Wellness/run clubs, founder/investor salons, art/design gatherings, hospitality-led owned IRL |
| SF | Tech, AI, developer, founder, climate, civic, and builder-community density | AI/builder dinners, founder breakfasts, run/walk clubs, climate/civic meetups, hackathons |

Do not push every city equally every week. Each active city needs one primary causeway, one sponsor perspective, one owned IRL ritual, one content lane, and 3 to 5 packaged opportunities.

## City Leads

City leads are local trust nodes, not generic event scouts.

Look for:

- respected local taste
- relationships across builders, spaces, creators, operators, and sponsors
- ability to host and convene
- comfort capturing interviews, field notes, and media
- sponsor judgment across eComm, Tech, and Public/Non-profit perspectives
- operational discipline around budgets and proof

Start them as retainers or scoped contractors. Weekly outputs should be supply candidates, field notes, connector intros, owned IRL support, and media capture.

## Contractor Bench

| Contractor type | Use for | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| City lead | Relationship discovery, field capture, host/connector mapping, owned IRL hosting | A city has a clear causeway and sponsor perspective |
| Connector bench | Scene access, intros, host scouting, opportunity packaging | One connector can unlock multiple options |
| Local producer | Venue, vendor, run-of-show, event-day details | A pilot or owned IRL moment has real logistics |
| Field media stringer | Photo, video, audio, interviews, event capture | A city has story yield worth publishing |
| Editor/designer | Clips, recaps, graphics, newsletter, print layout | The content/media lead has approved material |
| Specialist operator | Insurance, permits, payments, compliance, public-sector navigation | A repeated blocker appears |

Contractors should feed product objects: catalog records, profiles, proof assets, checklists, sponsor packages, or asset-need notes.

## GTM Funnel

| Step | Owner | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Find sponsor signal | GTM lead and sponsor partnerships | Target account, sponsor perspective, budget proxy, city/category hypothesis |
| Write the brief | Sponsor partnerships | One-page brief with objective, market, proof, budget range, guardrails, and target supply |
| Create supply ask | City/supply lead | City lead prompt with the exact host, connector, space, or community profile needed |
| Source options | City leads and city/supply lead | 10 to 15 candidates per active city, tagged by trust, sponsor surface, proof path, media angle, and blocker |
| Package top options | City/supply lead and sponsor partnerships | 3 to 5 sponsor-ready packages or Activation Opportunities |
| Launch pilot | CS, sponsor partnerships, product/build as needed | Campaign, event set, host event, spend rules, proof checklist, and support path |
| Prove and renew | CS and sponsor partnerships | Proof packet, renewal ask, unlock recommendation, product blocker list |
| Publish learning | Content/media lead | Recap, Connector item, social clip, field note, case story, or city insight |

The loop should normally fit inside 2 to 4 weeks for a small pilot. If it cannot, the motion is probably too custom.

## Weekly Cadence

| Day | Workflow | Decision |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | GTM priority review | Which 1 to 2 sponsor briefs matter this week? |
| Tuesday | Sponsor brief and outbound block | Which sponsors get a concrete pilot or fund angle? |
| Wednesday | City supply review | Which candidates become packages, catalog records, or no-go? |
| Thursday | Pilot and CS review | What is launching, stuck, proving, or renewing? |
| Friday | Product/media retro | Which blockers become product work, content, or playbook updates? |

Meetings should be short. The artifacts matter: sponsor brief, city supply packet, pilot checklist, proof packet, product blocker list, media intake, and city scorecard.

## Product Build Rules

Build when:

- the blocker appears in 2 or more pilots, cities, or sponsor conversations
- the blocker prevents a committed pilot from launching
- the blocker creates repeat CS load
- the workflow maps to a durable object: campaign, event set, application, host event, spend card, proof, catalog record, profile, file, task, shipment, or report
- the change improves spend integrity, proof quality, sponsor confidence, host onboarding, or city lead throughput

Do not build for one sponsor's custom report, one city's one-off logistics, speculative polish, or anything that does not improve launch, proof, renewal, or supply velocity.

## CS Rules

One CS person can support the motion only if CS stays bounded.

CS owns sponsor onboarding, host onboarding, launch readiness, support triage, proof QA, proof packet readiness, renewal handoff, and recurring docs/FAQ updates.

CS does not own sponsor sales, city scouting, custom event production, bespoke content production, or manual reporting that should become product, template, or no-go learning.

## 90-Day Launch Plan

| Time | Output per active city |
|---|---|
| Days 1-15 | City thesis, city lead scorecard, initial scene map, top 25 connectors/builders/spaces |
| Days 16-30 | 10 field notes or interviews, 1 owned Analog gathering, 3 sponsor-perspective briefs |
| Days 31-60 | 10 catalog records, 3 connector profiles, 3 Activation Opportunities, 1 media feature, 1 fund hypothesis |
| Days 61-90 | 1 sponsor-funded or fund-like pilot, 1 owned IRL repeat, proof report, city scorecard, staffing recommendation |

## Add Headcount Only When

- owned IRL attendance repeats
- sponsor or fund demand repeats
- media yield is obvious
- trusted host and connector profiles exist
- central operations are the bottleneck
- more staffing will create fundable supply, not just more activity

The first local add after a city lead is usually a producer if logistics are heavy or a field media stringer if the city is story-rich.

## Success Signals

The model works if fewer than 9 full-time people can produce:

- repeat sponsor briefs across at least 2 sponsor perspectives
- qualified supply in NYC, LA, Miami, and SF without full local teams
- at least 2 causeways moving from pilot to repeatable playbook
- city leads producing catalog records, host packages, content, and fundable opportunities
- bounded CS load
- product blockers becoming reusable workflow improvements
- proof packets leading to renewals, unlocks, or clear no-go calls

If every activation needs heavy custom work, narrow the briefs, cities, causeways, and product objects.
