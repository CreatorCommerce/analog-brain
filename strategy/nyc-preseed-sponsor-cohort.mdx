Use this page for the immediate brand-side and Sponsor-side pre-seed motion.

This page supersedes any earlier implication that the first closed-door Sponsor presell is split evenly across NYC and SF. Analog can still learn from SF and later sell into SaaS/founder rooms, but the first pre-seed Sponsor cohort is NYC-only.

The goal is not to sell software yet. The goal is to pre-sell the investment in community capture in New York City.

```text
Analog has built the largest directory of NYC communities
-> Analog is turning that directory into sponsor-ready cultural real estate
-> five serious Sponsors commit budget intent through LOIs and deposits
-> Analog matches them into the best-fit communities by hand
-> spend, proof, attribution, and unit economics teach the next market package
```

## Fundraising Ethos

Analog should not ask brands to pay a platform fee at this stage.

The ask is sharper:

```text
If you want exposure to the top of culture in the biggest market in the world,
put real partnership budget behind it.
```

The Sponsor is not buying a list. The Sponsor is earning entry into the earliest marketplace form, where Analog is using hard-won social capital to help a small group of aligned companies sponsor and collab with real NYC communities.

| Principle | Operating meaning |
|---|---|
| Budget proves seriousness | USD 50k is enough to be taken seriously. USD 100k+ is best. |
| LOI before contract | The target is an LOI for intended NYC deployment budget, not a full commercial contract. |
| Deposit creates focus | A USD 500 refundable deposit reserves a place in the first cohort and filters casual interest. |
| No Analog rake now | Analog should not take a fee, time rake, or platform cut from these first engagements. The incentive is to create maximum Sponsor value and proof. |
| Access is earned | Analog should not spend its community social capital curating lists for brands that have not committed. |
| NYC proves the model | NYC is the unit-economics lab for pricing and packaging regional supply before new markets scale. |

## Cohort Shape

The intended Q3 focus is five serious Sponsors.

Analog can accept more than five if they are aligned and can put up the money, but the operating posture should stay focused. The circle should feel selective, useful, and early, not like an open sales pipeline.

| Cohort rule | Standard |
|---|---|
| Market | NYC only for the pre-seed Sponsor presell. |
| Sponsor mix | Healthy balance of brands and SaaS/tech. |
| Budget floor | At least USD 50k dedicated to NYC partnerships or community activation. |
| Preferred budget | USD 100k+ intended deployment budget. |
| Commitment artifact | LOI for budget intent, target market, and deployment thesis. |
| Deposit | USD 500 refundable deposit to lock priority and signal seriousness. |
| Engagement type | Closed-door, hands-on, high-context access before the broader product launch. |
| Analog monetization | No platform fee, no time rake, no percentage take for this initial cohort unless later terms change explicitly. |

## Why NYC Only

NYC is the pressure test for culture, brand, retail, wellness, media, hospitality, and category-defining IRL.

The internal reason is unit economics:

```text
What does it cost to capture and package regional supply,
what will serious Sponsors commit to deploy,
and what CAC payback logic does that imply before Analog expands?
```

Do not describe NYC-only focus as a lack of ambition. It is discipline. Analog needs one regional market where supply density, Sponsor urgency, proof, and packaging economics can be measured cleanly.

SF still matters as a future transfer market for SaaS/tech, founder rooms, AI, developer, and operator communities. It should not dilute the first NYC Sponsor cohort.

## Directory And Social Capital Boundary

Analog is building a massive NYC community directory, from run clubs to founder circles. It may become publicly browsable soon.

That does not mean Sponsors get free high-context access.

The rule:

```text
Sponsors can see enough of the directory to believe the market is real.
Analog only does high-context matchmaking for Sponsors who commit to leaning in.
```

| Sponsor behavior | Analog response |
|---|---|
| Browses public directory | Show public-safe records, categories, examples, aggregate density, and enough signal to create confidence. |
| Asks for curated lists before commitment | Do not spend community social capital. Ask for LOI, target market, budget intent, and refundable deposit first. |
| Commits budget intent | Build the high-context match set, outreach logic, and Sponsor-specific Event sets. |
| Wants exclusivity | Treat separately. The default cohort gives early access, not category exclusivity. |
| Wants raw community data | Keep private fields, attendee identity, contact paths, and sensitive notes restricted. |

Obscuring can be practical: limited cards, blurred details, aggregate counts, sample archetypes, and sponsor-safe summaries. The Sponsor should understand the market without receiving the map for free.

## Host And Community Work

Analog is already working with communities to refine who is ready for Sponsor dollars.

The readiness work should capture:

| Readiness question | Product or operator output |
|---|---|
| Who is ready to go? | Host profile, claim status, availability, community fit, and next action. |
| What is their social history? | Authorized public social links, mention history, content examples, and proof boundaries. |
| What is their event history? | Past events, cadence, rough attendance, Venue context, photos, and proof links. |
| Are they best-in-class gatherers? | Field notes, referrals, reviews where available, social receptiveness, and operator judgment. |
| What do they need funded? | Cash ask, services/tools ask, insurance, space, vendors, print, proof kit, or operator support. |
| Which Sponsors fit? | Category fit, exclusions, acceptable Sponsor presence, and match reason. |

This is the supply-side work that makes the directory valuable. A raw directory is not the product. The product is the trusted, fundable, sponsor-ready community graph.

## Sponsor Pitch

The posture should feel like a no-brainer without sounding desperate:

```text
You are not paying Analog a platform fee today.
You are committing real NYC partnership budget so we can help deploy it
into the communities most likely to make your brand culturally present.
```

Good language:

```text
Analog today has built the largest directory of communities operating in NYC,
from run clubs to founder circles.
We are now turning that directory into prime real estate
for brands and tech companies to partner with the IRL moments those communities are making.
```

The invite:

```text
We are working with a small Q3 cohort of companies that are in a fantastic position
to take over the NYC market in their category.
The filter is alignment plus at least USD 50k in intended partnership budget.
To hold a place, we are using a refundable USD 500 deposit and an LOI for budget intent.
```

## Spreadsheet Operating Rule

Do not overbuild Sponsor CRM for this stage.

Run the pre-seed Sponsor cohort through a spreadsheet. Rebuild systems later when the pattern is proven.

The spreadsheet should track:

| Field | Why |
|---|---|
| Sponsor name | Basic pipeline identity. |
| Lane | Brand, SaaS/tech, civic/public, or other route. |
| Category | The NYC category they want to own. |
| Target market | The community family, neighborhood, circuit, or audience. |
| Budget intent | USD 50k floor, USD 100k+ preferred, or other stated range. |
| LOI status | Draft, sent, signed, declined, or blocked. |
| Deposit status | Requested, paid, refunded, waived, or blocked. |
| Fit reason | Why they deserve early access to the NYC community graph. |
| Matchmaking status | Not started, sample shown, committed match build, outreach, pilot, proof. |
| Proof need | What will make them deploy more. |
| Next action | One owner, one date, one clear move. |

Keep CreatorCommerce and Analog tracking separate. CreatorCommerce can be a demand bridge, but the Analog cohort should have clean Analog records, docs, and spreadsheet tracking.

## Boundaries

- Do not overcommit to five if more serious Sponsors are ready, but do not let the cohort become unfocused.
- Do not call the LOI a binding commercial contract.
- Do not imply the USD 500 deposit is non-refundable.
- Do not promise category exclusivity unless separately agreed.
- Do not give away curated community lists before commitment.
- Do not expose private Host data, raw contacts, attendee identity, or sensitive field notes.
- Do not sell this as a normal paid plan, credit pack, or product subscription.
- Do not mix CreatorCommerce CRM, customer records, or deal workflow into Analog's pre-seed cohort tracking.
- Do not treat communities as inventory. The Sponsor still needs fit, generosity, proof, and Host trust.
