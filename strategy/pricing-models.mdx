Pricing should reinforce the product thesis: Analog helps Sponsor capital find trusted IRL supply, move through governed budgets, produce proof, and earn the next allocation.

Read this after the Sponsor, Host, public/nonprofit, and high-ticket pages. Pricing only makes sense once the reader understands the money, actors, proof, and operating work being priced.

Use [MAC Versus CAC Contract](/strategy/supply-side-mac-community-champion) for the cost of acquiring a market. Use this page for the commercial structure that turns access, credits, governed usage, program work, payment flow, spend cards, and future treasury rails into Analog revenue.

<Info>
Treat every number on this page as a testing band, not an official price card. The first job is to learn which structure Sponsors understand, accept, renew, and can explain internally.
</Info>

<Warning>
The immediate NYC pre-seed Sponsor cohort is an exception to the normal pricing path. For that closed-door motion, Analog should seek LOIs for intended NYC deployment budget and a refundable USD 500 deposit, not a platform fee, time rake, percentage take, paid plan, or credit package. Use [NYC Pre-Seed Sponsor Cohort](/strategy/nyc-preseed-sponsor-cohort) for that operating rule.
</Warning>

The model should stay simple on the surface:

```text
Sponsor pays for Analog
-> plan includes credits
-> credits unlock useful work
-> activation budget stays separate
-> heavier programs get clear fees
-> fintech revenue stays mostly invisible until compliant rails exist
```

Do not make the Sponsor think in dozens of micro-fees. Make them think:

```text
I have credits.
Credits get spent when Analog does real work for me.
If I run out, I can upgrade or buy more.
```

## Revenue Stack

Analog should model revenue in layers.

| Layer | What it monetizes | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Sponsor plan | Access to Sponsor workflows, included credits, saved campaigns, applicant review, and basic proof | near-term test |
| Credit usage | Active sponsored events, advanced search, AI research, prospecting, applicant unlocks, proof packets, reporting, and other success-oriented work | near-term test |
| Program fees | Campaign setup, fund administration, sourcing, managed activation, media, public/nonprofit support, high-ticket underwriting | now for heavier pilots |
| Payments and processing | Contribution links, Sponsor funding, marketplace payments, payouts, invoices, checkout, claims, or ticket/payment surfaces if Analog enters processing | future or partner-led |
| Spend card interchange | Interchange or revenue share from Sponsor-funded spend cards once production card rails exist | future fintech upside |
| Treasury or float | Interest, yield share, or treasury economics on eligible funded balances through compliant partners | long-term, regulated |
| Partner platform-as-a-service usage | Approved partner apps, event platforms, ticketing tools, and consumer surfaces that use Analog to list communities, report normalized proof, route Sponsor opportunities, and unlock Sponsor funds | future or pilot-led |
| Premium Host tools | Invited power-user features that help strong Hosts package, discover, manage, forecast, and price serious opportunity | later, quiet test |

The operating formula:

```text
Analog revenue =
  sponsor plans
  + credit overage
  + program/platform fees
  + managed support and media
  + payment/processing margin
  + card interchange share
  + treasury or float economics
  + partner API/attribution usage
```

The boundary:

```text
Sponsor commitment =
  controlled activation budget
  + Analog revenue
  + optional pass-through or reserve costs
```

Controlled activation budget is Sponsor capital routed through the product for approved Host, Venue, vendor, or program spend. It is not the same thing as Analog revenue.

## Sequencing Rule

Near-term revenue should come from the Sponsor and from visible product value. Long-term fintech upside should improve the business model without becoming the first thing users have to understand.

| Stage | Revenue posture | Guardrail |
|---|---|---|
| NYC pre-seed cohort | No Analog fee or rake. Sponsor provides LOI budget intent and refundable deposit; activation budget is intended for community deployment. | Do not confuse budget intent with Analog revenue. Do not sell this as a normal paid plan. |
| MVP | Charge Sponsors for plans, credits, campaign fees, sourcing, support, media, and fund administration. | Do not imply production billing, payouts, stored balances, payment processing, interchange, or treasury revenue before rails exist. |
| Early marketplace | Let free demand sensing convert into paid plans, credit usage, active events, and program fees. | Do not paywall before the Sponsor sees supply or accepts the first applicant. |
| Governed spend scale | Add card, payment, vendor, invoice, and processing economics where they reduce friction. | Do not tax Hosts or create annoying per-transaction visible fees that make off-platform work feel easier. |
| Financial infrastructure | Layer in interchange, treasury, and Host treasury economics through compliant partners. | Do not describe float, yield, insured accounts, or investment products as current or guaranteed. |

The near-term model should not depend on Host subscriptions, unrestricted balances, payout fees, interchange, treasury revenue, or generic event-production margin. The long-term model should still track those rails carefully because they may become meaningful when Analog controls more governed spend volume.

## Sponsor Plan And Credits

The clean first test is a low-friction Sponsor plan with included credits.

| Plan idea | What it does | Why it fits |
|---|---|---|
| Sponsor Starter | Test around USD 10/month with included credits. | Makes payment normal without making the product feel expensive before value is obvious. |
| Sponsor Growth | More included credits, more active campaigns or event sets, more advanced search, more AI research, and more applicant review. | Converts Sponsors who run out for a good reason. |
| Sponsor Program | Plan plus program fee, larger credit allowance, support, fund administration, reporting, and advanced controls. | Fits brands, public/nonprofit funds, political action Sponsors, and SaaS/tech teams with real activation budget. |
| Enterprise or market access | Retainer, market unlock package, high-ticket underwriting, custom workflow, media, and compliance support. | Fits repeat city/category buyers and serious IRL investment. |

The USD 10/month version should not be positioned as the main economic engine. It is a behavior design tool:

- it normalizes that Sponsors pay to use Analog
- it creates a credit balance the Sponsor can understand
- it avoids charging a confusing fee for every feature
- it creates a natural upgrade moment when usage is real
- it keeps activation budget separate from software spend

Free demand sensing can still exist before the plan gate. A Sponsor should be able to draft or post a future-dated campaign, watch the first 48 hours, and preview the first five applicants before deeper paid access. The first accepted applicant is the clean moment to require a paid plan, campaign commitment, or program fee.

For the NYC pre-seed cohort, the free/paid gate works differently. A Sponsor can see enough public-safe directory signal to believe the market is real, but Analog should not curate lists or use its community social capital until the Sponsor signs an LOI, names intended NYC deployment budget, and places the refundable deposit.

## Credit Design

Credits should price work that feels valuable because Analog is doing the work.

Good credit events have at least one of these traits:

- they produce Sponsor-visible output
- they reveal scarce supply
- they run AI or enrichment work
- they unlock applicant depth
- they reduce Sponsor labor
- they make proof easier to renew against
- they create product memory that improves future allocation

Do not use credits to punish basic workflow completion. Applying, accepting funding, uploading receipts, completing proof, and preserving trust should feel easy.

## Credit Usage Menu

Use exact credit amounts only after product telemetry shows cost and willingness to pay. Until then, use light, medium, and heavy bands.

| Usage surface | Credit posture | Why it is fair |
|---|---|---|
| Active sponsored event | Monthly or event-window credit cost after the Sponsor accepts a Host. | The Sponsor is using Analog to manage a live funded moment. |
| Active collab | Credit cost when a Sponsor creates or keeps multiple active Host relationships. | Collabs carry budget, proof, tasks, files, and support load. |
| Event set | Credit cost for active prospecting lanes beyond a basic campaign. | Event sets organize market search, assets, and supply learning. |
| Advanced search | Credits for deep Event Prospecting Catalog search, saved searches, lookalikes, and filtered market maps. | Analog is revealing scarce supply. |
| AI research | Credits for research memos, match reasons, market scans, Sponsor fit analysis, and renewal recommendations. | Compute and research output replace Sponsor labor. |
| Applicant unlock | First five applicants free; credits or plan tier unlock deeper applicant detail beyond the free preview. | Sponsor sees value before paying, then pays for market depth. |
| Prospecting | Credits for ICP-targeted outreach, enrichment, direct-mail logic, or contact discovery where accepted pricing exists. | Analog is doing outbound and data work. |
| Proof packet | Credits for Sponsor-ready recap, benchmark, mention analysis, renewal memo, or executive summary. | The output helps the Sponsor justify next budget. |
| Venue mention coverage | Credits for authorization setup, monitoring, benchmark, and reward administration when the Venue matters. | Broader attribution coverage is extra value. |
| Public/nonprofit packet | Credits or program fees for guided applications, reporting packets, eligibility checks, and public-safe summaries. | The work is high-control and documentation-heavy. |
| Vendor/admin workflow | Credits or program fees for payee records, W-9 status, 1099 support context, duplicate cleanup, and invoice routing. | Distributed IRL creates real back-office burden. |
| Agent MCP or API use | Credits for partner or agent workflows that query public-safe supply or prepare packets. | External automation consumes platform value and should be metered. |
| Partner attribution events | Credits, usage fees, or program pricing for approved partner apps that send pixel/API proof into Sponsor reporting. | Analog is normalizing off-platform community data into fundable Sponsor evidence. |

The best credit pricing is hard to complain about because it appears at the moment of value:

```text
You found more applicants.
You ran deeper search.
You activated another Host.
You generated a proof packet.
You asked Analog to do advanced work.
```

## Upgrade Moments

Running out of credits should feel like evidence that the Sponsor is getting value.

| Moment | Product response |
|---|---|
| Sponsor tries to unlock more applicants | Show remaining credits, explain the value, offer plan upgrade or one-time top-up. |
| Sponsor opens another active event or collab | Show projected monthly credit burn before commit. |
| Sponsor runs advanced search repeatedly | Suggest Growth plan with more included search/research credits. |
| Sponsor requests proof or renewal memo | Offer credit spend or attach it to the campaign/program fee. |
| Sponsor funds a larger program | Move to program pricing so the credit system does not feel silly for serious budgets. |

Credits can renew monthly with the plan. Add-on credits can be one-time or recurring. Test expiration carefully: expiration improves margin, but overly punitive expiration can weaken trust.

## What Credits Should Not Cover

Do not burn credits for actions that keep the marketplace honest.

| Do not meter | Why |
|---|---|
| Host applications | Charging Hosts or Sponsors per application too early weakens supply response. |
| Receipt uploads and proof tasks | Proof is the trust layer. Do not make people avoid it. |
| Basic campaign drafting | Sponsors need a low-friction path to see whether the market responds. |
| Viewing the first five applicants | The free preview is the PLG hook. |
| Sponsor payments to Hosts | Do not make the money movement itself feel taxed before payments are production-grade. |
| Host profile upkeep | Analog needs Hosts to keep records current. |
| Correcting data or opting out | Trust controls should not cost credits. |

## Fintech Revenue Model

Fintech revenue is an upside layer. It can become powerful because Analog's workflow naturally routes activation budget, local contributor capital, Sponsor funds, vendor payments, and Host spend through governed rails.

It should not become the front-of-house message too early.

| Rail | Revenue possibility | Product condition | Compliance posture |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spend card interchange | Revenue share on eligible spend card transactions. | Sponsor-funded card spend is live, controlled, frequent, and card rails are production-grade. | Issuing partner, cardholder rules, loss liability, fraud controls, rewards rules, and counsel-reviewed terms. |
| Treasury or float | Interest, yield share, or treasury economics on eligible balances backing future spend. | Funds sit in approved accounts or treasury-like structures before deployment. | Banking partner, safeguarding, deposit/investment treatment, disclosures, permissions, and no unsupported yield promises. |
| Payment processing | Margin or revenue share on contribution links, Sponsor funding, invoices, tickets, claims, checkout, or payouts. | Analog enters the payment flow because it reduces friction or improves records. | Payments partner, KYC/KYB, tax, money movement, disputes, refunds, chargebacks, and program rules. |
| Host treasury | Account-like operating rail for trusted Hosts with cards, ACH, bill pay, invoices, envelopes, and proof. | Trusted recurring Hosts need banking-quality tools beyond one-off spend cards. | Partner-bank enabled, recipient authorized, purpose-limited reporting, sensitive financial data controls. |
| Vendor/payee workflows | Fee, credit, or program pricing for record cleanup and reporting support. | Sponsors repeatedly pay many Hosts, Venues, vendors, contractors, and service providers. | Secure tax-document workflows, payment context, audit logs, and partner filing boundaries. |

The business should track:

```text
card spend volume
average funded balance
days funds sit before deployment
payment volume
processing cost
interchange share
treasury/yield share
fraud/loss rate
support burden
```

But stakeholder copy should stay disciplined:

```text
Today: Analog charges for software, credits, program work, and governed execution.
Later: Analog may earn more from cards, payments, and treasury rails as spend volume grows.
```

## Premium Host Plan

Do not advertise Host payment as a requirement. Analog should be free or low-friction for Hosts until the supply-side ROI is obvious.

A later `Analog Plus` or premium Host plan can exist as an invited power-user offer, not as the public-market promise.

| Possible feature | Good version | Risky version |
|---|---|---|
| Early campaign reads and budget forecasting | Early alerts, saved preferences, priority prep windows, and demand signals that help proven Hosts forecast likely budget before staffing, vendor, or venue commitments. | Pay-to-win access to public-good funds or Sponsor budgets. |
| Advanced pitch tools | Better media kit, rate history, grant packet, Sponsor fit search, and proof analytics. | Charging Hosts for the basic record Analog needs to keep current. |
| Opportunity sourcing | Let strong Hosts ask Analog to find Sponsors, grants, or partner matches using public-safe records. | Selling raw Sponsor contact data or bypassing Sponsor controls. |
| Sponsor placement auction guidance | Help proven high-volume Hosts package scarce Sponsor placements, set reserve ranges, understand category conflicts, compare Sponsor fit, and run a controlled auction with proof-backed placement intelligence. | Letting the highest bidder buy raw community access, override Host trust, or turn public-good funding into a pay-to-win auction. |
| Profile and proof analytics | Help Hosts understand what proof unlocks better Sponsors and larger allocations. | Turning Host reputation into opaque scoring or vanity dashboards. |
| Media and roster perks | Premium profile polish, issue consideration workflows, or operator office hours. | Making community status feel bought rather than earned. |

The rule:

```text
Host premium can help power users move faster.
It can help serious Hosts forecast and price scarce Sponsor placements.
It cannot make the marketplace feel pay-to-play.
```

## Pricing Test Plan

1. Quote every pilot with activation budget, Analog revenue, credits, sourcing/prospecting, support, fulfillment, content rights, and reserve separated.
2. Test the USD 10/month Sponsor plan as a credit wrapper, not as the whole business model.
3. Meter credits only where Analog creates clear output: active events, advanced search, AI research, applicant unlocks, prospecting, proof packets, and reporting.
4. Test fixed campaign fee versus percentage of controlled budget for heavier pilots.
5. Let Sponsors post future-dated campaigns and gauge the first 48 hours of applicant response before paywalling.
6. Show the first five applicants on free and blur or limit the rest until the first applicant is accepted.
7. Preserve the product rule that paid prospecting cannot turn on until pricing is accepted.
8. Price managed support as a bridge, and tag each support task as product, template, contractor bench, or no-go learning.
9. Track proof value by Sponsor lane: trial/content for eComm, qualified room/follow-up for SaaS/Tech, access/reporting for Civic/Public-Non-profit Funds, and compliant field allocation learning for Political Action Sponsors.
10. Track long-term fintech volume without making it the near-term promise: card spend, average balances, payment volume, processing margin, interchange share, treasury/yield share, fraud/loss, and support burden.
11. Test retainers only after a Sponsor repeats, expands, or asks Analog to operate a continuing city/category motion.
12. Test deployment credits only after real deployment. Do not treat them as cash-back, referral CAC, or Host-funded discounts.
13. Treat grant discovery as MAC leverage before revenue. The first job is to pull Hosts into Analog with one reusable application, help them find money, and convert proof into Sponsor-ready supply.

## Decision Rules

| Signal | Decision |
|---|---|
| Sponsors accept USD 10/month but do not activate | Keep the plan, but improve value moments and credit use cases. Payment alone is not product-market fit. |
| Sponsors run out of credits while doing valuable work | Offer upgrade or top-up. This is the healthy credit moment. |
| Sponsors hoard credits and avoid work | Simplify pricing, lower anxiety, or move the work into a higher plan allowance. |
| Credits confuse buyers on large programs | Switch to program fees with included credit allowance. |
| Sponsors accept activation budgets but resist platform fees | Reframe around governed spend, proof, and renewal decisions; test a fixed deliverable fee. |
| Sponsors post campaigns but do not accept applicants | Keep them in free demand-sensing mode, improve campaign fit, or tighten the applicant quality threshold before asking for payment. |
| Sponsors accept sourcing but do not launch pilots | Treat sourcing as paid market research, but tighten qualification so Analog does not become a list vendor. |
| Credits create sales motion but no deployed spend | Remove or tighten the credit. The incentive should reward controlled activation budget moving through Analog with proof attached. |
| Managed support hurts margin | Narrow the use case, improve templates/product, add contractor support, or price support explicitly. |
| Hosts feel taxed or underpaid | Move monetization to Sponsor-side fees and protect Host/operator upside. |
| Public/nonprofit buyers object to percentages | Use transparent admin or implementation fees with clear pass-through accounting. |
| Media drives demand but creates trust risk | Separate editorial coverage from paid content rights and keep sponsor approval boundaries explicit. |
| Sponsor asks for ongoing city/category support | Test a retainer only after proof of repeatable value. |
| Card, payment, or treasury economics look attractive | Validate compliance, loss, support, user trust, and partner economics before treating them as core revenue. |

## Do Not Monetize Yet

- Do not charge Hosts a public subscription before supply-side ROI is obvious.
- Do not advertise `Analog Plus` as required for Hosts to access funding.
- Do not frame controlled budgets as unrestricted wallets, payouts, or withdrawals.
- Do not rely on near-term interchange, treasury, payout, or wallet economics in stakeholder copy.
- Do not promise float, yield, treasury returns, insured accounts, or card revenue until compliant partner rails and disclosures exist.
- Do not sell raw attendee data or raw PII.
- Do not promise public-sector outcomes that the product cannot measure.
- Do not bury agency-style labor inside platform pricing.
- Do not make credits burn on trust-building actions such as proof completion, receipt upload, opt-outs, or data correction.
