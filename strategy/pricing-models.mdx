Pricing should reinforce the product thesis: Analog helps Sponsor capital find trusted IRL supply, move through governed budgets, produce proof, and earn the next allocation.

Read this after the Sponsor, Host, public/nonprofit, and high-ticket pages. Pricing only makes sense once the reader understands the money, actors, proof, and operating work being priced.

Use [MAC Versus CAC Contract](/strategy/supply-side-mac-community-champion) for the cost of acquiring a market. Use this page for the commercial structure that turns market access, funding administration, sourcing, support, proof, media, and underwriting into Analog revenue.

<Info>
Treat every number on this page as a testing band, not an official price card. The first job is to learn which structure Sponsors understand, accept, renew, and can explain internally.
</Info>

The near-term model should not depend on Host subscriptions, unrestricted balances, payout fees, interchange, treasury revenue, or generic event-production margin. Analog should charge the party with the budget and renewal decision: the Sponsor.

```text
Sponsor commitment =
  controlled activation budget
  + Analog revenue
  + optional pass-through or reserve costs
```

Controlled activation budget is Sponsor capital routed through the product for approved Host/vendor spend. It is not the same thing as Analog revenue.

The first revenue question is not only which Sponsor category pays. It is whether Analog can monetize access to trusted regional supply while learning the P&L of expansion. SaaS/tech, eComm, civic/nonprofit, and political action should be priced as different buyer lanes into the same city operating layer.

For the first six months, pricing should model MAC before CAC. Sell access to an unlocked market, city circuit, or audience layer before pretending every community has precise unit-level attribution.

```text
early pricing question:
What is access to this market worth to this Sponsor?

later pricing question:
What is this community worth based on attributed outcomes?
```

Public and government grant discovery can reduce company-funded MAC. If Analog finds outside funds for Hosts, the Host gets more reason to join, the market gets more activation capital, and Analog does not have to carry every early support cost on its own balance sheet. Keep the universal Analog application, geo-dashboard grant discovery, guided grant-specific intake, and one-click apply intent free or low-friction for Hosts until the value is obvious; monetize only through transparent Sponsor, public/nonprofit, or managed-support fees when the buyer is paying for the program.

## Pricing Principles

| Principle | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Charge Sponsors first | Sponsors pay for structured access, governed budgets, proof, and renewal confidence. |
| Protect Hosts | Hosts should see more funding, clearer scopes, less reimbursement chaos, and real operator upside. |
| Separate line items | Activation budget, platform fee, sourcing, managed support, fulfillment, content rights, reserves, and public admin fees should not blur together. |
| Replace company-funded MAC when possible | Use Sponsor-funded selective brand funds, public grants, venue rewards, and media packages to carry activation capital while Analog contributes market thesis and operating energy. |
| Price market-making explicitly | If Analog is sourcing supply, prospecting, packaging Hosts, or coordinating operators, that work needs its own fee. |
| Keep public money defensible | Public/nonprofit programs need transparent admin, implementation, monitoring, and reporting fees. |

## What To Test

| Model | What Sponsor pays for | Test band | Use when |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Campaign platform fee | campaign setup, brief, budget guardrails, collab setup, card controls, proof packet, renewal recommendation | USD 2.5k to USD 7.5k per pilot or 10% to 20% of controlled budget with a floor | Sponsor has a real activation budget and wants a controlled pilot. |
| Fund administration fee | eligibility, Host approval, spend rules, proof, simple reporting, next-dollar recommendations | 10% to 20% private; 5% to 15% transparent admin for public/nonprofit | Sponsor funds many small local actions. |
| Selective brand fund launch | market thesis, public fund design, application flow, regional dashboard placement, Host sourcing, media launch, proof loop | activation budget funded by Sponsor; Analog revenue as platform/admin/support/media line items | A market-fit enterprise Sponsor can create supply gravity before the full marketplace is liquid. |
| Community collective fund setup | local fund design, contributor records, payment-link or invoice coordination where available, application flow, Host review, spend rules, proof, contributor recap | setup/admin/support/media fees separated from pooled activation capital | A neighborhood or local scene wants 10 to 12 businesses or funders to back more gatherings through one governed campaign. |
| First deployment credit | credit toward the next campaign fee, sourcing fee, media package, or approved next allocation after spend and proof clear | test USD 1k credit after first USD 10k controlled activation budget is deployed | Sponsor needs a reason to move first real budget through Analog instead of only taking a sales call. |
| Public grant discovery support | universal Analog application, geo-dashboard grant shelf, eligibility matching, guided grant-specific intake, Host packet creation, authorized one-click apply support, report prep | free Host wedge, or transparent admin/support fee when attached to a public/nonprofit Sponsor | Public or nonprofit money can reduce Host funding gaps and create public-fund readiness. |

| Services/tools package | insurance, docs, print, local designers, vendors, rentals, proof kits, space support | fixed package, pass-through plus coordination, or support add-on | Cash alone will not unlock execution. |

| Print business unit | city magazine subscriptions, retail, wholesale, placements, event bundles, QR flows | test subscription, retail, local sponsor, and bundle pricing | Analog uses print to own supply and distribution. |
| Regional market access retainer | ongoing access to a city supply layer, local advisory context, packaged opportunities, and proof review | USD 5k to USD 25k/month, or scoped to a city/category program | Sponsor wants repeated access to AnalogNYC, AnalogSF, or a future market without funding a one-off event each time. |
| Market unlock package | defined access to a category/city circuit such as Milwaukee community groups, NYC run clubs, or SF SaaS dinners | priced by market scope, supply density, proof depth, and Sponsor urgency | Attribution is not deep enough for community-level CAC, but market access is commercially valuable. |
| Political/advocacy grassroots fund | compliant field-fund setup, national/state/district/county/city/ward event sets, Host sourcing, spend controls, proof rollups, and renewal recommendations | fund admin, managed support, and compliance-review pass-through separated from field budget | Campaign, PAC, advocacy, ballot, or donor coalition needs governed grassroots spend. |
| High-ticket underwriting | memo, allocation model, operator diligence, risk register, tranche gates, AI monitoring, contract/invoice QA, renewal memo | strategy/underwriting, program management, monitoring/reporting, and clear admin fees | Sponsor considers six- or seven-figure IRL/community investment. |
| Repeat retainer | ongoing city planning, supply bench, proof review, renewal cadence | USD 5k to USD 25k/month after repeat intent | Sponsor is running multiple campaigns, cities, or funds. |

## Sponsor Packaging

| Sponsor perspective | Package shape | Renewal proof |
|---|---|---|
| eComm Brands | Product trial funds, creator/community IRL, location benefits, dinner or ritual funds that diversify spend beyond copycat digital, AI ad sameness, and influencer ops overload. | attendance, trial, content where appropriate, sentiment, repeat participation, category learning |
| SaaS/Tech Companies | founder rooms, operator dinners, developer salons, partner/customer councils, coffee office hours, multi-city connector rooms. | attendee fit, relationship density, content assets, strategic conversations, follow-up quality, account or ecosystem learning |
| Civic/Public-Non-profit Funds | access funds, starter kits, nonprofit downstream funding, civic/corridor/social connection programs. | access created, participation, local legitimacy, spend stewardship, public-safe reporting |
| Political Action Sponsors | grassroots activation funds, national/state/district/county/city/ward event sets, volunteer hospitality, local organizer support. | compliant spend, action proof where permitted, Host reliability, local sentiment, allocation learning |

## Example Pilots

| Use case | Test structure | What to learn |
|---|---|---|
| Community micro grant pool | USD 5k to USD 25k across small Host budgets with spend rules for coffee, libations, supplies, space, print, or accessibility. | Whether small cash unlocks repeat gatherings, stronger sentiment, and a cleaner supply map. |
| Community collective fund | USD 5k to USD 50k pooled from local businesses, funders, landlords, BIDs, family offices, or aligned operators into one neighborhood campaign. | Whether local capital can replace corporate dependency, create more IRL supply, and produce proof contributors understand. |
| Strategic macro grant cohort | 3 to 10 proven Hosts at USD 1k to USD 5k/month with standard proof and services support. | Whether the best operators create outsized category, account, civic, or community value. |
| Connector dinner network | Per-dinner or coffee/breakfast budgets plus Host/connector sourcing, coordination, proof, and Sponsor follow-up. | Whether trusted local connectors can create repeatable SaaS relationship CAC with lower Sponsor labor. |
| AnalogNYC print launch | Consumer subscription and retail distribution plus tasteful eComm/local placements and fund-drop integrations. | Whether print creates inbound Hosts, subscriber FOMO, sponsor demand, and QR response. |
| Public access program | Transparent admin/support plus controlled local budgets and reporting. | Whether Analog can route public-good capital to trusted Hosts with credible proof. |
| Campaign grassroots activation fund | Sponsor field budget plus fund admin, compliance-safe proof rollups, national/state/district/county/city/ward event sets, and local Host sourcing. | Whether political and advocacy Sponsors can fund grassroots events while improving downstream allocation decisions. |
| USD 2M city/category sponsorship | Investment memo, budget allocation model, Host/operator underwriting, staged tranches, risk monitoring, media/print plan, and executive reporting. | Whether Analog can be the investment safety layer for serious IRL market-formation budgets. |

## Pricing Test Plan

1. Quote every pilot with activation budget, Analog revenue, sourcing/prospecting, support, fulfillment, content rights, and reserve separated.
2. Test fixed campaign fee versus percentage of controlled budget.
3. Preserve the product rule that prospecting cannot turn on until pricing is accepted.
4. Price managed support as a bridge, and tag each support task as product, template, contractor bench, or no-go learning.
5. Track proof value by Sponsor lane: trial/content for eComm, qualified room/follow-up for SaaS/Tech, access/reporting for Civic/Public-Non-profit Funds, and compliant field allocation learning for Political Action Sponsors.
6. Test retainers only after a Sponsor repeats, expands, or asks Analog to operate a continuing city/category motion.
7. Test credits only after real deployment. Do not treat them as cash-back, referral CAC, or Host-funded discounts.
8. Treat grant discovery as MAC leverage before revenue. The first job is to pull Hosts into Analog with one reusable application, help them find money, and convert proof into Sponsor-ready supply.

## Decision Rules

| Signal | Decision |
|---|---|
| Sponsors accept activation budgets but resist platform fees | Reframe around governed spend, proof, and renewal decisions; test a fixed deliverable fee. |
| Sponsors accept sourcing but do not launch pilots | Treat sourcing as paid market research, but tighten qualification so Analog does not become a list vendor. |
| Credits create sales motion but no deployed spend | Remove or tighten the credit. The incentive should reward controlled activation budget moving through Analog with proof attached. |
| Managed support hurts margin | Narrow the use case, improve templates/product, add contractor support, or price support explicitly. |
| Hosts feel taxed or underpaid | Move monetization to Sponsor-side fees and protect Host/operator upside. |
| Public/nonprofit buyers object to percentages | Use transparent admin or implementation fees with clear pass-through accounting. |
| Media drives demand but creates trust risk | Separate editorial coverage from paid content rights and keep sponsor approval boundaries explicit. |
| Sponsor asks for ongoing city/category support | Test a retainer only after proof of repeatable value. |

## Do Not Monetize Yet

- Do not charge Hosts a subscription before supply-side ROI is obvious.
- Do not frame controlled budgets as unrestricted wallets, payouts, or withdrawals.
- Do not rely on near-term interchange, treasury, payout, or wallet economics in stakeholder copy.
- Do not sell raw attendee data or raw PII.
- Do not promise public-sector outcomes that the product cannot measure.
- Do not bury agency-style labor inside platform pricing.
