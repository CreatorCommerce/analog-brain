Dogfooding means Analog uses Analog to build Analog.

Analog should fund city-level activity through the platform, let city leads run supply, capture customer-grade proof, and use the results to build a lifestyle brand communities want to orbit.

## City Brand Model

AnalogNYC, AnalogSF, AnalogLA, and future city brands are owned city surfaces. They are not separate companies or vanity social handles. Each city brand should:

- discover Hosts, spaces, dot connectors, and rituals
- run owned IRL through campaigns and event sets
- deploy controlled budgets
- categorize spend
- capture RSVPs, claims, attendance, content, field notes, and sentiment
- create sponsor-ready supply before the marketplace is liquid

## City Sequence

Spend the first six months solely on NYC.

| Phase | City focus | Operating goal |
|---|---|---|
| Months 0-6 | AnalogNYC | Build the first deep city brand and KPI loop. |
| Months 7-12 | AnalogSF | Add only after NYC has repeatable supply, proof, spend categories, media cadence, and agency learning. |
| Later | AnalogLA, AnalogMiami, and others | Expand only when the playbook can travel. |

The goal is depth before surface area. Brands can rise and fall quickly. Analog should build the community memory, taste, and trust that outlasts a single brand cycle.

## Product Setup

When Analog funds its own city activity, set it up like a customer campaign.

| Product object | Dogfood setup |
|---|---|
| Sponsor | Analog or the relevant city brand, such as AnalogNYC. |
| Campaign | AnalogNYC city fund, owned IRL series, category fund, or agency-backed activation. |
| Event set | A city/category lane: run clubs, founder rooms, pet-parent rituals, dinners, arts/culture, or community-builder salons. |
| Host | The city lead, trusted local Host, connector, venue, or operator. |
| Spend card | City-scoped or event-scoped budget with categories, limits, receipts, and automatic categorization. |
| Proof packet | The same proof a customer would expect: social growth, category growth, community perspective, direct response, and spend transparency. |
| Unlock | Renew the Host, expand the event set, publish the playbook, invite a sponsor, or pause the format. |

## KPI Symmetry

Analog's internal KPIs should match customer KPIs.

| KPI | What it means for Analog | Product signal |
|---|---|---|
| Social growth | Analog's city brand grows audience and pull. | Posts, recaps, shares, inbound. |
| Category growth | The IRL category gets stronger. | More participation, Hosts, rituals, and mentions. |
| Community perspective | The community sees Analog as additive. | Field notes, Host notes, sentiment, referrals. |
| Direct response | People respond to invitations. | RSVPs, claims, replies, attendance, applications. |
| Spend transparency | Analog shows where budget went. | Categories, receipts, unused funds, anomalies. |
| Supply velocity | City leads create sponsor-ready options. | Catalog records, profiles, event sets, sponsor leads. |

If Analog cannot prove these for itself in NYC, it should not promise them to sponsors at scale.

## City Lead Role

The city lead runs the supply side of the city brand: Hosts, spaces, rituals, vendors, connectors, and trust constraints.

Judge the city lead by:

- qualified supply created
- owned IRL attendance and direct response
- spend categorized cleanly
- community sentiment
- social and media yield
- category growth in chosen causeways
- repeatable Hosts and playbooks
- sponsor or agency demand created from the local network

## Agency Engagement Bridge

Before the marketplace is materially liquid, Analog can run agency engagements with brands. This is not a retreat from the platform thesis. It is a bridge that lets brands pay for Analog's network, taste, and prospecting practices while the marketplace forms.

Agency engagements should still run through Analog primitives:

- sponsor perspective
- brief
- campaign
- event set
- supply search
- collab or Host approval
- spend rules
- proof packet
- renewal or unlock recommendation

The agency motion works when it creates product learning, sponsor demand, city supply, and repeatable proof. It fails when it becomes bespoke services that do not improve the platform.

## Lifestyle Brand Thesis

Analog should become more than a procurement layer. Brands fall as fast as they come. Analog should dig deep enough into communities that Hosts, connectors, and attendees believe Analog sees the real city and makes the right kind of IRL more possible.

The test is simple:

```text
Would the community still want Analog in the room if no sponsor logo were present?
```

If yes, sponsor access becomes more valuable. If no, Analog becomes another activation vendor.

## Dogfood Loop

```text
Analog funds AnalogNYC
-> city lead runs supply
-> Hosts run owned IRL
-> spend is categorized
-> RSVPs and proof are captured
-> media grows Analog
-> category and community learning improve
-> agency engagements use the human network
-> sponsor demand funds more supply
-> product learns what to automate
```

## Guardrails

- Do not expand beyond NYC before the first six-month dogfood loop creates enough proof.
- Do not treat city brands as vanity social accounts.
- Do not let agency engagements become disconnected custom services.
- Do not count uncategorized internal spend as good enough.
- Do not chase brands so hard that Analog loses community taste.
- Do not expose communities as inventory before trust, context, and permission are clear.
- Do not use owned IRL only for vibes; every owned moment should improve product memory, proof, sponsor trust, or host supply.
