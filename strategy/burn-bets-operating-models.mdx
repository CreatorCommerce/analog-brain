Analog should treat burn as a portfolio of bets, not a fixed org chart.

Read this after [City Lead GTM Operating Model](/strategy/lean-gtm-operating-model). The GTM page explains the operating motion. This page explains how to fund, constrain, and stop that motion.

The job of burn is to prove which path can repeat: trusted supply, Sponsor demand, governed spend, proof, renewal, and product memory. Spend more when a line item makes that loop clearer. Spend less when it creates activity without better supply, proof, or Sponsor conviction.

<Info>
Separate company burn from controlled activation budget. Sponsor money that funds Hosts, vendors, venues, grants, or spend cards is not Analog revenue and should not be hidden inside operating burn.
</Info>

## Burn Rule

Every burn line should answer one question:

```text
Does this make Analog better at owning supply, deploying Sponsor capital, proving outcomes, or repeating the motion?
```

If the answer is no, it is probably vanity, premature scale, or agency drift.

For early modeling, judge burn by MAC before CAC. The first six months should ask what it costs to unlock a market, not what it costs to acquire one perfectly attributed community.

## Burn Buckets

| Bucket | What belongs here | What good looks like |
|---|---|---|
| Core product and proof | engineering, design, product ops, spend/proof workflows, files/docs, applications, pitch surfaces, reporting | fewer manual steps, cleaner Sponsor trust, stronger Host execution |
| Supply capture | pitch tools, grant/raffle hooks, government grant discovery, city prospecting, catalog enrichment, Host packaging | more qualified Hosts, connectors, venues, and operators entering Analog |
| Supply-side MAC | free tools, QR asset packs, in-kind grants, photo/video support, services/tools, market-specific artifact drops | market escape velocity, Host affection, Sponsor urgency |
| City/media layer | AnalogNYC, AnalogSF, print, newsletters, owned IRL, field capture, local distribution | city density, QR/application response, Sponsor leads, supply memory |
| GTM and Sponsor packaging | Sponsor briefs, strategic customers, selective brand-fund design, pricing tests, partnerships, customer pilots | funded campaigns, repeatable briefs, renewal evidence |
| Services/tools scaffolding | insurance, docs, print vendors, local designers, proof kits, operator benches | blockers removed and turned into product, partner workflows, or no-go rules |
| Finance, legal, and trust controls | spend review, contracts, compliance review, public/nonprofit controls, political-action guardrails | safer money movement, clearer reporting, less hidden risk |
| Reserve | runway buffer and opportunistic experiments | enough room to double down after evidence, not before |

Do not count Sponsor-controlled activation budgets as company burn. Do count company-funded grant pools, owned IRL, print tests, and pitch-tool work as burn unless a Sponsor, media buyer, or partner pays for them.

## Bet Stack

The first burn stack should stay narrow:

| Bet | Why it deserves burn | Stop or narrow when |
|---|---|---|
| Pitch tools | A useful free/single-player surface can pull Hosts and connectors into Analog before the marketplace is liquid. | It creates profiles with no Sponsor-ready proof, asks, or follow-up. |
| Grants and raffles | Visible money-on-the-table hooks can turn every customer campaign into supply capture. | Applications are low quality, eligibility is unclear, or proof does not improve selection. |
| Selective brand funds | A few market-fit enterprise funds can replace company-funded MAC with Sponsor-funded activation capital while pulling supply into Analog. | The fund is copied across markets, lacks Sponsor credibility, or produces low-quality applications. |
| Government grant discovery | Researching public money and helping Hosts become application-ready can pull supply into Analog while reducing company-funded MAC. | The work becomes custom grant consulting without reusable Host files, grant maps, proof, or Sponsor-ready supply. |
| Seasonal print and newsletters | Print gives Analog a reason to interview leaders, list events, create status, and build city density. | It becomes brand expression without QR response, applications, Sponsor leads, or catalog records. |
| Supply-side MAC | Market-specific spend can win early Hosts, create artifacts, and time Sponsor entry. | MAC becomes random perks instead of supply density, proof, or committed Sponsor funds. |
| AnalogNYC and AnalogSF | The first two markets should prove city supply ownership across multiple Sponsor lanes. | One city still feels bespoke after repeated pilots. |
| Strategic customer dogfood | Crocs, Midol, Anthropic, Shopify, and similar Sponsors can test the product while pulling supply into the network. | The customer only wants custom production and does not improve product memory. |
| Public/nonprofit path | Mission capital can become a major lane if controls make downstream funding faster and safer. | Procurement, reporting, or risk requirements become custom consulting before the core loop works. |

## Grant Desk Burn Rule

The grant desk deserves burn only when it functions as MAC replacement and government workflow discovery.

| Burn line | Good use | Bad use |
|---|---|---|
| Grant research | Mapping active funds by market, Host type, amount, deadline, eligibility, allowed uses, docs, and source URL. | Reading grant sites without turning findings into product fields, Host matches, or no-go rules. |
| Host application support | Creating reusable packets from event history, proof, photos, budgets, venue context, and fiscal sponsor status. | Writing one-off applications that do not improve the Host's Analog record. |
| Operator review | Checking eligibility, source reality, deadline, sensitive claims, submission permission, and reporting obligations. | Letting AI or junior ops submit unsupported claims without human review. |
| Post-award proof | Tracking allowed uses, spend, receipts, photos, attendance, report dates, and outcomes. | Celebrating awards without proving what the money did. |
| Agency conversion work | Turning application volume into a clear pitch around intake, fraud/waste/abuse, reporting, and attribution. | Chasing government procurement before Analog has Host-side evidence. |

Approve more grant-desk burn when the desk creates qualified supply, outside capital applied for or won, Sponsor-ready Hosts, and repeatable packet templates. Cut or narrow it when it becomes manual grant consulting with no market memory.

## Operating Models

Use different operating models for different evidence levels.

| Model | Use when | Burn pattern | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lean core | The company is still proving product conventions and first repeatable markets. | Small full-time team plus focused contractors. | product loop, Sponsor pilots, proof, operating cadence |
| City pod | A market needs local trust but not a full department. | City lead plus connector bench, media stringer, producer, and central processing. | qualified supply, field notes, catalog records, fund drops |
| Selective brand-fund bridge | A Sponsor fund can create public market demand before the marketplace is liquid. | Ex-Crocs-style enterprise partnerships contractor/hire plus founder-level support, local media, and product ops. | market-fit fund, Sponsor capital, applications, Host supply, proof, renewal thesis |
| Campaign-funded city buildout | A serious Sponsor program requires deeper local infrastructure. | Sponsor budget pays for the city apparatus where possible. | city lead, advisory board, operator bench, proof, city P&L learning |
| Agency bridge | A Sponsor will pay before software liquidity exists. | Explicit managed-support fee, scoped deliverables, product-learning tags. | brief, Host package, pilot, proof packet, reusable template |
| Media business unit | Print/newsletters create real consumer demand and supply response. | Issue budget tied to distribution, sales, QR/application response, and Sponsor leads. | profitable or learning-positive media loop |
| Public/nonprofit control lane | Mission capital needs eligibility, reporting, and fraud/waste/abuse controls. | Admin, implementation, monitoring, and reporting fees separated from pass-through funds. | governed downstream funding with public-safe proof |

Default to the lean core. Add city pods and managed support only when they create product memory or paid learning.

## Example Burn Shape

For a USD 2M to USD 2.5M round, the burn posture should preserve runway while still placing real bets. Treat these as planning bands, not a finance plan.

| Area | Planning band | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Core product, engineering, design, proof | 30% to 40% | The product must absorb repeated manual work. |
| City supply, media, and owned IRL | 20% to 30% | Focus on AnalogNYC and AnalogSF before adding cities. |
| GTM, partnerships, and Sponsor packaging | 10% to 15% | Spend against specific Sponsor hypotheses, not generic awareness. |
| Services/tools, ops, finance, legal, trust | 10% to 15% | Keep money movement, public/nonprofit, and political-action risk controlled. |
| Reserve and experiments | 10% to 20% | Hold room for the bets that start working. |

Company-funded grant pools should be small and measured. A USD 10k to USD 50k city grant pool can be justified when it creates supply, proof cases, and market learning. It is not charity budget and not a substitute for Sponsor-funded programs.

## Hiring And Contractor Rules

| Need | Default |
|---|---|
| Repeated product bottleneck | hire or dedicate product/engineering capacity |
| Local access in one market | city lead or contractor before full-time local team |
| Event-day production | contractor bench unless it repeats across pilots |
| Editorial/design output | contractor bench plus templates until media revenue or supply yield is clear |
| Selective enterprise brand funds | use the existing ex-Crocs partnerships leader profile before hiring a broad Sponsor sales team |
| Legal, compliance, insurance, public-sector navigation | specialist support scoped to live risk |
| Sponsor packaging and proof | internal owner once pilots repeat |

Hire when the work repeats and compounds. Contract when the work is local, seasonal, specialized, or still being tested. Push cost to a Sponsor when the work exists because their campaign needs it.

## Monthly Burn Review

Review burn by bet, not by department.

| Question | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Which burn created qualified supply? | applications, catalog records, pitch packages, referrals, Host meetings |
| Which burn created Sponsor demand? | briefs, paid pilots, renewals, strategic-customer follow-up |
| Which burn unlocked a market? | density of Hosts, venues, connectors, proof, artifacts, and Sponsor willingness to pay |
| Which burn got replaced by outside funds? | Sponsor funds, public grants, in-kind support, media revenue, public/nonprofit admin fees |
| Which MAC was Sponsor-funded instead of company-funded? | selective brand funds, venue rewards, Sponsor-paid perks, co-funded media, campaign-funded city work |
| Which burn improved proof? | receipts, attendance, claims, sentiment, Sponsor summaries, next-dollar decisions |
| Which burn became product memory? | new defaults, templates, fields, workflows, no-go rules |
| Which burn should stop? | activity with no supply, no proof, no Sponsor belief, or no repeat path |

The best burn creates a sharper next allocation. The worst burn creates more meetings, more custom work, and no product memory.

## Stop Rules

- Do not add a new city for coverage.
- Do not fund print that does not create supply response.
- Do not fund grants without eligibility, spend rules, proof, and renewal logic.
- Do not call spend MAC unless it helps acquire the market's supply graph.
- Do not manufacture a brand fund unless the Sponsor, market, Host value, public CTA, and proof path fit.
- Do not hide managed services inside platform pricing.
- Do not hire for work that a contractor bench can test first.
- Do not let strategic customers pull Analog into custom production with no reusable object.
- Do not treat public, nonprofit, or political action work as normal brand activation.
