## What This Is

These are founding operating docs, not generic product docs. They explain how Analog makes community-led IRL marketing fundable, measurable, and repeatable without flattening gatherings into ads or one-off event services.

Analog starts Sponsors-first because capital is the unlock.

The company separates four commercial lanes:

- eComm Brands
- SaaS/Tech Companies
- Civic/Public-Non-profit Funds
- Political Action Sponsors

Those lanes should not split the company. Analog's first strategic job is to own trusted local supply and regional intelligence. Then it works backward to the organizations that need that supply.

## Narrative Spine

Every page should ladder back to the same spine:

```text
Digital gets abundant
-> real-world trust becomes scarcer and more valuable
-> Sponsors need safe ways to deploy IRL/community capital
-> Hosts need cash, services, tools, and proof to build better gatherings
-> Analog governs the spend and captures what happened
-> authorized mentions, attendance, claims, receipts, and notes become proof assets
-> proof decides the next dollar
-> product memory becomes an IRL culture graph
-> recursive AI memory improves city, market, and Sponsor judgment
-> public-safe opportunity data makes fundable supply discoverable
```

For first-year GTM, this shows up as one focused loop across AnalogNYC and AnalogSF:

```text
own supply
-> give Hosts and connectors a reason to surface themselves
-> move Sponsor money through governed funds
-> capture proof
-> earn the next issue, fund, city allocation, or renewal
```

The summer/fall supply-capture stack has three races:

1. **Pitch tools**: help Hosts package verifiable history, social proof, community reach, and Sponsor-ready asks.
2. **Grants and raffles**: turn every Sponsor campaign into a visible money-on-the-table supply engine.
3. **Seasonal print and newsletters**: use city zines, local distribution, and newsletters to interview community leaders, list events, and build density.

On the supply side, think MAC instead of CAC.

MAC means market acquisition cost. It wins a community graph through free tools, one reusable Analog application, grant discovery, QR asset packs, first-cohort support, grant pools, in-kind support, print artifacts, and Sponsor funds timed to the market.

The free product wedge is simple: one Analog application should help a Host apply for public grants, private grants, community collective funds, selective brand funds, and Sponsor opportunities that fit their organization, community, or event.

The company-wide outcome is broader than MAC: Analog should become the only sane place for Hosts and Sponsors to run community sponsorships by designing market acquisition cost, tools, support, artifacts, spend governance, and proof into one system.

Before the full brand marketplace is liquid, use selective brand funds as the bridge:

- Analog designs the market-fit public fund.
- A Sponsor supplies the activation capital.
- The regional Host dashboard routes local Hosts toward the right money, perks, media opportunities, and proof tasks.

On the proof side, treat mentions as assets. Host and Venue mentions should become attribution coverage, benchmarks, and renewal signals instead of scattered recap screenshots.

On the network side, public-safe discoverability is part of the product bargain. Hosts, Venues, operators, connectors, and organizers should know Analog is trying to expose them to funding, partnership, sponsorship, grant, media, and custom workflow opportunities. Search, APIs, Agent MCP, and partner surfaces should respect opt-outs and private or sensitive boundaries.

On the AI side, keep the mental model simple:

- Product records are the truth.
- AI memory is a small sourced note.
- Context is the small folder an agent gets for one job.

Recursive docs and a future Market Agent should help Analog understand regional markets over time without giving every agent every private fact.

On the Sponsor UX side, the platform should feel agentic even when it is mostly UI. It should understand the session intent, show the next useful step, reveal deeper controls when needed, and keep sensitive actions behind approval. Host UX should stay simple and snappy.

The company has five motions:

1. **Owned city dogfood**: AnalogNYC and AnalogSF use Analog to run IRL, categorize spend, capture proof, and build supply.
2. **Cash-funded playbooks**: Sponsors and public funders use micro grants, macro grants, controlled budgets, and services/tools to help Hosts accomplish real community goals.
3. **Agency engagements**: brands buy access to Analog's human network before the marketplace is liquid.
4. **Market formation**: city leads, local advisory boards, grant pools, and owned IRL create sponsor-ready supply.
5. **Media, connectors, and funding**: Analog Print Mag, `The Connector`, stories, and owned gatherings make the gathering economy legible and route builders into governed funds.

## What To Believe

```text
Communities make markets. Companies support them to access them.
```

Analog exists because the next great growth channel will not be another dashboard, feed, or ad surface. It will be the real-world rooms, routes, rituals, spaces, clubs, and local operators where trust is formed before it becomes data.

Analog is the funding and proof layer for real-world growth: stronger communities for Hosts and smarter event bets for Sponsors.
