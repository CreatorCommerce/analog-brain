## Sequence 1 — Welcome: Why Analog Exists

**Audience:** New Host, claimed listing owner, or community lead.

**Goal:** Explain the mission and get the Host back to their dashboard.

**Subject:** Welcome to Analog

**Preheader:** We are building the professional layer for real-world communities.

**CTA:** Open your Analog dashboard → `{{ dashboard_url|default:'https://analog.host' }}`

**Email:**

Hi {{ first_name|default:'there' }},

Welcome to Analog.

We are building Analog because real-world communities create more value than the internet gives them credit for.

The run club, supper series, reading group, founder dinner, pop-up, studio class, volunteer crew, neighborhood ritual, or recurring hangout is not just an event. It is trust, distribution, culture, and proof that people still show up for each other in person.

Analog exists to help Hosts turn that work into a professional record.

That means:

- a better public listing for discovery
- a clearer story about who you bring together
- event history that compounds over time
- reviews, media, and proof in one place
- grant, Sponsor, and partnership readiness
- more ways to get support without carrying every cost alone

The first step is simple: keep your Analog record current.

The more complete your listing, socials, events, and support needs are, the easier it is for Analog to understand where you fit and what opportunities may be relevant.

Open your dashboard and make sure your listing tells the truth about the room you are building.

**CTA button:** Open your dashboard

— Analog

## Sequence 2 — Five Ways To Optimize Your Listing For Discovery

**Audience:** Host with a claimed or created listing.

**Goal:** Improve public-safe listing quality and Sponsor-readiness signals.

**Subject:** 5 ways to help people discover {{ listing_name|default:'your community' }}

**Preheader:** A stronger listing helps attendees, partners, and future Sponsors understand what you are building.

**CTA:** Update your listing → `{{ listing_url }}`

**Email:**

Hi {{ first_name|default:'there' }},

Your Analog listing is more than a profile. It is the public record people use to understand what your community is, who it is for, and why it matters.

Here are five quick ways to make {{ listing_name|default:'your community' }} easier to discover:

1. **Say what actually happens.**
   Lead with the real ritual: runs, dinners, workshops, markets, salons, classes, meetups, volunteering, or something else.

2. **Name who it is for.**
   Help the right people self-select. Include audience, vibe, location, cadence, and any beginner-friendly notes.

3. **Add useful links.**
   Include your website, ticket link, Instagram, newsletter, group chat, or wherever people should go next.

4. **Keep categories accurate.**
   Categories help Analog route people through search, lists, local discovery, and future opportunity matching.

5. **Show proof that the community is active.**
   Add recent events, reviews, media, or a short story about what has been happening offline.

A complete listing helps attendees find you today. It also helps Analog understand what kinds of grants, Sponsors, media opportunities, or support might fit later.

Take five minutes and tighten the listing while it is fresh.

**CTA button:** Update your listing

— Analog

## Sequence 3 — Apply For The Analog Founding Grant

**Audience:** Host with a listing or community record.

**Goal:** Drive Founding Grant application completion and explain the reusable application logic.

**Subject:** Apply for the Analog Founding Grant

**Preheader:** Tell us what support would make your next gathering easier to run.

**CTA:** Start your application → `{{ grant_url|default:'https://analog.host' }}`

**Email:**

Hi {{ first_name|default:'there' }},

If you are building a real-world community, we want to understand what would help you keep going.

The Analog Founding Grant is our way of learning what Hosts actually need: cash, space, supplies, documentation, media, insurance, design help, Sponsor fit, or another kind of support.

Your application helps us understand:

- what your community does
- who you bring together
- what you are trying to host next
- what costs or blockers are in the way
- what kind of proof, media, or track record you already have
- what support would make the biggest difference

Even when a grant is not the immediate next step, your answers can make your Analog record more useful for future funds, Sponsor opportunities, public programs, and partner support.

Think of it as one reusable application for the opportunities that may fit your community over time.

If you know what you need, apply now. If you are not sure, start with the honest version. Specific is better than polished.

**CTA button:** Start your application

— Analog

## Sequence 4 — Connect Your Socials To Prepare For Sponsors

**Audience:** Host who has not connected social accounts or has incomplete social fields.

**Goal:** Encourage social connection as proof/readiness, without overclaiming automatic sponsorship.

**Subject:** Connect your socials before Sponsor opportunities open up

**Preheader:** Social proof helps Analog understand your reach, rhythm, and community fit.

**CTA:** Connect socials → `{{ social_connect_url|default:'https://analog.host' }}`

**Email:**

Hi {{ first_name|default:'there' }},

Quick reminder: connect your socials in Analog when you can.

Sponsors do not only care about follower count. They care about whether a community is real, active, trusted, and a good fit for the moment they want to support.

Your social links can help show:

- what your gatherings look and feel like
- how often your community shows up
- what kinds of people, places, and topics surround the work
- whether a Sponsor would feel additive instead of awkward
- what public proof already exists

This helps Analog prepare better matches and avoid forcing the wrong opportunities into the wrong rooms.

You stay in control of what you share. The goal is not to turn your community into an ad channel. The goal is to make your public proof easier to understand when funding, media, or Sponsor support becomes relevant.

Connect the accounts that best represent your community.

**CTA button:** Connect socials

— Analog

## Sequence 5 — Log Events To Create A Track Record

**Audience:** Host with claimed listing; sent periodically or after inactivity.

**Goal:** Encourage event logging as professional record and proof loop.

**Subject:** Log your events so your track record can build

**Preheader:** Every gathering you add makes your Analog record more useful.

**CTA:** Add an event → `{{ events_url|default:'https://analog.host' }}`

**Email:**

Hi {{ first_name|default:'there' }},

If you hosted something recently, add it to Analog.

Your event history is one of the clearest ways to show that your community is active and worth supporting.

It helps answer simple questions:

- What do you host?
- How often does it happen?
- Where does your community gather?
- What kind of support would make the next one better?
- What proof can you show when a grant, partner, or Sponsor asks?

This does not need to be complicated. Start with the basics: name, date, location, link, and a short description.

Over time, those small updates become a track record. That record can support grant applications, Sponsor conversations, media inclusion, local discovery, and future funding decisions.

Do not let the work disappear after the event ends. Log it while the details are still easy to remember.

**CTA button:** Add an event

— Analog
