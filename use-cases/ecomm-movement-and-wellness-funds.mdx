This page is for eComm Brands whose products become more believable inside a physical ritual: running, walking, Pilates, recovery, fertility wellness, hydration, supplements, apparel, shoes, or performance accessories.

Read this after [MVP Use Cases](/strategy/mvp-use-cases). It is the first applied proof case because movement and wellness have recurring rituals, clear use of funds, and obvious Sponsor fit.

This is a proof case, not the whole strategy. Use [Cash Funding Playbooks](/strategy/cash-funding-playbooks) for the broader doctrine: Analog should build around cash-based Host enablement across many community families, not only movement communities and not product-only gifting. Use [eComm Brand Landscape](/references/ecomm-brand-landscape) for the buyer reason: copycat culture, AI ad sameness, expensive influencer access, and creator ops overload make IRL diversification more urgent.

Movement and wellness work because they make the eComm problem concrete.

```text
digital creative gets copied
-> AI ads make more brands look competent
-> influencer programs get expensive and heavy
-> the brand needs a channel with physical trial, memory, and trust
-> trusted Hosts make the product believable in a real ritual
```

The core motion is:

```text
Sponsor budget -> movement or wellness Event set -> trusted Host -> class, club, or event -> product benefit + proof -> renew the best ritual
```

Movement community apps can become later partner surfaces for this motion. A platform like MobiusFit may own the supportive group experience, movement posts, and community motivation, while Analog owns the Sponsor funding, proof, access-fund, and renewal logic behind a campaign. Treat this as a target category example, not a confirmed integration.

## Lululemon-Style Community Wellbeing Grant

The [Lululemon Gives Community Wellbeing Grant](https://grants.lululemon.com/s/?language=en_US#time) shows how a large eComm/wellness brand can think like a funder, not only a campaign buyer.

The useful pattern is registration-first. Lululemon asks organizations to register before the full application, uses eligibility and verification, then invites eligible organizations to apply. The grant story is community-led wellbeing through movement, with locally led organizations as the supply side.

Analog should learn from that pattern, then make it IRL-native:

| Lululemon-style grant move | Analog movement/wellness version |
|---|---|
| Annual public grant page | Sponsor fund page for a city, category, or movement circuit. |
| Registration before application | Lightweight Host eligibility gate before asking for the full pitch, budget, and proof plan. |
| Organization verification | Host, fiscal sponsor, venue, business, or operator verification, depending on the fund. |
| Wellbeing-through-movement thesis | Event sets such as run clubs, walk crews, recovery mornings, community fitness, sober social, or accessible movement. |
| Operating budget and geography screen | Market-fit, Host-size, and allocation logic for NYC, SF, or another priority city. |
| Invitation to apply | Sponsor-safe shortlist, missing-field prompts, and guided application deltas. |
| Grant award | Approved spend, services/tools, card or invoice rail, and clear proof obligations. |
| Corporate impact story | Proof packet showing access created, participation, recurring ritual strength, spend stewardship, sentiment, and renewal recommendation. |

The Analog difference: the fund should not stop at selecting organizations. It should fund specific IRL activations, series, Hosts, spaces, and rituals, then show which local community loops deserve the next dollar.

## Nike-Style Play And Sport Fund

The [Nike Community Impact Fund](https://about.nike.com/en/mission/initiatives/nike-community-impact-fund) shows a different support model: a global sport brand uses community impact language, local employee proximity, nonprofit and school eligibility, regional grant partners, and trained teammate review to fund access to play and sport.

Analog should treat this as a corporate community impact fund, not as a normal brand activation.

| Nike-style move | Analog movement/wellness version |
|---|---|
| Support kids' access to play and sport. | Event sets for youth sport, rec leagues, schoolyard play, park-based activity, community coaching, beginner movement, and accessible sport. |
| Prioritize communities where employees live and work. | City or regional fund windows tied to employee-dense markets, stores, offices, or trusted local partner maps. |
| Invite nonprofits and schools. | Eligibility gates for schools, nonprofits, fiscal sponsors, youth-serving operators, community sport groups, and partner-run programs. |
| Use trained employee reviewers. | Local reviewer notes, city lead context, advisory input, and Sponsor teammate recommendations that explain community fit. |
| Work through regional grant administrators. | Partner records, fiscal sponsor status, reporting calendars, payment context, and files/docs for grant administration. |
| Disburse grants after review. | Award dates, spend windows, approved categories, card, invoice, or vendor rails, and closeout tasks. |
| Tell a play and sport impact story. | Proof packet showing access created, participation, recurring play, trusted operator, spend integrity, safeguards, and public-safe quotes or photos where permitted. |

This matters because many eComm Sponsors will not describe the goal as "IRL marketing." They may describe it as support, access, community impact, youth sport, school partnerships, or making movement possible. Analog should keep the Sponsor's support language intact, then turn it into governed local execution and proof.

## On Running-Style Run Club Fund

Use this when the Sponsor wants to grow a movement category, not just place a logo on a race.

| Component | Setup |
|---|---|
| Campaign | Run Club Performance Fund. |
| Event sets | NYC run clubs, women's run crews, beginner 5K groups, race-prep crews, recovery mornings, walk/run hybrids. |
| Host budget | Coach or Host stipend, hydration, coffee, bag check, route support, photographer, recovery perk, print/signage, affordable space, product demo when useful, or post-run breakfast. |
| Product role | Shoe try-on, apparel trial, recovery item, electrolyte or nutrition sample, QR claim, or local retail tie-in. |
| Proof | RSVPs, attendance, repeat runners, product trials, claims, content, Host note, sentiment, authorized Venue mentions when a space matters, and which clubs deserve renewal. |

Good first tests:

- sponsor a recurring neighborhood run without changing its identity
- fund a beginner-friendly training series before a local race
- pair a run with a recovery studio or coffee stop
- give top Hosts monthly allocations instead of one giant event budget

Avoid turning the club into a forced brand activation or content farm. The Sponsor should make the run better, safer, easier, or more generous.

## Bird&Be-Style Pilates And Fertility Wellness Fund

Use this when the Sponsor sells a sensitive wellness product and needs trust, education, and permission before product trial.

| Component | Setup |
|---|---|
| Campaign | Fertility Wellness And Movement Fund. |
| Event sets | Pilates classes, fertility education circles, nutrition workshops, low-stress movement mornings, creator-led wellness meetups. |
| Host budget | Studio rental, instructor fee, educator fee, tea or light hospitality, take-home kits, printed education, photo only where appropriate. |
| Product role | Optional take-home sample, QR education hub, expert Q&A, partner offer, or post-event follow-up. |
| Proof | Attendance, opt-ins, questions asked, kit claims, qualitative sentiment, Host trust note, and repeat class demand. |

This use case needs extra care:

- make product trial optional
- avoid medical claims unless approved
- let Hosts set privacy norms for photos, attendee lists, and story capture
- prefer education, support, and community over performance marketing language
- use proof that protects attendee dignity

## Product Setup

| Product object | Setup |
|---|---|
| Sponsor | eComm Brand with a product-adjacent ritual. |
| Campaign | Category fund, such as Run Club Performance Fund, Community Wellbeing Grant, Play And Sport Access Fund, or Fertility Wellness And Movement Fund. |
| Event sets | Segment by city, Host type, ritual, audience, employee and community market, and proof standard. |
| Hosts | Clubs, instructors, studios, creators, educators, schools, nonprofits, rec leagues, youth-serving operators, local operators, or venue partners with real audience trust. |
| Host events | Small recurring gatherings with a clear benefit, product role, spend cap, and proof requirement. |
| Spend card | Host stipend, studio or venue fee, hospitality, route support, recovery perk, photo, signage, education materials, or local partner costs. |
| Registration gate | Host eligibility, entity type, fiscal sponsor if needed, operating budget band, geography, contact, consent, and missing docs. |
| Local review context | City lead, advisory, or Sponsor teammate notes where the fund depends on employee proximity or community knowledge. |
| Proof packet | Attendance, claims, content where appropriate, Host note, authorized Host/Venue mentions, spend receipts, sentiment, access created, play and sport participation, safeguards, and renewal recommendation. |

## Proof Standard

The first proof packet should answer:

- Did the right community show up?
- Did the Sponsor make the ritual better?
- Did product trial or education feel natural?
- Did the Host want to repeat?
- Did spend stay inside approved rules?
- If youth, schools, or community sport are involved, did the activation preserve safeguards and create real access to play or movement?
- Did the activation create a growth signal the brand could not get from another AI ad test or influencer post?
- Which Host, city, or ritual deserves the next allocation?
