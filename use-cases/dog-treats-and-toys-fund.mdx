A dog treats and toys company is an eComm Brand Sponsor. The Analog job is to help the Sponsor deploy budget into trusted pet-parent supply, govern spend, capture product-trial proof, and decide which Hosts, cities, and rituals deserve more budget.

This should start as a small fund, not a giant festival. The Sponsor already has products people can understand quickly. The hard part is finding the local pet-parent contexts where trial feels useful, welcomed, safe, and repeatable.

```text
Sponsor budget -> city or event set -> trusted pet-parent Hosts -> governed spend + product kits -> proof -> renew the best Hosts
```

## Use Case

The Sponsor makes dog treats and toys. Analog takes the Sponsor on as a customer and deploys the activation budget through controlled host-side opportunities.

The best first motion is a pet-parent ritual fund across one or two dense cities. The fund backs small activations where dog owners already gather or already trust the Host.

Good first Hosts include:

- dog walkers with neighborhood trust
- trainers and behavior coaches
- groomers
- rescue and adoption organizers
- dog-friendly cafes, hotels, residential communities, and coworking spaces
- dog parks, private dog runs, and park stewardship groups
- dog shows, breed meetups, agility groups, and competition organizers
- local pet photographers
- dog park community organizers
- creator or ambassador Hosts with real pet-parent credibility
- run, walk, or recovery groups where dogs naturally participate

The Sponsor should not ask each Host to become a full event producer. The Host should be funded to make an existing pet-parent behavior better.

## Portfolio Mix

For a first customer deployment, split the budget across three tiers.

| Tier | Purpose | Campaign types | Allocation guidance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Recurring rituals | Find repeatable Hosts and low-cost product trial | Pack walks, trainer sessions, dog-friendly cafe mornings, new dog parent kits | 50% to 60% of controlled activation budget |
| Anchor sponsorships | Test higher-visibility places and organized audiences | Dog park stewardship, dog shows, agility events, rescue boosts | 25% to 35% of controlled activation budget |
| Lighthouse moment | Create one story worth publishing after early proof | Pet creator field day, seasonal drop, mini dog festival, best-Host showcase | 10% to 20% of controlled activation budget |

Do not lead with the lighthouse unless the Sponsor already has strong local supply, clear product logistics, and a proof standard. The first job is to find which Hosts and settings deserve more budget.

## Dog Park Sponsorships

Dog park sponsorships can work, but they need the right shape. Treat them as place stewardship first and brand visibility second.

Good dog park spend includes:

- cleanup and waste bag supplies
- hydration station or water support
- shade, seating, or comfort upgrades where permitted
- community event support
- trainer pop-ins or behavior Q&A
- sample and toy trial table outside congested play areas
- small donations to park stewards, friends groups, or approved partners
- clear signage that explains what the Sponsor made possible

Use a dog park campaign when the Sponsor can improve the park experience without creating safety issues or forcing product trial inside crowded off-leash areas.

Avoid dog park campaigns when permits, public-space rules, insurance, or sampling safety are unclear. For public parks, confirm the approved partner, permitted brand presence, and product trial boundaries before spend is deployed.

## Dog Shows And Competition Sponsorships

Dog shows, agility events, breed meetups, and competitions are useful because the audience is high-intent. These owners already care about training, rewards, behavior, enrichment, and dog-specific product fit.

Good dog show spend includes:

- sponsorship fee or vendor table
- prize packs for winners or participants
- handler hospitality
- sealed treat samples and toy demos
- QR claim or show-only offer
- expert-led mini demo with trainer or handler approval
- content rights only when explicitly agreed

Use dog shows to learn which claims resonate with serious dog owners: training reward, clean ingredients, durability, enrichment, size fit, breed fit, travel, gifting, or subscription.

Avoid judging dog shows only by raw attendance. The better proof is qualified owner density, product credibility, organizer relationship, claim rate, and whether the Sponsor should repeat in a circuit.

## Spend Rules

Keep controlled activation budget separate from Sponsor product inventory.

Approved spend should usually include:

- Host stipend or operator fee
- venue, cafe, or space cost
- water, waste bags, cleanup supplies, or basic safety supplies
- photographer or content support when content is part of proof
- trainer, groomer, or rescue partner fee
- adoption or local partner donation when mission-aligned
- printing, QR signage, or sample display materials

Avoid using the budget for generic influencer fees, large production builds, broad paid ads, or expensive festival infrastructure until small Host-led formats prove renewal value.

## Product Trial Rules

Product trial must be owner-permission first.

For treats:

- list ingredients and dietary guidance clearly
- require the owner to choose whether their dog receives the treat
- do not let Hosts feed unknown dogs without owner consent
- keep sealed take-home samples available for dogs with sensitivities or distracted environments
- capture which treat types owners cared about, such as training, reward, digestion, allergies, size, or flavor

For toys:

- match toys to dog size and chew style
- give supervision guidance
- avoid unsafe off-leash toy demos in crowded areas
- capture whether the toy created play, owner interest, content, or take-home demand

The proof should show that the Sponsor created a useful pet-parent experience, not that dogs were pushed through a sampling stunt.

## Proof Standard

The first proof packet should answer:

- Did the right pet parents show up?
- Did owners accept samples, claims, or take-home products?
- Did dogs engage with the treat or toy safely and naturally?
- Did the Host feel the Sponsor made the gathering better?
- Did the community welcome the Sponsor presence?
- Did the spend stay within approved categories and limits?
- Which Host should receive the next or larger allocation?
- Which city, neighborhood, or Host type should become the next event set?

Good enough first proof:

| Proof lane | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Spend stewardship | Card transactions, receipts, spend note, and unused funds. |
| Attendance | Estimated people and dogs, RSVP/check-in/claim count when available, and Host note on audience fit. |
| Product trial | Samples or toys distributed, claims scanned, take-home kits, and qualitative owner feedback. |
| Content and sentiment | Photos, short recap, social tags when natural, and comments from the Host or attendees. |
| Renewal logic | Which Host, format, and event set should get the next dollar. |

## Use When

Use this motion when:

- the Sponsor has budget to deploy beyond free product
- product trial, owner feedback, content, or claim capture matters
- the Sponsor is willing to fund Host upside, not only ask for exposure
- Hosts already have trusted pet-parent access
- safety and product guidance can be handled cleanly
- the motion can become a repeat city, Host-type, or life-stage playbook

## Avoid

Do not use this motion when:

- the Sponsor only wants product seeding with no Host budget
- the activation depends on a large, one-off festival before small formats are tested
- treat ingredients, dog size, toy safety, or owner consent cannot be explained clearly
- the Host has reach but no real pet-parent trust
- proof depends on intrusive attendee data or forced social posting
- animal welfare or community sentiment would be compromised for content

## First Pilot Recommendation

Run a four to six week pilot in one or two cities.

Set up five event sets:

1. Neighborhood dog walks.
2. Trainer-led enrichment or play.
3. Dog park stewardship.
4. Dog shows or agility events.
5. Dog-friendly local commerce or new dog parent kits.

Source 3 to 5 Hosts per event set, approve the strongest 10 to 15 total Hosts, fund one small activation per Host, and renew only the top performers. Keep at least half the budget in recurring rituals so the pilot does not overfit to one large event. The renewal decision should be based on spend stewardship, owner permission, product trial, Host quality, community sentiment, and whether the format can repeat with less operator work.
