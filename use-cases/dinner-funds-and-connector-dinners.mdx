Dinner is one of the cleanest Analog formats because the value is obvious to every side: a sponsor funds a trusted host, the host creates a room people actually want to be in, the community receives real hospitality, and proof can be captured through spend, attendance, photos, and follow-up.

This page is canonical for the two dinner use cases supplied by the founder:

1. John Deere funds a trusted Iowa restaurant owner to bring farmers together.
2. Tapcart funds eCommerce legends and dot connectors to run dinners across markets.

## Use Case 1: John Deere Farmer Dinner Fund

John Deere sponsors an Iowa restaurant owner with strong community ties. The sponsor provides a $5k balance that the restaurant owner can spend from to cover dinners for farmers in the community.

The restaurant owner does not need to run a complex campaign. The motion can start with one trusted relationship:

```text
"Hey John, I got some funds from John Deere to cover a few dinners for farmers.
If you have friends you would like to treat, bring them out.
I would love to host them and cover the cost thanks to John Deere."
```

The farmer brings other farmers. The restaurant hosts the dinner. The owner covers the bill from the John Deere-funded card. Everyone knows John Deere made the dinner possible.

<Info>
This is IRL hosting applied to a restaurant growing its business by bringing a real farming community together.
</Info>

| Layer | What happens |
|---|---|
| Sponsor | John Deere funds a local restaurant dinner balance. |
| Host | A restaurant owner with farmer trust invites through existing relationships. |
| Guest motion | A trusted farmer customer brings other farmers to dinner. |
| Spend | The owner uses the John Deere-funded card to cover the dinner. |
| Community value | Farmers receive hospitality, a reason to gather, and a sponsor-backed moment that feels generous instead of extractive. |
| Host value | The restaurant earns revenue, deepens existing relationships, and may win new regular customers. |
| Sponsor value | John Deere creates emotional goodwill inside a real farmer network and sees proof that spend went through a restaurant-hosted dinner. |

## Why It Is A Strong Analog Fit

This motion works because it does not manufacture community. It uses trust that already exists.

| Fit signal | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Strong local host | The restaurant owner already has community permission and daily operating capacity. |
| High-trust guest graph | One respected farmer can credibly bring others without a generic invite blast. |
| Simple spend path | A dinner can be paid with one card charge, restaurant receipt, and event note. |
| Emotional sponsor presence | John Deere is not buying impressions; it is making a meaningful evening possible. |
| Practical host upside | The restaurant gets real revenue and customer development, not exposure. |
| Light proof | Receipt, host note, attendee count, optional photo, and optional social tag are enough for a first pilot. |

## Product Setup

| Product object | Setup |
|---|---|
| Sponsor | John Deere or a John Deere dealer/co-op entity. |
| Campaign | Farmer Dinner Fund: Iowa restaurant-hosted dinners. |
| Host | Restaurant owner with strong farmer community ties. |
| Host event | Dinner for farmers, with date, location, guest count, and spend cap. |
| Spend card | $5k balance, restaurant/food category, event-scoped approval. |
| Proof packet | Receipt, amount spent, estimated attendance, host note, optional photo, optional tag, and whether new customers came in. |
| Unlock | Fund another dinner, expand to more trusted restaurant hosts, add dealer co-hosting, or stop if trust/proof is weak. |

## Proof Standard

Do not overbuild measurement for the first version. The clean proof is:

- card spend went through the restaurant
- dinner happened
- farmers attended
- sponsor was understood as the reason the meal was covered
- host can say whether the room created real relationship value
- restaurant can report whether new or lapsed customers came in

Photos and tags are useful but not required. In rural or relationship-heavy markets, social tagging may be less practical than a host note, receipt, and trusted follow-up.

## Use Case 2: Tapcart eCommerce Connector Dinner Network

Tapcart already sees major ROI from events, but events are hard to run directly. They require internal travel, production work, audience building, local marketing, and a constant need for novel new names.

The Analog alternative is to fund eCommerce legends and dot connectors in multiple markets. Instead of Tapcart sending a team member everywhere, trusted local operators host dinners for the right eCommerce founders, operators, agency leaders, and ecosystem people.

Markets can include Denver, Minneapolis, Chicago, London, and other cities where the eCommerce community has real local density.

| Layer | What happens |
|---|---|
| Sponsor | Tapcart funds a dinner network for eCommerce operators and leaders. |
| Host | Local eCommerce legend, agency connector, operator, founder, or community builder. |
| Guest motion | The host personally curates the room and invites people the sponsor would struggle to reach cold. |
| Spend | Dinner costs are covered through event-scoped budget or spend card. |
| Community value | Operators get a useful room, not a sales event with weak attendance. |
| Host value | Dot connectors get funded and recognized for real ecosystem labor. |
| Sponsor value | Tapcart creates authentic local impressions, learns new markets, and builds relationship density without running every event itself. |

## Why It Is A Strong Analog Fit

This motion turns field marketing from a heavy internal production burden into a distributed trusted-host network.

| Fit signal | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Clear ICP | Tapcart can name the people it wants in the room: eCommerce founders, operators, agencies, and ecosystem leaders. |
| Trusted host advantage | Local legends can fill better rooms than generic paid promotion or cold outbound. |
| Lower labor per dinner | Tapcart funds and governs the motion while hosts handle local curation and hospitality. |
| Market expansion | Dinners can run in many cities without needing a full-time field marketer in each one. |
| Strong proof loop | Attendance quality, account fit, follow-up meetings, referrals, and market notes can tell Tapcart where to double down. |
| Connector prestige | The best hosts become known as funded ecosystem builders, not unpaid community labor. |

## Product Setup

| Product object | Setup |
|---|---|
| Sponsor | Tapcart as a Tech Company with an eCommerce ecosystem motion. |
| Campaign | eCommerce Connector Dinner Network. |
| Event sets | Denver, Minneapolis, Chicago, London, and other city lanes. |
| Hosts | eCommerce legends, dot connectors, agencies, operators, founders, and community builders. |
| Host events | Small curated dinners by market, each with guest thesis and spend cap. |
| Spend card | Dinner budget by host event, with meal/category restrictions and receipt capture. |
| Proof packet | Guest list quality, account fit, photos if appropriate, host notes, follow-up meetings, market learnings, and next-city recommendation. |
| Unlock | Fund more dinners with the best hosts, expand city lanes, create repeat salons, or redesign the guest thesis. |

## Proof Standard

Tapcart should not judge this only by attendance count. The better proof is:

- did the host get the right people in the room?
- did Tapcart create authentic goodwill in a market it would otherwise have to travel into?
- did the dinner produce qualified follow-up, partner signal, customer insight, or ecosystem credibility?
- did the host run it with less sponsor labor than a Tapcart-operated event?
- did the market show enough density to fund another dinner?

## Shared Dinner Pattern

Both dinner motions follow the same underlying pattern:

```text
Sponsor funds a trusted host -> host curates the room -> spend is governed -> community receives hospitality -> sponsor gets proof -> best hosts unlock more budget
```

The strategic insight is that Analog should not only help sponsors run events. It should help sponsors find and fund the people who already know how to create the right room.

## Use When

Use dinner funds when:

- the sponsor wants trusted access, not generic awareness
- a host already has real community permission
- the room can be curated through relationships
- dinner spend is easy to govern and prove
- the sponsor is willing to be generous without turning the night into a pitch
- the result can teach the pipeline which host, market, or community deserves more budget

## Avoid

Do not use this motion when:

- the sponsor wants a captive sales pitch
- the host is only a vendor, not a trusted connector
- the room cannot be filled without heavy paid marketing
- the sponsor cannot tolerate light-touch attribution
- the budget does not create real hospitality or host upside
- the proof standard requires more tracking than the social context can bear
