Creator-led IRL is the safest first bridge from digital trust to real-world sponsorship.

## When To Use This

Use this play when:

- the sponsor already trusts a creator, affiliate, ambassador, customer, or community
- the creator has product love or category authority
- the activation can become a real community moment, not a shallow meet-and-greet
- the sponsor wants content, attendance, trial, category growth, or proof

## Setup

1. Define the sponsor objective.
2. Pick the creator or community cohort.
3. Write the community angle: what people are showing up to do together.
4. Create or choose a campaign.
5. Accept or create the host/collab.
6. Create the host event.
7. Attach sponsor budget and spend card.
8. Confirm proof lanes before money is used.

## Brief Checklist

Include:

- sponsor goal
- host or creator role
- event concept
- attendee benefit
- approved spend categories
- content expectations
- disclosure expectations when the creator receives value
- proof requirements
- renewal or unlock condition

## Good Examples

- cookware sponsor funds a creator-hosted cooking class
- running brand funds a creator-led recovery morning
- app sponsor funds a founder breakfast with a trusted community operator
- wellness brand funds a creator x run club collaboration

## Bad Examples

- sponsor sends product with no budget, proof, or host incentive
- creator appearance has no community angle
- spend cannot be watched
- host has no repeat path
- the event exists only for content extraction
