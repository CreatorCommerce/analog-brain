Location activations expand Analog beyond discrete events. A venue, studio, cafe, gym, bowling alley, restaurant, hotel, retail space, or entertainment location can become a sponsor-funded access surface.

## When To Use This

Use this motion when a sponsor can make a physical experience cheaper, better, or easier to attend.

Examples:

- 10 percent off bowling tonight
- sponsor-funded free class spots
- samples at check-in
- discounted creator or community night
- sponsor-funded venue series
- coffee shop claim flow for a local tech community

## Object Shape

```text
Sponsor campaign -> experience or event set -> location activation -> offer -> QR or page claim -> proof -> renewal
```

## Setup Checklist

1. Define the sponsor objective.
2. Identify the location and owner.
3. Confirm the consumer benefit.
4. Confirm rules: capacity, timing, staff, redemption, brand safety, insurance, and data permissions.
5. Create the sponsor campaign.
6. Create or link the host/space owner.
7. Build the activation brief.
8. Prepare QR, print, hosted page, or manual claim flow.
9. Track redemptions and proof.
10. Decide whether to renew, repeat, or expand.

## Guardrails

- Make the benefit clear to the consumer.
- Do not expose raw attendee or customer lists broadly.
- Treat geofencing or location proof as secondary and consented.
- Do not let the sponsor make the place feel worse.
- Report consumer value created alongside sponsor proof.
