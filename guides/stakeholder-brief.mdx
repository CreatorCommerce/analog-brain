Analog is being built from a simple premise: as digital creation gets cheaper and more abundant, trusted real-world presence becomes more valuable.

These are the founding operating docs for turning that premise into a company. Use this page as the shared narrative across internal onboarding, investor conversations, early sponsor calls, host recruiting, city lead work, and partner scoping.

<Info>
Analog turns sponsor budget into governed IRL market growth: trusted hosts get funded, sponsors get evidence, and the company learns which real-world communities are strongest candidates for the next allocation.
</Info>

## The Company Line

Analog is the operating system for sponsor participation in the gathering economy.

The market is not waiting for more event software. Communities, hosts, creators, spaces, city leads, and sponsors already exist. What is missing is the operating layer that decides where sponsor money should go, deploys it with guardrails, captures what happened, and informs the next allocation without rebuilding every activation from scratch.

```text
Sponsor demand -> trusted host supply -> governed spend -> proof -> renewal or unlock
```

The long-term opportunity is larger than sponsorship workflow. Analog is building the operating memory, funding workflows, trust graph, proof system, IRL culture data layer, and media surface for a more vibrant IRL economy.

| Stakeholder | What Analog is to them | What they should trust |
|---|---|---|

| City leads | A field operating role that turns local trust into sponsor-ready options, media, and product intelligence. | Every intro, interview, and event should route to a catalog record, profile, package, proof asset, fund idea, or asset-need note. |
| Partners | A sponsorship and proof workflow they can embed into their own community, creator, venue, club, or ticketing surface. | Analog can power the funding logic while partners keep their product surface, audience, and customer relationship. |

## The 12-Month Proof

Analog should not try to win the entire IRL economy at once. It should show that a lean team can find causeways where the market already wants to move, then turn those causeways into product, proof, media, and repeatable GTM.

| Proof area | What good looks like |
|---|---|
| Sponsor motion | eComm Brands, Tech Companies, and Public/Non-profit Funds each have clear use cases, proof needs, and budget logic. |
| Supply velocity | City leads can produce qualified host-side options quickly without full local teams. |
| Spend integrity | Sponsor money is scoped, watched, documented, and tied to approved activation work. |
| Proof quality | Sponsors believe the evidence enough to renew, unlock more supply, or refer. |
| Host affection | Hosts and connectors feel seen, paid, respected, and more powerful because Analog exists. |
| Product learning | Repeated friction turns into reusable campaigns, event sets, host events, spend cards, proof surfaces, profiles, catalog records, or fund workflows. |
| Media gravity | `The Connector`, social, print, YouTube, and owned IRL pull more people into the tent. |

## How To Talk About Analog

| Say | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Analog helps sponsors fund trusted real-world communities with governance and proof. | Analog is just an event marketplace. |
| Analog starts with sponsor budget and works backward to the right host-side supply. | Analog creates random events and then looks for sponsors. |
| Analog turns community trust into fundable, measurable, repeatable work. | Analog turns community builders into generic influencers. |
| Analog is testing causeways where sponsor demand, city supply, media, and product proof compound. | Analog can support every IRL use case equally right now. |
| Analog uses media and owned IRL to make the market legible and recruit supply. | Analog publishes content as a side project. |

## Operating Standard

When a stakeholder asks what progress means, answer with evidence:

- which sponsor perspective is moving
- which host-side supply became qualified
- what product object improved
- what proof was captured
- what renewal, unlock, or no-go decision became clearer
- what city, category, or causeway became easier to repeat

If a workstream cannot answer those questions, tighten it before it becomes company narrative.
