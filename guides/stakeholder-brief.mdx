Analog helps organizations participate in the IRL economy by sourcing and funding trusted Hosts and community gatherers. Hosts turn hidden balance sheets into income; Sponsors turn real-world participation into brand or mission growth; proof guides the next dollar.

These are the founding operating docs for turning that premise into a company. Use this page as the shared narrative across internal onboarding, investor conversations, early Sponsor calls, Host recruiting, city lead work, and partner scoping.

If you are reading the docs in order for the first time, use [Reading Path](/guides/reading-path) after this page.

<Info>
Analog turns Sponsor capital into community growth for Hosts, brand or mission growth for Sponsors, and proof for the next dollar invested.
</Info>

Analog is intentionally broader than any single community vertical or product-gifting model. Run clubs are one useful proof case, but the company should build around cash-based Host enablement: micro grants, macro grants, services/tools, print/publication, and public-money programs that make community assets easier to fund, prove, and renew.

Analog should also be broader than any single upstream Sponsor category. SaaS/tech, eComm, civic/nonprofit, and political action keep pointing toward overlapping communities and IRL leaders. The first company-building bet is to own supply: broadly, and in the markets that shape culture by category, such as SF for SaaS and NYC for brands.

The summer/fall plan is tactical: build Host-side pitch tools, launch Sponsor-backed grants and raffles, and publish seasonal city print/newsletters that pull more Hosts, connectors, venues, and operators into the network. This is CAC as an overhead bet, not unit-driven referral CAC. Analog should use small experiments and micro-investments to find the rooms, scenes, and operators that can compound.

The sharper supply-side frame is MAC: market acquisition cost. Analog should win key markets with free tools for everyone, starting with one reusable Analog application that helps Hosts apply for public grants, private grants, community collective funds, selective brand funds, and Sponsor opportunities. The grant experience should feel guided: one core application, grant-specific follow-up questions only when needed, background packet preparation, and Host approval before submission. Then layer designed support for high-signal Hosts: QR asset packs, photo/video, social/newsletter/print inclusion, grant pools, in-kind support, insurance, government grant discovery, Host-authorized application support, and brand funds that make sponsorship easier to do through Analog than around it.

Supply creation should not stop at discovering existing events. Analog can generate supply by funding starter rituals, commissioning missing operators, activating venues, publishing fund drops, launching community collective funds, and turning public or Sponsor demand into RFEs/RFCs. The strategic effect is pull: once new supply gets funded and proven through Analog, established Hosts and Venues move closer because Analog is where future funding is being deployed.

The broader company goal is to make Analog the only sane place for Hosts and Sponsors to run community sponsorships. That requires more than one marketplace screen. It requires market acquisition cost, Host tools, Sponsor controls, public and brand funds, local artifacts, proof, and renewal logic to compound together.

Before a full brand marketplace exists, Analog should close a few selective brand funds where fund-market fit is obvious. The Sponsor supplies activation capital; Analog supplies the market thesis, distribution, Host intake, governance, proof, and regional dashboard placement. This is how MAC can use Crocs-style, Midol-style, Baden-style, Tapcart-style, or Anthropic/Shopify-style funds without putting every early dollar on Analog's balance sheet.

The sharper proof frame is mention assets. In IRL, authorized Host and Venue mentions are not throwaway recap material. They help show who moved the room, which space amplified the moment, and what the Sponsor can underwrite next.

For public and nonprofit programs, Analog helps capital reach the edge. Agencies, foundations, NGOs, and nonprofits can keep the mission and accountability upstream while using Analog to fund downstream Hosts, community builders, small businesses, spaces, and local operators who can create participation.

For larger sponsorships, Analog becomes an underwriting layer. Once Analog knows which communities, Hosts, and playbooks work, larger Sponsors can use Analog to safely deploy six- or seven-figure IRL budgets through investment memos, allocation models, risk registers, proof gates, contract/invoice review, AI monitoring, and renewal recommendations.

## The Company Line

Analog is the financial infrastructure for Sponsor participation in the gathering economy.

The market is not waiting for more event software. Communities, Hosts, creators, spaces, city leads, and Sponsors already exist. What is missing is the funding layer that decides where Sponsor money should go, deploys it with guardrails, captures what happened, and informs the next allocation without rebuilding every activation from scratch.

```text
Sponsor demand -> trusted Host supply -> governed spend -> proof -> renewal or unlock
```

The long-term opportunity is larger than sponsorship workflow. Analog is building the operating memory, funding workflows, trust graph, proof system, IRL culture data layer, and media surface that make community-led IRL safe, measurable, and repeatable.

## The 12-Month Proof

Analog should not try to win the entire IRL economy at once. It should show that a lean team can find repeatable paths where the market already wants to move, then turn those paths into product, proof, media, and repeatable GTM.

| Proof area | What good looks like |
|---|---|
| Sponsor motion | eComm, SaaS/tech, civic/nonprofit, and political action each have clear use cases, proof needs, risk boundaries, and budget logic. |
| Supply velocity | City leads can produce qualified host-side options quickly, supported by local advisory boards, grant pools, media, and central processing. |
| Spend integrity | Sponsor money is scoped, watched, documented, and tied to approved activation work. |
| Proof quality | Sponsors believe the evidence enough to renew, unlock more supply, or refer. |
| Host trust | Hosts and connectors feel seen, paid, respected, and more powerful because Analog exists. |
| Product learning | Repeated friction turns into reusable campaigns, event sets, host events, spend cards, proof surfaces, profiles, catalog records, or fund workflows. |
| Media gravity | `The Connector`, social, print, YouTube, and owned IRL pull more people into the Analog network. |
| Regional market ownership | AnalogNYC and AnalogSF show city-specific supply depth, repeat Sponsor demand, and expansion economics before later markets are added. |
