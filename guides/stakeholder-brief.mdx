Analog helps organizations participate in the IRL economy by sourcing and funding trusted Hosts and community gatherers. Hosts turn hidden balance sheets into income; Sponsors turn real-world participation into brand or mission growth; proof guides the next dollar.

These are the founding operating docs for turning that premise into a company. Use this page as the shared narrative across internal onboarding, investor conversations, early Sponsor calls, Host recruiting, city lead work, and partner scoping.

If you are reading the docs in order for the first time, use [Reading Path](/guides/reading-path) after this page.

<Info>
Analog turns Sponsor capital into community growth for Hosts, brand or mission growth for Sponsors, and proof for the next dollar invested.
</Info>

The company question is not "can we build another event app?" It is: if the cost of software keeps converging toward zero, how do we use capital and network to build defensible brand and IRL systems?

Analog is broader than any one community vertical or product-gifting model.

Run clubs are one useful proof case. The broader strategy is cash-based Host enablement:

- micro grants
- macro grants
- services/tools
- print and publication
- public-money programs

These make community assets easier to fund, prove, and renew.

Analog is also broader than any one Sponsor category. SaaS/tech, eComm, civic/nonprofit, and political action all point toward overlapping communities and IRL leaders. The immediate company-building bet is NYC: prove that a dense directory of communities can become Sponsor-ready cultural real estate, LOI-backed budget intent, governed activation, and renewal-grade proof. SF stays the first transfer market once the NYC operating model is real enough to travel.

The investor context should stay anchored in three compounding trends:

1. **Communities need owned public presence.** Clubs, recurring events, spaces, and local operators need SEO-optimized listings and public records the way businesses need Yelp, Angi, Google Business Profile, or category directories. Analog should let them claim and maintain that presence without turning them into generic inventory.
2. **Sponsors are embracing community investment.** Brands, tech companies, civic funders, campaigns, local businesses, and foundations need a platform to find, fund, govern, and prove community investment instead of rebuilding sponsorship ops in DMs and recap decks.
3. **Consumer apps and event platforms need brand-fund rails.** Small event-page apps, community platforms, social fitness apps, ticketing tools, venue/nightlife platforms, and large consumer surfaces such as Partiful, Posh, Luma, Flite, LineLeap, MobiusFit, and Eventbrite can use Analog as later platform-as-a-service infrastructure: list downstream communities into a directory, improve SEO/GEO coverage, add attribution APIs and pixels, report normalized proof, and unlock Sponsor funds programmatically without forcing every community to use Analog directly.

This keeps the company strategy flexible without becoming vague. Analog can monetize through claimed public presence, Sponsor workflows, credits, program fees, partner APIs, attribution, and normalized proof. The shared asset is the same: public-safe community supply, governed funding, and data that makes Sponsors more confident deploying the next dollar.

The summer/fall plan is tactical:

1. Build Host-side pitch tools.
2. Launch Sponsor-backed grants and raffles.
3. Publish seasonal city print and newsletters.

This is CAC as an overhead bet, not unit-driven referral CAC. Analog should use small experiments and micro-investments to find the rooms, scenes, and operators that can compound.

The sharper supply-side frame is MAC: market acquisition cost.

Analog should win key markets with free tools for everyone, starting with one reusable Analog application. That application should help Hosts apply for public grants, private grants, community collective funds, selective brand funds, and Sponsor opportunities.

The grant experience should feel guided:

- one core application
- grant-specific follow-up only when needed
- background packet preparation
- Host approval before submission

Then layer support for high-signal Hosts: QR asset packs, photo/video, social/newsletter/print inclusion, grant pools, in-kind support, insurance, government grant discovery, Host-authorized application support, and brand funds.

Supply creation should not stop at discovering existing events. Analog can generate supply by funding starter rituals, commissioning missing operators, activating venues, publishing fund drops, launching community collective funds, and turning public or Sponsor demand into RFEs/RFCs.

The strategic effect is pull. Once new supply gets funded and proven through Analog, established Hosts and Venues move closer because Analog is where future funding is being deployed.

The broader company goal is to make Analog the only sane place for Hosts and Sponsors to run community sponsorships. That takes more than one marketplace screen. It requires MAC, Host tools, Sponsor controls, public and brand funds, local artifacts, proof, and renewal logic to compound together.

Analog should also be public-safe by design. Hosts, Venues, operators, connectors, and organizers should understand that Analog helps them get discovered for funding, partnerships, sponsorships, grants, media, and custom workflows. The network should expose opportunity data by default while protecting opt-outs, private campaign modes, raw attendee identity, partner-private notes, sensitive financial data, and compliance-sensitive contexts.

Analog should build AI memory like a great operator builds judgment: keep the factual product record, write down reviewed lessons, and use the right lesson in the right future workflow. A future Market Agent can watch public news, grants, city pages, event signals, and public-safe opportunity data. Product agents can combine that market context with real-time Analog records. Growth AI can use public and aggregate learning without default access to raw private product data.

The Sponsor product should feel agentic even when it is mostly UI: understand the session intent, show the next useful action, reveal deeper controls when needed, and route sensitive actions to approval. The Host product should stay fast and simple.

The Host-side emotional position is bigger than execution support:

```text
Analog is where IRL communities go pro.
```

When a community builder, organizer, event planner, or cultural Host joins Analog, they should feel included in something bigger than one event: a roster, a city network, a cultural map, and a professional track record. Print guides, Instagram content, city roundups, public-safe directories, and campaign materials should create status and visibility from day one. The best print guides should also work as useful city objects: beautiful enough to leave on a coffee-shop table and practical enough to help someone find things to do this summer. That visibility should compound into access to real brands, real funding, and real growth.

Before a full brand marketplace exists, Analog should close a few selective brand funds where fund-market fit is obvious. The first version is the [NYC Pre-Seed Sponsor Cohort](/strategy/nyc-preseed-sponsor-cohort): Sponsors commit intended NYC partnership budget through an LOI, place a refundable USD 500 deposit, and earn high-context matchmaking only after they lean in. The Sponsor supplies activation capital. Analog supplies the market thesis, distribution, Host intake, governance, proof, and regional dashboard placement. This is how MAC can use Crocs-style, Midol-style, Baden-style, Tapcart-style, or Anthropic/Shopify-style funds without putting every early dollar on Analog's balance sheet.

The sharper proof frame is mention assets. In IRL, authorized Host and Venue mentions are not throwaway recap material. They help show who moved the room, which space amplified the moment, and what the Sponsor can underwrite next.

For public and nonprofit programs, Analog helps capital reach the edge. Agencies, foundations, NGOs, and nonprofits can keep the mission and accountability upstream while using Analog to fund downstream Hosts, community builders, small businesses, spaces, and local operators who can create participation.

For larger sponsorships, Analog becomes an underwriting layer. Once Analog knows which communities, Hosts, and playbooks work, larger Sponsors can use Analog to safely deploy six- or seven-figure IRL budgets through investment memos, allocation models, risk registers, proof gates, contract/invoice review, AI monitoring, and renewal recommendations.

## The Company Line

Analog is the financial infrastructure for Sponsor participation in the gathering economy.

The market is not waiting for more event software. Communities, Hosts, creators, spaces, city leads, and Sponsors already exist. What is missing is the funding layer that decides where Sponsor money should go, deploys it with guardrails, captures what happened, and informs the next allocation without rebuilding every activation from scratch.

For Hosts, the market is also missing a professional track. Event pages, ticketing links, and calendar invites help someone execute a gathering. Analog should help the builder become visible, trusted, funded, promoted, and increasingly able to make a living around gathering people.

```text
Sponsor demand -> trusted Host supply -> governed spend -> proof -> renewal or unlock
```

The long-term opportunity is larger than sponsorship workflow. Analog is building the operating memory, AI context layer, funding workflows, trust graph, proof system, public-safe opportunity graph, IRL culture data layer, and media surface that make community-led IRL safe, measurable, discoverable, and repeatable.

## The 12-Month Proof

Analog should not try to win the entire IRL economy at once. It should show that a lean team can find repeatable paths where the market already wants to move, then turn those paths into product, proof, media, and repeatable GTM.

| Proof area | What good looks like |
|---|---|
| Sponsor motion | eComm, SaaS/tech, civic/nonprofit, and political action each have clear use cases, proof needs, risk boundaries, and budget logic. |
| Supply velocity | City leads can produce qualified host-side options quickly, supported by local advisory boards, grant pools, media, and central processing. |
| Spend integrity | Sponsor money is scoped, watched, documented, and tied to approved activation work. |
| Proof quality | Sponsors believe the evidence enough to renew, unlock more supply, or refer. |
| Host trust | Hosts and connectors feel seen, paid, respected, and more powerful because Analog exists. |
| Product learning | Repeated friction turns into reusable campaigns, event sets, host events, spend cards, proof surfaces, profiles, catalog records, or fund workflows. |
| Media gravity | `The Connector`, social, print, YouTube, and owned IRL pull more people into the Analog network. |
| Regional market ownership | AnalogNYC shows city-specific supply depth, repeat Sponsor demand, and expansion economics first. AnalogSF becomes the paired transfer market after the NYC loop is credible. |
