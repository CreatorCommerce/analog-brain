---
title: "Glossary"
description: "Canonical Analog vocabulary for sponsor, host, product, market-formation, and proof concepts."
pageType: "Reference"
---

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Sponsor | The product actor that funds a campaign. Analog is built around three sponsor perspectives: eComm Brands that sell physical products, Tech Companies that sell digital products to strategic users or accounts, and Public/Non-profit Funds that underwrite public-good experiences and outcomes. |
| Sponsor perspective | The strategic lens used to shape sponsor needs, proof, media framing, and city lead discovery. The three perspectives are eComm Brands, Tech Companies, and Public/Non-profit Funds. |
| Host | The product actor that creates events and uses sponsor budgets. Hosts include creators, communities, organizers, space owners, local operators, and dot connectors when they originate or run an activation. |
| Operating layer | Analog's role between sponsors and hosts: discover trusted supply, collaborate through governed spend and operations, then measure proof for renewal. |
| Fintech enablement | Analog's MVP posture around spend infrastructure: use budgets, allocations, card controls, transactions, and proof to deploy sponsor capital responsibly without positioning the MVP as a bank or wallet. |
| Marketplace enablement | The product work that makes sponsor demand and host supply structured enough for a marketplace to emerge: event sets, applications, host profiles, pricing hypotheses, proof standards, and renewal decisions. |
| Market-making services | Bounded manual GTM, outreach, city lead, media, and program design work used to create supply and learn which steps should become product, templates, partner workflows, or no-go rules. |
| Brand fund or program | Sponsor-backed budget lane that declares demand, invites or prospects host supply, governs spend, and creates proof for renewal. |
| Cash funding playbook | Repeatable Analog motion where a Sponsor or public funder uses governed cash, services, tools, proof, and renewal logic to help a Host accomplish a real community goal. |
| Micro Grant | Small recurring or event-scoped budget, often $100 to $500/month, for coffee, libations, supplies, rentals, printing, accessibility, or light venue support. |
| Macro Grant | Larger recurring Host budget, often $1k to $5k/month, for proven organizers, strategic rooms, recurring activations, operator margin, or deeper community infrastructure. |
| Operator retainer | Recurring budget or services agreement for a Host, connector, city lead, or operator who repeatedly creates sponsor-ready supply, strategic rooms, or public-good participation. |
| Services/tools enablement | Operational support that makes cash funding useful: insurance, COIs, documents, contracts, print-on-demand, local designers, vendors, rentals, proof kits, and space support. |
| Public-money program | Municipal, NGO, foundation, or public-good funding lane with eligibility, controlled spend, fraud/waste/abuse controls, participation proof, and transparent reporting. |
| High-ticket sponsorship underwriting | Analog's future role as the diligence, allocation, risk, proof, tranche, and renewal layer for six- or seven-figure IRL sponsorships. |
| Sponsorship investment memo | AI-assisted and operator-reviewed document that explains why a city, category, ecosystem, or public/private program deserves capital and how Analog would govern it. |
| Allocation model | Budget plan that splits large sponsorship capital across micro grants, macro grants, flagship activations, media, services/tools, operators, public-good access, contingency, and reporting. |
| Risk register | Sponsor-facing record of brand safety, fraud/waste/abuse, public sensitivity, operational, legal, venue, community trust, and reporting risks. |
| Tranche gate | Proof, risk, and approval checkpoint that decides whether the next budget tranche should release, hold, reallocate, or stop. |
| Budget convention | Repeated rule for how sponsor capital should be structured, approved, spent, proven, and renewed. |
| Free-market learning surface | Constrained market created by a sponsor fund or program where supply can apply, be sourced, compete, execute, and produce proof under governed spend rules. |
| Selection rule | The logic that decides which host, city, category, or playbook receives the next sponsor dollar. |
| Sponsor-shared campaign | Campaign, fund, or program that a sponsor, city lead, partner, media channel, or Analog-owned IRL moment can share to make a qualified opportunity visible to the right supply. |
| Demand beacon | Shareable sponsor-backed ask that communicates who the opportunity is for, what money is available, what spend is allowed, and what proof earns renewal. |
| Money-on-the-table signal | Visible evidence that real sponsor budget exists for qualified hosts or operators, without implying unrestricted access or guaranteed funding. |
| 12-month market focus | The operating goal of finding 2 to 3 repeatable causeways across NYC, LA, Miami, and SF where sponsor demand, rapid supply, media, moats, affection, and dogfood compound. |
| Lean GTM operating model | Analog's early company shape for national GTM: fewer than 9 full-time people, 3 product/build, 4 GTM, 1 CS, and fractional city/media/production capacity. |
| Stream | A pre-existing flow of people, money, attention, ritual, trust, or unmet demand that Analog can move with instead of inventing from zero. |
| Causeway | A repeatable bridge across a stream, such as a sponsor fund, city lead motion, Connector format, owned IRL series, event-set prospecting lane, or fund drop. |
| Affection moat | Trust, gratitude, status, and cultural pull that makes hosts, connectors, and sponsors want to be part of Analog before the product is fully inevitable. |
| Dogfood loop | Analog using its own product and field motion to run owned IRL, capture proof, publish media, recruit supply, and improve the platform. |
| Organization role | Current product bridge toward one organization model. A workspace can carry sponsor and host roles instead of being treated as a rigid account type forever. |
| Brand | A common sponsor category and market buyer. Use `Sponsor` when describing the product object. |
| Campaign | Sponsor-owned funding container with budget, objective, proof requirements, and host relationships. |
| Controlled activation budget | Sponsor capital routed through Analog for approved host, vendor, venue, meal, benefit, or supply spend. It should be separated from Analog revenue. |
| Platform or campaign fee | Sponsor-paid Analog revenue for structuring the campaign, setting guardrails, supporting setup, collecting proof, and helping the sponsor decide what to renew. |
| Fund administration fee | Transparent fee for operating a governed sponsor, public, category, or city fund with eligibility, spend rules, proof, and reporting. |
| Event-set sourcing fee | Sponsor-paid fee for building a city, category, audience, or host-supply lane before a campaign has enough qualified options. |
| Managed activation support | Bounded operator or CS support for launch readiness, host coordination, proof QA, and renewal handoff. It should be priced explicitly and productized over time. |
| Media or content rights package | Optional sponsor-paid package for approved content usage, recap assets, interviews, or co-branded presence. It should not erase editorial trust or community permissions. |
| Performance reserve | Optional budget reserve for pre-agreed upside, claim, bounty, or performance logic. Use only when the proof path and sponsor terms are clear. |
| Event set | Campaign subgroup for a market persona, activation lane, geography, special-request set, files/assets, and prospecting motion. |
| Collab | A sponsor-host relationship inside one campaign. It is the unit that grants a host access to sponsor budget. |
| Host event | Event created from the host side. The current wizard can create it with no sponsor or one selected sponsor budget, while the data model is future-shaped for richer sponsor summaries. |
| Hosted event page | Public event page attached to a host event. It can hold RSVP fields, ticket types, themes, custom CSS, and custom HTML. |
| Spend card | Temporary operational card that lets a host spend against sponsor-approved budget under limits, categories, and expiration. |
| Application portal | Sponsor-owned form surface where hosts submit structured sponsorship pitches. |
| Campaign outreach | Sponsor workspace motion for managed or future self-serve email/direct mail outbound tied to campaigns. |
| Event set prospecting | External prospecting run for one event set. It routes leads to the campaign application link. |
| Workspace file | Product file object for uploads, links, shared files, event set assets, application attachments, social content, and Analog Docs. |
| Analog Doc | Markdown document inside the files workspace. It can be manual, template-generated, or deep-research generated. |
| Deep research doc | Operator/AI-generated Analog Doc from a prompt and assignment context. |
| DM thread | Sponsor-host message thread available for active partnerships or pitch contexts. |
| Host task | Host event preparation or sponsor-required todo, including tasks sourced from event set special requests. |
| Asset request | Host request for a sponsor asset to be mailed. |
| Shipment log | Sponsor-side record of product samples, event supplies, printed materials, merch, signage, or other shipment references. |
| Proof | Sponsor-visible evidence that the activation happened and deserves renewal. This can include spend, attendance, social proof, content, claims, or matched outcomes. |
| Market-growth underwriting | The proof loop that helps sponsors decide whether to deploy more capital based on spend stewardship, social growth, operator upside, category growth, brand growth, and community sentiment. |
| IRL culture graph | Privacy-safe, provenance-aware data layer about events, recurring rituals, cultural operators, audience hypotheses, sponsor fit, spend behavior, proof quality, and renewal logic. |
| Audience graph | Privacy-safe intelligence layer built from hosted pages, attendance imports, check-ins, claims, and sponsor-side outcome matching. |
| Lookalike event | Event or recurring activation whose audience resembles a sponsor's best customers, target segment, or prior converting IRL audiences. |
| Existing customer overlap | Privacy-safe count or tier showing that attendees overlap with a sponsor's known customers, without exposing raw PII by default. |
| Market growth | Evidence that an activation increased category participation, content velocity, social conversation, recurring ritual strength, or sponsor-ready supply. |
| Media, connectors, and funding engine | The city-led motion that turns field discovery into `The Connector`, social, YouTube, print, owned IRL, connector profiles, governed funds, and proof loops. |
| Prestige media platform | Analog's future editorial surface for giving community builders, event organizers, city leads, and dot connectors high-status profiles, features, lists, films, and print artifacts. |
| Funding platform | Future Analog surface where builders can discover, apply for, unlock, and operate against governed sponsor, public, category, or city funds. |
| Fund page | Sponsor, public, category, or city fund surface with mission, budget, eligibility, geography, spend rules, proof requirements, and unlock conditions. |
| Editorial fund drop | A funding opportunity launched alongside a Connector issue, city report, video, print edition, or owned Analog gathering. |
| The Connector | Analog's editorial brand for documenting the gathering economy through Substack, city magazines, regional field notes, profiles, fund drops, event calendars, and event coverage. |
| Analog city print | Profitable city publication for AnalogNYC, AnalogSF, and later city brands that features upcoming events, Host profiles, scene data, fund drops, Sponsor-safe placements, retail distribution, and QR flows into Analog. |
| Owned IRL | Analog-run gatherings such as happy hours, salons, dinners, walks, live recordings, and community-leader meetups that dogfood the product and create more field context. |
| Head of Content | The operator who owns editorial planning, channel strategy, sponsor perspective, production standards, city priorities, and coordination with the media team. |
| Media team | Contracted or assembled writing, design, copy, video editing, and publishing operators who produce content from city lead field submissions. |
| Spend integrity | Evidence that funded money was used responsibly, with budget discipline, receipts, vendor costs, and proof quality. |
| Spend stewardship | A broader underwriting signal that asks whether the partner used sponsor capital responsibly across budgets, vendors, receipts, anomalies, and unused funds. |
| Community sentiment | Evidence that the sponsor presence was additive, brand-safe, and welcomed by the community. |
| Operator upside | The host-side economic upside that lets dependable organizers earn for the work of gathering people. |
| Funded work | The target host state where recurring community work becomes funded, scoped, measurable, and renewable instead of unpaid or scattered labor. |
| Unlock | A larger or next sponsorship allocation earned by market-growth proof and spend integrity. |
| Request for Events | Sponsor-side request for events that match a target audience, geography, category, proof requirement, and budget. |
| Request for Communities | Sponsor-side request for trusted communities that can originate the right IRL moment or recurring ritual. |
| Request for Sponsorship | Host-side package that asks sponsors to fund a specific event, community, venue, series, or activation. |
| Activation Opportunity | Connector- or operator-originated package that says "I can make this happen" before a sponsor campaign is fully formed. |
| Event Prospecting Catalog | Apollo-like internal market map of events, communities, venues, recurring rituals, and local activation surfaces. |
| Asset Need Intelligence | Map of missing communities, spaces, insurance, operators, vendors, proof, sponsors, and capital that block more IRL supply from existing. |
| City lead | Local operator who maps host supply, recruits or creates options, resolves missing assets, and helps more host-side unlocks become possible. |
| City pod | Flexible local bench around a city lead: connectors, producers, media stringers, sponsor scouts, and operators used before hiring a full local team. |
| Head of Cities | Central operator responsible for city strategy, city lead recruiting, scorecards, operating cadence, and escalation across launch markets. |
| Dot connector | Host-side persona who may not own a venue or audience directly, but knows the local people, spaces, vendors, creators, and sponsors needed to assemble an activation. |
| Community builder | Person who repeatedly creates belonging, participation, and trust inside a community or local scene. |
| Connector profile | Sponsor-safe profile that makes a dot connector's network, taste, proof, city context, and activation ability legible. |
| Rockstar connector | Dot connector or community builder whose trust, local judgment, proof, media visibility, and economic upside make them a visible talent class inside the gathering economy. |
| CreatorCommerce Demand Bridge | Demand-side motion where CreatorCommerce or partner deployments qualify creator/affiliate-heavy brands for Analog IRL pilots. |
| Partner API | API-first strategy that lets community, club, venue, creator, ticketing, or operator platforms embed Analog sponsorship workflows without surrendering their own product surface. |
| Agent MCP | Model Context Protocol interface for external AI tools to read and operate Analog objects with approvals. |
| MCP attribution workflow | Measurement, Commerce, and Proof workflow that matches attendance or claim identifiers to sponsor-side outcomes with consent, permissions, and privacy controls. |
