title: "Glossary"
description: "Canonical Analog vocabulary for sponsor, host, product, market-formation, and proof concepts."
pageType: "Reference"
---

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Sponsor | The product actor that funds a campaign. Analog is built around four commercial lanes: eComm Brands, SaaS/Tech Companies, Civic/Public-Non-profit Funds, and Political Action Sponsors. |
| Sponsor perspective | The strategic lens used to shape sponsor needs, proof, media framing, and city lead discovery. Use the four commercial lanes to separate buyer language without fragmenting the city supply model. |
| Host | The product actor that creates events and uses sponsor budgets. Hosts include creators, communities, organizers, space owners, local operators, and dot connectors when they originate or run an activation. |
| Commercial lane | Sponsor-side category used for packaging, proof, and risk controls: eComm, SaaS/tech, civic/nonprofit, or political action. It is a route into city supply, not the market Analog should own. |
| Operating layer | Analog's role between sponsors and hosts: discover trusted supply, collaborate through governed spend and operations, then measure proof for renewal. |
| Fintech enablement | Analog's MVP posture around spend infrastructure: use budgets, allocations, card controls, transactions, and proof to deploy sponsor capital responsibly without positioning the MVP as a bank or wallet. |
| Marketplace enablement | The product work that makes sponsor demand and host supply structured enough for a marketplace to emerge: event sets, applications, host profiles, pricing hypotheses, proof standards, and renewal decisions. |
| Marketplace logic | Platform doctrine for pairing Sponsor dollars with receptive Hosts, communities, Venues, event types, campaign visibility modes, and category benchmarks. |
| Campaign visibility mode | The distribution rule for a campaign: private link-only, broadly marketed, or ICP-targeted. |
| Social receptiveness | Analog's permissioned read on whether a community, Host, Venue, or event type is likely to welcome, amplify, ignore, or reject a Sponsor's presence. |
| Category benchmark | Proof comparison against organizations in the same Sponsor category, such as eComm/CPG, SaaS/tech, civic/nonprofit, political action, or local retail/hospitality. |
| Market-making services | Bounded manual GTM, outreach, city lead, media, and program design work used to create supply and learn which steps should become product, templates, partner workflows, or no-go rules. |
| Burn bet | Company-funded spend that tests a repeatable path to trusted supply, Sponsor demand, governed spend, proof, renewal, or product memory. |
| Operating model | The way Analog staffs and funds a motion: lean core, city pod, campaign-funded city buildout, agency bridge, media business unit, or public/nonprofit control lane. |
| Overhead-bet CAC | Supply-capture spend used to create market gravity through tools, funds, media, interviews, and experiments instead of paying for one referral at a time. |
| Dinner CAC | Founder shorthand for SaaS/Tech relationship acquisition through funded dinners, coffee, breakfasts, salons, and councils. Use it when the room is qualified, content-forward, enjoyable, and repeatable; do not imply perfect attribution from one meal. |
| Relationship CAC | Sponsor acquisition model where spend is judged by qualified relationships, account fit, follow-up, partner/customer learning, and repeatability instead of clicks or generic attendance. |
| Market acquisition cost (MAC) | Supply-side spend and support used to acquire a market's community graph, create escape velocity, and make Sponsors enter through Analog. It is not one-user CAC. |
| Company-funded MAC | The portion of market acquisition cost paid by Analog's own operating budget, separate from Sponsor activation budget, public grants, partner support, media revenue, or in-kind value routed through Analog. |
| Qualified supply node | A Host, Venue, connector, operator, recurring ritual, or local supply surface with enough profile, proof, permission, Sponsor fit, or trust to become useful supply. |
| Existing supply | Hosts, Venues, communities, operators, rituals, or spaces that already gather people and can be packaged, verified, funded, and proven through Analog. |
| Latent supply | Trusted people, audiences, spaces, or operators that could become recurring fundable supply if Analog removes a blocker such as money, proof, services, insurance, coordination, or media visibility. |
| Generated supply | New or newly fundable Host, Venue, operator, community, event, public-fund, or service surface created when Analog uses funding, tools, media, or field work to remove a real market blocker. |
| Existing-supply pull | The effect where generated supply, visible funds, media artifacts, or proof make established Hosts, Venues, operators, and communities move closer to Analog to claim records, apply for money, and preserve future opportunity. |
| Replacement ratio | Planning metric for whether outside support is replacing company-funded MAC: external support routed through Analog divided by company-funded MAC. |
| Community CAC | Later-stage metric for the cost to acquire one community once Analog can estimate that community's attributable Sponsor value. Do not use it as the primary first-six-month model. |
| Market unlock | The point where a city, category, or circuit has enough supply density, proof, artifacts, and Sponsor demand for Analog to sell access with confidence. |
| Digital edge erosion | eComm market pressure where copycat products, similar growth playbooks, AI ad parity, influencer saturation, and creator ops load make it harder for a brand to defend growth only through digital channels. |
| IRL diversification | Sponsor strategy for adding governed community-led activations, local rituals, product trial, and proof loops outside paid social, affiliate, creator, and AI-generated ad channels. |
| Community spectrum | Market view that communities range from premium membership spaces and company-backed ecosystems to bootstrapped local clubs, with different norms but shared operating costs, growth needs, and proof gaps. |
| Community career path | The ladder from hobby Host to reliable organizer, funded Host, community operator, community business, and local institution. Analog should support the path without forcing every community to professionalize. |
| Community champion stack | Host-side support bundle that can include event history, first-party data, pitch/proof tools, QR asset packs, content inclusion, print, grant access, insurance, discounts, and in-kind support. |
| QR asset pack | Free or low-cost event kit with QR-linked posters, table cards, stickers, claim flows, links, or other IRL assets that route attendance and proof back into Analog. |
| In-kind grant | Non-cash support such as photo/video, print, insurance, design, venue help, proof kits, or operator support that helps a Host execute and prove an activation. |
| Analog credit | Host-side reputation earned through clean execution, proof, spend stewardship, and community trust. It should unlock better rates, larger allocations, stronger Sponsors, and media visibility. |
| Deployment credit | Sponsor-side incentive that unlocks only after controlled activation budget is deployed through Analog and proof clears. It should drive funded behavior, not generic CAC. |
| Brand fund or program | Sponsor-backed budget lane that declares demand, invites or prospects host supply, governs spend, and creates proof for renewal. |
| Selective brand fund | Analog-designed, Sponsor-funded public fund chosen for a specific market before the full brand marketplace is liquid. Analog supplies market thesis, distribution, application flow, governance, and proof; the Sponsor supplies activation capital. |
| Fund-market fit | The standard that a fund should feel native to a city, circuit, category, Host need, and Sponsor narrative before Analog publishes it. |
| Selective enterprise muscle | Focused partnerships capacity, such as the existing ex-Crocs partnerships leader profile, used to close a few market-fit enterprise funds before hiring or operating a broad Sponsor sales motion. |
| Grant or raffle hook | Sponsor-backed money-on-the-table CTA that helps qualified Hosts self-select into a campaign, fund, or event set while keeping eligibility, spend rules, and proof clear. |
| Free money pitch tool | Host-side pitch surface that turns event history, proof, files, audience notes, grant matches, and Sponsor fit into a reusable funding file. |
| Universal Analog application | Reusable Host funding record that can power public grants, private grants, community collective funds, selective brand funds, Sponsor opportunities, and direct pitches without asking the Host to start over each time. |
| Geo-dashboard grant shelf | Regional Host dashboard module that shows market-specific grants and funds based on Host geography, category, eligibility, deadline, and allowed use. |
| One-click apply intent | Host action that authorizes Analog to prepare or route an application from the Host's reusable Analog record. It is not final submission, legal representation, guaranteed eligibility, or an award promise. |
| Guided grant application UX | TurboTax-style grant flow that asks only for the specific fields, documents, confirmations, or signatures a chosen grant needs beyond the universal Analog application, then lets Agent MCP and operators prepare forms in the background for Host approval. |
| Grant discovery | Analog research motion for finding public, nonprofit, foundation, BID, university, hospital, public/private, or Sponsor-backed funds a Host may qualify for. |
| Auto-apply grant workflow | Host-authorized workflow where Analog helps prepare, track, submit, co-submit, or support grant applications using the Host's Analog record. Do not imply unapproved submissions. |
| OpenClaw tactics | Founder shorthand for systematic, aggressive, permissioned grant discovery: search funding sources, match eligibility, package proof, draft materials, track deadlines, and support submission with Host authorization. |
| Grant opportunity catalog | Internal market map of grant and public-fund opportunities by geography, category, allowable use, deadline, eligibility, and proof requirement. |
| Host grant readiness file | Reusable Host file containing event history, proof, photos, budget, eligibility documents, fiscal sponsor status, insurance, compliance notes, and report plan for grant applications. |
| Grant stewardship desk | Analog operating motion that finds grants, matches Hosts, prepares applications, tracks status, supports execution, and packages reports with Host authorization. |
| Public attribution rail | Public-safe reporting layer that connects grant money to outputs such as events, attendance, access, public-space use, local commerce support, cultural preservation, accessibility, sentiment, and renewal recommendations. |
| Fraud/waste/abuse prevention | Public-fund control posture that flags duplicate applicants, duplicate vendors, unsupported costs, weak receipts, restricted-use risk, conflicts, and missing proof while preserving human review. |
| Cash funding playbook | Repeatable Analog motion where a Sponsor or public funder uses governed cash, services, tools, proof, and renewal logic to help a Host accomplish a real community goal. |
| Micro Grant | Small recurring or event-scoped budget, often USD 100 to USD 500/month, for coffee, libations, supplies, rentals, printing, accessibility, or light venue support. |
| Macro Grant | Larger recurring Host budget, often USD 1k to USD 5k/month, for proven organizers, strategic rooms, recurring activations, operator margin, or deeper community infrastructure. |
| Operator retainer | Recurring budget or services agreement for a Host, connector, city lead, or operator who repeatedly creates sponsor-ready supply, strategic rooms, or public-good participation. |
| Services/tools enablement | Operational support that makes cash funding useful: insurance, COIs, documents, contracts, print-on-demand, local designers, vendors, rentals, proof kits, and space support. |
| Public-money program | Municipal, NGO, foundation, or public-good funding lane with eligibility, controlled spend, fraud/waste/abuse controls, participation proof, and transparent reporting. |
| Public and nonprofit funding infrastructure | Analog's role as the operating layer that helps governments, foundations, NGOs, nonprofits, public/private coalitions, and civic institutions move mission capital downstream to Hosts with eligibility, spend controls, proof, reporting, and renewal logic. |
| Political Action Sponsor | Campaign, PAC, ballot initiative, party committee, advocacy organization, or donor coalition that funds grassroots activation, field events, or advocacy participation with compliance-safe controls and proof. |
| Political funding cascade | Political action allocation model where Sponsor budget moves through scoped national, state, district, county, city, ward, precinct, and Host-level envelopes with approved uses, proof, privacy boundaries, and reallocation logic. |
| Campaign grassroots activation fund | Political action use case where Sponsor budget is routed from national or campaign strategy into state, district, county, city, ward, Host, and event-level spend with approved uses, proof, privacy boundaries, and allocation learning. |
| Downstream Host funding | Public, nonprofit, or sponsor capital routed to the community builders, small businesses, spaces, local groups, operators, or partners closest to real-world participation. |
| Nonprofit regranting lane | Program where a nonprofit, NGO, or foundation funds aligned downstream Hosts or community partners while keeping mission governance, accountability, and reporting upstream. |
| Public trust and grant safety | The proof and control standard for public or nonprofit funding: eligibility, restricted uses, spend stewardship, conflict review, fraud/waste/abuse checks, privacy-safe participation proof, public-safe summaries, and human approval. |
| High-ticket sponsorship underwriting | Analog's future role as the diligence, allocation, risk, proof, tranche, and renewal layer for six- or seven-figure IRL sponsorships. |
| Sponsorship investment memo | AI-assisted and operator-reviewed document that explains why a city, category, ecosystem, or public/private program deserves capital and how Analog would govern it. |
| Allocation model | Budget plan that splits large sponsorship capital across micro grants, macro grants, flagship activations, media, services/tools, operators, public-good access, contingency, and reporting. |
| Risk register | Sponsor-facing record of brand safety, fraud/waste/abuse, public sensitivity, operational, legal, venue, community trust, and reporting risks. |
| Tranche gate | Proof, risk, and approval checkpoint that decides whether the next budget tranche should release, hold, reallocate, or stop. |
| Budget convention | Repeated rule for how sponsor capital should be structured, approved, spent, proven, and renewed. |
| Free-market learning surface | Constrained market created by a sponsor fund or program where supply can apply, be sourced, compete, execute, and produce proof under governed spend rules. |
| Selection rule | The logic that decides which host, city, category, or playbook receives the next sponsor dollar. |
| Sponsor-shared campaign | Campaign, fund, or program that a sponsor, city lead, partner, media channel, or Analog-owned IRL moment can share to make a qualified opportunity visible to the right supply. |
| Demand beacon | Shareable sponsor-backed ask that communicates who the opportunity is for, what money is available, what spend is allowed, and what proof earns renewal. |
| Money-on-the-table signal | Visible evidence that real sponsor budget exists for qualified hosts or operators, without implying unrestricted access or guaranteed funding. |
| Campaign start date | Applicant-visible date that tells Hosts when review, funding, outreach, or activation is expected to begin; it also lets Sponsors post future-dated campaigns and gauge demand before accepting applicants. |
| Free applicant preview | Sponsor-side PLG state where the first five applicants are visible on free while later applicants are blurred, limited, or aggregated until the Sponsor commits. |
| First accepted applicant gate | Sponsor conversion moment where payment or committed campaign flow begins only after the Sponsor accepts an applicant and is ready to create a collab, budget responsibility, and proof workflow. |
| 12-month market focus | The focused entry plan for proving AnalogNYC and AnalogSF as round-one supply markets, then using Sponsor demand, Host supply, media, governed spend, and proof to decide what repeats. |
| City Lead GTM operating model | Analog's early GTM shape: the city lead acts as the field gateway, while a central processing stack turns interviews, intros, leads, IRL content, local spend, and field notes into media, product memory, Sponsor packages, fund drops, proof, and renewal decisions. |
| City layer | Regional Analog operating layer such as AnalogNYC or AnalogSF that owns local supply, market-specific media, relationships, proof, and city-level operating learning. |
| City operating department | Mature city layer inside Analog, staffed by a city lead and supported by a local advisory board, operator bench, market-specific media, grant pool, central stack, and city scorecard. |
| Regional Host dashboard | Host-side local product layer that displays the active city identity, such as AnalogNYC or AnalogSF, plus local funds, grants, raffles, MAC perks, media asks, and proof prompts. |
| Advisory group | Cross-market working group of 12 to 15 advisors in a specific lane, such as Brand, Software, Community, or Non-profit, that turns market access and judgment into Sponsor leads, Host referrals, fund theses, proof standards, no-go rules, and product memory. |
| Local advisory board | Region-representative group that helps a city layer interpret local trust, identify supply, avoid sponsor-fit mistakes, and understand cultural rhythms. |
| Community grant pool | USD 10k to USD 50k market-level budget used by a city lead to create supply, fund micro grants, remove blockers, launch fund drops, and create proof cases. |
| Regional intelligence layer | The city-specific memory Analog builds about communities, spaces, leaders, cultural rhythms, Sponsor fit, proof standards, and expansion economics. |
| Pitch tool | Host or connector packaging surface that turns event history, social proof, community reach, photos, prior Sponsor context, and funding asks into Sponsor-ready supply. |
| Stream | A pre-existing flow of people, money, attention, ritual, trust, or unmet demand that Analog can move with instead of inventing from zero. |
| Market-entry path | A repeatable path across a stream, such as a sponsor fund, city lead motion, Connector format, owned IRL series, event-set prospecting lane, or fund drop. |
| Affection moat | Trust, gratitude, status, and cultural pull that makes hosts, connectors, and sponsors want to be part of Analog before the product is fully inevitable. |
| Dogfood loop | Analog using its own product and field motion to run owned IRL, capture proof, publish media, recruit supply, and improve the platform. |
| Organization role | Current product bridge toward one organization model. A workspace can carry sponsor and host roles instead of being treated as a rigid account type forever. |
| Brand | A common sponsor category and market buyer. Use `Sponsor` when describing the product object. |
| Campaign | Sponsor-owned funding container with budget, objective, proof requirements, and host relationships. |
| Controlled activation budget | Sponsor capital routed through Analog for approved host, vendor, venue, meal, benefit, or supply spend. It should be separated from Analog revenue. |
| Vendor/payee record | Reusable Analog record for a Host, Venue, contractor, vendor, operator, or service provider that may receive campaign-related funds, including role, campaign context, payment context, files, proof links, and tax-document status where needed. |
| 1099 support workflow | Future or partner-supported workflow where Analog helps collect authorized payee/tax-document status, track reportable-payment context, dedupe vendor records, and support exports, filing, or furnishing where required. Do not imply current automatic tax filing, withholding, or tax advice. |
| Platform or campaign fee | Sponsor-paid Analog revenue for structuring the campaign, setting guardrails, supporting setup, collecting proof, and helping the sponsor decide what to renew. |
| Fund administration fee | Transparent fee for operating a governed sponsor, public, category, or city fund with eligibility, spend rules, proof, and reporting. |
| Event-set sourcing fee | Sponsor-paid fee for building a city, category, audience, or host-supply lane before a campaign has enough qualified options. |
| Managed activation support | Bounded operator or CS support for launch readiness, host coordination, proof QA, and renewal handoff. It should be priced explicitly and productized over time. |
| Media or content rights package | Optional sponsor-paid package for approved content usage, recap assets, interviews, or co-branded presence. It should not erase editorial trust or community permissions. |
| Performance reserve | Optional budget reserve for pre-agreed upside, claim, bounty, or performance logic. Use only when the proof path and sponsor terms are clear. |
| Event set | Campaign subgroup for a market persona, activation lane, geography, special-request set, files/assets, and prospecting motion. |
| Collab | A sponsor-host relationship inside one campaign. It is the unit that grants a host access to sponsor budget. |
| Host event | Event created from the host side. The current wizard can create it with no sponsor or one selected sponsor budget, while the data model is future-shaped for richer sponsor summaries. |
| Hosted event page | Public event page attached to a host event. It can hold RSVP fields, ticket types, themes, custom CSS, and custom HTML. |
| Spend card | Temporary operational card that lets a host spend against sponsor-approved budget under limits, categories, and expiration. |
| Host treasury | Future Analog checking-style operating account for trusted Hosts and community businesses, delivered through compliant banking partners, where a larger funded balance can support recurring IRL work while Analog reports recipient-authorized spend stewardship, anomalies, proof, and renewal confidence. Not current MVP behavior. |
| Analog checking | Shorthand for the future Host treasury rail. Use carefully: it should mean partner-bank enabled cash management for trusted Hosts, not an unsupported claim that the MVP is a bank. |
| Derived financial grade | Sensitive derived financial data produced from bank, treasury, or account activity and tied to a recipient. It requires explicit recipient authorization, disclosed Sponsor use, objective methodology, explainability, dispute rights, safer labels, and contract limits. |
| Application portal | Sponsor-owned form surface where hosts submit structured sponsorship pitches. |
| Campaign outreach | Sponsor workspace motion for managed or future self-serve email/direct mail outbound tied to campaigns. |
| Event set prospecting | External prospecting run for one event set. It routes leads to the campaign application link. |
| Workspace file | Product file object for uploads, links, shared files, event set assets, application attachments, social content, and Analog Docs. |
| Analog Doc | Markdown document inside the files workspace. It can be manual, template-generated, or deep-research generated. |
| Deep research doc | Operator/AI-generated Analog Doc from a prompt and assignment context. |
| DM thread | Sponsor-host message thread available for active partnerships or pitch contexts. |
| Host task | Host event preparation or sponsor-required todo, including tasks sourced from event set special requests. |
| Asset request | Host request for a sponsor asset to be mailed. |
| Shipment log | Sponsor-side record of product samples, event supplies, printed materials, merch, signage, or other shipment references. |
| Proof | Sponsor-visible evidence that the activation happened and deserves renewal. This can include spend, attendance, social proof, content, claims, or matched outcomes. |
| Mention asset | Authorized Host, Venue, or Sponsor social mention that becomes durable proof instead of disappearing into the feed. |
| Mention coverage | The scope of social listening and mention capture around a campaign, including whether proof comes from the Host only or from both Host and Venue. |
| Venue reward | Flat campaign-funded amount offered to a Venue for authorizing campaign-window social listening or eligible social account connection. It is controlled activation budget, not Analog revenue. |
| Venue mention authorization | Permissioned workflow where a Venue approves campaign-window mention capture. Future production flows need payout, tax, platform, and consent controls. |
| Mention performance benchmark | Comparison of Host mentions, Venue mentions, Sponsor mentions, attendance, and historical percentile to judge whether an event overperformed. |
| Host/Venue mention ratio | Underwriting signal that compares Host mentions to Venue mentions, often alongside attendance, to understand who created visible IRL social signal. |
| Market-growth underwriting | The proof loop that helps sponsors decide whether to deploy more capital based on spend stewardship, social growth, operator upside, category growth, brand growth, and community sentiment. |
| IRL culture graph | Privacy-safe, provenance-aware data layer about events, recurring rituals, cultural operators, audience hypotheses, sponsor fit, spend behavior, proof quality, and renewal logic. |
| Audience graph | Privacy-safe intelligence layer built from hosted pages, attendance imports, check-ins, claims, and sponsor-side outcome matching. |
| Lookalike event | Event or recurring activation whose audience resembles a sponsor's best customers, target segment, or prior converting IRL audiences. |
| Existing customer overlap | Privacy-safe count or tier showing that attendees overlap with a sponsor's known customers, without exposing raw PII by default. |
| Market growth | Evidence that an activation increased category participation, content velocity, social conversation, recurring ritual strength, or sponsor-ready supply. |
| Media, connectors, and funding engine | The city-led motion that turns field discovery into `The Connector`, social, YouTube, print, owned IRL, connector profiles, governed funds, and proof loops. |
| Prestige media platform | Analog's future editorial surface for giving community builders, event organizers, city leads, and dot connectors high-status profiles, features, lists, films, and print artifacts. |
| Funding platform | Future Analog surface where builders can discover, apply for, unlock, and operate against governed sponsor, public, category, or city funds. |
| Fund page | Sponsor, public, category, or city fund surface with mission, budget, eligibility, geography, spend rules, proof requirements, and unlock conditions. |
| Editorial fund drop | A funding opportunity launched alongside a Connector issue, city report, video, print edition, or owned Analog gathering. |
| The Connector | Analog's editorial brand for documenting the gathering economy through Substack, city magazines, regional field notes, profiles, fund drops, event calendars, and event coverage. |
| Analog city print | Profitable city publication for AnalogNYC, AnalogSF, and later city brands that features upcoming events, Host profiles, scene data, fund drops, Sponsor-safe placements, retail distribution, and QR flows into Analog. |
| Seasonal print and newsletters | City mini-zines, guides, and newsletters that create a reason to interview leaders, list events, distribute locally, and route readers into funds, applications, profiles, and proof. |
| Owned IRL | Analog-run gatherings such as happy hours, salons, dinners, walks, live recordings, and community-leader meetups that dogfood the product and create more field context. |
| Head of Content | The operator who owns editorial planning, channel strategy, sponsor perspective, production standards, city priorities, and coordination with the media team. |
| Media team | Contracted or assembled writing, design, copy, video editing, and publishing operators who produce content from city lead field submissions. |
| Spend integrity | Evidence that funded money was used responsibly, with budget discipline, receipts, vendor costs, and proof quality. |
| Spend stewardship | A broader underwriting signal that asks whether the partner used sponsor capital responsibly across budgets, vendors, receipts, anomalies, and unused funds. |
| Community sentiment | Evidence that the sponsor presence was additive, brand-safe, and welcomed by the community. |
| Operator upside | The host-side economic upside that lets dependable organizers earn for the work of gathering people. |
| Funded work | The target host state where recurring community work becomes funded, scoped, measurable, and renewable instead of unpaid or scattered labor. |
| Unlock | A larger or next sponsorship allocation earned by market-growth proof and spend integrity. |
| Request for Events | Sponsor-side request for events that match a target audience, geography, category, proof requirement, and budget. |
| Request for Communities | Sponsor-side request for trusted communities that can originate the right IRL moment or recurring ritual. |
| Request for Sponsorship | Host-side package that asks sponsors to fund a specific event, community, venue, series, or activation. |
| Activation Opportunity | Connector- or operator-originated package that says "I can make this happen" before a sponsor campaign is fully formed. |
| Event Prospecting Catalog | Apollo-like internal market map of events, communities, venues, recurring rituals, and local activation surfaces. |
| Asset Need Intelligence | Map of missing communities, spaces, insurance, operators, vendors, proof, sponsors, and capital that block more IRL supply from existing. |
| City lead | Local operator who maps host supply, recruits or creates options, resolves missing assets, and helps more host-side unlocks become possible. |
| City pod | Flexible local bench around a city lead: connectors, producers, media stringers, sponsor scouts, and operators used when a city needs more field leverage without turning into a fixed local team. |
| Head of Cities | Central operator responsible for city strategy, city lead recruiting, scorecards, operating cadence, and escalation across launch markets. |
| Dot connector | Host-side persona who may not own a venue or audience directly, but knows the local people, spaces, vendors, creators, and sponsors needed to assemble an activation. |
| Community builder | Person who repeatedly creates belonging, participation, and trust inside a community or local scene. |
| Connector profile | Sponsor-safe profile that makes a dot connector's network, taste, proof, city context, and activation ability legible. |
| Rockstar connector | Dot connector or community builder whose trust, local judgment, proof, media visibility, and economic upside make them a visible talent class inside the gathering economy. |
| CreatorCommerce Demand Bridge | Demand-side motion where CreatorCommerce or partner deployments qualify creator/affiliate-heavy brands for Analog IRL pilots. |
| Partner API | API-first strategy that lets community, club, venue, creator, ticketing, or operator platforms embed Analog sponsorship workflows without surrendering their own product surface. |
| Agent MCP | Model Context Protocol interface for external AI tools to read and operate Analog objects with approvals. |
| MCP attribution workflow | Measurement, Commerce, and Proof workflow that matches attendance or claim identifiers to sponsor-side outcomes with consent, permissions, and privacy controls. |
