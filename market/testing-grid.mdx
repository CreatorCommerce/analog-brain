Use this rubric before and after a pilot. The goal is to decide whether a market dynamic deserves more product investment, more operator work, a different sponsor category, or no further pursuit.

Score each criterion from 1 to 5.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 1 | Weak or mostly assumed |
| 2 | Some evidence, but high friction |
| 3 | Works with operator help |
| 4 | Strong and repeatable with modest product support |
| 5 | Strong enough to become a core product motion |

## Rubric

| Criterion | What To Look For | Score |
|---|---|---|
| Sponsor budget clarity | Real buyer, real budget, clear approval path, and urgency | 1-5 |
| Sponsor category fit | The sponsor naturally benefits from this IRL audience, behavior, or market-growth goal | 1-5 |
| Host supply potency | Trust x monetization permission x repeat cadence x sponsor surface x data capture x operator reliability | 1-5 |
| Activation feasibility | Space, vendors, timing, permissions, insurance, staffing, and budget can clear | 1-5 |
| Consumer value | Sponsor money makes the experience cheaper, better, more useful, or more accessible | 1-5 |
| Proof readiness | Attendance, claims, content, social, spend, or attribution can be captured credibly | 1-5 |
| Spend integrity | Budget limits, card use, receipts, categories, timing, and anomalies can be watched | 1-5 |
| Repeatability | The activation can become a series, template, host package, city playbook, or category loop | 1-5 |
| Product leverage | The workflow maps to durable product objects instead of bespoke services | 1-5 |
| Unlock potential | Success would justify a larger sponsor allocation or more host-side supply creation | 1-5 |

## Interpretation

| Total | Decision |
|---|---|
| 10-24 | Do not run unless there is a strategic learning reason |
| 25-34 | Run only as a manual learning pilot |
| 35-42 | Good MVP test with operator support |
| 43-48 | Strong candidate for repeatable playbook |
| 49-50 | Core motion candidate |

## Required Notes

For every score, write:

- the strongest evidence
- the biggest assumption
- the blocker most likely to kill repeatability
- the product object that should improve next

## Product Perspective

The best pilot is not always the highest-revenue pilot. The best pilot teaches Analog which market dynamics can become software:

- sponsor demand becomes campaign setup
- host trust becomes sponsor-ready profiles
- city context becomes catalog data
- spend behavior becomes integrity scoring
- proof becomes renewal logic
- missing assets become city lead tasks or market-formation strategy
