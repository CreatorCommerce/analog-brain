Use this rubric before and after a pilot. The goal is to decide whether a market dynamic deserves more product investment, more operator work, a different sponsor perspective, or no further pursuit.

Score each criterion from 1 to 5, then use the average score for the decision table. This keeps the rubric stable when criteria are added.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 1 | Weak or mostly assumed |
| 2 | Some evidence, but high friction |
| 3 | Works with operator help |
| 4 | Strong and repeatable with modest product support |
| 5 | Strong enough to become a core product motion |

## Rubric

| Criterion | What To Look For | Score |
|---|---|---|
| Sponsor budget clarity | Real buyer, real budget, clear approval path, and urgency | 1-5 |
| Sponsor perspective fit | The pilot clearly serves one of the three sponsor perspectives: eComm Brand, Tech Company, or Public/Non-profit Fund | 1-5 |
| Stream and causeway fit | The motion swims with existing community, sponsor, city lead, media, funding, repeat, and product streams instead of inventing all demand from zero | 1-5 |
| Host supply potency | Trust x monetization permission x repeat cadence x sponsor surface x data capture x operator reliability | 1-5 |
| Activation feasibility | Space, vendors, timing, permissions, insurance, staffing, and budget can clear | 1-5 |
| Consumer value | Sponsor money makes the experience cheaper, better, more useful, or more accessible | 1-5 |
| Proof readiness | Attendance, claims, content, social, spend, or attribution can be captured credibly | 1-5 |
| Spend integrity | Budget limits, card use, receipts, categories, timing, and anomalies can be watched | 1-5 |
| Community sentiment | The community is likely to welcome the sponsor, not treat the activation as extractive | 1-5 |
| Operator upside | The host has a clear path to earn, renew, or build a track record without fronting cash | 1-5 |
| Funding unlock strength | Sponsor funding would reduce burnout, raise quality, reactivate supply, or create more repeat participation | 1-5 |
| Data and catalog learning | The pilot enriches audience graph, catalog records, or asset-need intelligence | 1-5 |
| Media leverage | The story can help Analog document the gathering economy and bring more hosts, sponsors, or dot connectors into the tent | 1-5 |
| Owned IRL potential | Analog can use a meetup, salon, happy hour, live recording, or city lead gathering to deepen the market | 1-5 |
| Repeatability | The activation can become a series, template, host package, city playbook, or category loop | 1-5 |
| Product leverage | The workflow maps to durable product objects instead of bespoke services | 1-5 |
| Unlock potential | Success would justify a larger sponsor allocation or more host-side supply creation | 1-5 |

## Underwriting Lanes

When scoring proof, separate reporting from underwriting. Analog should learn whether the next dollar should be deployed, not only whether the last event happened.

| Lane | Question |
|---|---|
| Spend stewardship | Is this partner acting responsibly with sponsor capital? |
| Social growth | Are we reaching the sponsor's ICP through content, posts, and recap momentum? |
| Community operator upside | Is the partner happy, dependable, and earning enough to keep doing the work? |
| Category growth | Is the community increasing attendance, mentions, participation, or habit formation? |
| Brand growth | Are awareness, benefit claims, signups, sales lift, or downstream conversions moving? |
| Community sentiment | Was the sponsor presence additive, brand-safe, and welcomed? |

## Interpretation

| Average | Decision |
|---|---|
| 1.0-2.4 | Do not run unless there is a strategic learning reason |
| 2.5-3.4 | Run only as a manual learning pilot |
| 3.5-4.2 | Good MVP test with operator support |
| 4.3-4.8 | Strong candidate for repeatable playbook |
| 4.9-5.0 | Core motion candidate |

## Required Notes

For every score, write:

- the strongest evidence
- the biggest assumption
- the blocker most likely to kill repeatability
- the product object that should improve next

## Product Perspective

The best pilot is not always the highest-revenue pilot. The best pilot teaches Analog which market dynamics can become software:

- sponsor demand becomes campaign setup
- host trust becomes sponsor-ready profiles
- city context becomes catalog data
- spend behavior becomes integrity scoring
- proof becomes underwriting and renewal logic
- missing assets become city lead tasks or market-formation strategy
- strong streams become 12-month causeways
- field stories become media, owned IRL, and inbound supply

If a pilot scores low on revenue but high on catalog learning or asset-need learning, label it as a market-formation pilot instead of pretending it is a repeatable paid motion.

If a pilot scores high on media leverage or owned IRL potential, route it to [Media, Connectors, And Funding Engine](/strategy/media-owned-irl-engine) even when it is not ready for sponsor budget yet.

If a pilot scores high on stream and causeway fit, route it to [12-Month Market Focus](/strategy/twelve-month-market-focus) before expanding it, so the team can decide whether it belongs in the core 12-month agenda.
