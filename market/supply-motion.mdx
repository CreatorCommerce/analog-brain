Analog should not treat all supply work as the same job.

Sometimes the right motion is to access existing IRL supply. Sometimes the right motion is to create new supply because the market does not have enough usable hosts, spaces, operators, or sponsor surfaces yet. Sometimes the market exists, but it only becomes legible when Analog documents it, convenes it, and routes people back into product objects.

<Info>
Use this page when a sponsor, city lead, or operator asks: do we source what already exists, assemble something new, use media and owned IRL to reveal the market, or prospect off-platform supply into a campaign?
</Info>

## Operating Thesis

The seed-deck rule is simple: IRL demand is already exploding, and it has been mostly bootstrapped. Funding is the unlock that reduces burnout, reactivates dormant communities, raises event quality, and converts more first-timers into regulars.

The media rule is just as important: many of the best hosts and communities become legible only when Analog shows up, documents the scene, and runs its own IRL. Media creates supply by making people feel seen, bringing more organizers into the tent, and giving sponsors a clearer view of what is already happening.

For the 12-month focus, supply work should prioritize streams and causeways where sponsor budget, trusted hosts, city leads, media, and product proof compound. Use [12-Month Market Focus](/strategy/twelve-month-market-focus) before treating a new supply lane as core.

<Check>
The practical goal is not more lists. The goal is more sponsor-ready options that can become campaigns, event sets, host profiles, proofs, catalog records, content, funds, or unlocks.
</Check>

## Choose The Motion

<Tabs sync={false}>
  <Tab title="Access" icon="package-check">
    Use this motion when the event, community, space, or operator already exists and can become sponsor-ready with packaging.

    **Signals**

    - recurring attendance or strong one-time demand
    - clear host or owner
    - known audience or category
    - sponsor permission already exists
    - RSVP, ticketing, check-in, or claim data can be captured
    - the host can execute with a brief and spend card
    - the host already has enough trust that funding improves the ritual instead of manufacturing it

    **Actions**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Claim the record">
        Claim or create the catalog record and host profile.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Capture sponsor boundaries">
        Add sponsor preferences, category exclusions, trust constraints, and acceptable brand presence.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Add proof surfaces">
        Attach attendance, social, content, claims, prior sponsors, hosted pages, or manual proof.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Package the surface">
        Turn the host, venue, or recurring ritual into a sponsor-ready package.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Capture the media angle">
        When useful for the city, capture an interview, field note, social clip, newsletter feature, print profile, or documentary moment.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Route the opportunity">
        Route into a sponsor campaign, RFE, host-side Request for Sponsorship, or content follow-up.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Create" icon="wrench">
    Use this motion when sponsor demand exists but the right host-side option does not.

    **Signals**

    - sponsors want a category, city, or audience but no qualified host appears
    - local communities exist but do not have sponsor surfaces yet
    - spaces exist but no operator owns programming
    - hosts want to do something but lack venue, vendor, insurance, or funding
    - a city lead can assemble the missing parties
    - a strong community is stuck as a side hustle because organizers are fronting money, chasing reimbursements, or absorbing unpaid coordination work

    **Actions**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Name the missing asset">
        Identify whether the blocker is host, space, vendor, insurance, operator, sponsor, capital, or proof.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Recruit or assemble">
        Use grants, city leads, owned activations, partner lists, dot connectors, RFEs, or RFCs to create the missing option.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Start small">
        Run a smaller activation that can prove the operating model without creating a services trap.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Package the activation">
        Convert the activation into a reusable host package, catalog record, or sponsor brief.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Avoid fronting money">
        Create a no-fronting-money path when possible: approved funds, scope, spend card, proof expectations, and operator upside.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Use owned IRL when needed">
        Use Analog-owned IRL when the missing asset is trust, convening power, or local momentum.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Score before scaling">
        Score the result with [Testing Grid](/market/testing-grid) before asking sponsors for larger budget.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Publish" icon="radio">
    Use this motion when the city has community energy but the market is not yet legible enough for sponsors or the product.

    **Actions**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Pick the city thesis">
        Pick a city lead and a sponsor-aware city thesis.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Document what already happens">
        Capture communities, rituals, hosts, spaces, and amazing events already happening.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Submit field content">
        Route raw field content into Slack for the Head of Content and media team.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Publish through owned channels">
        Publish through social, `The Connector`, YouTube, regional print, and owned Analog gatherings.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Run owned gatherings">
        Run Analog-owned meetups for community leaders, dot connectors, hosts, and sponsors.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Route inbound back to product">
        Convert inbound interest into catalog records, host profiles, sponsor leads, RFEs, RFCs, or asset-need notes.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Note>
    This is not separate from supply work. It is how Analog earns the trust and local context needed to create supply city by city.
    </Note>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Prospect" icon="search">
    Use event set prospecting when the sponsor has a clear event set and the right hosts probably exist, but they are not on Analog yet.

    **Actions**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Create the campaign and event set">
        Define the campaign, event set, sponsor perspective, market persona, geography, and proof need.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Define special requests">
        Capture the market persona, special requests, host count, event count, budget range, and sponsor guardrails.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Build the prospect list">
        Source from the [Event Prospecting Catalog](/market/event-prospecting-catalog), public discovery, partner lists, city lead knowledge, or sponsor-known communities.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Route outreach to the application link">
        Keep outreach tied to the campaign application link so responses become structured.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Convert responses">
        Convert responses into applications, collabs, catalog updates, content follow-ups, or asset-need notes.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Running Vertical Playbook

Use running and adjacent movement communities as the first verticalized supply play when the city has enough club density.

<Card title="Why running first" icon="activity" horizontal>
  Running and walking communities create a clean proof loop: dense supply, recurring weekly rituals, obvious spend categories, clear brand demand, and social proof sponsors already understand.
</Card>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Invite clubs into a fund">
    Invite local clubs to apply to a sponsor or category fund.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Approve best-fit clubs">
    Approve based on trust, cadence, spend use, proof readiness, and community sentiment.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fund with controlled spend">
    Fund with branded spend cards, store credit, approved reimbursements, or other governed budget.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Activate obvious categories">
    Start with coffee, race entries, recovery, photography, perks, samples, and meetup supplies.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Prove the result">
    Capture spend, attendance, category lift, social moments, content, and community sentiment.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Renew the best operators">
    Renew top operators and let them earn more when proof and spend integrity are strong.
  </Step>
</Steps>

This creates a reusable proof loop before Analog generalizes into every community category.

## Decision Rule

Use this matrix when a sponsor brief, city lead idea, or host opportunity appears.

| Question | If yes | If no |
|---|---|---|
| Does a trusted host already exist? | Access supply | Create or recruit host supply |
| Is there a natural sponsor surface? | Package it | Design one with the host |
| Can we capture proof? | Run a pilot | Add hosted page, claim flow, check-in, or manual proof first |
| Can this repeat? | Score for unlock | Treat as a learning activation |
| Is the blocker local? | Use a city lead | Solve through product, sponsor, or operator motion |
| Is the blocker unknown? | Log an asset-need note | Keep researching until the missing asset is clear |
| Would funding create more good supply? | Create a fund, grant, or operator path | Access and package existing supply first |
| Would media make the market legible? | Use The Connector, social, YouTube, print, or owned IRL | Keep the motion inside product/prospecting |
| Does this swim with an existing stream? | Treat it as a 12-month causeway candidate | Keep it as a learning pilot until the stream is clearer |

## Output Router

Every supply motion should end with a concrete output.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="If the host already exists" icon="package-check">

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="If the market is missing an asset" icon="wrench">
    Create an [Asset Need Intelligence](/market/asset-need-intelligence) note and route it to city lead work, partner search, owned IRL, grant design, or product workflow.

  <Accordion title="If the story is strong" icon="radio">

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="If the sponsor brief is clear" icon="search">
    Route it into campaign setup, event set prospecting, application portal, host outreach, or RFE/RFC packaging.

  </Accordion>

## What To Avoid

<Warning>
Do not confuse supply work with busywork. A long list of events is not progress unless it becomes sponsor-ready options, proof, media, catalog data, asset-need intelligence, or a repeatable playbook.
</Warning>

Avoid:

- sourcing hosts without a sponsor-backed or causeway-backed ask
- creating one-off events that cannot teach the product
- treating city leads as generic event scouts
- forcing sponsor presence into communities without trust or permission
- building new supply when existing supply only needs packaging
- ignoring media when media would make the market legible
- scaling a supply lane before it passes the decision rule
