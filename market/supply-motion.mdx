Supply work has one job: turn sponsor demand and city context into sponsor-ready options.

Use this page to decide whether to access what already exists, create what is missing, make the market legible through media and owned IRL, or prospect off-platform supply into a campaign.

<Info>
Prefer the motion that produces a concrete product object fastest: catalog record, host profile, event set prospect, sponsor package, proof, fund, asset-need note, or unlock.
</Info>

## Operating Thesis

IRL demand already exists and has mostly been bootstrapped. Funding is the unlock that reduces burnout, reactivates dormant communities, raises event quality, and converts more first-timers into regulars.

Media also creates supply. Some of the best hosts become legible only when Analog shows up, documents the scene, runs its own IRL, and routes the resulting trust back into product objects.

<Check>
The goal is not more lists. The goal is more sponsor-ready options.
</Check>

## Choose The Motion

<Tabs sync={false}>
  <Tab title="Access" icon="package-check">
    Use when the supply already exists and can become sponsor-ready with packaging.

    | Signal | Action | Output |
    |---|---|---|
    | Recurring attendance, clear owner, known audience, sponsor permission, and captureable proof | Create or claim the catalog record, add sponsor boundaries, attach proof, and package the surface | Host profile, sponsor package, event set prospect, RFE, or Request for Sponsorship |
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Create" icon="wrench">
    Use when sponsor demand exists but the host-side option is missing or underbuilt.

    | Signal | Action | Output |
    |---|---|---|
    | Sponsor wants a category, city, or audience but no qualified host exists yet | Name the missing asset, assemble it through city leads, grants, owned IRL, partner lists, or dot connectors, then start with a small proofable activation | New host package, Activation Opportunity, fund hypothesis, asset-need note, or owned IRL lab |
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Publish" icon="radio">
    Use when a city has energy but the market is not yet legible.

    | Signal | Action | Output |
    |---|---|---|
    | Strong communities, rituals, or builders exist, but sponsors do not understand the scene | Capture field notes, interviews, social clips, `The Connector` items, video, print, and owned gatherings | Inbound hosts, connector profiles, catalog records, sponsor leads, and city thesis |
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Prospect" icon="search">
    Use when the sponsor brief is clear and the right supply probably exists off-platform.

    | Signal | Action | Output |
    |---|---|---|
    | Campaign, event set, market persona, geography, budget, and proof need are defined | Build a prospect list from the catalog, public discovery, partner lists, city lead knowledge, and sponsor-known communities | Applications, collabs, catalog updates, host outreach, or asset-need notes |
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Running First

Running and adjacent movement communities are the first verticalized supply play when a city has enough club density.

<Card title="Why it works" icon="activity" horizontal>
  Dense supply, recurring rituals, obvious spend categories, clear sponsor demand, and social proof sponsors already understand.
</Card>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Invite clubs into a fund">
    Start with a sponsor or category fund and clear eligibility.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Approve best-fit operators">
    Score trust, cadence, spend use, proof readiness, and community sentiment.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fund obvious needs">
    Coffee, race entries, recovery, photography, perks, samples, and meetup supplies.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Prove and renew">
    Capture spend, attendance, content, category lift, and sentiment. Renew the best operators.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Decision Rule

| Question | If yes | If no |
|---|---|---|
| Does a trusted host already exist? | Access and package supply | Create or recruit host supply |
| Is there a sponsor surface? | Package it | Design one with the host |
| Can proof be captured? | Run a pilot | Add hosted page, claim flow, check-in, or manual proof first |
| Is the blocker local? | Use a city lead | Solve through product, sponsor, or operator motion |
| Would media make it legible? | Route to media and owned IRL | Keep inside product/prospecting |
| Does it swim with an existing stream? | Score as a 12-month causeway candidate | Keep it as a learning pilot |

## Output Router

Every supply motion should end in one of these outputs.

| Situation | Route to |
|---|---|
| Host already exists | Catalog record, host profile, proof surface, sponsor package |
| Market is missing an asset | [Asset Need Intelligence](/market/asset-need-intelligence), city lead task, partner search, grant, or owned IRL lab |
| Story is strong | [Media And Owned IRL Engine](/strategy/media-owned-irl-engine), connector profile, `The Connector`, social, YouTube, or print |
| Sponsor brief is clear | Campaign, event set, application portal, host outreach, RFE, or RFC |
| Motion looks repeatable | [Testing Grid](/market/testing-grid) and [12-Month Market Focus](/strategy/twelve-month-market-focus) |

## Avoid

<Warning>
A long list of events is not progress unless it becomes sponsor-ready options, proof, media, catalog data, asset-need intelligence, or a repeatable playbook.
</Warning>

- Do not source hosts without a sponsor-backed or causeway-backed ask.
- Do not create one-off events that cannot teach the product.
- Do not treat city leads as generic event scouts.
- Do not force sponsor presence into communities without trust or permission.
- Do not build new supply when existing supply only needs packaging.
