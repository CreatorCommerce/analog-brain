Analog should measure not only what happened, but what almost happened and why it did not clear.

The strongest market data often comes from blocked opportunities:

- a sponsor has budget, but no trusted host in the city
- a community has demand, but no affordable space
- a space has capacity, but no operator
- an event has audience, but no insurance path
- a host has a strong idea, but no vendor, proof, or sponsor
- a location can create consumer value, but no claim flow exists yet

Asset Need Intelligence turns those blockers into a map of what IRL infrastructure the market needs next.

## Missing Asset Types

| Missing asset | What it means | Possible response |
|---|---|---|
| Community hole | Sponsor, space, or consumer demand exists, but trusted host supply is thin | Grants, starter kits, city leads, organizer cohorts, Play Fair acquisition |
| Space hole | Communities and sponsors exist, but usable spaces are scarce or expensive | Venue partnerships, off-peak programming, controlled access, location activation |
| Insurance hole | Risk, COI, permit, waiver, or coverage friction blocks execution | Licensed partner workflow, broker/MGA program, COI automation |
| Operator hole | The idea and demand exist, but no one can reliably run it | Local operator bench, producer support, city lead, managed pilot |
| Vendor hole | Food, photo, staffing, rentals, security, print, or production cannot clear | Vendor network, service provider catalog, preferred packages |
| Services/tools hole | Insurance, contracts, print-on-demand, local design, vendor routing, or proof kits are missing | Product workflow, partner workflow, contractor bench, template docs, or managed support |
| Sponsor hole | Host supply exists, but sponsor demand or fit is missing | Request for Sponsorship, brand prospecting, category sales, CC demand bridge |
| Nonprofit distribution hole | A nonprofit, foundation, or NGO has mission capital or program goals, but lacks trusted downstream community operators | Downstream fund lane, partner applications, city lead sourcing, eligibility review, proof templates |
| Proof hole | The activation can happen, but sponsor-trusted proof is weak | Hosted page, import, check-in, claim flow, social proof, Analog Doc |
| Capital hole | The activation needs upfront cash, float, grant, or working capital | Grant, sponsor allocation, civic fund, or future compliant finance workflow |
| Public-trust hole | Public or NGO money exists, but eligibility, fraud/waste/abuse controls, or reporting are weak | Public fund design, controlled cards, spend restrictions, receipt proof, anomaly checks, and public reporting |

## Asset Need Index

Score each geography, category, or activation type on:

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| Demand pressure | Are sponsors, consumers, hosts, or spaces asking for this? |
| Community supply | Are there reliable hosts with cadence and trust? |
| Space availability | Are affordable, sponsor-safe spaces usable? |
| Insurance accessibility | Can risk, COIs, waivers, and permits clear? |
| Sponsor demand | Is there budget and a buyer with proof expectations? |
| Operator capacity | Who can run the activation repeatedly? |
| Vendor coverage | Are required vendors available and priced reasonably? |
| Attribution readiness | Can invite, RSVP, check-in, claim, or outcome data be captured? |
| Capital readiness | Should this be solved with software, grants, partner capital, or owned/controlled assets? |
| Social impact | Does the gap matter for civic, wellness, affordability, youth, climate, arts, or local commerce? |

## Logging Rule

Whenever an activation stalls, log:

- what the sponsor, host, or operator wanted to do
- which asset was missing
- whether the opportunity was abandoned, delayed, restructured, funded, or solved manually
- what would have unblocked it
- whether the gap is local, category-specific, seasonal, regulated, capital-related, or trust-related
- whether the next response should be marketplace matching, product work, partner workflow, grant, city lead, operator support, or capital strategy

## How It Changes Market Formation

Use this page when deciding whether to access existing supply or create new supply.

| Repeated signal | Product or market response |
|---|---|
| High sponsor demand, weak community supply | Run grants or city lead sourcing. |
| High community supply, weak space access | Build venue/location partnerships. |
| Strong host demand, weak sponsor fit | Package Requests for Sponsorship and use sponsor prospecting. |
| Strong activation but weak proof | Add hosted page, claim, import, or proof templates. |
| High risk friction | Explore licensed insurance partners or compliance workflows. |
| Repeated print/design friction | Build print-on-demand templates, local designer bench, approved vendors, and proof-photo requirements. |
| High commercial repeatability with fragmented operators | Consider operator network, managed program, or future capital strategy. |
| Public money wants speed but needs controls | Build eligibility, spend governance, public reporting, and anomaly review before promising outcome scale. |
| Nonprofit has mission capital but weak local reach | Build a downstream Host funding program with applications, support needs, proof templates, and donor/board reporting. |

Asset Need Intelligence should make Analog smarter about where to deploy grants, city leads, Play Fair, partner APIs, managed pilots, and product development.
