City leads are local operators who help Analog see and create options the product cannot discover from a dashboard alone.

Their job is not only to source events. Their job is to run the supply side of the local Analog business and create more host-side paths that can unlock sponsor budget.

The seed deck makes city leads especially important because the best supply is often bootstrapped, fragile, and underfunded. A city lead should find where funding could turn existing trust into more repeatable, sponsor-ready supply.

City leads are also Analog's field media nodes. They should circulate through communities, record interviews, gather audio or video, submit raw material to the content team, and run their own community-leader gatherings that dogfood Analog's product and media loop.

They should also identify which dot connectors and community builders deserve to become visible local talent. The strongest candidates should move into the [Media, Connectors, And Funding Engine](/strategy/media-owned-irl-engine) talent ladder.

For the first six months, focus this model on AnalogNYC. SF should come next only after NYC has enough proof. Use [Lean GTM Operating Model](/strategy/lean-gtm-operating-model) and [Dogfooding Analog](/strategy/dogfooding-analog) for the staffing and dogfood sequence.

## City Lead KPIs

City lead KPIs should mirror the customer KPIs Analog promises.

| KPI | City lead responsibility |
|---|---|
| Social growth | Create field moments, interviews, recaps, and local stories that help AnalogNYC grow audience and cultural pull. |
| Category growth | Strengthen the chosen IRL category through more participation, repeat Hosts, better rituals, and clearer sponsor-ready supply. |
| Community perspective | Capture whether Hosts, connectors, and attendees see Analog as additive, tasteful, and useful. |
| Direct response | Turn city relationships into invite RSVPs, claims, applications, replies, and attendance. |
| Spend transparency | Make sure AnalogNYC spend is categorized, attached to the right event or campaign, and supported by receipts or notes. |
| Supply velocity | Move candidates into catalog records, Host profiles, event sets, Activation Opportunities, and sponsor-ready packages. |

## What A City Lead Does

A city lead maps:

- existing communities and recurring rituals
- space owners and underused venues
- dot connectors
- creators and local tastemakers
- vendors, photo, production, security, and rentals
- insurance or permit blockers
- sponsor perspectives that make sense locally: eComm Brands, Tech Companies, or Public/Non-profit Funds
- public or non-profit funding sources
- local media stories, interview subjects, and documentary moments

Then the city lead turns that map into host-side options:

- a sponsor-ready host profile
- a catalog record with provenance
- a Request for Sponsorship
- a venue-anchored series
- a creator-led activation
- a grant candidate
- a Request for Communities response
- an Analog-owned activation lab
- an asset-need note for missing space, insurance, vendor, proof, operator, sponsor, or capital
- a field story for social, `The Connector`, print, YouTube, or an owned Analog gathering
- a connector profile or rockstar candidate

## Host Unlock Ladder

Use this ladder to move a host from interesting to unlockable.

| Stage | Host state | City lead action | Unlock target |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Found | Host, space, or connector exists | Capture facts and trust context | Catalog record |
| 2. Legible | Audience, cadence, surface, and constraints are known | Build host profile and sponsor boundaries | Sponsor-ready review |
| 3. Packaged | There is a concrete activation or series | Draft Request for Sponsorship or campaign fit | First sponsor allocation |
| 4. Proved | Activation happened and proof exists | Collect spend, attendance, content, claims, interviews, and blockers | Renewal or bigger allocation |
| 5. Repeatable | Host can run again with less operator help | Create playbook and benchmark | City or category expansion |
| 6. Amplified | Host or scene becomes a story others can join | Route to social, `The Connector`, YouTube, print, or owned IRL | More inbound supply and sponsor demand |
| 7. Rockstar | Connector or builder becomes trusted public talent | Build profile, proof ledger, media arc, owned IRL stage, and paid opportunities | Compounding connector network |

## When To Use A City Lead

Use a city lead when:

- the product knows sponsor demand but lacks enough host options
- the best supply is relationship-driven or not public
- venues and spaces are the bottleneck
- trust judgment matters more than search volume
- a city needs new supply created, not just sourced
- public or non-profit money needs local legitimacy
- Analog needs field media and community-leader relationships, not only a list of prospects

## Practical Workflow

1. Give the city lead a sponsor or category target.
2. Ask for 10 to 25 candidate hosts, spaces, or dot connectors.
3. Tag each candidate with supply potency: trust, monetization permission, cadence, sponsor surface, data capture, and operator reliability.
4. Identify whether funding would reduce burnout, improve quality, reactivate a dormant group, or convert first-timers into regulars.
5. Capture field media: short interviews, event footage, audio notes, photos, and story context when permission is clear.
6. Submit content to the shared Slack intake for the Head of Content and media team.
7. Choose 3 to 5 candidates for packaging.
8. Turn each into one of: host profile, Request for Sponsorship, grant candidate, location activation, creator-led activation, or media story.
9. Use a small pilot budget, sponsor allocation, agency-backed activation, or owned AnalogNYC meetup to test proof.
10. Categorize spend against the correct campaign, event set, Host event, merchant category, and proof expectation.
11. Score the result with [Testing Grid](/market/testing-grid).
12. Send repeated blockers to [Asset Need Intelligence](/market/asset-need-intelligence).
13. Route the strongest stories to [Media, Connectors, And Funding Engine](/strategy/media-owned-irl-engine).

## Media And Owned IRL Responsibilities

City leads should help Analog publish the market, not only source it.

Their media responsibilities:

- record interviews with community leaders, hosts, space owners, and dot connectors
- capture video, audio, photos, and field notes from real gatherings
- document amazing events and communities already happening everywhere
- identify the sponsor perspective without forcing sponsor presence into fragile communities
- submit raw material and context in Slack for the content team
- help the Head of Content understand regional taste, norms, and trust risks

Their owned-IRL responsibilities:

- run happy hours for community leaders
- host city lead salons, walks, dinners, or live recordings
- convene hosts, spaces, sponsors, and dot connectors around a city thesis
- use Analog's own event, proof, and recap motion whenever possible
- turn each owned gathering into content, catalog learning, and new host-side options

## What To Avoid

- Treating the city lead as a generic event scout.
- Prioritizing culturally fragile communities where sponsor permission is weak.
- Creating one-off events that cannot teach the product.
- Ignoring spaces, vendors, and insurance when those are the real blockers.

The city lead should make the host side more liquid by turning local context into structured product objects.
