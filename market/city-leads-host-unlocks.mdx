City leads are local operators who help Analog see and create options the product cannot discover from a dashboard alone.

Their job is not only to source events. Their job is to create more host-side paths that can unlock sponsor budget.

## What A City Lead Does

A city lead maps:

- existing communities and recurring rituals
- space owners and underused venues
- dot connectors
- creators and local tastemakers
- vendors, photo, production, security, and rentals
- insurance or permit blockers
- sponsor categories that make sense locally
- public or non-profit funding sources

Then the city lead turns that map into host-side options:

- a sponsor-ready host profile
- a Request for Sponsorship
- a venue-anchored series
- a creator-led activation
- a grant candidate
- a Request for Communities response
- an Analog-owned activation lab

## Host Unlock Ladder

Use this ladder to move a host from interesting to unlockable.

| Stage | Host state | City lead action | Unlock target |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Found | Host, space, or connector exists | Capture facts and trust context | Catalog record |
| 2. Legible | Audience, cadence, surface, and constraints are known | Build host profile and sponsor boundaries | Sponsor-ready review |
| 3. Packaged | There is a concrete activation or series | Draft Request for Sponsorship or campaign fit | First sponsor allocation |
| 4. Proved | Activation happened and proof exists | Collect spend, attendance, content, claims, and blockers | Renewal or bigger allocation |
| 5. Repeatable | Host can run again with less operator help | Create playbook and benchmark | City or category expansion |

## When To Use A City Lead

Use a city lead when:

- the product knows sponsor demand but lacks enough host options
- the best supply is relationship-driven or not public
- venues and spaces are the bottleneck
- trust judgment matters more than search volume
- a city needs new supply created, not just sourced
- public or non-profit money needs local legitimacy

## Practical Workflow

1. Give the city lead a sponsor or category target.
2. Ask for 10 to 25 candidate hosts, spaces, or dot connectors.
3. Tag each candidate with supply potency: trust, monetization permission, cadence, sponsor surface, data capture, and operator reliability.
4. Choose 3 to 5 candidates for packaging.
5. Turn each into one of: host profile, Request for Sponsorship, grant candidate, location activation, or creator-led activation.
6. Use a small pilot budget or sponsor allocation to test proof.
7. Score the result with [Testing Grid](/market/testing-grid).

## What To Avoid

- Treating the city lead as a generic event scout.
- Prioritizing culturally fragile communities where sponsor permission is weak.
- Creating one-off events that cannot teach the product.
- Ignoring spaces, vendors, and insurance when those are the real blockers.

The city lead should make the host side more liquid by turning local context into structured product objects.
