Analog should use one Market Agent with city packs.

Do not create one agent per city by default. A city pack gives the Market Agent scoped local context without multiplying repos, memories, or operating rules.

## What A City Pack Is

A city pack is a reviewed market artifact for one city or metro.

It should help agents answer:

- what is happening here?
- which communities, venues, connectors, and operators matter?
- what Sponsor categories fit the market?
- what grants, funds, or public programs may matter?
- what local risks, exclusions, or trust constraints matter?
- what should Analog test first?

## Standard Shape

```yaml
city:
metro:
status: proposed | active | stale | retired
owner:
last_reviewed:
confidence: low | medium | high
source_summary:
primary_sponsor_lanes:
host_supply_pockets:
connector_map:
venue_and_space_patterns:
funding_and_grant_context:
market_risks:
no_go_rules:
first_tests:
open_questions:
stale_claims:
linked_sources:
linked_product_records:
```

## Sections

| Section | What it should contain |
|---|---|
| Market snapshot | Current read on the local IRL, community, sponsorship, and funding environment. |
| Sponsor lanes | eComm, SaaS/tech, civic/nonprofit, political action, or other Sponsor demand that fits the city. |
| Host supply pockets | Communities, operators, spaces, event types, and recurring rituals that could become fundable. |
| Connector map | People or organizations that create access, intros, field judgment, or cultural translation. |
| Venue and space patterns | Places that shape what is possible, expensive, restricted, or culturally native. |
| Grants and funds | Public, nonprofit, foundation, BID, university, hospital, or private funding signals. |
| Proof standards | What proof a Sponsor, public funder, or internal operator would need to renew. |
| Risks and no-go rules | Brand-safety, community trust, compliance, public sensitivity, or operational blockers. |
| First tests | Three to five practical pilots or research motions. |
| Sources | Links, source notes, product records, field notes, and confidence labels. |

## City Pack Lifecycle

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Proposed | Useful hypothesis, not enough reviewed evidence yet. |
| Active | Reviewed enough to guide operators and agents. |
| Stale | Important claims need refresh before use. |
| Retired | Replaced by a new pack or no longer relevant. |

Mark claims stale when:

- a source is older than the market's review rhythm
- a grant or program deadline passed
- the local operator says the context changed
- a product pilot contradicts the pack
- a Sponsor or Host trust concern appears

## Market Agent Use

The Market Agent should:

- retrieve the relevant city pack before generic market advice
- label confidence and stale claims
- separate public-safe context from partner-private context
- suggest updates when new field notes or product usage contradict the pack
- produce short snapshots for GTM, product, finance, and people planning

It should not:

- pretend public signals are verified truth
- treat one field note as city doctrine
- expose raw private product data to broad market context
- create city agents without operational volume

## When A City Becomes More Than A Pack

Consider a dedicated market repo or service only when the city has:

- a named owner or city lead
- recurring data ingestion
- active Sponsor or fund demand
- repeated field notes and product records
- city-specific operational playbooks
- scheduled refresh or API needs
- enough volume that one city pack is no longer readable

Until then, use one Market Agent and one city pack.

## First Packs

Start with:

| City | Why |
|---|---|
| NYC | First operating proof and dense supply/media market. |
| SF | Transfer market with tech, founder, SaaS, and operator-room relevance. |
| Miami | Add when Sponsor demand, public funding, or field access creates a concrete test. |

The pack is not the market. It is the working folder the Market Agent gets before it helps Analog make a local judgment.
